# Callings v3 — Story Arcs

*The design spine for the v3 rebuild. Each calling is an arc the character is carried along; the beats are derived from it. Minor beats are the texture of Act One (and persist throughout); major beats are Act Two's turning points; the two zenith beats are Act Three's endings — one fulfilment, one inversion, neither named, neither privileged.*

---

## Hunger — The Chase

**Act One · The Trail.** Life lived lead to lead: rumours bought, rivals glimpsed, small scores standing in for the big one, debts accumulating quietly behind.

**Act Two · The Price.** The trades become sacrifices. The prize turns out to be real — and guarded, cursed, or worse than the stories. Rivals become enemies; the hunt starts costing other people.

**Act Three · The Door.** Either claim the thing and pay whatever it asks, or stand before it at last and discover the wanting was the only part that was yours — and turn away.

## Monument — The Build

**Act One · Foundations.** Influence planted port by port: stories seeded, favours banked, a name that travels ahead of the ship.

**Act Two · Load.** The structure starts bearing weight. Rivals test the walls, legitimacy arrives with strings, and a trusted brick turns out to be hollow.

**Act Three · The Standing.** Either step back and watch the work hold without you — or refuse to let anyone else have it, and bring it down with your own hands.

## Vendetta — The Hunt of One

**Act One · The Trail of a Name.** Circling: intelligence gathered, proxies struck, messages left where the target will find them.

**Act Two · The Complication.** The target answers back. The hatred meets inconvenient facts; the cost starts landing on the crew; someone else turns out to be hunting *you*.

**Act Three · The Eye.** Face to face at last. Either settle the account in full — or, with them finally in your power, discover what you are without it, and let them go.

## Freedom — The Unfinished Escape

**Act One · Free Air.** Living loud and unowned: chains broken wherever met, every leash refused, the old owner a shadow on the horizon.

**Act Two · The Reach of the Leash.** The shadow moves. The price on you changes hands; your freedom starts costing others theirs; you find chains you chose without noticing.

**Act Three · The Open Water.** Either bring down the thing that owned you and stand in the ashes — or sail beyond every claim there is, and learn what is left when nothing holds you.

## Cursed Tides — The Claim

**Act One · The Whisper.** Signs, dreams and small obediences. The water is right too often to ignore.

**Act Two · The Demand.** The deep recognises you. The asks grow teeth; the crew pays for your listening; you learn how the previously-called ended.

**Act Three · The Surface.** Either answer in full — go beneath, and do not return as anything the world knows — or tear the claim out of yourself and live with the silence.

## Legacy — The Inheritance

**Act One · The Wake.** Sailing in someone else's story: recognition before introduction, doors that open and slam, expectations worn like borrowed clothes.

**Act Two · The Truth of the Line.** The buried thing surfaces — the crime, the bargain, the heir. The living consequences arrive with dues, and you must choose with or against the blood.

**Act Three · The Name.** Either become the one the line is remembered by, eclipsing everyone who came before — or pay its last debt, burn its flag, and sail out from under the name.

## Fellowship — The Bond Tested

**Act One · The Weave.** Becoming the keel: trust built watch by watch, costs quietly absorbed, the crew turning from shipmates into the only thing that matters.

**Act Two · The Strain.** The sea finds the seam. Loss, betrayal and rifts test whether the devotion is strength or a hostage; what you took on for them starts coming due.

**Act Three · The Last Watch.** Either give everything in one moment so the crew sails on — or outlast them, and find out what you are when the thing that made you whole is gone.

## Piracy — The Crew's Story

The shared calling carries the whole table through one campaign-length arc: a crew rises from nothing, the Shackles takes notice, and the notice must be answered. Its minor beats are the texture of the life; its major beats are the rungs of the rise; its two zeniths end the campaign — the crew carved permanently into the Shackles, or scattered the way all crews end. The variations below are the same skeleton wearing different weather; the beats serve all of them.

**Variation One · The Rise.** A prize too big to ignore pulls the crew into Free Captains politics. Act One: no-names working the margins. Act Two: courted, used and tested by the powers of Port Peril; every favour is a hook. Act Three: a seat, a fleet and a flag of their own — or devoured by the institutions they tried to join.

**Variation Two · The Hunt.** An early score wakes a patient enemy — a Chelish admiral, an Aspis ledger that never forgets. Act One: the crew spends freely, unaware of the price accruing. Act Two: ports close one by one; allies are bought out; the map shrinks. Act Three: break the hunter, outlast them — or sail off the edge of every chart to be free of them.

**Variation Three · The Score.** A legend-prize — a treasure, a ghost-fleet, a drowned vault — that keeps being *almost* true. Act One: chasing fragments while paying the bills with ordinary piracy. Act Two: seasons of preparation; rival crews converge; the final fragment costs someone everything. Act Three: the score remakes the crew's world — or ruins every hand that touched it.
