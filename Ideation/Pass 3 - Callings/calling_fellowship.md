# FELLOWSHIP

*Other pirates sail for gold or glory. You sail for the faces around the watch-lantern — these people, this ship, this unrepeatable luck. They are the only wealth you have ever counted. The sea knows exactly how to hurt you, and one day it will.*

## CORE ABILITY

**THE BODY BETWEEN.** What hits them hits you first, if you're fast enough. Once per session, when another PC or named crewmember within reach marks stress, you may mark it instead of them.

When you create your character, answer these questions:

- Choose another player character. You would die for them without a thought. What made it true — and do they know?
- Choose another player character. You failed them once, badly. What changed in you?
- Who did you lose before this crew, and what of theirs do you still carry?
- The ports say this crew holds together because of you. What did people see that started that story?

## MINOR BEATS

- Take Minor Luck fallout.
- Put your body between a crewmate and harm.
- Learn a secret a crewmate has told no one.
- Share true rest with the crew: a meal, a calm watch, a shore night.
- Make the call the crew hates, and carry the friction.
- Quietly cover another PC's mistake.
- Acquire a bond who becomes family, not contact.
- Pass up a windfall that would mean leaving someone.
- Tell a crewmate the truth they didn't want.
- Cheer another PC's victory louder than your own.
- Grieve for one of the crew's dead.
- Take blame that wasn't yours.
- Be seen at your worst by the people you hold up.
- Fight with a crewmate over values, not tactics.
- Keep everyone moving while everything burns.
- Fail someone who needed you, and face them.
- Suffer fallout from stress you took for someone else.
- Vouch for a stranger with your own standing.
- Pay a crew debt out of your own share, silently.
- Be caught keeping count of everything that could take them.

## MAJOR BEATS

- Take Major Luck fallout.
- Save a crewmate when the sensible call was letting go.
- Lose one of the crew for good.
- Learn something that tests your loyalty to its limit.
- Hold the crew together through shipwreck, mutiny or massacre.
- Tear the crew apart, and mend what you tore.
- Give up safety, fortune or freedom so the crew keeps theirs.
- Take back someone who truly wronged you.
- Refuse honours that would split the crew.
- Build something with them that outlasts the voyage: a code, a tradition, a haunt of your own.

## ZENITH BEATS

- Give everything you have, in one moment, so the crew sails on.
- Outlive them — scattered, sunk or sailed on — and carry what remains.

Roll 1d10 on the following table or choose to determine what you carry with you to mark your calling:

1. Small things they gave you, kept secretly together
2. A letter never sent, added to for months
3. Every name you've ever sailed with, one word beside each
4. A carved figure of a crewmate, never shown
5. A dead crew's tradition, kept alone
6. A scar a friend gave you and doesn't know about
7. An old crew's song, sung badly, alone
8. The crew's accounts, quietly balanced from your own share for years
9. Something of the lost one's, worn out of sight
10. A memorial of this crew's deeds, written for whoever reads it after you
