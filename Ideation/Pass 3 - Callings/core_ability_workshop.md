# Core Ability Workshop — Callings v3

*Working document. Diagnoses the info-tap problem, audits the v3 calling cores, offers a large possibility list for the replacements, and lists the classes carrying the same disease.*

---

## The Problem, Named

The failing abilities all share one shape: **the payload is information, not mechanics.** "The GM tells you a true rumour." "The GM tells you the greatest obstacle." "Establish a fact; the GM incorporates it." These read well and do almost nothing at the table — they are GM-prompts wearing an ability's clothes, the same disease as the Sharper's information empire (NOSE FOR GOLD, THE BRIBED HARBOUR, WHAT THE WALLS HEARD).

Heart's five calling cores never do this. Every one moves dice, stress, fallout or protection: LEGENDARY refreshes, COLLATERAL reallocates, UNORTHODOX METHODS forces a result, NOT YET suspends fallout, IN THE BLOOD adds protection and reroutes stress.

**The test for every candidate below:** the mechanical effect is statable in one sentence; it touches dice, stress, fallout, protection or the resource economy; it fires reliably (no whiff, no GM mood); information is never the payload.

## Audit of the v3 Cores

| Calling | Ability | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| Hunger | THE SCENT | ~~Replace~~ **Resolved:** ONE MORE THROW (reroll with escalating stress — the set's seventh verb) |
| Monument | FOUNDATIONS | ~~Replace~~ **Resolved:** BRICK BY BRICK (LEGENDARY port) |
| Vendetta | THE ACCOUNT | ~~Replace~~ **Resolved:** NOT WHILE THEY BREATHE (NOT YET port; Mind→Reputation per the project remap) |
| Freedom | UNCHAINED | ~~Replace~~ **Resolved:** NEVER AGAIN (COLLATERAL port) |
| Cursed Tides | SALT IN THE VEINS | **Keep** — direct Heartsong port |
| Legacy | THE NAME REMEMBERS | ~~Replace~~ **Resolved:** IN THEIR FOOTSTEPS (UNORTHODOX METHODS port; skill grant = a skill the ancestor was known for) |
| Fellowship | THE BODY BETWEEN | **Keep** — COLLATERAL inverted; mirrored pair with Freedom |

All seven cores are now Heart ports or Heart-derived. Hunger is the only open slot — candidates remain in the list below.

The Freedom/Fellowship mirror is the anchor of the new set: **Fellowship takes the crew's stress onto itself; Freedom hands its stress to the crew.** Devotion and liberation as the same mechanic running opposite directions — and both are native Heart grammar (COLLATERAL, from Forced).

## Possibility List

### HUNGER — Focused Iteration (the open slot)

**The constraint.** With the other six cores assigned, every Heart core structure is now spoken for:

| Calling | Mechanical verb |
|---|---|
| Monument | Refresh on advances (LEGENDARY) |
| Vendetta | Suspend fallout effects (NOT YET) |
| Freedom | Hand stress away (COLLATERAL) |
| Fellowship | Take stress in (COLLATERAL inverted) |
| Legacy | Force a 6 + skill grant (UNORTHODOX METHODS) |
| Cursed Tides | Protection + stress reroute (IN THE BLOOD) |
| **Hunger** | **open** |

So Hunger either reuses a structure with a sharply different trigger, or contributes a verb the set doesn't have yet — still in Heart grammar.

**Collision check.** The Sharper's cores both ride Luck-as-gamble (banked dice, threshold bets). Hunger should not feel like playing a Sharper: avoid banked results and Luck-threshold coin flips. The differentiator: Sharper manufactures certainty; Hunger refuses to stop.

**Candidates, by axis:**

*The Taking — acquisition as fuel*

1. **WANT IS A COMPASS.** When you acquire something you have openly coveted, refresh D6. If it cost you blood, danger or a friend, refresh D8. *(Refresh verb reused from Monument, but fiction-triggered rather than advance-triggered. Safest pick; fires as often as the player schemes.)*
2. **FINDERS KEEPERS.** When you steal, win or claim something of D6 value or higher, clear D4 from Luck or Gold. *(Smaller, fires constantly; almost invisible bookkeeping.)*
3. **GLUTTON'S CHOICE.** Once per session, when you claim a prize, choose: refresh D6, or the prize's resource dice steps up one size. *(Acquisition with teeth — heal or fatten the take.)*

*The Wanting — declared desire as engine*

4. **APPETITE.** At the start of each session, name something you want and do not have. Roll with mastery the first time you act directly to take it. *(The want is mechanical; light, ritualised, table-visible.)*
5. **MARKED FOR ME.** Once per session, declare something in the scene yours. Until you hold it or the session ends, +1 protection on Luck and Gold; if the session ends without it, mark D4 Luck. *(Wanting protects you; leaving empty-handed costs. Two clauses — at the edge of the budget.)*

*The Spending — expenditure as fuel*

6. **THE EMPTY HOLD.** Once per session, consume or give away a resource and clear its rolled value from Luck or Gold. *(Spend the stash to stay standing; makes hoarding vs burning a live decision every session.)*
7. **PAID IN PROMISE.** Once per session, buy anything purchasable by marking Luck instead of Gold. *(Narrow — only fires when shopping.)*

*The Overreach — refusal to stop, priced*

8. **ONE MORE THROW.** Once per session, after failing a roll in pursuit of something you want, reroll it. If you fail again, the incoming stress steps up one size. *(A verb the set lacks entirely: the refusal to accept no. The mechanic IS the pathology — and the world hits harder when it says no twice.)*
9. **NO SUCH THING AS TOO FAR.** When an action would carry you measurably closer to what you want, you may treat Risky as Standard; mark D4 Luck each time. *(Quality-grammar; always-on, always priced.)*
10. **ALL IN.** Once per session, stake a resource before rolling: success steps its dice up one size, failure destroys it. *(Flagged — gambling resources is Sharper territory.)*

**Shortlist.**

- **ONE MORE THROW** — best for set diversity (the only reroll in the system's cores) and the truest expression of insatiability. Risk: rerolls slow the table slightly.
- **WANT IS A COMPASS** — best fiction-trigger; makes players narrate coveting, which makes Hunger visible at the table. Risk: refresh verb shared with Monument.
- **THE EMPTY HOLD** — best economy hook; turns the Gold/Luck relationship into the calling's heartbeat. Risk: rewards generosity as much as greed unless worded tightly.

### MONUMENT (Reputation)

1. **SPEND THE LEGEND.** Once per session, mark D4 Reputation to roll with mastery on any Command, Deceive or Carouse action — the name does the work, and the name wears down.
2. **ON CREDIT.** Once per session, buy anything purchasable by marking Reputation instead of Gold. Your name is currency.
3. **THE NAME ARRIVES FIRST.** In any port that has heard of you, treat Risky social actions as Standard.
4. **WITNESSES.** When you succeed at a Risky or Dangerous action in front of a crowd, clear D4 Reputation.
5. **BRICKS IN THE WALL.** When you donate to a haunt, it gains two upgrade marks instead of one, and your Reputation refresh from the donation rolls one size higher.
6. **THE STRUCTURE HOLDS.** Once per session, transfer up to D6 of your stress, any track, into the structure: the GM records it as a problem somewhere in your holdings, to surface later.
7. **MORTAR.** Your bonds absorb up to D10 stress instead of D8, and you may hold four bonds instead of three.
8. **UNFINISHED BUSINESS.** When you suffer Reputation fallout, you may pay an equal Gold cost to downgrade it one step — buy the story back.
9. **LOAD-BEARING.** +1 Reputation protection. +2 instead while you visibly hold something — a berth, a route, a council favour.
10. **FEAR IS CHEAPER.** When you compel someone under your true name, roll with mastery; on a failure, mark D4 Reputation.

### VENDETTA (Blood)

1. **THE ACCOUNT, UPGRADED.** Once per session, when you act directly against your target, clear D6 Blood — and step it to D8 the first time they hurt you back this session.
2. **WHETSTONE.** When your target or their agents inflict Blood stress on you, reduce it by 1 (minimum 1). The wound knows its maker.
3. **SWORN.** Your stress dice against your target and their agents increase one size.
4. **KEPT ALIVE BY IT.** Once per situation, when you would mark stress to any other resistance while pursuing your vendetta, mark it to Blood instead. *(Route everything into the wound.)*
5. **THE RECKONING FUND.** When you refuse peace, settlement or payment connected to your vendetta, refresh D6.
6. **PATIENT KNIFE.** Once per session, before rolling against someone who serves your target, state your result is a 6. You succeed, but take stress. *(UNORTHODOX METHODS, narrowed.)*
7. **PRACTISED.** The first time each session you fight your target's people, roll with mastery — you have rehearsed this.
8. **NOTHING THEY CAN DO TO ME.** Once per session, ignore the effects of Minor Blood fallout until the end of the situation if your target caused it, directly or by proxy.
9. **COLD LEDGER.** When you inflict stress on your target's assets, clear the same amount from Blood, up to D4, once per situation.

### FREEDOM (Blood) — the selfish mirror

*Direction fixed: Fellowship takes stress for others; Freedom gives its stress away. Variants by how dark you want it:*

1. **NEVER AGAIN.** Once per session, allocate stress you would mark to the nearest friendly target instead. *(COLLATERAL verbatim. The cleanest, and proven.)*
2. **SOMEONE ELSE'S CHAIN.** Once per session, when you would mark stress, a willing crewmate in reach marks it instead. If no one volunteers, the nearest takes it anyway — and you mark D4 Luck.
3. **THE WEIGHT GOES OVERBOARD.** Once per session, transfer up to D6 of your stress to one of your bonds without visiting them. Roll their fallout as normal. They find out later what carrying you costs.
4. **NOT MY PRICE.** When debt, obligation or coercion would mark stress on you, once per session someone else present and connected to the scene marks it instead.
5. **CUT LOOSE.** Once per session, escape any bond, hold, cell or pursuit without a roll. Someone who helped you, sheltered you or stood beside you marks D4 — the door always costs somebody.
6. **NO CHAINS, NO BRAKES.** +1 Blood protection. Once per situation, when you refuse an order, a deal or a demand outright, clear D4 Blood.
7. **THE ESCAPE ARTIST'S ARITHMETIC.** Once per situation, halve incoming stress from anyone with formal authority over the situation — captains, harbourmasters, officers — rounding the remainder onto the nearest ally.

### LEGACY (Reputation)

1. **THE HEIRLOOM.** Begin play with a renowned item of your line; you have its knack while you carry it. Lose the item, lose the knack — and the name starts asking questions. *(Plugs straight into the renowned-item rules.)*
2. **OLD ACCOUNTS.** Once per session, access any haunt without paying — the keeper remembers your line. The GM records the debt against your name.
3. **DEAD MAN'S CHARTS.** Journeys along routes your family sailed count as charted routes for you — half resistance.
4. **BORROWED GLORY.** Once per session, mark D4 Reputation to roll with mastery on something your ancestor was famous for doing.
5. **THE FAMILY SEAL.** In ports where your line is remembered fondly, treat Risky social actions as Standard. In ports where it is hated, they become Dangerous. You don't always know which is which until you're talking.
6. **ENTAILED.** When you suffer Reputation fallout, downgrade it one step; the GM banks the difference as a debt your line owes, payable on demand.
7. **BLOOD DOWRY.** Begin with a D10 heirloom resource. You cannot trade it down; when you finally spend, lose or give it, refresh Reputation in full.
8. **WHAT THE NAME IS WORTH.** Once per session, when you invoke your lineage aloud, clear D4 Reputation or mark D4 — GM's call on how the room remembers your family. *(A coin-flip with teeth; only if the table likes gambling.)*
9. **THE GHOST'S HABITS.** Choose one skill your ancestor was known for. Gain it. When using it in a place your line worked, the first failure each session counts as a partial success.

### Keepers — alternates, only if wanted

- **Cursed Tides.** SALT IN THE VEINS stands. Alternate: **THE TIDE PROVIDES** — once per session, consume nothing and treat the sea as a Tend Blood or Tend Gold equipment of D6: it washes something up. *(Weirder, weaker.)*
- **Fellowship.** THE BODY BETWEEN stands as Freedom's mirror. Alternate: **SHARED POT** — once per session, distribute up to D6 of one crewmate's stress among all willing PCs, including you. *(More communal, more bookkeeping.)*

## Classes Carrying the Same Disease

From the Version 4 core abilities:

**Need replacement (information is the core payload):**

- **Chronicler — THE ARCHIVE.** Both clauses are info: free knowledge plus two GM-tells on every ruin. The whole core is a prompt. (WHERE THE PAST LINGERS is healthy.)
- **Sin Bosun — THE CHART AND THE PRAYER.** One true piece of information per session plus a thin once-per-journey mastery. Rebuild around the rite/journey economy MINISTRATIONS already proves works.

**Need surgery (an info clause grafted onto a real mechanic):**

- **Errant — READ THE FIGHTER.** "The GM tells you their strongest technique and worst vulnerability" is the payload; the mastery rider is the mechanic. Cut the telling, keep the mastery: *once per session, after watching someone fight, roll with mastery against them for the rest of the situation.*
- **Sawbone — THE SAME SAW.** The dual flesh/timber Tend clause is excellent and should survive any rewrite. The examine-and-be-told second clause is an info-tap — trim it.
- **Tidecaller — THE PACT.** The ask-a-question clause is info, but the obey/defy D4/D6 Tide rider is real. Defensible as a hybrid; cutting the question and keeping the rider would make it clean.

**Healthy cores, but flagged:**

- **Sharper.** The v4 cores (THE SURE THING, DOUBLE OR NOTHING) are mechanically rich Heart ports — the disease now lives in its minors: THE BRIBED HARBOUR, NOSE FOR GOLD, THE TELL, WHAT THE WALLS HEARD are all info-taps. A class-pass problem, not a core problem.
- **Daredevil — ALWAYS A WAY OUT.** Mechanically real but carries v5-style wordiness (the exclusion list). Compression candidate, not replacement.

**Clean:** Castaway, Corsair, Powder Mage, Daredevil (MOMENTUM), Sawbone (first clause), Errant (THE FORM).
