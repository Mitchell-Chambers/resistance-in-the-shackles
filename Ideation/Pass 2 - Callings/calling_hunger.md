# HUNGER

*The insatiable need to acquire, reach, and possess*

The wanting never stops. It wears different clothes — treasure, the next horizon, the cargo of a ship you have not taken yet — but it is the same hunger underneath. You will not be satisfied. The only question is what you spend in pursuit of the next thing, and whether it was worth it.

> **CORE ABILITY — THE SCENT.** Once per session, the GM tells you a true rumour about what you hunt. It is never the whole truth, and you are never the only one who has heard it.

**Dominant tracks:** Luck, with Gold close behind.

## Character Questions

- Name the thing you are hunting. Is it something you can hold, or somewhere you can only stand?
- Choose another player character. They were there the last time you nearly had it. What went wrong?
- Choose another player character. You owe them a share you have already spent. Why do they let it stand?
- Who else hunts the same thing, and how far behind you are they?

## Minor Beats

- Take Minor Luck fallout.
- Take Minor Gold fallout chasing a lead.
- Acquire something a rival was about to close their hand on.
- Get caught taking something that was not yours to take.
- Refuse payment you desperately need.
- Sell or sacrifice a D8 or higher resource to get closer to it.
- Spend a resource you have been hoarding at the worst possible moment.
- Shake hands on a deal you know you cannot honour.
- Tell another PC the history of the thing you hunt.
- Let the hunger lead you into danger without a plan.
- Acquire a bond who can get you closer: a fence, a cartographer, a pilot.
- Follow a rumour into Tier 2 waters or deeper.
- Set foot somewhere no one on your crew has been.
- Discover something you carry is worth far more than you knew.
- Convince another PC to come somewhere dangerous by promising a share.
- Pass up a chance to do right because the right thing means stopping.
- Give something away the moment you finally hold it.
- Find something worthless you refuse to discard.
- Return somewhere you knew and find it changed beyond recognition.
- Win a haggle you had no business winning.

## Major Beats

- Take Major Luck fallout in pursuit of what you hunt.
- Take something that significant opposition tried to keep from you.
- Betray an ally for a share of what you were hunting.
- Lose your most prized possession: stolen, sunk, or taken as debt.
- Learn that what you hunt is cursed or worse than imagined, and press on.
- Sell or sacrifice a D12 resource to learn what lies ahead.
- Reach a landmark in Tier 3 waters that no reliable chart shows.
- Come to hold something the Aspis Consortium will kill to reclaim.
- Reach the place it was supposed to be, and find it already taken.
- Help another PC reach what they hunt, at real cost to yourself.

## Zenith Beats

- ***The Prize.*** Claim the thing you have hunted, and pay what it asks.
- ***The Letting Go.*** Stand before it at last — and turn away, leaving it for whoever comes next.
- ***The Downfall.*** Die for it, or lose everything chasing it, and decide at the end whether the wanting was ever about the thing at all.

## What You Carry

*Roll 1d10 or choose:*

1. A hand-drawn chart of a place on no official map, annotated in three different hands.
2. A small brass compass that does not point north. You have not worked out what it points at.
3. A coin from a mint that no longer exists, taken from the first thing you ever stole.
4. A folded letter of provenance for an item you do not own yet.
5. A set of lockpicks worn smooth with daily handling.
6. A list of everyone who has hunted the same thing, with notes on what happened to each of them.
7. A treasure map you drew yourself, as bait, that you have started to half-believe.
8. A spyglass with a cracked lens you refuse to replace, because it was your first.
9. A small clay jar sealed with wax, contents unknown, from a place you have told no one about.
10. Three overlapping maps of the same coastline, none of which agree.
