# MONUMENT

*The patient construction of something that will stand after you are gone*

Brick by brick. Every ship that turns at your flag, every story told in a dockside tavern, every captain who owes you a favour — bricks, all of them. Most pirates take; you are making something. The question is not whether you will finish it. It is what the building will cost the builder.

> **CORE ABILITY — FOUNDATIONS.** Once per session, name something you intend to claim or consolidate — a port, a route, a loyalty, a vote. The GM tells you the single greatest obstacle, honestly.

**Dominant track:** Reputation.

## Character Questions

- What are you building, and what do you call it in your honest moments?
- Choose another player character. They knew you before you started building. What do they know that could bring it down?
- Choose another player character. Their loyalty is load-bearing — it holds up part of the structure. What did you do to earn it?
- Where is your name already carved: a flag, a favour, a story told in a port you have never visited?

## Minor Beats

- Take Minor Reputation fallout when something you built fails in public.
- Be mistaken for something more powerful than you are.
- Publicly humiliate a rival, and make it look effortless.
- Hear a story about you told by someone who does not know you are present.
- Refuse a sensible retreat because retreat would undo something built.
- Deliver a speech designed for the retelling, not the room.
- Have a ship change course because of your flag alone.
- Perform an act of calculated mercy because the story of it is load-bearing.
- Acquire a bond who holds power: a debt, a secret, a favour owed to you.
- Donate a significant resource to a haunt, and make sure everyone hears.
- Take credit for something another pirate did.
- Lay a foundation in a new port: a small piece of influence to build on.
- Demand terms from an enemy who has the upper hand.
- Tell another PC what the finished structure looks like in your head.
- Choose a person over the structure, and let a brick fall.
- Learn that a piece of what you built has spread somewhere you never placed it.
- Back a side in a port dispute because the outcome matters to the work.
- Hear your name used as a benchmark by a stranger.
- Commission, cultivate, or correct a piece of your own myth.

## Major Beats

- Take Major Reputation fallout as the cost of overbuilding.
- Have your name formally entered into an enemy power's record: a Chelish mandate, an Aspis dossier.
- Secure formal acknowledgement from the Free Captains or the Pirate Council.
- Seize a port berth, a trade route, or an allegiance, and hold it against opposition.
- Put another PC somewhere load-bearing, and tell them what that means.
- Watch a piece of the structure collapse in public, and choose: rebuild, or let it fall.
- Discover someone you trusted has been undermining the work for longer than you knew.
- Be offered legitimacy — a letter of marque, a pardon, a council seat — and decide whether it is a brick or a trap.
- Take the thing you reached for, and feel the foundation crack under its weight.
- Raise a haunt you patronise to D10 or higher, and put your name on it.

## Zenith Beats

- ***The Monument.*** What you built stands without you. Step back, and watch it hold.
- ***The Throne.*** Take the seat the structure was always pointing toward, and learn what it costs to sit in it.
- ***The Downfall.*** Watch it come down — the name, the power, the believers — and decide in the wreckage whether it was worth the life it took.

## What You Carry

*Roll 1d10 or choose:*

1. A ship's manifest bearing your name as owner of a vessel you do not yet possess.
2. A broadsheet about you, wrong in three specific ways, none of which you have corrected.
3. Signed letters of debt from captains who owe you, bundled in red cord.
4. A custom seal and wax, so correspondence is known to come from your hand.
5. A flag of your own design, never yet flown, waiting for a ship worthy of it.
6. A ledger of every significant thing you have done, with dates and witnesses.
7. A portrait of yourself, commissioned at great expense, that does not quite look like you.
8. A copy of the Pirate Code annotated in your own hand: corrections, additions, objections.
9. A key to a lock you have not found, from someone who said you would know it when the time came.
10. A half-finished proclamation, unsigned, declaring something you are not yet ready to claim.
