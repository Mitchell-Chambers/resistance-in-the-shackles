# Callings v2 — Overview

*Pass 2 of the Shackles callings, rebuilt against `callings_v5_vs_heart_analysis.md`. One file per calling; this document holds the shared rules and the choosing guide.*

**The set:** Piracy (shared) · Hunger · Monument · Vendetta · Freedom · Cursed Tides · Legacy · Fellowship

---

## What a Calling Is

Your calling is your reason for sailing — the obsession that keeps you from a quiet life ashore. It is not a class, a background, or a profession; it is closer to a wound that has organised itself into a direction. The calling tells you what your character cannot stop wanting. Everything else follows from that.

Every calling in this set is something the character *chose to sail with*. Nobody here is a pirate by sentence or by penance — the Shackles are entered by choice, and the tragedy is that the choice was real.

## How Callings Work

Each calling grants a core ability, four character questions, and beats. At the end of each session, choose two active beats from your personal calling or the shared Piracy calling — the pools are combined. When you hit a beat, take an advance from your class at the appropriate level. You can hold only two active beats and cannot hit more than one in a single situation.

Minor beats resolve in a session; major beats take two or three; zenith beats begin the end — one or two sessions remain for that character. Beats that cost you something are invitations, not punishments: selecting one, you are directing your character's story, not playing your character. A character does not know they have beats.

## The Downfall

Every personal calling's last zenith is the Downfall: the specific collapse that this obsession was always pointing toward. It is never required. It is not a failure state. In the Shackles, it is often the most honest ending available.

## What You Carry

Each calling ends in a d10 table of objects. They are not equipment — no bonuses, no slots. They are evidence of the life that brought you here, and they should invite questions you do not have to answer yet.

## Choosing Your Calling

Each entry is a decision test: if you are torn between two callings, the answer to the question tells you which one fits.

**Hunger or Vendetta?** If your target surrendered what they took but walked away free — enough? Yes: Hunger. No — you need to look them in the eye: Vendetta.

**Hunger or Monument?** Hunger consumes and moves on; Monument keeps and builds. Is the question what is over the horizon, or what you will leave behind?

**Hunger or Cursed Tides?** Could you stop? A Hunger character could, in principle. A Cursed Tides character is pulled whether they consent or not.

**Hunger or Legacy?** Begin with nothing and take it, or begin holding something you never earned.

**Monument or Legacy?** Monument constructs weight from nothing; Legacy inherits weight and must decide what to do with it.

**Monument or Fellowship?** Which would you sacrifice first — the friendship or the structure? Monument keeps people because they are useful; Fellowship keeps them because they are irreplaceable.

**Vendetta or Freedom?** If the target died tomorrow: grief at a lost purpose is Vendetta; getting back to work is Freedom. One points at a person, the other at a system.

**Freedom or Fellowship?** Freedom burns a stranger's cage on principle; Fellowship counts the cost to these specific people first. Both are generous — differently.

**Freedom or Cursed Tides?** One refuses every claim; the other is already claimed. (In a single character, this contradiction is fuel, not a problem.)

**Cursed Tides or Legacy?** Can you negotiate with it? A legacy can be refused, honoured, or transformed. The call cannot.

**Legacy or Vendetta?** Vendetta has a heading; Legacy has ballast. One is in pursuit, the other under weight.

**Legacy or Fellowship?** The dead, or the living. Legacy faces what came before; Fellowship faces who is here.

## Changes from v5

- **Concision.** Each calling cut from ~1,200 words to Heart's ~650–750. Flavour reduced to one paragraph; trailing editorial clauses ("...and justify it to yourself") stripped — the beat names the event, the table supplies the meaning.
- **Party-facing questions.** Every calling now has two "Choose another player character..." bond questions (v5 had zero across the set), plus one motivation and one world-visibility question.
- **Current stress tracks.** Nerve/Loot/Hex retired; Luck now in play (Hunger, Fellowship, Piracy).
- **Core abilities repaired.** Vendetta capped at once per session; Monument cut to one effect; Hunger's rumour is now always true (but never whole, never private); Freedom's immunity costs Blood instead of being absolute; Cursed Tides ported Heartsong's economy ability (Tide protection + reallocation); Fellowship inverted COLLATERAL — take a crewmate's stress instead of giving them yours. Four of seven abilities now touch the stress/dice economy, matching Heart's profile.
- **Mechanical anchors.** Each calling has 3–5 beats referencing real subsystems: tiers, journeys and charted routes, resource dice, haunts and donations, bonds, ship quality.
- **Tone rebalance.** Each minor tier carries triumph and swagger alongside the toll; each carry table has at least one item with teeth *and* a grin.
- **Zenith fixes.** Hunger gained its missing inversion (The Letting Go); Legacy's Severance renamed The Burial (Cursed Tides keeps Severance); all zeniths remain named, Downfall always last.
- **Distinctions consolidated.** The 24 duplicated comparison paragraphs became the choosing guide above.
- **Folded territories.** The Forced and Penitent analogues were deliberately not added — coercion or penance as a primary identity cuts against piracy-by-choice. Their best material is folded in instead: present-tense leashes into Freedom (the price bought by someone new, debts taken to break debts, hidden terms) and Piracy (inspections, debts); guilt and amends into Fellowship (the PC you failed, reparations from your own share), Legacy (amends to the ancestor's victims), and Vendetta (mercy your target would never show, discovering someone hunts *you*).
- **Piracy explicitly has no zeniths.** The life does not end; people do.
