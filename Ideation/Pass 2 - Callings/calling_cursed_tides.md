# CURSED TIDES

*The sea has claimed you, and the calling has no bottom*

The sea spoke to you — or something beneath it did. You did not ask for the dreams, the pull, the stretches of water that watch you back. Other pirates have reasons; you have a calling with no bottom. Stopping feels like drowning from the inside. So you sail.

> **CORE ABILITY — SALT IN THE VEINS.** +1 Tide protection. Once per situation, when you would mark stress to any other resistance, you may mark it to Tide instead.

**Dominant track:** Tide.

*The Arcane domain is a natural complement to this calling. A suggestion, not a rule.*

## Character Questions

- When did you first feel the call, and what does it feel like when it is strong?
- Choose another player character. They have seen what happens when you listen to the water. What did they watch you do?
- Choose another player character. The sea has shown you something about them they do not know. What — and why haven't you told them?
- How has the call marked your body?

## Minor Beats

- Take Minor Tide fallout.
- Be warned by something inexplicable, and be right to listen.
- Consume something of the deep: relic salt, sea-beast flesh, drowned-ruin water.
- Let something into a port that should have stayed out.
- Terrify or fascinate an NPC with the way you talk about the water.
- Perform a rite at a place of power in Tier 2 waters or deeper.
- Communicate with something beneath the surface.
- Name the thing that calls you. Use only that name from now on.
- Wake somewhere you do not remember sailing to.
- Spare something of the deep when killing it was easier.
- See something from your dreams in the waking world.
- Build a shrine on the water: floated, sunk, or fixed to the mast.
- Convince the crew to sail a course only you believe in, and be right.
- Acquire a bond who serves Besmara, or the deep.
- Act against your own interest because the tide demanded it.
- Gain a physical mark that shifts with the moon.
- Find evidence of others who were called, and how they ended.
- Shelter someone else touched by the deep.
- Stay ashore when the call is loudest, for someone else's sake.

## Major Beats

- Take Major Tide fallout as the claim presses closer.
- Perform a rite at a drowned ruin in Tier 3 waters, and receive something in return.
- Sacrifice something you love to the tide.
- Show what calls you to an outsider, and watch them break, believe, or run.
- One of your bonds suffers fallout because of what follows you.
- Be recognised by something of the deep, and treated accordingly.
- Follow the call into uncharted waters (this adds D12 to the journey's resistance).
- Be claimed as one of their own by Besmara's church, or by the cults of the Eye.
- Destroy a safe anchorage because the tide required it.
- Find another who answered the same call differently.

## Zenith Beats

- ***The Answer.*** Go beneath the waves, and do not come back as anything the world would recognise.
- ***The Severance.*** Refuse the sea's final claim — tear it out of yourself, and live with the silence where the call used to be.
- ***The Downfall.*** Let the tide take what it always said it would — ship, crew, self — and go down with something like peace.

## What You Carry

*Roll 1d10 or choose:*

1. A piece of coral that grew around your wrist while you slept. You cut it back. It returns.
2. A journal of visions, almost entirely water-damaged. The legible pages are worse.
3. A fragment of hull timber from a ship lost with all hands, which is sometimes warm.
4. A polite, water-stained note declining your last offering. You have not told anyone the sea can write.
5. A tattoo of a creature you have seen only in dreams, placed by a priest who said you would understand it eventually.
6. A page of script you do not recognise, written in your own hand, in a fugue.
7. A length of rope that cannot be cut, knotted in a pattern you do not remember tying.
8. A tooth from something deep-sea that appears in no bestiary.
9. A mirror that shows its reflections half a second behind.
10. A name — not yours — burned into the sole of your foot the morning after you first heard the call.
