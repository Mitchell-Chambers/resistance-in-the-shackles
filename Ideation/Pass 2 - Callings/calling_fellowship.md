# FELLOWSHIP

*The crew as the only thing that matters — and the cost of that being true*

You have never needed much: a good ship, fair winds, people worth dying for. Not a crew as an idea — these people, whose faces you could draw in the dark. The only freedom you have ever trusted is the kind you share. You have handed them the power to destroy you, on purpose.

> **CORE ABILITY — THE BODY BETWEEN.** Once per session, when another PC or named crewmember within reach marks stress, you may mark it instead of them.

**Dominant track:** Luck.

## Character Questions

- Choose another player character. You would die for them without hesitation. Do they know — and what happened that made it true?
- Choose another player character. You failed them once, badly. What do you do differently now?
- Who did you lose before this crew, and what do you carry of theirs?
- Everyone in port knows you are the one who holds this crew together. What did they see that proved it?

## Minor Beats

- Take Minor Luck fallout.
- Put yourself between danger and a crewmate.
- Learn something about another PC they have told no one.
- Share real rest with the crew: a meal, a calm watch, a night ashore.
- Make a call the crew disagrees with, and live with the friction.
- Cover for another PC's mistake without telling them.
- Acquire a bond who becomes part of the crew's life, not a contact.
- Refuse a personal windfall because taking it means leaving someone.
- Tell another PC a truth they needed and did not want.
- Celebrate another PC's triumph louder than your own.
- Grieve for someone the crew has lost.
- Take the blame for something that was not your fault.
- Let the crew see you at your lowest.
- Argue with another PC about values, not tactics.
- Hold things together in a crisis while everyone else breaks.
- Fail someone who needed you, and face them after.
- Take fallout from stress you took for someone else.
- Vouch for a stranger and bring them into the crew's trust.
- Make reparations, out of your own share, to someone the crew wronged.
- Be caught keeping a list of everything that could take them from you.

## Major Beats

- Take Major Luck fallout.
- Risk your life for a crewmate when the sensible choice was letting go.
- Lose someone from the crew permanently, and decide what it means.
- Discover something about a crewmate that tests your loyalty to its limit.
- Hold the crew together through catastrophe: shipwreck, mutiny, massacre.
- Cause a serious rift in the crew, and rebuild what you damaged.
- Choose the crew over your own safety, future, or freedom.
- Accept back into the crew someone who genuinely wronged you.
- Turn down the Free Captains' favour — a star, a seat, a commission — because it would split the crew.
- Build something with the crew that outlasts the voyage: a tradition, a code, a haunt of your own.

## Zenith Beats

- ***The Gift.*** Give everything — your life, your future, the last of your luck — for them, and make it count.
- ***The Dispersal.*** Watch them scatter, or sail on without you, and find that what they carry of you is enough.
- ***The Downfall.*** Lose them — all of them, or the ones who mattered most — and learn what is left of you after.

## What You Carry

*Roll 1d10 or choose:*

1. A small collection of things crew members gave or left behind. You keep it without telling anyone.
2. A letter to someone who may or may not be alive, added to for months, never sent.
3. A list of everyone you have ever sailed with, living and dead, one word beside each name.
4. A rough carved figure of someone on this crew, which you have never shown them.
5. A tradition from a previous crew that you keep alone, because stopping would be forgetting.
6. A scar a crewmate gave you by accident. You have told them nothing, and blame them for nothing.
7. A song your old crew sang together, which you still sing alone. Badly.
8. The crew's accounts, which you have quietly balanced out of your own share for years. Nobody has noticed.
9. Something that belonged to someone you lost, worn where no one can see it.
10. A running record of everything this crew has done together — not a log, a memorial, written for whoever reads it after you.
