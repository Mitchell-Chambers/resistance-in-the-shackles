# FREEDOM

*The need to burn every cage — yours, and everyone else's*

You were owned once — by law, by debt, by a press-gang, by a name on a contract. You know in your body what it means to have your choices removed. The sea is the only place with no walls, and you will burn every cage you find. Every chain is yours to break. That is the trap.

> **CORE ABILITY — UNCHAINED.** When anyone attempts to coerce you — threat, blackmail, or compulsion — you may mark D4 stress to Blood to refuse outright, without a roll. No one moves you but you.

**Dominant track:** Blood.

## Character Questions

- Who held the leash, and how did you get free?
- Choose another player character. You were freed together, or one of you freed the other. Which was it, and what is owed?
- Choose another player character. They have watched you risk the crew for a stranger in chains. What did they say afterwards?
- What does whoever owned you still hold: your papers, your name, your family, a price on you?

## Minor Beats

- Take Minor Blood fallout refusing to run.
- Free a prisoner, thrall, or press-ganged sailor.
- Refuse a direct order from someone you respect.
- Destroy a binding document: a contract, a writ, a bill of sale.
- Be briefly captured, bound, or held.
- Meet someone who chose their cage.
- Describe your captivity to another PC for the first time.
- Acquire a bond who was freed by you, or alongside you.
- Refuse a safe arrangement because of the conditions attached.
- Commit an act of solidarity with strangers.
- Pass up coin rather than work under someone's authority.
- Speak about what you escaped in front of people who profit from it.
- Help another PC refuse something they had accepted.
- Start a mutiny, work stoppage, or act of defiance aboard any vessel.
- Take on a debt to buy someone else out of theirs.
- Defend someone suffering a cruelty you know personally.
- Burn a slaver's ledger worth D8 or more rather than sell it.
- Discover the terms hidden inside something you accepted freely.
- Shelter a runaway aboard, and hide them through an inspection.

## Major Beats

- Take Major Blood fallout from what freedom costs.
- Liberate a vessel and hand it to its crew.
- Burn a slaving operation or press-gang network out of a port.
- Be recaptured long enough for it to cost something real, and fight back out.
- Refuse an alliance that would make you the keeper of someone else's cage.
- Free someone who uses their freedom in a way you cannot forgive.
- Confront the person or institution that owned you.
- Buy another PC's freedom — from debt, a bargain, a sentence — without being asked.
- Learn that the price on your head has been bought by someone new.
- Establish a charted route that runaways can sail without you.

## Zenith Beats

- ***The Horizon.*** Sail beyond every jurisdiction, every debt, every claim on you — and find out what is left to want.
- ***The Fire.*** Bring down the thing that owned you, and stand in what remains.
- ***The Downfall.*** Die unchained — or learn too late that the cage was one you carried with you.

## What You Carry

*Roll 1d10 or choose:*

1. A broken manacle, one half of a pair, worn on a cord around your neck.
2. Forged papers granting you freedom in a nation that would never issue them.
3. The brand of whoever owned you, reworked by your own hand into something that is yours.
4. A book of songs that sustained you in captivity, in handwriting that is not yours.
5. A list of names still inside, carried until you can do something about each one.
6. A key taken from the one who held you. It fits no lock you have ever found.
7. A bill for your own purchase, paid in full, framed in driftwood. You bought yourself back at a discount.
8. A map of the place you were held, drawn from memory, in extraordinary detail.
9. An object you were not allowed to have, kept as proof that no one says that anymore.
10. A folded paper where someone wrote your name — the first time anyone asked it, rather than your number.
