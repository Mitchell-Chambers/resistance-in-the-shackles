# LEGACY

*The weight of a name the sea remembers*

You are not the first. Your blood carries a name the Shackles remembers, and the past is not past — it is present, pressing, and it wants something from you. The legacy opens doors and slams them, pays debts and incurs them. The only thing it never is, is neutral.

> **CORE ABILITY — THE NAME REMEMBERS.** At creation, establish one true thing about your lineage. Once per session, establish a connected fact about the world; the GM incorporates it as true.

**Dominant track:** Reputation.

## Character Questions

- Who came before you, and what did they do that still has consequences?
- Choose another player character. Your families' stories are tangled — an old debt, an old rescue, an old crime. Which is it, and who knows?
- Choose another player character. They knew you before they knew the name, and treat you differently since learning it. How?
- What do people expect of you, on sight, because of where you came from?

## Minor Beats

- Take Minor Reputation fallout from old debts coming due.
- Be recognised for your name before yourself.
- Meet someone who knew your ancestor, and hear something you would rather not have.
- Repeat, without intending to, a decision your lineage is known for.
- Reject a piece of the legacy: burn a letter, refuse a title, abandon a route.
- Accept a piece of the legacy you previously rejected.
- Protect, or make amends to, someone your ancestor wronged.
- Be refused something you need because of what your bloodline did.
- Find a record, a chart, or a D8 or higher resource that belonged to your lineage.
- Discover the famous deed was a lie, a crime, or a cover-up.
- Have a young sailor attach themselves to you because of who you come from.
- Make a choice that contradicts what your lineage would have done, and say so.
- Meet the descendants or the creditors of someone your family wronged.
- Claim a berth, a passage, or a fee on your name alone.
- Describe your ancestor to another PC: what they wanted, how they ended.
- Uphold a family tradition that costs you something.
- Discover someone else claiming your family's name.
- Silence a room by invoking your lineage.
- Acquire a bond who served your ancestor, and expects things of you.
- Sail a charted route your family established, and pay what using it costs.

## Major Beats

- Take Major Reputation fallout when the legacy turns on you.
- Formally take on a role or burden your lineage is known for.
- Confront the living consequence of the famous act: the saved, the burned, the enemy made.
- Be disowned or denounced by a faction that counted your bloodline as its own.
- Discover a buried truth: a crime, a bargain, a living heir.
- Make the choice your ancestor could not.
- Bring a piece of the legacy to an end: retire the ship, pay the generational debt, burn the flag.
- Another PC takes fallout because of a feud your blood started.
- Win back a family claim — a berth, a right, a stretch of water — and find it costs more to hold than to win.
- Forge something future generations will inherit from you.

## Zenith Beats

- ***The Ancestor.*** Become the one they will speak of — fulfil what the line was building toward, or outsail it entirely.
- ***The Burial.*** Lay the legacy down for good — give it to the sea, the flame, or an heir — and meet yourself with nothing behind you.
- ***The Downfall.*** Go down as the latest in a long line of people who could not carry what they were handed.

## What You Carry

*Roll 1d10 or choose:*

1. Your ancestor's signet ring, which opens doors it should not still open.
2. A portrait of your ancestor in which you can see your own face.
3. The log of their final voyage, the last entries water-stained and barely legible.
4. An unpaid debt your ancestor incurred, still legally valid. The collectors have not given up.
5. A letter from your ancestor to someone specific, never delivered. You have it memorised.
6. An object of no value that mattered to them, passed to you with no explanation.
7. Their weapon, repaired so many times it is technically a different weapon. Except that it is not.
8. A deed granting your family rights over something someone else currently controls.
9. A coded message your ancestor sent that was never cracked. You have been working on it for years.
10. Their name, tattooed in the old style of your bloodline's home port, which you were not asked about.
