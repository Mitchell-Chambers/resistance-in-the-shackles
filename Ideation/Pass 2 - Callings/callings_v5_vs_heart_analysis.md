# Callings v5 vs. Heart — Comparative Analysis for the v2 Pass

This document measures the v5 ideation callings (Hunger, Monument, Vendetta, Freedom, Cursed Tides, Legacy, Fellowship, plus shared Piracy) against the five Heart base callings (Adventure, Enlightenment, Forced, Heartsong, Penitent). It builds on `Reference/heart_callings_analysis.md` and is the working brief for the v2 pass.

**Verdict in one line:** the v5 callings are thematically strong but twice as long as they should be, structurally inward-facing where Heart's are party-facing, and mechanically unmoored from the system they live in.

## Set-Wide Findings

### 1. Over-writing (your stated pain point — confirmed everywhere)

Three distinct layers of bloat, in descending order of cost:

**a) Trailing editorial clauses on beats.** The single biggest offender. v5 beats routinely append an interpretation that pre-scripts the emotional outcome:

> *"Acquire something that rightfully belongs to another PC or allied NPC, **and justify it to yourself**."*
> *"Encounter a stranger who suffered at the same hands, **and handle it badly**."*
> *"Hear a rumour in a port that turns out to be true, **and wish it had not**."*
> *"Learn something new about what your target did, **and have it make things worse**."*

Heart never does this. *"Betray someone because of your true nature"* trusts the table to find the feeling. The trailing clause does the player's job for them, removes the possibility of the beat resolving in an unexpected register, and doubles beat length. **v2 rule: the beat names the event; the table supplies the meaning. Strike nearly every clause after an em-dash or ", and".**

**b) Flavour inflation.** Heart opens each calling with 2–3 italic sentences (~50 words). v5 opens with a subtitle plus two full paragraphs (~130–160 words) that mostly restate the subtitle. The second paragraph is almost always cuttable in full — e.g. Hunger's "Some Hunger characters are treasure hunters... Both are expressions of the same pathology" is calling-selection guidance that belongs in Distinctions, not flavour. **v2 rule: subtitle + one paragraph, 60-word ceiling, following Heart's three-sentence skeleton (condition → drive → cost).**

**c) Duplicated apparatus.** The Distinctions & Overlaps sections repeat each pairing in both callings — Legacy vs. Monument appears twice verbatim. Framing text (beat rules, Downfall explanation) appears at both the top and bottom of the document. **v2: state each pairing once in a single "Choosing Your Calling" page; state the beat rules once.**

Net effect of a–c: v2 should target ~650–750 words per calling without losing a single beat.

### 2. Zero PC bond questions — the most important structural finding

Every Heart calling has at least one character question using the formula *"Choose another player character. [Shared event]. What happened?"* — most have two. These pre-weave the party before session one and seed beats that pay the questions off.

**No v5 question references another PC.** All 28 questions across seven callings face inward: *what do you want, what did you lose, what are you afraid of.* Even Fellowship — the calling that is constitutively about other people — asks "Who on this crew would you die for?" as introspection rather than establishing a concrete shared history with a chosen PC. The result is seven beautifully realised loners.

This also violates the project's own `calling_design_guide.md` (§6: "At least 2 questions should reference other PCs").

**v2 rule: rewrite every question set to Heart's profile — 4 questions: 2 PC bonds (specific shared past event, not "how do you feel about them"), 1 motivation, 1 world-visibility.** Most existing questions are good enough to keep one of; the introspective surplus can move into flavour or be cut.

### 3. Core abilities have drifted out of the game's economy

All five Heart core abilities touch the dice/stress machinery: refresh dice (LEGENDARY), reallocate stress (COLLATERAL, IN THE BLOOD), force a result (UNORTHODOX METHODS), suspend fallout (NOT YET). Zero are "ask the GM a question."

In v5, only Vendetta and Fellowship touch stress. Four of seven are narrative-authority abilities (declare a rumour, ask what the sea wants, ask what the obstacle is, establish a fact). Individually each is defensible; as a set they make the callings feel interchangeable in play — every calling's button is "talk to the GM." Specific problems:

- **Vendetta has no usage cap.** "When you take actions that directly advance your vendetta, remove one stress" fires on every qualifying action — unbounded healing, far stronger than anything in Heart. Needs *once per session*.
- **Monument is two abilities bolted together** (name-introduction read + once-per-session obstacle question), violating the guide's one-effect and thirty-word rules. Keep the obstacle question; demote the name-landing read to flavour or fold it in ("when you introduce yourself by name, you may also ask...").
- **Hunger's ability can do nothing.** "The GM must confirm whether it is true or false" means the once-per-session button can return "false — sorry." A limited-use ability should always produce value. Fix: the rumour is always *true but incomplete, dangerous to act on, or known to a rival* — cost instead of whiff.
- **Freedom's immunity is absolute** in a way no Heart ability is. "Automatically succeed on resisting" threats, blackmail, *and magical compulsion*, always-on, deletes entire scene types for the GM. Heart's closest analogue (NOT YET) is once per session and temporary. Fix: keep the always-on flavour but make resistance cost something (mark stress to resist automatically) or cap it per session.
- **Fellowship's trigger is misaligned.** Removing your own stress *when a crewmate takes fallout* mildly rewards the player for allies getting hurt. Heart's COLLATERAL owns this perversity explicitly (and hangs a beat off it). Either own it the same way (add a beat: "your ability fires on the worst day of someone's life") or re-key it to *spending/suffering on a crewmate's behalf*.

**v2 rule: at least 4 of 7 core abilities should touch stress, fallout, dice, or resources. Every limited-use ability always does something. One effect, ≤30 words.**

### 4. Beats float free of the system

Heart beats are studded with mechanical anchors — *resources of D10 or higher, tier 3 of the Heart, upgrade a haunt to D12, mark D8 stress to an NPC bond, increases the difficulty of the delve by D12* — typically 4–6 per calling. They make beats verifiable and tie advancement to the game's other subsystems.

v5 beats are almost entirely fiction-only. Beyond the fallout beats, the set has roughly one mechanical anchor per calling (Legacy's "Clear one Reputation stress"; Cursed Tides' rites come closest in spirit). Meanwhile the project now *has* subsystems to anchor to: location tiers, Journeys, ship upgrades, ports and haunts, resource dice.

**v2 rule: 3–5 beats per calling reference a real subsystem.** Examples of the conversion:
- *"Reach somewhere that no reliable chart shows"* → tie to a Tier 3 location or a Journey into uncharted waters.
- *"Spend a resource you have been hoarding"* → "Sell or sacrifice a D8+ resource to get closer to what you hunt" (direct Enlightenment port).
- Cursed Tides should get an Enlightenment-tower-style beat: *"Follow the call into a drowned ruin (this increases the Journey's difficulty by D12)."*
- Monument: "Upgrade your ship/haunt to D12" is sitting right there in Adventure.

### 5. Retired stress-track names throughout

v5 uses **Loot** (Hunger, Piracy), **Nerve** (Vendetta's core ability, Freedom, Fellowship, Piracy), and **Hex** (Cursed Tides). Current tracks are **Blood / Tide / Reputation / Luck / Gold**. Also: no v5 calling uses **Luck** at all, despite the guide naming it the natural track for Hunger and recklessness. Suggested v2 mapping:

| Calling | v5 track | v2 dominant track |
|---|---|---|
| Hunger | Loot | **Luck** (the universe collecting on audacity), Gold secondary |
| Monument | Reputation | Reputation ✓ |
| Vendetta | Blood/Nerve | Blood ✓ (fix core ability reference) |
| Freedom | Nerve | Blood or Luck — decide; Blood fits recapture/defiance |
| Cursed Tides | Hex | **Tide** |
| Legacy | Reputation | Reputation ✓ |
| Fellowship | Nerve | Blood (taking the hit for them) or Luck — decide |
| Piracy | Blood/Loot/Nerve/Rep | Blood/Gold/Luck/Reputation spread ✓ in concept |

### 6. Tone skew: the fun half of the premise is missing

Heart's Adventure proves beats can be swagger: *win an unarmed brawl in a haven, have a cocktail named after you, make a dramatic entrance, kick someone off a tall structure (they really deserved it).* Across all five Heart callings the minor tiers mix triumph with toll, and every carry table has 1–2 jokes (*letters from your mum, RAVENOUS SHE-WITCHES OF HALLOW, taxidermied owl (name them)*).

v5's beats are overwhelmingly cost, grief, and loss, and the carry tables are uniformly elegiac. But the campaign thesis is *liberty and light-hearted fun that must come crashing down* — the Resistance arc only lands if the high times are mechanically real. Right now players advance almost exclusively by suffering. The Downfall can't fall from anywhere if nothing ever stood tall.

**v2 rule: roughly a quarter of each calling's minor beats should be triumphant, greedy, joyful, or funny — the life as it feels before the bill arrives. Add 1–2 humour items per carry table.** (The carry tables are otherwise excellent — see "Keep" list.)

### 7. Overlap is structural, not editorial

The set needed 24 distinction paragraphs because the callings are defined by *interior abstractions* (wanting, building, freeing, belonging) where Heart's are defined by *concrete external referents* — a master, an order, the Heart, the City Above. Forced never needs disambiguating from Penitent because each points at a different thing in the world.

The two v5 callings with concrete anchors (Vendetta: the named target; Cursed Tides: the sea) are also the two that read most distinct. **v2 fix: give every calling a named external anchor established at creation** — Hunger names the prize, Monument names the structure, Freedom names the institution that owned you, Legacy names the ancestor, Fellowship binds to named crew. The questions should force the naming; the beats should reference "your" anchor. Then consolidate all distinctions into one "Choosing Your Calling" page of decision-point questions (the Vendetta-vs-Hunger "would walking away free be enough?" test is the model — keep that one verbatim).

### 8. Coverage gaps relative to Heart's emotional territories

Mapping the sets: Hunger ≈ Adventure/Enlightenment, Cursed Tides ≈ Heartsong, Monument/Legacy/Vendetta/Freedom/Fellowship are new territory. Two Heart territories have no v5 analogue, and both are intensely piratical:

- **Forced (present-tense coercion).** Freedom is about *escaped* ownership; nobody covers *currently owned* — the press-ganged sailor, the debtor to a Bloodcove crime lord, the blackmailed navigator, the spy aboard. Forced is arguably Heart's best-designed calling (most tension pairs, antagonist baked into questions) and its skeleton ports almost directly. Working name: **The Leash** or **Debt**.
- **Penitent (guilt for harm you caused).** Every v5 grievance is a wrong done *to* the character. No calling serves the pirate who did something unforgivable and is working it off. Working name: **Atonement** or **Wake** (what the ship leaves behind).

Recommendation: add both in v2 (set of nine) or consciously decide the set excludes them and note why. Don't leave it implicit.

### 9. Smaller findings

- **Hunger has only 2 zeniths** (Prize, Downfall) — every other calling has 3. It's missing the inversion (e.g. ***The Letting Go*** — stand before the prize and walk away, or give it to someone who needs it less badly than you needed the chase).
- **"The Severance" is used twice** (Cursed Tides and Legacy). The guide's own rule: zenith names belong to one calling only. Rename one — Legacy's could be ***The Burial***.
- **Legacy's "Clear one Reputation stress" minor beat** is the only beat in the set granting mechanical relief. Either adopt beat-embedded effects as a deliberate pattern (Heart does this in *major* tiers only) or cut it.
- **Monument has duplicate Reputation-cost minors** ("Take Minor Reputation fallout when something fails visibly" / "Suffer a public blow to your Reputation track") — merge.
- **Monument lacks a true counter-drive tension beat.** Every minor builds. Add the opposite pressure: *"Choose a person over the structure, and let a brick fall to do it."*
- **Hunger's 22 minors contain a near-duplicate cluster** — four beats about arriving/reaching/following rumours to new places. Merge to two; this also relieves the calling of straddling two archetypes (acquirer vs. explorer). If the strain persists in v2 drafting, split into two callings.
- **Several beats are internal states, not stageable events**: *"Recognise your own patterns of captivity in a place or situation that looks like freedom"* (Freedom), *"sit with it"* (Vendetta). A beat must be something the table can see happen. Rewrite as observable behaviour or cut.
- **Fellowship's minors skew generic-good-crewmate** (share a meal, celebrate a success, take responsibility). Any character does these. Sharpen toward the calling's specific pathology — total investment as vulnerability: *"Realise you have planned, in detail, what you would do if each of them died."*

## What v5 Does Better Than Heart — Protect These in v2

- **The Downfall zenith.** Not in Heart; perfectly serves the Resistance thesis. Keep, including the "never required, a form of completion" framing.
- **Named zeniths.** Strictly better than Heart's bare checkboxes.
- **The shared Piracy calling.** Genuine innovation; correctly carries the economics and the life so personal callings can carry the obsession. Two notes: (a) its lack of zeniths is right — the *life* doesn't end, *people* do — but say so explicitly; (b) audit personal callings for pirate-life beats that belong in Piracy (Hunger's "belongings inspected by a port authority" is the clearest migrant).
- **Carry-table item quality.** "A folded letter of provenance for an item you do not own yet," "a broadsheet wrong in three specific ways, none of which you have corrected," "the first time anyone asked what your name was rather than what your number was" — better than most of Heart's tables. They only need the humour leavening (§6).
- **The Vendetta-vs-Hunger decision test.** The "would you let them walk away?" framing is the best disambiguation paragraph in the document and the template for the consolidated choosing-guide.
- **Distinctions as a concept.** Heart didn't need it (five callings, concrete anchors); a seven-to-nine calling set does. Just centralise it.
- **The "beats are invitations, you are the director" note.** Clearer than Heart's own rulebook framing. Keep once, at the top.

## The v2 Pass — Prioritised Checklist

### P1 — Structural (do first, affects every calling)
1. Rewrite all question sets: 4 questions — 2 PC bonds ("Choose another PC...") + 1 motivation + 1 world-visibility.
2. Concision pass: strike trailing editorial clauses; flavour to subtitle + ≤60-word paragraph; target ~650–750 words per calling.
3. Stress-track migration to Blood/Tide/Reputation/Luck/Gold per the table in §5; put Luck into play.
4. Core ability repairs: cap Vendetta (once/session); split Monument; make Hunger's rumour always-true-but-costly; soften Freedom's immunity; re-key or own Fellowship's trigger.

### P2 — Content (per calling)
5. Mechanical anchoring: 3–5 beats per calling referencing tiers, Journeys, resource dice, ship/haunt upgrades.
6. Tone rebalance: ~25% of minors triumphant/funny; 1–2 humour items per carry table.
7. Hunger: add third zenith; merge duplicate exploration beats. Monument: merge duplicate Reputation beats; add counter-drive tension beat. Legacy: rename The Severance; resolve the stress-clearing beat. Fellowship: sharpen generic crewmate beats toward the pathology. Freedom/Vendetta: rewrite internal-state beats as observable events.
8. Name every calling's external anchor at creation; reference it in beats.

### P3 — Set-level
9. Consolidate Distinctions & Overlaps into one "Choosing Your Calling" page of decision tests.
10. Decide on the two missing territories: add Leash/Debt (Forced analogue) and Atonement (Penitent analogue), or document the exclusion.
11. Migrate pirate-life beats from personal callings into Piracy; state explicitly that Piracy has no zeniths and why.
12. Update `calling_design_guide.md` wherever it canonised a v5 weakness (it currently permits the inward-facing question profile it nominally forbids).
