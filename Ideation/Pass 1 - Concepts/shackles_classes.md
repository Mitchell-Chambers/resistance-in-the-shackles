# Iron Tides: Classes of the Shackles

*A Pirate Campaign using the Resistance System*

---

## The Tidecaller

*A bargain sealed in saltwater. The sea remembers, and so does the thing that lives in it.*
The Eye of Abendego did not form by accident. Something lives at the heart of that immortal storm — ancient, patient, and interested in very specific things. You found it, or it found you. The terms were unclear. They remain unclear. You carry two things that used to be separate: the wild magic of the ocean, and the hexed, bargained, familiar-haunted power of the witch tradition. The sea does not distinguish between the druid who speaks for it and the witch who bargains with what swims beneath it. You are not sure you do either, anymore.

| Core Skill | Core Domain |
|---|---|
| Brave | Arcane |

**Refresh:** *Call upon your pact entity or complete a sea-rite at open water; remove D6 Hex stress.*

### Core Abilities

**The Pact** `[CORE]`

At character creation, name your pact entity — something drawn from the Eye, the deeps, or the storm. It is real, has wants, and communicates through signs rather than words. Once per session, you may ask it one direct question. The GM answers honestly but in the language of omens. When you act in accordance with what the entity wants, remove D3 Hex stress. When you act against it, mark D4 Hex stress.

**Salt and Hex** `[CORE]`

You can work magic. When you perform a working — weather-turning, hex-laying, spirit-speaking, transformation — describe what you are attempting and mark D6 Hex stress. On a success the working functions as described. On a partial success, the GM adds a complication. On a failure, the working misfires: it still happens, but not the way you intended.

### Minor Abilities

**Familiar** `[MINOR]`

You have a bound creature — a gull, a crab, a serpentine thing that shouldn't exist — that serves as your eyes and messenger. It can travel up to a day's sail from you and return. While it lives, you can see through its senses by marking D3 Hex stress.

**Witch's Hands** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Mend skill. Your pact gives you knowledge of bodies and their unmaking — you know which herbs bind a wound, which roots draw poison, which rites settle a fever the sea gave. Once per scene, when you treat Blood stress or fallout, you may also clear D3 Hex stress from the same character as part of the same working.

**Storm-Debt** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Wilderness domain. You know the islands where the Eye's weather breaks — the hidden volcanic shelters, the caldera anchorages, the old groves where storm-witches performed their rites. You can call a weather working — squall, fog bank, sudden calm — once per session without marking Hex stress, provided you are at sea. The weather lasts until the end of the scene.

**Hex-Mark** `[MINOR]`

You can place a lingering hex on a person or vessel by touch and intention. Name one condition: when it triggers, the target marks D6 Luck stress. You can have one active hex at a time. Removing it requires ritual purification.

**Tideblood** `[MINOR]`

Saltwater heals you. When you are immersed in seawater after taking Blood stress, remove D4 Blood stress. You can survive underwater for twice as long as a normal person before drowning becomes a risk.

**Deepspeak** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Faith domain. You understand that sea creatures exist within the same web of divine attention that your pact inhabits. You can communicate with them — not language, but intent and image. Sharks, dolphins, and stranger things will answer basic questions about what they have seen or sensed nearby. Besmara's sacred animals treat you as kin.

**Curse of the Turning Tide** `[MINOR]`

Once per situation, when an enemy succeeds on an action against you or an ally, you may declare the tide turns. They must reroll, taking the new result. Mark D4 Hex stress when you use this.

**Ward Against Salt** `[MINOR]`

You can lay protective wards on a location — a ship, a cabin, a cove. Those inside gain D4 protection against Hex stress for the duration of one scene. Laying a ward costs D4 Hex stress.

**Witch's Bargain** `[MINOR]`

Once per session, you can offer something to a supernatural entity — information, a favour, a personal cost — to gain a specific boon. The GM determines the exchange rate. Your pact entity watches these negotiations with interest.

**Stormwrack Form** `[MINOR]`

You can take on aspects of the storm: your voice carries over howling wind without effort, your eyes see through rain and fog, your body resists the physical punishment of extreme weather. Mark D3 Hex stress to activate for a scene.

**The Naming** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Faith domain. You know the true name of your pact entity — or a syllable of it. Once per session, speaking it aloud forces the entity to manifest in some legible form and respond directly rather than through omens. This is not safe.

**Curse Eater** `[MINOR]`

You can consume a curse afflicting another person. Mark Hex stress equal to the curse's severity (D4 minor, D6 major) and the curse transfers to you. You then deal with it through your own means. This is a service people pay for.

**Hex Sight** `[MINOR]`

You always know when you are in the presence of active magic, curses, or supernatural attention. You do not need to roll to detect Arcane effects — you simply know. You can identify their approximate nature without a roll.

**Sea-Salved** `[MINOR]`

The salt and spray of the ocean is restorative to you in ways that unsettle ship surgeons. Gain +1 Blood protection. You heal faster at sea than anywhere else — what would fester on land closes clean in the open water.

**Pact Ward** `[MINOR]`

Your entity extends a measure of its attention to your protection. Gain +1 Hex protection. Curses and supernatural workings directed at you must first contend with something that has been watching you since before you knew it was there.

**Patron's Tithe** `[MINOR]`

The thing in the storm pays its debts in strange currency. Gain +1 Loot protection. When material resources slip away through supernatural interference or Hex-adjacent misfortune, your entity quietly compensates — a found coin, a price that comes out lower than it should, a creditor who suddenly becomes less urgent.

### Major Abilities

**The Entity Steps Closer** `[MAJOR]`

Your pact entity's influence becomes physically manifest. You develop one permanent supernatural trait — something visible and unsettling: eyes that reflect storm-light, a faint aura of salt and cold, coral growth at your knuckles. You gain +2 Hex protection. Other magic-workers recognise you immediately as someone claimed.

  - **Deep Form:** Once per session, you may fully manifest your pact entity's influence. For the scene, your Kill actions deal D8 stress, your body resists normal weapons (D4 Blood protection stacks), and you are visibly, unambiguously not entirely human. Afterwards, mark D6 Hex stress.
  - **Claimed Ground:** Wherever you sleep three nights in a row becomes warded. Hostile supernatural entities cannot enter without your permission. This ward lasts until broken.
  - **Known by Name:** Your pact entity whispers in your ear in moments of genuine danger. You may ask the GM 'is this a trap?' once per situation, and receive an honest answer.

**Witch Queen of the Tide** `[MAJOR]`

You can perform full rituals — workings that reshape circumstances rather than just scenes. A ritual can: curse a ship with persistent misfortune; bind a spirit to a location; call a named weather event to a specific place within a week; open communication with a dead sailor. Each ritual marks D8 Hex stress and requires materials and time.

  - **Blood Rite:** Rituals you perform using your own Blood stress (substituting D6 Blood for D6 Hex) are more powerful — the GM upgrades their effect by one tier.
  - **Coven of Two:** Another character who assists in your rituals shares the Hex stress cost equally. This requires no special training on their part — your knowledge carries both of you.
  - **Permanent Working:** Once per arc, a ritual you perform is permanent unless specifically countered. Curses last indefinitely. Bindings hold until broken by someone who knows what they're doing.

**Storm Made Flesh** `[MAJOR]`

When you are at sea in foul weather, you are in your element. You take no stress from weather effects and roll with mastery on all actions taken during storms, high seas, or supernatural weather events. The Eye of Abendego treats you as a native rather than a victim.

  - **Eye of the Storm:** Once per session, you can create a zone of dead calm around your vessel in the middle of any storm. This lasts one scene. Ships outside the zone are still affected normally.
  - **Lightning Rod:** You can direct lightning during a storm. Once per situation in stormy conditions, you can call a strike on a target within sight — D10 Blood stress, One-Shot, cannot be used again that scene.
  - **The Storm Follows:** When you are seriously harmed (Major Blood fallout or worse), the sky responds. A storm begins to gather. It arrives in full force within D6 hours, regardless of local conditions.

**Familiar Ascended** `[MAJOR]`

Your familiar is no longer merely a creature — it has been elevated by your pact. It can speak, reason, and act independently. It has its own opinions, which are occasionally inconvenient. It can interact with the world at a greater distance and can act as your proxy in negotiations with supernatural entities.

  - **Vessel:** You can inhabit your familiar's body completely, leaving your own body behind. Your body is insensible while you are doing this. You retain your full capabilities in the familiar's form. If the familiar is killed while you are inside it, mark D8 Hex stress and return to your body badly shaken.
  - **Undying:** If your familiar is killed, it reforms within three days from the nearest salt water. It returns changed in some small way each time.
  - **Two Minds:** Your familiar can take independent actions in a scene simultaneously with you — it does not need your attention to act. It can conduct negotiations, steal objects, or perform reconnaissance while you are engaged elsewhere.

**The Tidebreaker** `[MAJOR]`

Once per session, you may attempt to reshape a supernatural situation outright — break a curse on a ship, dismiss an entity from its claimed territory, or sever a magical compulsion. This is contested: you mark D8 Hex stress and roll Brave or Reckon + Arcane. On a success, the working is undone. On a failure, it intensifies.

  - **Severance:** When you successfully break a working, you may keep a thread of it. This thread can be used once to replicate the original effect, directed at a target of your choosing.
  - **The Price Paid Forward:** When you break a curse or working that was afflicting someone else, they take no fallout from its removal. The stress passes to you directly.
  - **Unworking:** You can dismantle another spellcaster's active working in the same scene it was cast. This does not require them to cooperate and does not require a roll if they are of lesser ability — GM determines.

### Zenith Abilities

**The Pact Fulfilled** `[ZENITH]`

Your entity calls in everything. You understand, at last, what it wanted — and the terms become clear. You may accept or refuse. If you accept: the working is titanic. A storm that swallows a fleet. A sea-change that reshapes a coastline. A hex that lays a city low. You cease to be entirely human. How much of you remains is a question the table answers together. If you refuse: the pact shatters. You lose all Hex-related abilities permanently. In exchange, you are free — truly, completely free — of every supernatural claim on you. Both options end your arc.

**Witch-Sovereign of the Eye** `[ZENITH]`

You have become a force the Eye itself acknowledges. Once, you may speak a permanent working into the world — a change to the Shackles itself that outlasts you. A new current. A dead reef come alive. An island that appears for those who need it. A named ship that sails forever without a crew. The GM incorporates this into the world as a permanent truth. You may retire into legend, or carry the weight of it forward.

## The Daredevil

*Always in motion. Never quite where you left them.*
You learned what you know in places that don't appear on any map the respectable world keeps: the travelling circus that wintered in Bloodcove, the knife-throwing act that went wrong in ways you don't discuss, the year you spent with the escapologists and fire-eaters who work the dockside crowds of Port Peril. You are not a planner. You are a mover. Your survival strategy has always been the same: be faster than what's coming, be gone before anyone works out what happened, and if you absolutely must fight, make it spectacular enough that the crowd forgets to stop you leaving. The Shackles is full of people who are very good at standing still and waiting. You have never understood why anyone would do that.

| Core Skill | Core Domain |
|---|---|
| Vanish | Outlaw |

**Refresh:** *Escape from somewhere or something that should have had you, or pull off a physical feat that leaves witnesses genuinely uncertain about what they saw; remove D6 Luck stress.*

### Core Abilities

**Always a Way Out** `[CORE]`

At the start of each situation, you may declare one exit — a route, a gap, a prepared escape — that exists regardless of circumstances. Once per situation, you can use this exit to remove yourself (and one ally who stays close) from immediate danger without a roll. This cannot be used if you are bound, submerged, or inside something sealed. It can be used if you are outnumbered, surrounded, on fire, or in the middle of a conversation that has gone badly.

**Momentum** `[CORE]`

As long as you keep moving, you are harder to catch and harder to hit. When you take any action that involves moving — running, climbing, swinging, diving, vaulting — and succeed, you may immediately move again as part of the same action without a second roll. On a failure, you still move, but something goes wrong along the way. You cannot benefit from this ability if you are standing still.

### Minor Abilities

**Acrobat** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Brave skill. Your body does things that most people's bodies do not. You can climb, vault, flip, and contort through spaces that look impassable. Any action involving movement through difficult terrain — rigging, rooftops, crowded docks, collapsing structures — never increases in difficulty because of the physical challenge. The only things that slow you down are things that would slow down a fish in water.

**Smoke and Shadow** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Carouse skill. Your social ease is your best camouflage. You can disappear into any crowd — a tavern, a market, a dockside gathering — by becoming the most natural person in it. Drinking, laughing, belonging. The GM cannot pursue you out of a social scene if you declare you are running into a crowd, unless they can explain how pursuit is possible through it.

**Escapologist** `[MINOR]`

Being restrained is a professional inconvenience. Ropes, manacles, locked rooms, tied-up-in-the-hold, thrown-in-the-brig — you treat all of these as puzzles with known solutions. You can escape from any physical restraint in one scene, without tools, without a roll. If you want to escape faster — during a situation, under pressure — roll Brave or Vanish. On any success, you are free before it matters.

**Knife Act** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Hunt skill. You can throw a knife with the precision of someone who has been paid to do it without hitting the person standing next to the target. When you throw a blade at a specific, named point — a held weapon, a rope, a lock, a candle — you hit it. When attacking a person with a thrown blade, your Kill dice increases to D8 and gains the Ranged tag. You carry enough knives that running out is never the first problem.

**Quick Draw** `[MINOR]`

You are never caught without a weapon. Regardless of searches, disarmament, or social situations where weapons are forbidden, you always have something concealed — a blade, a garrote, a weighted coin. It is always smaller than ideal. It is always sufficient. You were not caught without a weapon; they simply didn't find the one you had.

**Pickpocket** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Reckon skill. You can lift anything a person is carrying without them noticing, provided you have a moment of proximity and a moment of distraction — real or manufactured. Keys, letters, coin, weapons, the ring on their finger. The GM will tell you what they're carrying before you reach for it. If a target is actively watching for theft, roll Vanish; on a partial success, you take it but they notice something was wrong.

**Face in the Crowd** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Shallows domain. You have memorised the bolt-holes, sea-caves, and reef passages that the Shackles' coastlines offer to those who know how to look. You can vanish into coastal terrain as easily as a port crowd — a cove not on any chart, a passage through the reef that looks like a dead end from the surface. You can pass through a coast that is actively searching for you without a roll, unless you do something memorable.

**Cannonball** `[MINOR]`

Once per situation, you may use a ship's cannon, a catapult, a large trebuchet, or any comparable ballistic device as personal transport. You survive the launch. You land where you aim, roughly. On a success you arrive exactly as intended; on a partial success you arrive close, having taken D4 Blood stress; on a failure the GM decides where you end up, but you are still alive, still moving, and the expression on everyone's face was worth it. The enemy never expects this.

**Misdirection** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Deceive skill. You have the stage magician's gift: people look where you point. Once per situation, you can direct everyone present to look at something other than what you are actually doing. The distraction must be plausible and briefly real — a shout, a thrown object, a staged stumble. While the attention is elsewhere, your next action is automatic, requires no roll, and no one present remembers you were the one who did it.

**Rigging Rat** `[MINOR]`

You move through a ship's rigging at full speed in any conditions without requiring a roll. In combat aboard a vessel or any environment with significant vertical structure, you always have the high ground if you want it, and you can drop on an opponent from above — the first attack after descending from height gains the Brutal tag. Opponents cannot effectively fight you while you are in the rigging unless they are also in the rigging.

**Backstabber's Footwork** `[MINOR]`

In any melee situation, you are always in the best position relative to your target. You never take increased difficulty from flanking disadvantage, fighting in cramped spaces, or enemies attempting to surround you. Moving through a fight costs you nothing — you treat engaged opponents as terrain rather than impediments, stepping past and through them as the situation requires.

**Street-Learned** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Colonial domain. You grew up watching the colonial system and learned how to abuse it from the inside — which paperwork holds up under scrutiny, which officials are worth bribing, which laws have convenient loopholes. When you arrive somewhere with a colonial presence, you know how to move through it without looking like what you are.

**Knife Work** `[MINOR]`

You have a specific and unhurried relationship with knives at close range. Your blade attacks gain the Brutal tag. When you attack someone who is restrained, climbing, or otherwise compromised in their movement, your Kill stress dice increases to D10. You do not fight. You create a situation where the other person cannot fight back, and then you resolve it.

**Always Running Hot** `[MINOR]`

You have been in enough desperate situations that your body has learned to keep functioning through the kind of punishment that stops other people. Gain +1 Blood protection. You do not go down easily, and when you do go down, it is always somewhere inconvenient for whoever put you there.

**Contingency Luck** `[MINOR]`

You do not leave things to chance. The preparations you make before every job — the second exit, the dropped distraction, the alibi arranged days in advance — insulate you from misfortune. Gain +1 Luck protection. Bad luck finds it harder to land on someone who has already partially anticipated it and is moving too fast to be caught by it cleanly.

**The Name No One Knows** `[MINOR]`

Your real identity is so thoroughly buried under performance, movement, and misdirection that attacks on your visible persona do not reach anything that matters. Gain +1 Reputation protection. When your cover is blown or a name is burned, the fallout stops at the surface. You were never that person. You were always already someone else.

### Major Abilities

**The Impossible Route** `[MAJOR]`

Once per situation, you can declare a path through an environment that should not exist — across a burning deck, up the outside of a moving ship, through a crowd of armed opponents, across a gap that is clearly too wide. You take it. It works. The GM describes what it looks like from the outside. Anyone attempting to follow you must roll, and the difficulty is one higher than it would normally be.

  - **Take Someone With You:** One ally can follow The Impossible Route without rolling. They must stay right behind you and do exactly what you do.
  - **Leave Something Behind:** While using The Impossible Route, you may plant something en route — a message, a stolen object returned, a small explosive charge. It is exactly where you put it.
  - **The Impossible Return:** The Impossible Route works in reverse. You can re-enter a location you have already escaped from using the same path, arriving exactly where you want to be, once per session.

**Knife Storm** `[MAJOR]`

Once per situation, you produce and throw more blades than you should reasonably be carrying. Every opponent in a zone you can see must roll to avoid being hit. Each one that fails marks D6 Blood stress. The Spread tag applies. Where these knives came from is a question no one gets a satisfying answer to.

  - **Specialty Load:** Some of the knives in the Storm are alchemical — incendiary, soporific, or contact-poisoned. Choose one effect when you use Knife Storm: all affected targets are on fire (D4 Blood per round), are immediately drowsy (next action Risky), or are poisoned (Degenerating, triggers end of scene).
  - **Pinned:** Targets who fail to avoid Knife Storm are also physically pinned to whatever surface is behind them. They are restrained until they can work free, which takes a full action.
  - **Encore:** Knife Storm can be used twice per situation. The second use is at difficulty 1.

**The Vanishing Act** `[MAJOR]`

Once per session, disappear completely from a scene — mid-conversation, mid-combat, mid-arrest. You simply are not there anymore. No roll, no explanation required. You reappear at the start of the next scene somewhere that makes sense, carrying everything you had. Those who witnessed your disappearance must decide whether to believe what they saw.

  - **Take the Evidence:** When you use The Vanishing Act, you may take one object from the scene with you. It vanishes when you do.
  - **Leave a Double:** When you vanish, something remains in your place briefly — a hat, a coat, a chair occupied by a silhouette that is clearly not quite right. It buys D3 rounds before anyone realises you are gone.
  - **Crowd Work:** The Vanishing Act can be used to remove one ally from the scene alongside you. They must be touching you when you go.

**The Read** `[MAJOR]`

Once per situation, after observing a physical environment or an ongoing fight for one exchange, you narrate exactly how the next minute unfolds — who moves where, what breaks, which escape route opens, which opponent overcommits. It happens as you describe, unless an opponent of exceptional tactical ability is present and the GM rules they can counter it. This is not magic. It is the pattern recognition of someone who has survived a great many rooms.

  - **The Opening:** The Read always identifies one moment in the narrated sequence where your next attack would deal maximum stress automatically, if you choose to take it.
  - **The Counter:** When an enemy attempts a prepared ambush or tactical surprise against you, you may invoke The Read to reverse it: you anticipated this, and they are the ones who walked into something.
  - **The Audience:** The Read can be shared: spend a moment describing it aloud to allies and they all act with mastery on the first exchange. They saw what you saw.

**Daredevil Reputation** `[MAJOR]`

Your name is attached to physical feats that people repeat because no one has a better explanation for what they saw. In any port where your reputation has preceded you, opponents who do not have a strong professional reason to fight you will hesitate before engaging you in a physical confrontation — the first action taken against you in any situation is Risky. Additionally, once per session, invoke your reputation to walk through a door that should be closed to you: your name gets you in.

  - **Audience Effect:** When witnesses are present, your physical actions gain the Brutal tag once per situation, as adrenaline and performance instinct combine. The crowd makes you better.
  - **They Want to See It:** Once per session, the fact that people have heard of you means they will not immediately escalate to violence — they want to see what you do first. This buys you one exchange of conversation or positioning before anything goes wrong.
  - **The Story Travels:** After a situation where you did something genuinely spectacular, the GM introduces a consequence of your growing legend in the next session: a new contact who sought you out, a rival who wants to test you, or a door that opened because of what someone heard you did.

### Zenith Abilities

**The Grand Exit** `[ZENITH]`

Once, at a moment of your choosing, you can leave. Not run — leave. The most spectacular, inexplicable, impossible departure the Shackles has ever witnessed. You define what it looks like: the burning ship you walked off, the naval blockade you sailed through laughing, the prison you were never seen leaving. You go somewhere of your choosing and you arrive intact and free of every consequence that was chasing you. The story of what you did will be told in ports for years. Whether you come back — whether you want to — is entirely up to you.

**Unkillable** `[ZENITH]`

You have cheated death so many times and in such creative ways that it has become a kind of professional credential. You cannot be killed by ordinary violence, ordinary misfortune, or ordinary circumstance any longer. Something always goes wrong for whoever tries — a rope frays, a pistol misfires, a jailer falls asleep. You survive. You keep surviving. The question your arc has been building toward is not whether you will make it out — you will always make it out — but what you are making it out for, and whether endless escape is the same thing as freedom.

## The Corsair

*A force of violence that the sea has sharpened to a point.*
You have fought on more decks than you can count, in storms and calms, outnumbered and alone. Your body carries the testimony — the scars, the missing pieces, the things that healed wrong. You are not merely a fighter. You are a philosophy of controlled destruction, an argument made in blood and roaring iron that some people simply should not be crossed. The sea made you this way. You have made your peace with it.

| Core Skill | Core Domain |
|---|---|
| Brave | Deepwater |

**Refresh:** *Win a fight you were not certain you could win, or stand your ground when retreat was the wiser choice; remove D6 Blood stress.*

### Core Abilities

**The Tested Body** `[CORE]`

Gain an additional Blood stress slot. When you suffer Minor Blood fallout, choose: take the fallout as written, or push through — ignore the fallout until the end of the session, then take it at double severity. You have been broken before. You know what it costs to keep moving.

**Violence as Argument** `[CORE]`

Your reputation for controlled, purposeful violence precedes you. When you make a credible threat of violence to someone who knows your history, they must act as though it is real — the GM treats it as having the weight of action. Once per session, a demonstration of your capability (breaking something, surviving something that should have killed you, ending a fight with impossible efficiency) clears D4 Reputation stress.

### Minor Abilities

**Boarding Master** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Command skill. When you lead a boarding action, your voice and presence organise the chaos — you don't need to yell orders, people just move when you move. You and any allies following your lead add D4 to Blood protection for the first exchange. They fight better with you at the front because they have seen what happens when you are.

**Hard to Kill** `[MINOR]`

The first time per session you would be taken out of a fight by Blood stress, you are not. You are badly hurt — mark the highest available Minor Blood fallout — but you are still standing, and still fighting.

**Weapon Mastery** `[MINOR]`

Choose one weapon type: cutlass, boarding axe, pistol, or naval cannon. Your chosen weapon gains the Brutal tag permanently. When fighting with your chosen weapon against a single opponent, increase the stress dice by one step.

**Scar Tissue** `[MINOR]`

Each Major Blood fallout you have taken and survived increases your Blood protection by 1. The sea has tried to kill you in very specific ways. It will have to try differently next time.

**The Killing Ground** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Plunder skill. After a fight, you move through the aftermath with deliberate efficiency — stripping the fallen, claiming the best weapons, identifying what is worth taking before anyone else has gathered their wits. When you loot a battlefield, a boarded ship, or a defeated enemy, you always find the most valuable item first and take no stress from the process.

**Fleet Feet** `[MINOR]`

Despite your size and the damage your body has taken, you move with purpose. You never take difficulty from difficult terrain in a fight — cramped corridors, rain-slicked decks, bodies underfoot. You find the path because you have always found it.

**Intimidating Presence** `[MINOR]`

Your history is written on you. Opponents who see you in a fight and are of roughly equivalent capability must make a Brave action to close with you. Those who fail hesitate — the GM treats their first action against you as Risky.

**Two-Handed** `[MINOR]`

You fight with two weapons as naturally as breathing. When you attack with two weapons, you inflict stress twice — once with each, though the total is the higher of the two rolls. The secondary attack always gains the Brutal tag.

**Damage Assessment** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Reckon skill. Once per situation, by observing an opponent through one exchange of fighting, you identify their worst injury, their dominant weapon hand, and the one attack they cannot stop. The GM tells you all three.

**Warcry** `[MINOR]`

Once per situation, you can release a battle cry that carries over the noise of combat. Allies within hearing remove D3 Nerve stress. Opponents must make a Brave action or treat their next action as Risky.

**Relentless** `[MINOR]`

You do not stop moving forward. When an opponent retreats, disengages, or attempts to put distance between you and them, you close that distance without a roll unless the environment makes it genuinely impossible. They cannot simply walk away from you.

**Armour of Scars** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Mend skill. You have stitched yourself back together enough times that the process is routine. You can treat your own Blood stress and fallout without assistance and without a Risky penalty. Non-magical weapons that deal less than D6 stress do not trigger fallout rolls against you. You simply absorb them, and you know how to close what they leave behind.

**Iron Stomach** `[MINOR]`

Poisons, diseases, and physical corruption affect you more slowly than they should. When you would mark stress from disease or poison, mark it at one dice size smaller. Major Blood fallout from these sources is instead Minor for you.

**Veteran's Hide** `[MINOR]`

Decades of fighting has made your body efficient at absorbing punishment. Gain +1 Blood protection. The same wound that would sideline another fighter is an inconvenience to you — you have had worse, and you kept moving then too.

**Iron Frame** `[MINOR]`

You are not merely tough. You have been shaped by sustained violence into something that takes more killing than seems reasonable. Gain +1 Blood protection. This stacks with Veteran's Hide — a Corsair who takes both has +2 Blood protection total.

**Earned Fear** `[MINOR]`

Your reputation does part of the defensive work for you. Opponents who know who you are hesitate at the crucial moment. Gain +1 Reputation protection. The legend you have built is a kind of armour — the hesitation it creates is real, and it buys time that keeps you alive.

### Major Abilities

**The Unstoppable** `[MAJOR]`

When you are in active combat, you cannot be stopped by injury alone. Ongoing Blood fallout does not reduce your combat effectiveness — you fight at full capacity regardless of what is written on your sheet until the situation ends. Afterwards, the fallout hits all at once.

  - **The Inevitable:** Opponents who know about The Unstoppable must actively decide to fight you rather than run. NPCs of less than exceptional will choose the latter unless they have no choice.
  - **Roaring Iron:** While The Unstoppable is active, your Kill stress dice increases by one step.
  - **The Body Remembers:** After a situation where The Unstoppable carried you through, remove D4 Blood stress at the start of the next scene. The body settles. It has done this before.

**Dread Name** `[MAJOR]`

Your name is a weapon. When you announce yourself to opponents who have heard of you, they must act on that information — either stand down, attempt to flee, or attack immediately in the desperate hope of getting it over with. The GM determines how well-known you are in any given context, but in the Shackles, the answer is usually 'very'.

  - **The Legend Grows:** Each session in which you do something that would add to your legend, clear D4 Reputation stress. Your reputation maintains itself.
  - **Preemptive Surrender:** Once per session, invoke your reputation before a fight begins. The GM rolls to see if opponents fold before violence starts. Ordinary pirates and soldiers almost always do.
  - **Hunted:** Your reputation attracts challengers — people who want to make their own name by ending yours. Once per arc, the GM introduces a credible rival driven by this logic. Defeating them increases your protection against Reputation stress by 1 for the arc.

**Boarding Pike and Cannon** `[MAJOR]`

You gain fluency with shipboard warfare at scale. When you are directing a boarding action or ship-to-ship engagement, treat each exchange as if you have the Hunt skill and Deepwater domain regardless of whether you have them. Allies acting under your direction in ship combat roll with mastery for the first exchange.

  - **Firing Solution:** Once per naval engagement, you can direct cannon fire with precision. One ship takes D8 Hull stress directly, bypassing normal crew action requirements.
  - **No Quarter:** When the engagement is going your way, you can press so hard that the opposing ship cannot disengage. They must fight or surrender; they cannot simply run.
  - **Aftermath:** After a successful boarding, the captured crew is cowed and compliant without further violence. You have done this enough times that they know what the alternative looks like.

**The Long Memory** `[MAJOR]`

Your body carries its history efficiently. You can read any physical environment — dock, ship, jungle, ruin — and immediately know: the exits, the ambush points, the structural weaknesses, the distance to every threat. The GM provides this information before you act in any new location.

  - **Never Surprised:** You cannot be ambushed. You may be outnumbered, outgunned, and walking into a trap — but you are never caught entirely unaware. The GM must telegraph anything that would constitute surprise.
  - **The Ground I Choose:** Once per situation, you can declare that you anticipated this location. You have prepared something — a stash, a barricade, a loaded weapon hidden somewhere useful. Describe it briefly and it is there.
  - **Escape Route:** You always know the fastest way out of any location. Using it never requires a roll unless the route itself is physically blocked or guarded.

**Iron Will** `[MAJOR]`

Gain +2 to all stress protection. Your history of surviving things that should have killed you has made you genuinely, unreasonably difficult to destroy. The GM may not narrate your death unless you are at zero capability across multiple tracks simultaneously.

  - **Spite:** When you reach zero capability in a stress track, you may act one more time before falling. The action automatically succeeds. Then you go down.
  - **What Doesn't Kill:** Each time you suffer Major fallout in Blood, gain 1 permanent Blood protection. There is no cap.
  - **Not Yet:** Once per arc, you survive something that should have ended your story. The GM describes how. You return the following session changed in some small way, but alive.

### Zenith Abilities

**The Last Stand** `[ZENITH]`

Once, when you are outnumbered, outgunned, and cornered, you may invoke the Last Stand. For the rest of the situation, you cannot be killed, stopped, or driven back. Every attack you make deals maximum stress. Every attack against you deals minimum stress. When the situation ends — one way or another — the reckoning arrives. The GM determines what the sea finally claims.

**Living Legend** `[ZENITH]`

You have fought long enough and hard enough that the Shackles has decided you belong to it permanently. Your death, if it comes, becomes a story. Ships are named for you. Enemies are measured against you. But while you live, ordinary violence simply does not end you — it adds to the account. You can retire into the role of the unkillable old corsair, or press forward into waters where even legends drown.

## The Powder Mage

*The sea taught you to kill at range. Magic taught you to kill better.*
Magic and gunpowder have one thing in common: they both kill you if you handle them carelessly. You have learned to handle both carefully enough that it's everyone else who dies. You channel arcane force through iron and smoke, stitching spellwork into the moment of firing, turning a pistol shot into something that defies physics and good sense. The cost is your own body, slowly, inexorably. The magic is eating through you. You have decided the price is worth it. For now.

| Core Skill | Core Domain |
|---|---|
| Hunt | Arcane |

**Refresh:** *Make a shot that was technically impossible, or push your magic to its limit and survive; remove D6 Blood stress.*

### Core Abilities

**Powder Bond** `[CORE]`

You have a metaphysical connection to gunpowder. You can detonate any powder charge or loaded firearm within thirty paces by will alone, without a roll. You can also sense the presence of loaded weapons and powder stores nearby — the tingle in your fingers tells you. This sense extends to magical equivalents: fire-based workings, alchemical bombs, any ignition source. You always know where the fire is.

**Arcane Load** `[CORE]`

When you fire a weapon you have prepared with arcane investment (a free action at the start of a situation), you may apply one of the following effects: Seeking (the shot bends toward its target, ignoring cover and intervening obstacles); Shatter (on hit, the target's Blood protection is reduced to zero for this situation); Overcharge (increase the stress dice by two steps but mark D4 Blood stress yourself). Each prepared weapon holds one effect. Re-preparing takes ten minutes.

### Minor Abilities

**Called Shot** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Reckon skill. Before firing, you read the geometry of a situation — wind, distance, angle, the target's movement — and the answer arrives complete. When you name a specific target on a person or object before firing — a hand, a rope, a lock, a lantern — and succeed, the shot hits exactly that target. The GM tells you the mechanical consequence. This is not magic. It is calculation.

**Powder Sense** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Outlaw domain. Your need for gunpowder has put you deep into the black market networks that move it — the smugglers, the stolen colonial stockpiles, the alchemical suppliers who don't ask questions. You know the exact location and rough quantity of any gunpowder within fifty paces through your arcane bond. You can detect freshly fired weapons. You also always know where to source powder in any Shackles port, regardless of legal status.

**Detonation Field** `[MINOR]`

Once per situation, you can detonate all powder charges in a zone you can see. This is catastrophic: every weapon misfires, every charge explodes, every grenade triggers simultaneously. Mark D6 Blood stress on yourself from the expenditure. Everyone in the zone takes D8 Blood stress.

**Iron Constitution** `[MINOR]`

The magic eating through you has also toughened you to it. Reduce all Blood stress you take from your own abilities by one dice size. The price is lower — not gone, never gone, but lower.

**Rapid Reload** `[MINOR]`

Firearms you carry never have the Reload tag's hesitation effect — you reload with the speed and efficiency of someone who has made a science of it. You can fire twice in the same exchange if you are using a pistol. The GM may still impose cost for firing into an impossible situation.

**Distant Death** `[MINOR]`

Your maximum effective range is double what it should be. Shots that should be impossible are merely Risky. Shots that are merely Risky are routine. Extreme Range is your preferred distance. Add the Extreme Range tag to all weapons you use.

**The Guided Bolt** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Shallows domain. Your eye for reef geometry, coastal fog banks, and the tight angles of island channels has trained you to shoot around obstacles that others cannot even perceive. Once per session, you may fire a shot that you guide through obstacles — around a corner, through a porthole, along a reef channel — as though you could see around the bend. The shot takes whatever path is needed to reach the target. You must know roughly where the target is.

**Powder Mage's Sight** `[MINOR]`

In any situation involving firearms or explosives, you act first. You read the ballistic geometry of a fight instinctively. When an enemy draws a ranged weapon to fire at you, you may interrupt their action with your own shot before theirs lands.

**Fire Blindness** `[MINOR]`

Opponents in your line of fire cannot see you clearly through the smoke and heat-shimmer you generate. When you have fired at least once in a situation, all ranged attacks against you treat you as though you have partial cover.

**Alchemical Load** `[MINOR]`

You can prepare alchemical rounds — incendiary, concussive, or poisoned. Incendiary: D6 Blood, sets target on fire (D4 Blood per round until extinguished). Concussive: D6 Blood, stuns the target (next action Risky). Poisoned: D4 Blood, Degenerating. You can carry three alchemical rounds between scenes.

**Two Pistols** `[MINOR]`

You fight with two pistols as your standard configuration. Both can be Arcane Loaded with different effects simultaneously. When you fire both in the same action, apply both effects to the same target. Mark D4 Blood stress for the arcane strain of the double working.

**Overclock** `[MINOR]`

Mark D6 Blood stress. For this situation, all your Kill stress dice increase by one step and your shots gain the Brutal tag. The magic runs hot. So does your blood.

**Smoke and Mirrors** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Vanish skill. The smoke from your weapons is under your arcane influence. When you fire, you can direct the smoke to obscure vision — granting all characters in the area the Obscuring benefit, and giving yourself the chance to reposition without being tracked.

**Adapted Flesh** `[MINOR]`

The arcane energy moving through you constantly has toughened you to it. Your body has recalibrated around the damage in ways that would alarm a physician. Gain +1 Blood protection. The magic is eating through you — but slowly, and with less immediate effect than it should have.

**Smoke Cover** `[MINOR]`

The perpetual haze of powder smoke you generate in combat makes you harder to target precisely. Gain +1 Luck protection. What should have been an accurate shot or a clean piece of bad fortune is deflected by the chaos your presence generates.

**Arcane Tolerance** `[MINOR]`

You have been exposed to enough active magical workings — your own and others' — that supernatural interference slides off you more readily than it should. Gain +1 Hex protection. Your body has learned to treat Hex-level effects as background noise.

### Major Abilities

**Powder Keg** `[MAJOR]`

Once per session, you may detonate a powder magazine, a cannon battery, or a large alchemical store. The explosion deals D10 Blood stress to everyone in the area, Hull stress to any vessel present, and destroys the source entirely. You take D6 Blood stress. The area is uninhabitable for the rest of the scene.

  - **Controlled Burn:** You can designate one person or object in the blast radius that does not take stress from the detonation. Everything else is still destroyed.
  - **Distance Detonation:** You can trigger Powder Keg from extreme range, provided you have sensed the powder. You do not need to be nearby.
  - **Chain Detonation:** Powder Keg triggers a chain: every powder charge or weapon within the blast radius detonates as well, potentially expanding the zone. The GM determines the cascade.

**The Duellist's Art** `[MAJOR]`

In single combat with a named opponent, you are at your most precise. When fighting one-on-one, you can apply two Arcane Load effects simultaneously, your shots gain the Piercing tag, and you never suffer difficulty from environmental conditions. You were built for this.

  - **Perfect Shot:** Once per duel, you may declare a shot perfect. It automatically succeeds and deals maximum stress. You must have had at least one exchange first.
  - **Read the Draw:** You always know when your duelling opponent is about to fire. You may interrupt their action with a shot of your own, rolling as normal, before their attack resolves.
  - **Second Gun:** In a duel, you always have a loaded weapon ready as though you prepared for this specific opponent. The first exchange cannot catch you without options.

**Arcane Amplifier** `[MAJOR]`

Your body has adapted to conducting arcane force through gunpowder and iron. The Blood stress cost of your arcane abilities is halved (round up). The range of Powder Bond and Powder Sense doubles. Your arcane workings are visibly inhuman — your shots leave trails of light, your detonations smell of ozone rather than smoke.

  - **Resonance:** When you and another magic-user are in the same situation, your arcane abilities reinforce each other. Your Arcane Load effects can be applied to their magical workings, and vice versa.
  - **Living Powder:** You can store arcane energy in your own blood. Once per session, without spending Blood stress, use any Arcane Load effect twice in the same exchange.
  - **Conduit:** You can channel magical effects from other sources — a spell being cast, a supernatural item, an active ritual — through your weapons, adding their effect to your shot. The GM determines what is available to channel.

**The Cost Made Flesh** `[MAJOR]`

The magic has been eating through you long enough that it has become part of you. Your wounds from arcane strain never fully heal — but they no longer slow you either. You have permanent Minor Blood fallout that cannot be removed, but it never restricts you mechanically. Your body has adapted to operating in this state.

  - **Burning Bright:** The accumulated damage makes you visibly incandescent in arcane sight. Magical entities and magic-users can see you clearly at all times, but you also intimidate them — they treat you as more powerful than your actual level.
  - **Last Resort:** Once per arc, when you are at zero capability, you may burn the cost completely. You act at full power for one scene. After it ends, you are out of the fight for the rest of the session. What the GM does with your character while you are down is between them and the story.
  - **Scar Arcanist:** Your permanent damage carries arcane charge. Your unarmed strikes and grapples deal D4 Blood stress with the arcane tag. The sea is in your blood and it burns.

**Fire and Smoke Command** `[MAJOR]`

You can direct the battlefield through powder and fire at scale. Once per situation, describe a tactical use of gunpowder — a controlled explosion to collapse a structure, a chain of shipboard fires, a coordinated volley from a gun deck you are personally directing. It succeeds as described unless the GM has specific reason it cannot. Mark D6 Blood stress.

  - **Naval Bombardment:** This ability can be used to direct a naval cannon battery. The targeted vessel takes D10 Hull stress. Crew on deck take D6 Blood stress.
  - **Demolition:** Structures you destroy with this ability cannot be rebuilt without significant effort. Walls, fortifications, and dockside buildings fall completely rather than partially.
  - **Signal Fire:** A controlled fire you create can be seen for twenty miles. This is useful for signals, but also for drawing attention to something specific. The GM determines who notices.

### Zenith Abilities

**The Final Charge** `[ZENITH]`

Once, you can let it all go. Every drop of arcane force you have stored in your body detonates simultaneously. The effect is apocalyptic at scale — a city block, a ship, a fortress, a fleet. Everything within a wide radius takes maximum stress. The magic burning through you fulfils its purpose. You survive, but only barely, and the cost is everything you spent a career building: your body will never hold arcane force again. You live, but you are no longer a powder mage. The question is: was it worth it?

**The Perfectionist** `[ZENITH]`

You take the shot that cannot be taken. Once, at a moment of your choosing, you can make a shot of impossible precision — through time, through walls, across an ocean, into a wound that was never healed — and it lands. The target suffers its full consequence. There are no conditions on this. It simply happens, as the universe finally agrees that you are right. The cost is the price you have always been paying, come due.

## The Sin Bosun

*The chart and the prayer book are the same document. You are the only one who can read both.*
Every ship has a sin bosun. The crew might call them the padre, the holy, the god-botherer, the one who talks to the sky. What they mean is: the person you find when someone is dying, and also the person you find when you are lost. In the Shackles those two jobs have always been the same job, because the sea does not distinguish between a body it is killing and a ship it is sinking. Both need someone who knows where they are going. You serve your god by moving through the world they made. Besmara who owns every plank that touches her water, Gozreh who is the wind in the sail and the storm against the hull, or something older and less named that speaks in the language of current and depth. Your theology is practical: the god's domain is the sea, and to navigate the sea correctly — to read its weather, to know its routes, to bring ships and people through it alive — is an act of worship. The chart is the scripture. The stars are the congregation. The passage completed is the prayer answered. The crew does not always understand this. They come to you when someone goes over the side in the dark and you say the words that settle the debt between that soul and the water. They come to you when the charts run out and you find the route anyway, reading the sky and the current and the colour of the water and something else, something that does not have a nautical name. They come to you in the hour before a fight and you tell them what the god requires, which is usually that they survive it. You are not a soft person. You are not the priest who offers comfort as a substitute for honesty. The sea is not kind and your god is not kind and the mercy you offer is the specific, practical mercy of someone who has studied how to get people through things. You know the route. You know the rite. You know, better than most, that the difference between them is mostly a matter of what is doing the killing.

| Core Skill | Core Domain |
|---|---|
| Navigate | Faith |

**Refresh:** *Complete a successful passage through genuinely dangerous waters, or perform a rite that settles something the sea was watching; remove D6 Hex stress.*

### Core Abilities

**Divine Sanction** `[CORE]`

Name your deity at character creation: Besmara, Gozreh, or something else that claims the sea as its domain. Work with the GM to establish two things the god requires of those who cross their waters, and two things the god forbids. When you act in direct accordance with what the god requires, roll with mastery. When a member of your crew violates what the god forbids, mark D4 Hex stress — the god noticed, and so did you. Once per session, you may ask your deity for a sign. The GM answers as the god would: honestly, obliquely, and with an agenda. The sign always relates to the passage ahead.

**The Chart and the Prayer** `[CORE]`

You maintain two things that are, to you, the same document: a navigational chart more accurate than anything commercially available in the Shackles, and a record of every rite, omen, offering, and divine intervention you have witnessed or performed on the water. When you consult either before acting, the GM gives you one true piece of information about what lies ahead — navigational, spiritual, or both. Once per voyage, you may automatically succeed on a navigation challenge that would otherwise require a roll. Once per session, a sign from the god confirms or contradicts what the chart says, and both are always true.

### Minor Abilities

**The Eye's Edge** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Shallows domain. You understand the boundary where the Eye of Abendego meets the reef-threaded islands as both a navigational and spiritual frontier — the place where your god's attention is most concentrated and the water most demanding. You can navigate the perimeter of the Eye without requiring rolls in conditions that would force them on others, reading the reef geometry through the chaos as a form of scripture. What the storm destroys, it destroys for reasons. You know the reasons.

**Last Rites** `[MINOR]`

When you preside over a death at sea — a funeral, a burial, a sailor going over the side — the deceased's debt to the water is settled. They cannot be raised as undead without overcoming your god's claim on them. The rite takes five minutes and requires only your words and your attention. The crew who witnesses it marks D3 fewer Reputation stress from the loss. The protection is permanent. The god keeps what it is given properly.

**Read the Omen** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Reckon skill. You read signs in the natural world the way a scholar reads text — the behaviour of seabirds, the colour of the water, the way a storm forms against the prevailing wind. Once per scene, the GM gives you an omen: an interpretation of something observable as divine communication. This is always true. It may not be immediately useful. It will be relevant before the session ends.

**Sermon on the Deck** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Carouse skill. Your faith is expressed in shared meals, rum poured for the dead, songs sung off-watch with the crew in the last hour before a hard day. You lead worship the way a good mate leads a crew — by belonging to it. When you drink, feast, or mark a moment alongside your crew in the god's name, allies remove D4 Nerve stress. Enemies who follow a conflicting faith must succeed on a Brave action to move against you directly.

**Tide Prayer** `[MINOR]`

Once per session, you may pray at sea for a specific weather condition — a wind from the right quarter, a fog bank, a storm that arrives early or a calm that holds past when it should break. The GM determines how long the answer takes and how precisely it matches your request. In a crisis, the response is faster but less exact. The god is not a vending machine. The god is, however, paying attention to you specifically, and the gap between what you need and what you receive is usually negotiable.

**Star-Reader** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Arcane domain. The stars carry information that has nothing to do with navigation and everything to do with it. Once per session at night, you can read an omen from the sky about a specific person, ship, or course of action. The GM answers honestly but in astronomical metaphor. You have spent long enough at the rail staring upward that you have learned the difference between what a star means and what the god is saying through it, and that the two are rarely the same thing.

**The Secret Route** `[MINOR]`

You know routes that appear on no official chart — passages that require both navigational knowledge and an understanding of what the god permits to cross safely. Once per arc, take a ship somewhere in half the expected time through an unmapped channel, an uncommon current, a passage that should not be navigable but is navigable if the right words are said before the helm is put over. You have said the words before. You know which ones apply here.

**Ward the Ship** `[MINOR]`

You can ward a vessel against supernatural attention — hostile workings, spirit possession, the kind of Hex-level interference that the sea generates around ships that have drawn the wrong kind of notice. The ward lasts one voyage and requires renewal at each port: a rite performed at the bow and stern, an offering thrown over the side, words said at the change of watch for three consecutive nights. While the ward holds, the crew does not suffer Hex stress from the voyage's natural strangeness, and the ship is harder to locate by magical means.

**Dead Reckoning** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Mend skill. Keeping a course through the dark requires keeping the crew alive and functional — the sin bosun who lets their helmsman go down with fever loses the ship along with the soul. You know how to treat exhaustion, exposure, and the slow attrition of a hard passage with what is available at sea. Even without instruments, in total darkness, fog, or supernatural obscuration, you can maintain a vessel's course accurately for D6 hours without a roll. You do not get lost. This is not arrogance. It is the product of knowing where you are going and believing that you will get there.

**Confessor** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Outlaw domain. People tell you things. Once per port visit, an NPC confesses something to you — their real fear, their hidden plan, the thing they have done that they cannot tell anyone else — because your presence drew it out of them or because you asked the right way. The GM determines who and what. The information is real. What you do with it is between you and your god, who has opinions about betrayal and about silence in roughly equal measure.

**Weatherwise** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Deepwater domain. The best weather intelligence comes from the water itself — the temperature of the current, the smell of the air three days before the storm, the silence of the seabirds an hour before the squall. When you read the sea and sky with time and attention, the GM provides accurate weather intelligence for the next D6 days of sailing without a roll. When you forecast weather and act on it, your predicted window is always correct. The god does not surprise those who are paying attention.

**Consecrated Ground** `[MINOR]`

You can consecrate any location as a temporary shrine: a ship's bow, a cave above the tideline, a clearing on an island no one has named. Consecrated ground costs hostile supernatural entities D4 Hex stress to enter. Rites performed there cost half the normal Hex stress. Your god's attention is easier to call and faster to arrive. Consecrating takes ten minutes and a small offering. The effect lasts one session. The god remembers where you have made places for it.

**Miracles Cost** `[MINOR]`

When you call for a miracle — an immediate, visible, concrete divine intervention — your god may answer. Mark D8 Hex stress and describe what you need. The GM determines whether the deity agrees, at what cost beyond the stress, and what form the answer takes. The answer is never exactly what was requested. It is always sufficient. Miracles are real. They are not free. The god is not charitable. The god is interested in you, which is different, and considerably more useful.

**Divine Ward** `[MINOR]`

Your god's attention is a shield as much as a weight. Gain +1 Hex protection. Supernatural workings directed at you must first contend with a prior divine claim — whatever is trying to curse or hex you has to explain to your god why it thinks it has the right. This conversation takes long enough to matter.

**God's Own Name** `[MINOR]`

You carry your deity's authority in the way you move and speak. Gain +1 Reputation protection. Attacks on your standing are attacks on your god's standing, and the Shackles understands this even when it does not believe. Rumour and slander lose their edge when they brush up against something that entire fleets have made offerings to.

**Prepared Mind** `[MINOR]`

You do not leave outcomes to chance when preparation is possible. Gain +1 Luck protection. What others experience as bad luck, you have frequently anticipated through the specific combination of divine sign-reading and navigational preparation that defines your practice. The god warns those who listen. You have always listened.

### Major Abilities

**Avatar Moment** `[MAJOR]`

Once per session, your deity speaks through you directly. Your eyes change. Your voice carries past where it should reach. What you say lands with divine authority — your Command actions automatically succeed against mortal targets for one exchange, and those who hear you in this state carry something of the encounter forward. The god is not using you as a tool. The god is present, briefly, in the way that the sea is present in a cup of seawater: genuinely, completely, and with implications that persist after the vessel has been set down.

  - **The Route Revealed:** During Avatar Moment, you may ask the GM the safest, fastest, or most meaningful route to any destination your crew needs to reach. The god knows every passage. The answer is accurate and immediate.
  - **Healing Word:** During Avatar Moment, once, you can remove D8 Blood stress from any character in your presence. The god tends what crosses its waters. You are the instrument of that tending.
  - **Extended Presence:** Avatar Moment lasts a full scene rather than a single exchange. Mark D6 Hex stress at the end. The god does not linger without cost, but it lingers.

**Storm Calling** `[MAJOR]`

You can call a storm at sea in your god's name. It arrives within D3 hours, is severe, and lasts until you release it or until D6 hours have passed, whichever comes first. You can direct it broadly: toward a fleet, away from a harbour, into a specific stretch of ocean. Mark D8 Hex stress to call it. Ships caught without your protection take D8 Hull stress per exchange. This is not magic. This is asking the god to do what the god does, and having the relationship that means the god agrees.

  - **The Eye:** You can create a zone of calm within your storm where your vessel sails safely. Enemies in the storm who lack this protection cannot follow without taking Hull stress each exchange.
  - **Lightning:** Once per scene within your storm, call a lightning strike on a specific target you can see. D10 Blood or Hull stress. The god's aim is not always precise. It is always intentional.
  - **Rider of the Storm:** While within a storm you have called, you take no Hex or Blood stress from weather effects and roll with mastery on all navigation and shipboard actions. You asked for this. You are ready for it.

**The Perfect Course** `[MAJOR]`

Once per session, you can chart a course that accounts for every variable — weather, current, spiritual hazard, divine attention, the specific dangers of the route, and the requirements of the destination. If the crew follows it exactly, the voyage to the destination succeeds without incident, regardless of what would normally have made it difficult. This is not divine intervention. This is complete navigational and spiritual preparation meeting complete competency. The god clears the way for those who have done the work of being ready.

  - **Pursuit Course:** The Perfect Course can be designed to intercept a specific vessel. If you know where it started and have time to plan, you can catch anything that sails the Shackles.
  - **Escape Course:** The Perfect Course can guarantee escape from any pursuit. Enemies without equivalent navigational and spiritual knowledge cannot follow.
  - **The Impossible Passage:** Once per arc, The Perfect Course can take a ship through genuinely impassable waters — through the Eye of Abendego, across a divine boundary, into waters the charts name as void. The god permits what the god permits. You have asked correctly.

**The Hungry Church** `[MAJOR]`

Your faith has taken root. You have followers in multiple ports — not a formal congregation, but a loose community of pirates, fisherfolk, dockworkers, and sailors who acknowledge your god through you. They come to you when someone goes over the side. They come to you when the weather turns wrong. They come to you before a hard voyage and you tell them what the god requires, which is that they pay attention and respect what they are crossing. Once per arc, call on this community for a significant collective action: a port-wide offering before a battle season, a coordinated search for a lost vessel, a funeral rite for a dead legend that settles something the Shackles has been carrying.

  - **Tithe:** Your community contributes to your material position out of something between gratitude and insurance. Once per port visit, clear D4 Loot stress from their support.
  - **Network of the Faithful:** Your congregation functions as an intelligence network with a specific character: they notice things that feel wrong before they become things they can name. Once per session, ask the GM what your congregation has sensed that would be useful to you. They always know something. It is rarely comforting.
  - **The Compact:** Your god has, through your congregation's sustained devotion, formally acknowledged this community as under its protection. Ships belonging to congregation members do not suffer Hex stress from the Shackles' natural supernatural strangeness. The god looks after what has been given to it.

**Divine Compact** `[MAJOR]`

## The Castaway

*The island changed you. You are still changing.*
It was not the shipwreck that did it. The shipwreck was just how you arrived. What did it was the weeks and months after. The interior of an island in the Shackles is not like the coast — the coast is just rock and water and the ordinary violence of the sea. The interior is something else. Something that has been sitting there, largely undisturbed, for longer than the Shackles has had a name, longer than Cheliax has been an empire, longer than human beings have been coming to these islands and calling them discovered. The jungle does not care that you are there. The ruins scattered through it — the Ghol-Gan stonework with its geometries that do not quite resolve into sense, the older things below the Ghol-Gan that you tried not to think about too hard — those did not care either. You ate what grew there. You drank from its springs. You slept in its ruins because the stone held heat and the things that nested in the trees did not come inside. You came back. You are still not entirely sure what that means. The changes were gradual and are ongoing. Some of them are useful. Some of them are not exactly comfortable. None of them have a satisfying explanation, which you have made your peace with because the explanations people offer — the island was cursed, you were touched by something, you ingested a spore or a toxin or a blessing — are all approximately as useful as each other, which is to say not very. What you know is that your body does things now that it did not do before. That the jungle feels familiar in a way it should not. That the creatures that live in the deep interior of the Shackles' islands treat you as something adjacent to kin. That occasionally, in the dark, you hear something that is not exactly a voice say something that is not exactly words, and it has not been wrong yet. You have decided not to worry about what it means. You have decided to be useful.

## The Bonesaw

*The ship is a body. The body is a ship. Both can be fixed.*

You have built splints from spare belaying pins and rigged replacement tillers from the spars of enemy ships, and the problems felt identical. You do not distinguish. You diagnose, you cut, you fix, you move. Whether the patient thanks you is their business. Whether the patient functions is yours. And always, underneath the immediate work, there is the project — the thing you are building toward, the great problem you have been solving in your head across every voyage. A prosthetic hand that closes when the muscles in the forearm flex. A pump system that cannot be outrun by any rate of flooding. A mechanical lung for a sailor whose own gave out. It will take years. It may not be possible. You are going to build it anyway.

## The Errant

*They brought a tradition. The Shackles does not have one. This is proving to be an advantage.*
You came from somewhere with rules. Not the Pirate Code — that is a practical document, a contract between people who distrust each other, enforced by the threat of violence. You came from somewhere with forms: the bow before the engagement, the offered hilt, the acknowledgement of terms, the understanding that violence conducted correctly is something distinct from violence conducted badly, and that the distinction matters. Maybe you were Chelish nobility before a political miscalculation. Maybe you trained in the duelling academies of Absalom, or in one of the old Tian sword traditions that crossed the Crown of the World a century ago and never entirely went home. Maybe you were a knight, or a soldier-scholar, or a court champion, before whatever happened happened and the Shackles became the only place that would have you. You carry the form with you the way some people carry a language — not as an affectation but as the actual structure through which you perceive things. When you look at a fight, you see lines and angles and the grammar of engagement that your tradition mapped out centuries before you were born. The pirates of the Shackles are dangerous, many of them, in ways that are real and serious. None of them were trained. None of them have a lineage. None of them have spent ten years in a school learning to fight a problem before it arrived. This is not arrogance. It is simply a fact about what formal training does, and the Shackles is currently in the process of learning it.

Gain the Carouse skill. You understand that combat in the Shackles is audience as much as violence — the crew watching from the rigging, the captains taking notes, the port that will hear the story tomorrow. When fighting where others can observe you, roll with mastery on the first exchange. Witnesses mark it. Afterwards, you know how to work a room: the right drink bought for the right person, the story told with the right emphasis, the reputation managed as carefully as the blade.

**Blade Before Powder** `[MINOR]`

In any engagement that begins with melee weapons, you move first regardless of other factors. Your first exchange cannot be interrupted by anything short of a supernatural intervention. The form demands a first word. You have already said it before most people realise the conversation has begun.

**The Patient Riposte** `[MINOR]`

When an opponent misses you or fails their action against you in single combat, you may immediately respond with an attack that rolls with mastery. You were not waiting for an opening. You were manufacturing one by presenting something worth swinging at. The opportunity is created by their decision. You simply had the patience to let them make it.

**Disarm** `[MINOR]`

Instead of dealing stress with an attack, you may disarm your opponent. They lose their primary weapon until they can recover it, which requires a full action. Against a trained opponent, this is often more psychologically effective than a wound — they know what it means. Against an untrained one, it is simply efficient. The disarmed weapon lands where you choose.

**Second Steel** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Brave skill. You fight equally well with either hand. When disarmed, you produce a second weapon without a roll — it was always there. A tradition that does not account for the loss of the primary weapon is not a complete tradition. Yours is complete.

**Named Technique** `[MINOR]`

Develop one signature technique drawn from your tradition — a specific attack, defence, or combination that has a name in the old language of your school. Name it. When you use it, roll with mastery. It does not work against the same opponent twice in the same session — they have seen it now, which tells you something about their quality. The fact that they could adapt at all is worth noting.

**Measured Threat** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Endure skill. When you indicate with precision — not bluster, not volume, but the cold technical implication of someone who has done this before in formal settings — that a situation is about to become violent, NPCs of reasonable intelligence believe it without demonstration. The colonial world produced a specific kind of person who can make death sound like a scheduling matter. You are that person.

**Counter-Read** `[MINOR]`

Once per situation, when an opponent successfully uses a technique against you, you may choose to learn it immediately — adding it to your own understanding for the rest of the situation. You can use their technique against them. Against this specific opponent, it is now expected rather than surprising, which tells them something they probably did not want you to know: that you can learn anything they have in a single exchange.

**The Open Invitation** `[MINOR]`

Gain the Vanish skill. Your footwork is precise enough to serve as controlled invisibility in a crowd. In any fight with multiple opponents, you can split your attention without penalty — your positioning makes you difficult to surround and your movement through a group of fighters is unhurried and exact. You retain the benefits of The Form as long as you address each opponent individually. You never lose the thread of a fight.

**Scar of the Old School** `[MINOR]`

You trained somewhere or with someone whose name means something in the civilised world. When dealing with anyone who recognises your form or your lineage — colonial officers, other trained fighters, people who understand what a named school represents — you begin the encounter at a social advantage that has nothing to do with your current standing in the Shackles. They know who taught you. That is information about what you are capable of.

**Schooled Defence** `[MINOR]`

Your training includes not merely how to strike but how to absorb and redirect force efficiently. Gain +1 Blood protection. The Errant's body is an instrument, and part of that instrument's maintenance is knowing how to take a hit without it ending the argument. Your tradition spent centuries working out how to survive being wrong once.

**The Record Speaks** `[MINOR]`

Your documented history — in formal challenge records, in the archives of whatever institution trained you, in the accounts that travel ahead of you — does protective work independently. Gain +1 Reputation protection. Slander and deliberate reputation damage must contend with a paper trail in multiple colonial jurisdictions. You are harder to unmake than most people in the Shackles, because most people in the Shackles do not officially exist.

**Reading the Room** `[MINOR]`

The same analytical eye that reads a fighter reads a situation. Gain +1 Luck protection. You identify the shape of oncoming misfortune the same way you identify a telegraphed attack — not through supernatural awareness but through the pattern recognition of someone who was trained to see what is actually happening rather than what appears to be happening.

### Major Abilities

**The Reputation of the Blade** `[MAJOR]`

Your record — formal challenges accepted and resolved, the names of those you have fought, the tradition you represent — is known and legible to anyone with colonial connections, which in the Inner Sea means most ports worth calling at. In colonial-influenced territories, fighters who know your reputation must make a Brave action to engage you directly in single combat. Those who fail find reasons to delay. Additionally, once per session, your name opens a door: a colonial official who will hear you out, a naval officer who stands down rather than escalate, a port that waves you through.

  - **The Inevitable Result:** NPCs who are aware of your full record and are not under compulsion do not challenge you to a fight they believe they will lose. They find other methods — which is often worse for everyone, and frequently more interesting.
  - **The Young Ones Come:** Rising fighters seek you out to prove themselves against a named opponent. Once per arc, one arrives who is genuinely trained. How you handle them — whether you defeat them, teach them, recruit them, or send them away changed — is yours to determine.
  - **Colonial Weight:** In formal negotiations with colonial representatives, your name and lineage carry institutional weight independent of your current status as a pirate. Once per arc, invoke this to achieve an outcome that would require significant political capital from anyone without a formal fighting pedigree.

**The Impossible Defence** `[MAJOR]`

Once per session, when you would take Blood stress from a melee attack, declare that the attack does not land. You parry, redirect, or step out of something that should have connected. This is not magic and not luck. It is the product of training so thorough that your body has answers it did not consciously formulate. It works once, cleanly, without discussion. You do not explain how.

  - **Twice the Parry:** Use The Impossible Defence twice per session.
  - **The Redirected Blade:** When you use The Impossible Defence, the attack is redirected to the nearest enemy of your attacker. If none exists, it passes harmlessly into space in a way that is technically impressive.
  - **The Teaching Moment:** After The Impossible Defence, your attacker has revealed something about their approach that they cannot conceal again. They cannot make the same attack against you for the rest of the situation, and you may ask the GM one additional question about their capabilities that must be answered honestly.

**The Point That Ends Arguments** `[MAJOR]`

Once per session, in single combat, make one attack that functions as a final statement. Roll to hit as normal. On any success — partial or full — the target is not merely damaged but made to understand the conclusion of the exchange. They retreat, surrender, yield, or accept defeat. They are not dead unless you choose. The argument has been made completely, and they know it. The form demands this option exist. You have always offered it.

  - **The Witnessed Defeat:** If others observe the defeat, the target suffers Reputation fallout as well as whatever physical consequence the fight produced. The Shackles runs on witnessed events. This was witnessed.
  - **The Spare:** When you use this ability and choose not to kill, the defeated opponent owes you a debt that the social codes of both the colonial world and the Shackles consider binding. The GM tracks what they owe and when it becomes relevant.
  - **The Final Argument:** Once per arc, this ability works against a supernatural entity, an inhuman creature, or something that should not be subject to the conventions of formal engagement. They understand, regardless of their nature, that something has been concluded. The form is older than they are.

**The Question of Why** `[MAJOR]`

You have been fighting long enough, and far enough from where you started, to have developed a considered position on what the form is actually for. Choose one: You fight because the tradition demands it — because you owe a debt to everyone who refined it before you, and the debt is paid in flawless execution (once per situation, fighting to honour the form itself gives mastery on all Kill actions this scene). Or you fight because you have decided it no longer matters why — because the form is simply what you are, regardless of what it was built for (once per situation, fighting for purely practical ends gives mastery on all Kill actions this scene). The choice is permanent until you take the Reversal upgrade.

  - **The Reversal:** Once per arc, change which position you hold. The session in which you change, all your Kill actions roll with mastery. The change is genuine and costs something. What it costs is between you and the GM.
  - **The Third Position:** You have found an answer that is neither of the original options. Work with the GM to define it — it must be specific to your character, your lineage, and what the Shackles has done to both. Once per session, fighting from this position gives mastery and removes D4 Reputation stress. You have stopped translating. This is what the form always meant.
  - **The Students Know:** Your position on this question has become part of what your school teaches. Any student you have taken on is asked to find their own answer. The relationship between your answer and theirs shapes how they develop and what they are capable of at their best.

**The School** `[MAJOR]`

You have formalised your tradition into a living school — named, located (at least nominally), with students and a documented lineage that now includes you. It has a philosophy, two or three defining principles you have refined or inherited, and a presence in the world that outlasts any single engagement. Work with the GM to establish the school's name, its origin tradition, and its current standing — whether it is a secret society, a formal colonial institution, a scattered diaspora of graduates, or something operating entirely outside the conventions of either world.

  - **The Alumni Network:** Your school's graduates are a faction — scattered across the Inner Sea, recognisable to each other by form and footwork, sharing intelligence and occasionally resources. This functions as a web of contacts with expertise in fighting technique, colonial navigation, and the social protocols of formal violence.
  - **The Rival School:** A competing tradition disputes yours — philosophically, politically, and in blade-work. Their graduates are well-matched opponents and the rivalry generates story. Defeating a notable rival graduate in a witnessed engagement provides significant Reputation recovery and advances the school's standing.
  - **The Colonial Charter:** Your school has, through your efforts and your lineage, obtained formal recognition from at least one colonial power — a charter, a patronage, a letter of endorsement from someone whose name carries weight. This opens doors in colonial ports and institutions that are otherwise closed, and gives your challenges legal standing in colonial jurisdictions.

### Zenith Abilities

**The Last Bout** `[ZENITH]`

The argument reaches its final form. You face the opponent who represents the ultimate test of what you and your tradition have been building toward — the one person, institution, or force whose defeat or defeat of you answers the question your arc has been asking. Win or lose, the exchange is perfect in form. Whatever the Shackles has done to you, whatever the tradition has cost you, this is what it was for. The Shackles will remember it. So will the colonial world, when word reaches it. Both will interpret it differently. Both will be partially right.

**Beyond Argument** `[ZENITH]`

You have fought enough and translated enough and survived enough that you have stopped being a person who practices a tradition and become, in some fundamental sense, what the tradition was always pointing toward. You cannot be surprised in single combat. You cannot be outmatched by technique alone — only by things that are not, in any meaningful sense, fighting. Once per session, you may declare that a fight is over before it begins: the opponent sees what you are, what you carry, the centuries of refinement behind the way you are standing, and they make a different choice. This is not intimidation. This is the form, fully expressed, doing what it was always designed to do.
