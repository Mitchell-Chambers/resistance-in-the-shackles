# Sawbone

_The saw that opens a chest cavity and the saw that cuts a rotten plank are the same saw. The pins that hold a shattered femur together and the pins that splice a split timber are the same gauge. You worked out early that a ship's carpenter and a ship's surgeon are doing the same job — assess the damage, remove what cannot be saved, reinforce what can, return the patient to function — and the Shackles has spent your whole career confirming it. You have taken off arms below decks during a broadside and crawled into the bilge to patch shot-holes in the same afternoon, and the problems felt identical. The others aboard traffic in pacts and omens and reputations; you traffic in copper, tar, catgut and load. When the witch's familiar dies, when the god goes quiet, when the luck runs out — they bring it to you, because you are the only one aboard whose power does not have an opinion. And underneath the daily work there is the project: the thing you are building that the Shackles says is impossible. The hand that closes when the forearm flexes. The pump no flooding can outrun. The heart that beats without flesh to drive it. It will take years. It may not work. You are going to build it anyway._

## Core Traits
- SKILL: Tend
- DOMAIN: Deepwater
- RESOURCE: A jar of copper pins and good tar D6 Deepwater
- EQUIPMENT: Bone saw Brave D6
    - Pick one:
        - Carpenter's maul Brave D8, Brutal, Tiring
        - Surgeon's field roll Tend Blood D6
        - Tar, canvas and copper patching kit Tend Hull D6

## CORE ABILITIES

### THE SAME SAW
_The ship is a body. The body is a ship. Both can be fixed._ Your Tend rolls apply equally to flesh and timber — you never choose a specialism. When you spend a minute examining a person or a vessel, the GM tells you what is broken, how badly, what will fail next if nothing is done, and what it would take to fix it. You always know what you are looking at.

### THE GRAND PROJECT
_It will take years. It may not be possible. You are building it anyway._ At character creation, define your grand project: a piece of engineering or surgery beyond what the Shackles believes possible — a thinking prosthetic, an unsinkable hull, a mechanical organ. Work with the GM to establish three milestones: discoveries to be made, materials to be sourced, techniques to be proven in play. When the project is complete, it works. Exactly as designed.

## MINOR ABILITIES

### AMPUTATION
_This is not a last resort. Sometimes it is the most efficient solution._ Gain the Brave skill. When a limb or extremity is the site of a Major Blood fallout, you can remove it cleanly in one action: the fallout is removed and all Blood stress clears immediately. The limb is gone. You carry everything you need to do this at any time.

### BONESETTER
_Most surgeons would not call it medicine. Most surgeons lose more patients._ Gain the Wilderness domain. You can treat wounds, infections and tropical disease with what grows or washes up anywhere in the Shackles; when treating Blood fallout with improvised materials in the field, your Tend dice increases by one step.

### THE CARPENTER'S EYE
_Everything built is a diagram of how it fails._ Gain the Plunder skill. When you assess any structure — ship, fort, dock, ruin — you immediately know its load-bearing points, its failure modes, and the fastest way to strip it, shore it up or bring it down.

### JOURNEYMAN'S PAPERS
Gain access to one of the following skills: Brave, Carouse, Command, Deceive, Endure, Navigate, Plunder, Reckon, Tend. You can take this advance more than once.

### THE INDISPENSABLE
_Touch the cook and the crew grumbles. Touch the sawbones and the crew mutinies._ Gain +2 Reputation protection.

### SALVAGE INSTINCT
_You know what the wreck is worth before the water settles._ Gain the Port domain. You know every chandlery, ship-breaker and no-questions supplier in the Shackles, and when you encounter a wreck or damaged vessel you instantly know its recoverable value and who will pay for it.

### SHIPS' GRAVEYARDS
_Low tide is a market and you have the only key._ Gain the Shallows domain. You know where the wrecks lie along any coast you've worked, and when careening or repairing a beached vessel, the work takes half the usual time.

### STEADY HANDS
_The deck heels, the guns roar, the lantern gutters. The stitch goes where you put it._ Gain the Endure skill. Environmental conditions — ship movement, battle, darkness, exhaustion — never increase the difficulty of work done with your hands.

### TOOLS OF THE TRADE
_Your kit is your capital, and your skills convert to coin in any port in the world._ Gain +2 Gold protection.

### TRIAGE
_Who dies without you, who can wait, who is already gone._ Gain the Reckon skill. When multiple people are injured, the GM tells you all three answers in a single look, without a roll.

### WHERE THE WORK IS
Gain access to one of the following domains: Deepwater, Shallows, Outlaw, Port, Colonial, Ruins. You can take this advance more than once.

### WORKED HANDS
Gain +1 Protection in the Blood, Reputation, Luck or Gold resistance. You can take this advance more than once.

### YARD-TRAINED
_You apprenticed where the standards were written down._ Gain the Colonial domain. You know the navies' yards, stores, surgeries and paperwork from the inside, and your work passes their inspections — which opens doors a pirate's work should not.

## MAJOR ABILITIES

### CANNIBALISED PARTS
_Everything is parts, if you're honest about it._ Once per session, consume a resource with the Port or Colonial domain by stripping it for materials. Roll the resource's dice and choose one:
- Remove stress from a person's Blood or a vessel's Hull equal to the amount rolled.
- Inflict stress on a journey or an adversary equal to the amount rolled, as you jury-rig exactly the wrong surprise for them.
- (D8 or higher) Gain access to a skill or domain for the rest of the session — the manual was in the crate.
- (D8 or higher) Increase your Blood protection by 1 for the rest of the situation — armour the work.
- MINOR: ANYTHING'S PARTS. Resources with any domain qualify. A relic is brass and leverage; a holy text is paper and binding wire.
- MINOR: THE GOOD STUFF. When you consume a resource of D8 or higher value, choose two benefits instead of one.
- MINOR: TWICE OVER. You may use CANNIBALISED PARTS twice per session. The second time, mark D4 Luck stress — somewhere, something you stripped is missed.

### THE LIVING SHIP
_You have mapped every weakness, tracked every repair, predicted every failure. The ship does not surprise you._ Once per session, the GM warns you of a threat to your vessel before it lands — the shot that will find the magazine, the reef that will open the hull, the plank that gives in the next storm. While you are aboard, Hull stress marked against your vessel is reduced by 1.
- MINOR: REPAIR IN MOTION. Once per situation, clear D4 Hull stress as a free action during an engagement. You were already down there.
- MINOR: THE SOUL PERSISTS. If your ship is lost, salvage one essential piece of her from the wreckage. The next vessel you fit it to becomes your ship immediately — you know her weaknesses as if you'd served aboard a year.
- MINOR: NO DEAD SHIPS. A vessel you actively maintain cannot sink from weather, flooding or reef damage alone — only sustained, deliberate, overwhelming force. You keep finding the solution just in time.

### ENGINEERING MIND
_If it could exist, you can build it._ You can design and build anything achievable with Shackles materials: pumps, siege engines, fire-weapons, mechanical locks, surgical instruments that do not yet exist, and prosthetics that restore full function to lost limbs and worse. The GM sets the time and materials required. The product functions as designed.
- MINOR: RAPID BUILD. A week's construction takes a day; a day's takes an hour. Your hands know the answer before your mind finishes articulating it.
- MINOR: SABOTEUR. You can identify and exploit the structural weaknesses of enemy ships, fortifications and machinery without a roll. Your sabotage is effective, subtle, and always looks like an accident.
- MINOR: THE PERFECT INSTRUMENT. Once per arc, build one item of exceptional quality: it gains one additional tag of your choice and cannot break under normal use.

### THE MILESTONE
_Progress is progress, whatever the context._ You complete one milestone of your grand project. Work out with the GM what was proven, sourced or built, and gain an immediate, concrete mechanical benefit related to the project's nature — a working prototype element, a tested technique, a processed material. Each subsequent major advance you take may represent a further milestone if you choose.
- MINOR: WIDER APPLICATION. The milestone's benefit applies in one additional context beyond its obvious use. The prosthetic technique repairs rigging; the pump design drains a wound. The overlap was always there once you saw it.
- MINOR: THE PROTOTYPE. Build a functional prototype from the milestone. It works at reduced capacity — but it works now, in the field, before the project is done. The GM sets its profile.
- MINOR: COLLABORATOR. One character who has worked alongside you gains a benefit from the milestone as well — less refined than yours, but functional. The knowledge has transferred.

### NOT TODAY
_You are not performing a miracle. You are refusing the outcome, and you have the competence to make the refusal stick._ Once per arc, save a ship or a person that should be lost: a vessel taking its killing blow stays functional for one more scene; a patient who should be dead is not. The bill for the materials, the labour and the nerve arrives later, and you pay it without comment.
- MINOR: THE IMPOSSIBLE REPAIR. Once per session, clear all Hull stress from your vessel. You were working on it the whole time; you simply didn't mention it.
- MINOR: THE IMPOSSIBLE SURGERY. Once per session, spend a scene operating: remove all Blood stress from one character and clear one of their Blood fallouts, regardless of severity. They are functional when it ends. Comfortable was never the service offered.
- MINOR: RESURRECTION. Restore a destroyed ship, or a character who has suffered Critical Blood fallout but not yet been written out of the story, to full function. This takes an arc of work, significant materials and problems worth playing through. What the patient remembers is between them and the GM.

## ZENITH ABILITIES

### THE PROJECT COMPLETE
_It works. Exactly as you designed it._ The grand project is finished: the hand that answers thought, the hull that will not drown, the heart that beats without flesh. Work with the GM to determine its full expression and what it does to the world — because things that work this well change the Shackles, and they attract everything that profits from change. Your character's story ends alongside the project's beginning: retire as the one who built the impossible thing, or be swallowed by the consequences of its existence. Either way, the Shackles now contains something it cannot un-know, and your name is on it.

### THE SHIP ETERNAL
_To activate this ability, give yourself to the work completely._ One vessel — yours, or one you choose to save — becomes unsinkable, forever. You go into her the way everything you have goes into the work: your knowledge, your attention, and finally yourself. No body is recovered; there was no death anyone witnessed; there is simply a ship on which the pumps start themselves, fires gutter out before they catch, and sprung planks close overnight like wounds. She cannot be lost for the remainder of the campaign. Her crews swear the tools in the carpenter's walk are always warm, and that something below decks hums when the work is done right.

### EVERY PATIENT LIVES
_To activate this ability, work yourself to death._ Once, when the dying outnumber the living — a battle's aftermath, a plague port, a fleet broken on a reef — you refuse all of it. You work through the night, the next day, the next night, performing surgery and salvage beyond what hands should manage, and at the end of it every patient brought to you survives: all nearby allies and every named character present recover all Blood stress and all Blood fallout. You do not. The Shackles' surgeons still argue about that night, and teach it. Every crew in these waters knows the toast: the sawbones pays the bill.

## Design Notes (not player-facing)

**The contrast class.** Every other Version 2 class borrows power from something — a pact, a god, a tradition, the island, the dead, the powder, the web. The Sawbone's power has no opinion: it is competence, applied. Mechanically this shows in three deliberate choices. (1) **No Tide protection** — the materialist has no defence against what cannot be diagnosed; the Errant shares this, but the Errant's gap is cultural while the Sawbone's is epistemological. (2) **Forbidden: Hunt and Vanish** — the only class in the game that cannot access Hunt. Everyone else can kill coldly; the Sawbone fixes, openly, and is never the one hiding. (3) No spell-suites: the majors are all craft.

**Sources & rebuild rationale:** The cores are Pass 1's own — THE SAME SAW and THE GRAND PROJECT were the most original design in the ideation document and survive trimmed rather than replaced. The engine is the one major Heart mechanic still unclaimed: CANNIBALISED PARTS is the Vermissian Plate's consume-a-resource menu, refitted (Technology/Occult→Port/Colonial; the menu maps one-to-one, with the jury-rig option standing in for inflict-on-delve). TWICE OVER echoes Galvanic Crucible's second-use-at-a-cost. EVERY PATIENT LIVES is the medical Sunder the Veil; THE SHIP ETERNAL is Dimensional Bastion with a hull for a landmark.

**Aggressive cuts from Pass 1:** *Field Surgeon, Steady Hands* merged (they were the same minor twice). *Wrong Tools* cut as a minor — improvised tooling is what CANNIBALISED PARTS and ENGINEERING MIND are for. *Mechanical Prosthetic* folded into ENGINEERING MIND's base text. *Efficient Violence* cut entirely (Hunt is now forbidden — the anatomical-killer angle undercuts the contrast thesis). *Navigator's Backup, The Good Ship, The Ship Knows You, The Project Bleeds Through* cut or folded (Project Bleeds Through survives as THE MILESTONE's Prototype sub). *Shipwright Supreme* folded into ENGINEERING MIND and THE LIVING SHIP; *The Memory of the Sea* zenith replaced — it was an ongoing per-session power, not a retirement trigger.

**Checklist compliance:**
- Core combo Tend+Deepwater — unique (pair-unique vs the Corsair's Brave+Deepwater; the only Tend-core class). Cores: identity (Same Saw) + scaling engine (Grand Project, upgraded by THE MILESTONE).
- Minors: 3 generic + 10 named (4 skill+text: Brave, Plunder, Endure, Reckon; 4 domain+text: Wilderness, Port, Shallows, Colonial; 2 bare +2). Named skills incl. core: 5. Named domains incl. core: 5.
- Protection matrix: **Reputation 2 (The Indispensable), Gold 2 (Tools of the Trade), no Tide** — excluded from WORKED HANDS and granted nowhere.
- Majors: 5 — engine/support (Cannibalised Parts), area/ship (Living Ship), builder (Engineering Mind, the weapon-grant slot by way of building them), core upgrade (Milestone), support (Not Today). No spatial major — the Sawbone travels nowhere strangely, which is the point.
- Zeniths: 3 — triumphant completion, dissolution into the work (Bastion analog), sacrificial gift (Sunder analog).

**Open questions:**
1. THE IMPOSSIBLE SURGERY (full clear + any fallout, once per session) is the strongest sub-minor in Version 2; it's gated behind a major and a scene-long operation, but if it trivialises Blood as a track, restrict to Minor fallout or once per arc.
2. THE GRAND PROJECT needs a paragraph in the GM guidance: milestones should be priced like major story beats, or the zenith arrives too early.
3. Hull stress mechanics (Living Ship, Cannibalised Parts, equipment Tend Hull D6) assume the ship-stress system carries forward from Pass 1 — same dependency flagged on the Sin Bosun.
