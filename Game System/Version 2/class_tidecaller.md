# Tidecaller

_The Eye of Abendego did not form by accident. Something lives at the heart of that immortal storm — ancient, patient, and interested in very specific things. You found it, or it found you. The terms were unclear. They remain unclear. What is clear is what you got: the wild magic of the ocean and the bargained, familiar-haunted power of the witch tradition, braided into one trade — and what it is costing you, a little at a time. Your blood runs colder than it used to. Salt crusts where you sleep. There are mornings when your reflection takes a moment too long to do what you did. Crews fear you and captains court you, because a witch aboard is luck and weather and hexes turned aside, and everyone has heard what happens to ships that put one over the side. You are treated as something between an officer and an omen. They are right to do so. You are not entirely sure, anymore, which side of the bargain you are standing on — whether you are a person who commands the sea's power, or a place where the sea is slowly coming ashore._

## Core Traits
- SKILL: Command
- DOMAIN: Arcane
- RESOURCE: A jar of storm-water from the Eye that never stops spinning D6 Arcane
- EQUIPMENT: Pick one:
    - Coral-hilted ritual knife Brave D6, Bloodbound
    - Salvaged carbine, shot etched with wave-sigils Hunt D6, Ranged, Reload
    - Sea-chest of brines, weeds and leeches Tend Blood D6

## CORE ABILITIES

### THE PACT
_It is real, it has wants, and it speaks in signs rather than words._ At character creation, name your pact entity — something drawn from the Eye, the deeps, or the storm. Once per session, ask it one direct question; the GM answers honestly, in the language of omens. When you act in accordance with what it wants, remove D3 Tide stress. When you act against it, mark D4 Tide stress.

### SEA-CHANGE
_Your body is half the sea's already._ Whenever you want to, or when you suffer Major fallout, you enter your true form — scaled, kelp-wreathed, eyes like drowned moons; describe it. In your true form, you roll with mastery on Brave and Hunt checks, but all other checks become Risky. At the end of the current situation, you revert to your mortal shape.

## MINOR ABILITIES

### BLOOD-QUIET
_The deep water beats black within your veins._ Gain the Vanish skill. When you are in your SEA-CHANGE form, Vanish rolls are no longer Risky.

### DEEPSPEAK
_Not language — intent and image._ Gain the Faith domain. Sharks, dolphins and stranger things will answer basic questions about what they have seen or sensed nearby, and Besmara's sacred animals treat you as kin.

### THE DROWNED PLACES
_The sea showed you where it keeps what it takes._ Gain the Ruins domain. You always know when a wreck, drowned ruin or sunken thing lies beneath your keel, and once per session the pact lets you breathe water for a scene to walk its halls.

### FAMILIAR
_A gull, a crab, a serpentine thing that shouldn't exist._ Gain the Hunt skill. You have a bound creature that serves as your eyes and messenger; it can travel up to a day's sail from you and return, and you can mark D3 Tide stress to see through its senses.

### HEX SIGHT
_You can smell a working the way sailors smell weather._ Gain the Reckon skill. You always know when you are in the presence of active magic, curses or supernatural attention, and can identify its approximate nature without a roll.

### OLD WATERS
Gain access to one of the following domains: Deepwater, Shallows, Outlaw, Arcane, Faith, Ruins, Wilderness. You can take this advance more than once.

### THE PRIOR CLAIM
_Whatever tries to curse you must first negotiate with the thing that holds the lien._ Gain +2 Tide protection.

### SALT-TAUGHT
Gain access to one of the following skills: Brave, Command, Deceive, Endure, Hunt, Navigate, Reckon, Tend, Vanish. You can take this advance more than once.

### STORM-DEBT
_The weather owes you, and you collect in instalments._ Gain the Deepwater domain. Once per session, while at sea, call a weather working — a squall, a fog bank, a sudden calm. It costs nothing and lasts until the end of the scene.

### TIDEBLOOD
_What would fester on land closes clean in open water._ Gain the Endure skill. When you are immersed in seawater after taking Blood stress, remove D4 Blood stress, and you can survive underwater twice as long as a normal person.

### WARDED AND WATCHED
Gain +1 Protection in the Blood, Tide, Reputation or Luck resistance. You can take this advance more than once.

### THE WITCH'S DUE
_No one laughs twice._ Gain +2 Reputation protection.

### WITCH'S HANDS
_The sea is a pharmacopoeia for those it has claimed._ Gain the Tend skill. When you have time to prepare, you can turn a resource with the Deepwater or Wilderness domain into a draught; when drunk, it removes Blood or Tide stress equal to its dice size minus one step — choose which when you brew it.

## MAJOR ABILITIES

### BRINE CALLS TO BRINE
_You call to the salt in their blood, and it answers by leaving._ This spell functions as a weapon with the following profile: (Hunt D6, Ranged). You can mark stress accrued as a result of using this spell to Tide.
- MINOR: RETCH. The stress dice increases to D8.
- MINOR: MAESTRO. The weapon gains the Piercing tag.
- MINOR: AS ABOVE, SO BELOW. You do not need line of sight as long as you have a sympathetic token of your target — their hair, a carving in their shape, a favoured possession — but you are still limited to the usual distance of the Ranged tag.

### THE SCRYING BOWL
_You bleed into a bowl of seawater, and omens swim in the clouding._ Roll Reckon+Arcane to cast this spell before you embark on a voyage. On a success, you see three omens; describe them — the vaguer, the better. They are fated to come up on the voyage, and the first time you interact with whatever each omen concerns, you roll with mastery.
- MINOR: SHARED VISIONS. Your allies also roll with mastery when they interact with the portentous things.
- MINOR: SCARLET INSIGHT. Once per session, when you cast this spell, clear D8 stress from Tide or Luck.
- MINOR: DIRE PORTENTS. Once per session, re-roll any dice that you or anyone else rolled — the original roll was a vision you received, and you shout the warning in time.

### THE PACT-BEAST
_Your familiar has been elevated by the pact, and it accepts what the magic does to flesh so you don't have to._ You are accompanied by a small creature — cowardly, strange to look at, indisputably yours. When you take stress from magical sources (including your own spells), you may assign that stress to your familiar instead. When 4 total stress is marked to it, it is unavailable until the start of the next session, when it returns changed — describe what the magic has transformed. At the start of each session, remove all stress marked against it.
- MINOR: RESILIENT. Your familiar can hold 6 total stress before it is lost for the session.
- MINOR: HUNGRY. Your familiar functions as a (Hunt D8, Ranged, Unreliable) item.
- MINOR: CURIOUS. Your familiar functions as a (Navigate D8, Unreliable) item.

### GREAT AND TERRIBLE
_The sea stops borrowing your shape and starts wearing it proudly._ You are no longer forced into your SEA-CHANGE form when you take Major fallout. When you choose to enter your true form, all adversaries who can see you mark D4 stress.
- MINOR: ENTHRALL. When you enter your true form, you may mark D4 Tide stress; all who see you transform are stunned into inaction, and you have time to make a single action entirely unopposed.
- MINOR: SLOUGH. Once per session, when you assume your true form, downgrade one Blood fallout you are suffering by one step — the wound stays behind on the shed skin.
- MINOR: UNION. Once per situation, when you are in your true form and an action would be Risky, treat it as Standard instead.

### THE TIDE COMES IN
_You sing the sea into the space around you._ Roll Command+Arcane to cast this spell and draw the ocean's attention into the area: tide-pools where there was dry deck, fog that moves against the wind, barnacles blooming on stone. The area gains the Arcane domain and reacts appropriately until the end of the current situation.
- MINOR: BYPASS. The first time you cast this spell on a voyage, it functions as a D6 boon.
- MINOR: BLOOD CALLS FOR BLOOD. All weapons used in the area increase their stress dice by 1 step — for adversaries and player characters alike.
- MINOR: REFUGE. Clear D6 Luck stress from yourself or an ally each time you cast this spell.

### THE SEA'S MANY BODIES
_The pact does not much care which body you use._ Roll Command+Arcane to send your spirit into a sea creature you can see — a shark, an octopus, a gull riding the same wind. On a success, you direct it to perform basic tasks, though the control is imperfect. Your body slumps where you left it, and you come to when your spirit returns. At the end of the situation, the creature dies; the sea collects its fee.
- MINOR: THE BORROWED MOUTH. The creature can speak with your voice — negotiate, threaten, deliver a message from somewhere no one expected.
- MINOR: THE SHOAL. You can ride a shoal, swarm or flock as a single body, moving as a cloud of eyes and teeth.
- MINOR: LEVIATHAN VESSEL. Once per arc, the vessel can be something truly vast. The GM determines what the deep sends when you ask.

## ZENITH ABILITIES

### THE PACT FULFILLED
_The entity calls in everything, and at last you understand the terms._ You may accept or refuse. If you accept: the working is titanic — a storm that swallows a fleet, a sea-change that redraws a coastline, a hex that lays a city low — and you cease to be entirely human; how much of you remains is a question the table answers together. If you refuse: the pact shatters, you lose every ability of this class permanently, and you are free — truly, completely free — of every supernatural claim on you. Both answers end your arc. There was never a third.

### SOVEREIGN OF THE DROWNED
_The Eye itself acknowledges you._ Once, you may speak a permanent working into the world — a change to the Shackles that outlasts you: a new current, a dead reef come alive, an island that appears only for those who need it, a named ship that sails forever without a crew. The GM incorporates it as a permanent truth of the setting. You retire into legend — the witch the working is named for, spoken of in the same breath as the weather.

### WHAT THE EYE KEEPS
_You sail into the Eye of Abendego and do not come out. Something else happens instead._ Choose a stretch of sea — a strait, a reef-maze, the waters around one island. You are omnipresent within it: while the situation lasts, you choose who lives and who drowns inside its borders. When the situation ends, the water is stained with you forever: it counts as one tier stranger than it was, its weather answers to no chart, and sailors learn to speak kindly when crossing it. You live on as the thing the locals leave offerings for. In a generation, someone desperate will stand at its edge and ask for terms. You will be interested in very specific things.

## Design Notes (not player-facing)

**Sources & rebuild rationale:** The Witch's chassis, almost whole, with the Pass 1 warlock framing as the entry point and the Deep Apiarist's not-human-anymore physicality in the margins. SEA-CHANGE is TRUE FORM verbatim; GREAT AND TERRIBLE keeps its name and all three subs (Sacred Object→Slough); THE PACT-BEAST is the Witch's FAMILIAR major with its stress-sink mechanic intact (Delve→Navigate on the Curious sub); BRINE CALLS TO BRINE is EXSANGUINATE; THE SCRYING BOWL is CRIMSON MIRROR (Mind/Fortune→Tide/Luck); THE TIDE COMES IN is ASCENDANCY; BLOOD-QUIET, WITCH'S HANDS (Wild-Witch) and the protection pair (Red Dominion/Mind of Many Doorways → matrix-correct Tide/Rep) complete the set. THE SEA'S MANY BODIES is the Apiarist's INTRUSION, fee included. Core skill moved Brave→Command to mirror the Witch's Compel and leave Brave/Arcane breathing room.

**Aggressive cuts from Pass 1:** *Salt and Hex* (freeform casting core) deleted — same reasoning as the Chronicler's Historical Magic; spells live in majors. *Storm Calling/Storm Made Flesh* ceded to the Sin Bosun (their weather is petition; the Tidecaller's STORM-DEBT minor keeps a personal-scale version). *Witch Queen of the Tide, The Entity Steps Closer, The Tidebreaker, Familiar Ascended* — folded into the Witch-chassis majors or cut; *Hex-Mark, Curse of the Turning Tide, Ward Against Salt, Witch's Bargain, Curse Eater, Stormwrack Form, The Naming* — cut (most are knack- or sub-minor-grade; The Naming's "force the entity to manifest" is good campaign material for THE PACT itself).

**Checklist compliance:**
- Core combo Command+Arcane — unique. Cores: engine/oracle (The Pact) + identity/transformation (Sea-Change).
- Minors: 3 generic + 10 named (5 skill+text: Vanish, Hunt, Reckon, Endure, Tend; 3 domain+text: Faith, Ruins, Deepwater; 2 bare +2). Named skills incl. core: 6. Named domains incl. core: 4.
- Protection matrix: **Tide 2 (The Prior Claim), Reputation 2 (The Witch's Due), no Gold** — excluded from WARDED AND WATCHED and granted nowhere. Thematically: the pact is paid in other currency; witches do not hold wealth, and the sea takes its tithe of whatever they try to keep.
- **Forbidden skills: Plunder, Carouse** — the sea provides and the crew keeps its distance. The uncanny one drinks alone, and taking for greed offends the pact.
- Majors: 6 (spell-heavy, matching the Witch) — weapon (Brine), info (Scrying Bowl), companion (Pact-Beast), core upgrade (Great and Terrible extends Sea-Change), area (Tide Comes In), spatial/social (Sea's Many Bodies). No category exceeds two.
- Zeniths: 3 — the bargain concluded both ways, the permanent working, FINAL FORM as haunted water.

**Open question:** THE PACT's remove-D3/mark-D4 loop plus +2 Tide protection makes Tide very manageable for this class; that's intentional (the Witch is the class that *handles* Echo), but if the Tidecaller never feels haunted, drop The Prior Claim to a different resistance and let the matrix flex.
