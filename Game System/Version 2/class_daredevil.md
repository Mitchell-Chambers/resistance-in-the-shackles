# Daredevil

_You learned what you know in places that don't appear on any map the respectable world keeps: the travelling circus that wintered in Bloodcove, the knife-throwing act that went wrong in ways you don't discuss, the year with the escapologists and fire-eaters who work the dockside crowds of Port Peril. You are not a planner. You are a mover. Your survival strategy has always been the same: be faster than what's coming, be gone before anyone works out what happened, and if you absolutely must fight, make it spectacular enough that the crowd forgets to stop you leaving. The Shackles is full of people who are very good at standing still and waiting — braced behind gunwales, dug in behind harbour walls, sitting on their reputations like dragons on gold. You have never understood why anyone would do that. Everything you own fits in your pockets, everything you owe is outrun, and every room you have ever entered had at least three ways out. You checked. You always check._

## Core Traits
- SKILL: Vanish
- DOMAIN: Outlaw
- RESOURCE: A wanted poster bearing a bad likeness of you D6 Outlaw
- EQUIPMENT: A knife in your boot Brave D6
    - Pick one:
        - Matched throwing knives Hunt D6, Ranged, Brutal
        - Collapsible grapnel and fifty feet of silk line Navigate D6
        - Pouch of smoke vials Vanish D6

## CORE ABILITIES

### ALWAYS A WAY OUT
_There are three exits from this room. You checked._ At the start of each situation, declare one exit — a route, a gap, a prepared escape — that exists regardless of circumstances. Once per situation, you may use it to remove yourself (and one ally who stays close) from immediate danger without a roll. This cannot be used if you are bound, submerged or sealed inside something. It can be used if you are outnumbered, surrounded, on fire, or in a conversation that has gone badly.

### MOMENTUM
_As long as you keep moving, the world can't get a grip on you._ When you succeed on any action that involves moving — running, climbing, swinging, diving, vaulting — you may immediately move again as part of the same action without a second roll. On a failure, you still move, but something goes wrong along the way. You cannot benefit from this ability while standing still.

## MINOR ABILITIES

### ACROBAT
_Your body does things most bodies do not._ Gain the Brave skill. Actions involving movement through difficult terrain — rigging, rooftops, crowded docks, collapsing structures — never increase in difficulty because of the physical challenge.

### COASTWISE
_A cove not on any chart; a reef passage that looks like a dead end from the surface._ Gain the Shallows domain. You can pass through a coastline that is actively searching for you without a roll, unless you do something memorable.

### KNIFE ACT
_You were paid to do this without hitting the person standing next to the target._ Gain the Hunt skill. When you throw a blade at a specific, named point — a held weapon, a rope, a lock, a candle — you hit it. Your thrown blades inflict D8 stress and you carry enough that running out is never the first problem.

### MISDIRECTION
_People look where you point._ Gain the Deceive skill. Once per situation, direct everyone present to look at something other than what you are actually doing — a shout, a thrown object, a staged stumble. Your next action, taken immediately and quietly, requires no roll, and no one present remembers you were the one who did it.

### NINE LIVES
_You do not go down easily, and when you do, it is always somewhere inconvenient for whoever put you there._ Gain +2 Blood protection.

### THE PRESTIGE
Gain access to one of the following skills: Brave, Carouse, Command, Deceive, Hunt, Navigate, Plunder, Reckon, Vanish. You can take this advance more than once.

### PICKPOCKET
_A moment of proximity and a moment of distraction._ Gain the Reckon skill. You can lift anything a person is carrying without them noticing; the GM will tell you what they're carrying before you reach for it. If a target is actively watching for theft, roll Vanish — on a partial success you take it, but they notice something was wrong.

### RIGGING RAT
_The tops are yours._ Gain the Deepwater domain. You move through a ship's rigging at full speed in any conditions without a roll, you always have the high ground aboard a vessel if you want it, and your first attack after dropping from height gains the Brutal tag.

### THE ROVING LIFE
Gain access to one of the following domains: Deepwater, Shallows, Outlaw, Port, Colonial, Ruins. You can take this advance more than once.

### SMOKE AND SHADOW
_Drinking, laughing, belonging._ Gain the Carouse skill. You can disappear into any crowd by becoming the most natural person in it; you cannot be pursued out of a social scene through a crowd unless the GM can explain how pursuit is possible.

### STREET-LEARNED
_You watched the colonial system from underneath and learned to abuse it from inside._ Gain the Colonial domain. You know which paperwork holds up, which officials are worth bribing and which laws have convenient loopholes; you can move through colonial-held territory without looking like what you are.

### TIGHTROPE LUCK
Gain +1 Protection in the Blood, Tide, Luck or Gold resistance. You can take this advance more than once.

### THE UNIVERSE KEEPS MISSING
_Bad luck has to catch you first._ Gain +2 Luck protection.

## MAJOR ABILITIES

### ALL OR NOTHING
_You forsake the safe footing for a single, decisive blow._ When you attack an adversary, you can choose to lose your Blood protection for the attack before you roll. If you do so and succeed or partially succeed, add your Blood protection value to the stress inflicted.
- MINOR: INEXORABLE. If the first dice you roll to determine the stress inflicted shows 1 or 2, roll an additional dice of the same size and add the result to the stress inflicted.
- MINOR: BLOODIED BUT UNBROKEN. When you have 4 or more stress marked to Blood or are suffering from ongoing Blood fallout, gain +2 Blood protection.
- MINOR: SCYTHING BLOW. Once per situation, treat your weapon as though it has the Spread tag when you inflict stress on an adversary.

### KNIFE STORM
_More blades than you should reasonably be carrying._ Once per situation, every opponent in a zone you can see must roll to avoid being hit; each one that fails marks D6 stress. The Spread tag applies. Where the knives came from is a question no one gets a satisfying answer to.
- MINOR: SPECIALTY LOAD. Some of the knives are alchemical. Choose one effect when you use KNIFE STORM: affected targets catch fire (D4 stress per round until extinguished), are drowsy (their next action is Risky), or are poisoned (the knives gain the Degenerating tag).
- MINOR: PINNED. Targets who fail to avoid KNIFE STORM are pinned to whatever surface is behind them, restrained until they work free — which takes a full action.
- MINOR: ENCORE. KNIFE STORM can be used twice per situation. The crowd demands it.

### THE IMPOSSIBLE ROUTE
_Across the burning deck, up the outside of the moving ship, through the crowd of armed opponents._ Once per situation, declare a path through an environment that should not exist. You take it. It works. The GM describes what it looks like from the outside. Anyone attempting to follow must roll at one difficulty step higher than normal.
- MINOR: TAKE SOMEONE WITH YOU. One ally can follow THE IMPOSSIBLE ROUTE without rolling. They must stay right behind you and do exactly what you do.
- MINOR: CANNONBALL. Once per session, a ship's cannon, catapult or comparable device counts as the start of an Impossible Route. You survive the launch. You land where you aimed, roughly. The enemy never expects this.
- MINOR: THE IMPOSSIBLE RETURN. Once per session, THE IMPOSSIBLE ROUTE works in reverse: re-enter a location you escaped by the same path, arriving exactly where you want to be.

### THE READ
_This is not magic. It is the pattern recognition of someone who has survived a great many rooms._ Once per situation, after observing an environment or an ongoing fight for one exchange, narrate how the next minute unfolds — who moves where, what breaks, which route opens, which opponent overcommits. It happens as described, unless an opponent of exceptional tactical ability is present and the GM rules they can counter it.
- MINOR: THE OPENING. THE READ always identifies one moment where your next attack would inflict maximum stress automatically, if you choose to take it.
- MINOR: THE COUNTER. When an enemy attempts a prepared ambush or tactical surprise against you, invoke THE READ to reverse it: you anticipated this, and they are the ones who walked into something.
- MINOR: THE AUDIENCE. Describe THE READ aloud to your allies: they all roll with mastery on the first exchange. They saw what you saw.

### THE VANISHING ACT
_You simply are not there anymore._ Once per session, disappear completely from a scene — mid-conversation, mid-combat, mid-arrest. No roll, no explanation. You reappear at the start of the next scene somewhere that makes sense, carrying everything you had.
- MINOR: TAKE THE EVIDENCE. When you use THE VANISHING ACT, take one object from the scene with you. It vanishes when you do.
- MINOR: LEAVE A DOUBLE. Something remains in your place briefly — a coat, a hat, a silhouette that is not quite right. It buys D3 rounds before anyone realises you are gone.
- MINOR: CROWD WORK. THE VANISHING ACT can remove one ally from the scene alongside you. They must be touching you when you go.

## ZENITH ABILITIES

### THE GRAND EXIT
_Not run. Leave._ Once, at a moment of your choosing, you make the most spectacular, inexplicable, impossible departure the Shackles has ever witnessed — the burning ship walked off, the blockade sailed through laughing, the prison no one saw you leave. You go where you choose, intact and free of every consequence that was chasing you, and the story is told in ports for years. Your character leaves the game the way they always intended to leave everything: perfectly, and on their own terms.

### UNKILLABLE
_Cheating death has become a professional credential._ Ordinary violence, misfortune and circumstance can no longer end you — a rope frays, a pistol misfires, a jailer falls asleep, every single time. Play out the current situation untouchable, and then the character passes out of play and into legend: sighted everywhere, confirmed nowhere, reported dead at least annually and never accurately. The Shackles stops asking whether the stories are true, because the answer has stopped mattering. That might be what you were performing for all along. It might not be the same thing as freedom.

### THE LAST TRICK
_To activate this ability, die — saving someone, mid-stunt, in front of witnesses._ The death is so audacious, so impossible, so perfectly timed that everyone who sees it is changed by it. All nearby allies remove all ongoing fallout and all stress marked to all resistances, and roll with mastery for the remainder of the situation: the crowd you played to your whole life finally moves the way you always moved. No body is ever recovered. No one who saw it can quite believe you're gone. Perhaps you aren't.

## Design Notes (not player-facing)

**Sources & rebuild rationale:** Pass 1 cores survive trimmed (they were already Heart-shaped). The Deadwalker is the structural donor: ALL OR NOTHING is Reaper's Strike with all three sub-minors verbatim — recklessness-for-damage is a perfect daredevil engine without inventing new math — and THE LAST TRICK is Sunder the Veil with a circus death instead of a torn heaven. The remaining majors are Pass 1's best four, with CANNONBALL demoted from minor to Impossible Route sub-minor where it belongs.

**Aggressive cuts from Pass 1:** *Daredevil Reputation* (major) deleted — a no-Reputation-protection class shouldn't anchor a major on managing its legend; the Daredevil's legend is wild and unmanaged by design. Cut minors: Escapologist (this is what Vanish rolls and ALWAYS A WAY OUT are for), Quick Draw (given to the Powder Mage), Face in the Crowd (merged into COASTWISE/SMOKE AND SHADOW), Backstabber's Footwork, Knife Work, Always Running Hot/Contingency Luck/The Name No One Knows (replaced by the matrix-correct protection pair).

**Checklist compliance:**
- Core combo Vanish+Outlaw — unique. Cores: engine (Momentum) + escape valve (Always a Way Out).
- Minors: 3 generic + 10 named (5 skill+text: Brave, Hunt, Deceive, Reckon, Carouse; 3 domain+text: Shallows, Deepwater, Colonial; 2 bare +2). Named skills incl. core: 6. Named domains incl. core: 4.
- Protection matrix: **Blood 2 (Nine Lives), Luck 2 (The Universe Keeps Missing), no Reputation** — excluded from TIGHTROPE LUCK and granted nowhere.
- **Forbidden skills: Endure, Tend** — the Daredevil never holds, never heals, never sits still. The body is spent, not maintained.
- Majors: 5 — technique (All or Nothing), weapon (Knife Storm), spatial ×2 (Impossible Route, Vanishing Act — the class's whole thesis), info (The Read).
- Zeniths: 3 — triumphant departure, passage into legend, sacrificial gift (Sunder the Veil analog).

**Open question:** MISDIRECTION's no-roll action is the strongest minor here; if it dominates, constrain to non-attack actions.
