# Sharper

_Every Free Captain thinks they understand the game — raids, plunder, loyalty, fear. You understand the game that happens underneath that game. Who is sleeping with whom. Which Pirate Council member owes a debt to which merchant house. Which harbourmaster is on three separate payrolls, and which informant changed sides on Thursday. You have built an empire of knowledge that nobody can see, and you protect it the way a spider protects a web: by being invisible, and by ensuring that anything that touches it gets stuck. The cards, the dice, the cons — those are the visible trade, and you are very good at them, but they were only ever apprenticeship. The real game taught you its one true rule early: everything in the Shackles is a wager, every person is a hand to be read, and the house — given enough patience, enough ledgers, enough friends in enough back rooms — the house is you. You only bet on certainties. The trick is manufacturing them._

## Core Traits
- SKILL: Deceive
- DOMAIN: Port
- RESOURCE: A marked deck of cards D6 Port
- EQUIPMENT: Pick one:
    - Sleeve pistol Hunt D6, Ranged, One-Shot
    - Sword-cane Hunt D6, Brutal
    - Forger's kit of inks, seals and papers Deceive D6; sleeve-knife Hunt D6

## CORE ABILITIES

### THE WEB
_You have a network, and the network has you._ At character creation, establish three contacts, each in a different port, faction or position, and each with one type of information they can access — maritime traffic, colonial politics, criminal networks, arcane rumour. Once per session per contact, draw on their information without a roll. They may ask for something in return. You track these debts carefully. So do they.

### DOUBLE OR NOTHING
_Fate plays cards like everyone else, and like everyone else, it can be hustled._ At any time, roll a D6. If the result is equal to or under your current Luck stress, clear that much stress from Luck and roll with mastery on your next action. If it's over, add that much stress to Luck.

## MINOR ABILITIES

### THE BRIBED HARBOUR
_Officials are just locks with salaries._ Gain the Colonial domain. In any port you have operated in for at least a session, you have an official in your pocket: they can suppress your arrival records, misdirect pursuit, or warn you when authority turns your way. They are real, have their own interests, and can be endangered if pushed.

### CARDSHARP'S FINGERS
_A moment of proximity, a moment of distraction._ Gain the Plunder skill. You can lift anything a person carries — keys, letters, the ring off their finger — given a moment of distraction, and you can rig any game of chance unless someone is actively watching for it.

### THE COLD READ
_New people are not new situations._ Gain the Reckon skill. When you meet someone for the first time, the GM tells you their approximate standing in the local hierarchy, their primary concern right now, and the best approach for getting them to cooperate.

### CONTROLLED PROFILE
_The face people know is a construction; damaging it costs them effort and buys you time._ Gain +2 Reputation protection.

### DEAD DROP NETWORK
_Fence shops, gambling dens, the back rooms of black-market surgeons._ Gain the Outlaw domain. Intelligence reaches you within a session of being generated, regardless of where in the Shackles it originates, and you can send word back just as fast. The GM tells you when something relevant has been left for you.

### EVERY ROOM'S NATIVE
_Tavern, council chamber, ship's mess or merchant ball._ Gain the Carouse skill. You never take stress from navigating social environments above or below your apparent station, because your station is whatever you need it to be right now.

### FALSE FACE
_Your real name is a question the GM answers only if you choose to._ Gain the Vanish skill. You maintain two working identities; switching between them takes only a moment — a different coat, a different posture, a different name. People who know one cannot connect it to the other without dedicated investigation.

### LEVERAGE
_You have been building the next file already._ Gain the Command skill. Once per arc, produce the file you keep on someone powerful and use it: they do what you want, completely and without resistance, once. Afterwards, they know it was you.

### LOADED DICE
_You only bet on games you have already fixed._ Gain +2 Luck protection.

### THE LONG GAME
Gain access to one of the following skills: Carouse, Command, Deceive, Hunt, Navigate, Plunder, Reckon, Tend, Vanish. You can take this advance more than once.

### OLD HAUNTS
Gain access to one of the following domains: Deepwater, Shallows, Outlaw, Port, Colonial, Arcane. You can take this advance more than once.

### TABLE STAKES
Gain +1 Protection in the Tide, Reputation, Luck or Gold resistance. You can take this advance more than once.

### THE UNCANNY TRADE
_Sensible fences won't touch it. You are not sensible; you are thorough._ Gain the Arcane domain. You can find a buyer or seller for anything supernatural — cursed relics, true names, favours owed by things that are not people — and the entities involved consider you a fair dealer, which is its own kind of protection.

## MAJOR ABILITIES

### THE HOOK
_Every mark already wants something. You just introduce them to it._ Roll Deceive+Port to work someone in conversation. On a success, you instil in them a fierce desire for something you have dangled — until the end of the current situation, they pursue it. If the desire is unusual for them, working the hook is Risky.
- MINOR: THE DEEP HOOK. The desire lasts until the end of the session. If they don't get what they want by then, they descend into violence to acquire it or fall apart in despair — GM's choice.
- MINOR: THE WHISPER CAMPAIGN. The desire spreads: anyone the mark talks to for more than a minute has a 1-in-6 chance of catching it. You are immune. Your allies are not.
- MINOR: THE STANDING ARRANGEMENT. Mark D6 Luck stress to set the hook permanently: the first time the mark sees you each session, the desire rekindles, same as the day you planted it.

### EVERYBODY OWES
_Once you know what keeps someone awake at night, the rest is arithmetic._ Roll Reckon+Port on a target you can see and hear. On a success, you learn their deepest debt: the greatest thing they have ever taken from someone else — money, freedom, a name, a life. When you act on this information, roll with mastery.
- MINOR: THE CREDITOR. You also learn who the debt is owed to, and where to find them. The thread is easy enough to follow.
- MINOR: AN EVENING'S WORK. Once per session, a night spent in the right rooms reveals someone nearby with an outstanding debt worth knowing. The GM tells you who.
- MINOR: THE BROKERED DEBT. Once per arc, sell what you know: introduce creditor to debtor on your terms, and clear D6 stress from Gold or Reputation as the grateful parties settle up around you.

### THE PERFECT LIE
_A false history, a fabricated organisation, a created person — load-bearing, fully documented, and believed by people who should know better._ You can construct and maintain one lie of architectural complexity at a time. It survives investigation, has genuine documentation, and holds without rolls so long as you give it regular attention.
- MINOR: LIVING FICTION. Your Perfect Lie can act in the world — send letters, make purchases, employ people who believe it is real. The GM runs it as a minor faction.
- MINOR: THE FALLBACK. Your Perfect Lie includes a complete second self. If your real identity is ever burned, you step into this one seamlessly, mid-scene if necessary.
- MINOR: LAYERS. Beneath the first false explanation lies a second, more satisfying one. Investigators who break the surface find the basement and stop digging. Only exceptional effort reaches the truth.

### THE KILLING WORD
_Information, weaponised at scale._ Once per arc, release a piece of information that destroys someone's position entirely — a captain's secret handed to their crew, a councillor's corruption handed to their rivals, a merchant's debts made public. The target automatically suffers Major Reputation fallout. You need not be present. You usually aren't.
- MINOR: DOUBLE DROP. THE KILLING WORD harms two targets at once: the primary, and whoever was protecting them.
- MINOR: THE SLOW BURN. Set THE KILLING WORD on a trigger — an event, a betrayal, your own death. The GM tracks it. It always fires.
- MINOR: UNTRACEABLE. THE KILLING WORD cannot be traced to you — not by investigation, not by magic, not by torture. You were simply never there.

### THE BACK ROOM
_As far as anyone knows, it's always been here._ When you are in a port, roll Deceive+Port to establish your back room — behind a gambling den, above a chart-house, beneath a counting floor. Inside it, meetings happen on your terms: you roll with mastery on all social actions within, and you know everything that happens inside its walls.
- MINOR: THE HOUSE ALWAYS WINS. Once per session, the room's games and ledgers clear D4 Gold stress.
- MINOR: WHAT THE WALLS HEARD. Once per session, the room yields a secret a guest let slip — the GM tells you something true and useful about anyone who has been inside this session.
- MINOR: NEVER WAS. On a night's notice, the room vanishes: furniture, records, staff, all of it. Patrons misremember the address. Investigators find a storeroom that has clearly been empty for years.

## ZENITH ABILITIES

### THE WEB COMPLETES
_It doesn't need you anymore._ Your network has become a force in the Shackles: it gathers, acts and remembers with or without your involvement. You may retire into being the spider at the centre, directing rather than doing — or burn the whole thing down in the most spectacular act of self-destruction these waters have seen, calling in every debt, exposing every secret, all at once. Either way, your character's story ends here: what you built is real, and the question of what it does without you watching now belongs to the campaign.

### THE NAME BEHIND THE NAME
_Everything that appeared to be coincidence was arrangement._ You reveal — to the table and the Shackles at the same moment — that you have been the hidden hand behind this arc's events: every obstacle manufactured, every ally cultivated, every escape permitted. The revelation is total and irreversible. You pass out of play and into the setting's machinery: from now on, nothing significant happens in the Shackles that you do not know about first, and the surviving characters live in a world where one of the powers watching them used to share their table.

### THE GRIFT ETERNAL
_You con death itself._ You stage your own death so perfectly that everyone is convinced — enemies, allies, creditors, the sea, and whatever keeps the ledgers beyond it. The Sharper everyone knew is mourned, buried, and settled into legend; every debt dies with the name. You walk away as someone new entirely — new face, new port, new game — and your character retires from play. The truth of where you went exists in exactly one place: a sealed file, held by the person at the table you trusted most. Whether they ever open it is up to them.

## Design Notes (not player-facing)

**Sources & rebuild rationale:** The Witch and the Incarnadine, with every drop of magic translated into craft. DOUBLE OR NOTHING is the Witch's CRUCIBLE verbatim with Echo→Luck — the push-your-luck engine *is* the gambler fantasy, and it gives the Sharper a reason to ride high Luck stress despite the Loaded Dice protection, exactly as the Witch rides Echo. THE HOOK is the Incarnadine's CRAVE with all three subs (Addict/Viral/Conditioning), recast as pure salesmanship; EVERYBODY OWES is KARMIC LEDGER (the murder-harvest sub replaced with debt-brokering, which is more Sharper and less Incarne); THE BACK ROOM is the Witch's LAIR with the predatory building turned into a predatory establishment, NEVER WAS standing in for the lair's supernatural deniability. THE PERFECT LIE and THE KILLING WORD survive from Pass 1 — they were already the right size.

**Aggressive cuts from Pass 1:** *Ghost Economy* and *Architecture of Secrets* deleted (THE WEB core plus THE BACK ROOM cover both; Council-level contacts are campaign material, not a class feature). *Controlled Reputation* deleted (the matrix protection pair does this honestly). Cut minors: The Innocent Question, Planted Story, Insurance Policy, Who I Know, Exit Strategy, The Right Ears, The Shape of a Lie — Pass 1 had roughly twice as many once-per-session information taps as any Heart class; the survivors (COLD READ, DEAD DROP, BRIBED HARBOUR, LEVERAGE) are the four with the most distinct fictional positioning. The lie-detection from Shape of a Lie is what the Tell knack is for.

**Checklist compliance:**
- Core combo Deceive+Port — unique. Cores: identity/info (The Web) + push-your-luck engine (Double or Nothing).
- Minors: 3 generic + 10 named (5 skill+text: Plunder, Reckon, Carouse, Vanish, Command; 3 domain+text: Colonial, Outlaw, Arcane; 2 bare +2). Named skills incl. core: 6. Named domains incl. core: 4.
- Protection matrix: **Reputation 2 (Controlled Profile), Luck 2 (Loaded Dice), no Blood** — excluded from TABLE STAKES and granted nowhere. Thematically: soft hands. The Sharper survives by never being where the blow lands.
- **Forbidden skills: Brave, Endure** — never commits, never absorbs. When a plan requires courage or stamina, the plan is wrong.
- Majors: 5 — social/control (Hook), info (Everybody Owes), core upgrade (Perfect Lie extends False Face/Web), social weapon (Killing Word), area (Back Room).
- Zeniths: 3 — legacy released, apotheosis into the setting, the escape (conning death is the Witch's Perfect Resurrection played as a grift).

**Open questions:**
1. DOUBLE OR NOTHING interacts with Luck's "only clears when fallout fires" rule — this ability is deliberately one of the rare direct Luck-clears the stress doc reserves for class abilities, but confirm it doesn't undercut the track's doom-engine identity. If it does, restrict to once per scene.
2. THE KILLING WORD's automatic Major fallout against PCs (if ever turned on one) needs a ruling before it comes up.
