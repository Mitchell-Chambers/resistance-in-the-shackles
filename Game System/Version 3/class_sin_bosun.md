# Sin Bosun

_Every ship has a sin bosun, whether the muster book records one or not. The crew might call them the padre, the holy, the god-botherer, the one who talks to the sky — what they mean is: the person you find when someone is dying, and also the person you find when you are lost. In the Shackles these have always been the same job, because the sea does not distinguish between a body it is killing and a ship it is sinking. You serve your god by moving through the world they made — Besmara who owns every plank that touches her water, Gozreh who is the wind in the sail and the storm against the hull, or something older and less named that speaks in current and depth. Your theology is practical: the god's domain is the sea, and to navigate it correctly — to read its weather, to know its routes, to bring ships and souls through it alive — is an act of worship. The chart is the scripture. The stars are the congregation. The passage completed is the prayer answered. You are not a soft priest. The sea is not kind, your god is not kind, and the mercy you offer is the specific, practical mercy of someone who has studied how to get people through things. You know the route. You know the rite. You know, better than most, that the difference between them is mostly a matter of what is doing the killing._

## Core Traits
- SKILL: Navigate
- DOMAIN: Faith
- RESOURCE: A salt-stiffened prayer book interleaved with hand-drawn charts D6 Faith
- EQUIPMENT: Pick one:
    - Storm-lantern of blessed whale-oil Dark: Brave D6 / Lit: Brave D8, Obscuring, Dangerous
    - Breech-loading pistol and rite-knife Hunt D6, Ranged, Reload; Brave D6, Brutal
    - Scripture-inked bandages, blessed rum and a belaying pin Tend Blood/Tide D6; Brave D6

## CORE ABILITIES

### THE CHART AND THE PRAYER
_To you they are the same document: a chart more accurate than anything sold in the Shackles, and a record of every rite, omen and intervention you have witnessed on the water._ Once per session, when you consult it before acting, the GM gives you one true piece of information about what lies ahead — navigational, spiritual, or both. Once per journey, roll with mastery on a navigation challenge of your choosing.

### MINISTRATIONS
_You offer the practical mercy of someone who knows the words that settle debts between souls and the water._ Once per session, lead your allies in a rite — a sea burial, a blessing before battle, rum poured for the dead (how you practise your faith is up to you). All player characters who took part can remove one Minor Blood or Tide fallout, or downgrade one Major Blood or Tide fallout to Minor, at the end of the rite. If you perform this rite whilst on a journey, add +D4 to the journey's resistance.

## MINOR ABILITIES

### CONFESSOR
_People tell you things. You asked the right way, or your presence drew it out of them._ Gain the Outlaw domain. Once per session, while you are in port, an NPC confesses something real to you — a fear, a plan, a thing they have done that they cannot tell anyone else. The GM determines who and what. Your god has opinions about betrayal and about silence in roughly equal measure.

### DEAD RECKONING
_The bosun who lets the helmsman go down with fever loses the ship along with the soul._ Gain the Tend skill. You can treat exhaustion, exposure and the slow attrition of a hard passage with whatever is available at sea, and in total darkness, fog or supernatural obscuration you can hold a vessel's course accurately for D6 hours without a roll.

### DIVINE WARD
_Whatever is trying to curse you must first explain to your god why it thinks it has the right. The conversation takes long enough to matter._ Gain +2 Tide protection.

### THE GOD'S GIFTS
Gain access to one of the following skills: Brave, Carouse, Command, Endure, Hunt, Navigate, Plunder, Reckon, Tend. You can take this advance more than once.

### LAST RITES
_The god keeps what it is given properly._ Gain the Port domain. When you preside over a death — a funeral, a burial, a sailor gone over the side — the deceased's debt to the water is settled: they cannot be raised as undead without overcoming your god's claim, and crew who witness the rite each remove D4 Tide stress.

### PILGRIM ROUTES
Gain access to one of the following domains: Deepwater, Shallows, Outlaw, Faith, Arcane, Port, Ruins. You can take this advance more than once.

### READ THE OMEN
_You read the natural world the way a scholar reads text._ Gain the Reckon skill. Once per session, the GM gives you an omen: an interpretation of something observable — the behaviour of seabirds, the colour of the water, a storm forming against the wind — as divine communication. It is always true. It will be relevant before the session ends.

### SACRED TATTOO
_Every passage survived is inked into your skin at the next port, a ledger the universe can read and finds balanced._ Gain +2 Luck protection.

### SCRIPTURE OF SALT
_You have argued the god's requirements with captains, cultists and the dying._ Gain the Command skill. When you invoke what the god requires in conversation with those who share — or fear — your faith, roll with mastery.

### SERMON ON THE DECK
_Your faith is shared meals, songs off-watch, and the last hour before a hard day._ Gain the Carouse skill. Once per session, when you drink, feast or mark a moment alongside an ally in the god's name, they gain +1 Tide protection until the end of the session.

### STAR-READER
_You have stood long enough at the rail to learn the difference between what a star means and what the god is saying through it._ Gain the Arcane domain. Once per session at night, read an omen from the sky about a specific person, ship or course of action. The GM answers honestly, but in astronomical metaphor.

### UNWAVERING FAITH
Gain +1 Protection in the Tide, Reputation, Luck or Gold resistance. You can take this advance more than once.

### WEATHERWISE
_The best weather intelligence comes from the water itself: the temperature of the current, the smell of the air, the silence of the seabirds an hour before the squall._ Gain the Deepwater domain. When you read the sea and sky with time and attention, the GM provides accurate weather intelligence for the next D6 days of sailing without a roll.

## MAJOR ABILITIES

### INTERCESSION
_You beseech your god on another's behalf. The god is not charitable. The god is interested in you, which is different, and considerably more useful._ Roll Tend+Faith to cast this spell. On a success, remove D6 stress from any resistance (other than Tide) for one nearby character other than yourself.
- MINOR: THE BOSUN TOO. INTERCESSION can now be used on yourself.
- MINOR: TRANSFERRAL. Roll Tend+Faith to cast this spell; on a success, move a Minor fallout from any willing target other than yourself to any other willing target. Both targets must be within arm's reach of one another, and the receiving party must be able to bear the fallout.
- MINOR: THE GOD DOES NOT DISTINGUISH. When you cast TRANSFERRAL, the target receiving the fallout does not have to be willing to receive it. Casting the spell in this way uses the target's difficulty instead of standard difficulty.

### THE HUNGRY CHURCH
_Your faith has taken root: pirates, fisherfolk and dockworkers who acknowledge your god through you._ When in a port, roll Command+Faith to cast this spell as you consecrate a shrine and gather the faithful; on a success, add the port to your church. When you enter a port that is part of your church, gain a D4 (Faith) resource. Increase the dice size of this resource by one step for every additional port in the church after the first, to a maximum of D10.
- MINOR: TITHE. The faithful contribute out of something between gratitude and insurance. Once per session, whilst at a port in your church, clear D4 Gold stress.
- MINOR: EYES OF THE FAITHFUL. Your congregation notices things that feel wrong before they become things they can name. Once per session, whilst at a port in your church, ask the GM what your church has sensed that would be useful to you. They always know something. It is rarely comforting.
- MINOR: RITE OF SAFE HARBOUR. Whilst you are at a port in your church, you and your allies gain +1 Tide protection, and attacking you on consecrated ground is a divine transgression — the aggressor immediately marks D4 Tide stress.

### THE NAME SPOKEN OVER WATER
_You have learned one of the secret names of your god, and when you speak it across open water your enemies are struck senseless by its weight._ The name functions as the following weapon: (Brave D6, Spread, Debilitating, Maddening, One-Shot).
- MINOR: THE WAVE'S WEIGHT. The weapon gains the Brutal tag.
- MINOR: THE FOG'S QUIET. The weapon gains the Smoke tag (it manifests like fog rolling off cold water) and no longer creates noise — in fact, it dampens all nearby noise.
- MINOR: THE TIDE'S MERCY. When you speak THE NAME SPOKEN OVER WATER, you and all nearby allies remove D4 stress from Blood.

### THE PERFECT COURSE
_Complete navigational and spiritual preparation, meeting complete competency. The god clears the way for those who have done the work of being ready._ Once per session, roll Navigate+Faith to chart a course that accounts for every variable — weather, current, spiritual hazard, divine attention. On a success, if the crew follows it exactly, the passage to the destination succeeds — but the god grants no passage without instruction: choose one incident you still face along the way. Everything else parts around you.
- MINOR: PURSUIT COURSE. THE PERFECT COURSE can be designed to intercept a specific vessel. If you know where it started and have time to plan, you can catch anything that sails the Shackles.
- MINOR: ESCAPE COURSE. THE PERFECT COURSE can guarantee escape from any pursuit. Enemies without equivalent navigational and spiritual knowledge cannot follow.
- MINOR: THE IMPOSSIBLE PASSAGE. Once per campaign, THE PERFECT COURSE can take a ship through genuinely impassable waters — the edge of the Eye of Abendego, a divine boundary, waters the charts name as void. The god permits what the god permits. You have asked correctly.

### STORM CALLING
_This is not magic. This is asking the god to do what the god does, and having the relationship that means the god agrees._ Roll Endure+Faith and mark D8 Tide stress to call a storm in your god's name. It arrives within a few hours, is severe, and lasts until you release it or D6 hours pass, whichever comes first. You can direct it broadly: toward a fleet, away from a harbour, into a specific stretch of ocean. Ships caught in it take D8 Hull stress per exchange.
- MINOR: THE EYE. You can create a zone of calm within your storm where your vessel sails safely. Enemies cannot follow into the calm without taking Hull stress each exchange.
- MINOR: LIGHTNING. Once per situation within your storm, call a strike on a target you can see: D10 Blood or Hull stress. The god's aim is not always precise. It is always intentional.
- MINOR: RIDER OF THE STORM. While within a storm you have called, you take no stress from weather effects and roll with mastery on all navigation and shipboard actions. You asked for this. You are ready for it.

## ZENITH ABILITIES

### THE GOD'S CHAMPION
_The god has been arranging this since before you were born._ Once, you may act in your god's name with their full power behind you. The effect is mythic: a storm that swallows a fleet, a passage opened through waters closed for a century, a blessing that turns a dying ship into something that refuses to sink. Afterwards, the god's agenda is yours, completely and without reservation — you sail where it sends you, beyond the edges of the campaign. Whether this is the highest expression of freedom — doing exactly what you were made for — or the final surrender of it, is the question your arc has been building toward.

### SAINT OF THE DROWNED
_To activate this ability, die at sea, settling a debt the water was owed._ The place where you went down becomes holy: a sea-mark every sailor in the Shackles comes to know, where the water is calm when it has no right to be and no one drowns who does not deserve it. Over the following months, mariners leave offerings over the spot. For the remainder of the campaign, the sea-mark functions as a landmark with appropriate haunts. Once per campaign, when the surviving player characters sail over it, they can beseech your spirit for a true heading. You will deliver valuable wisdom.

### THE WAY THROUGH
_The sea opens for you._ Once, you can navigate anywhere: through the heart of the Eye, into waters no chart has reached, to a place that exists only because your god made it and you are the only one who knows the way. Where you go and what you find is the final question your arc has been asking. The god promised passage to those who served it. You have served it. The passage is real. Whether coming back is part of it is a question the god has not answered, and may not answer until you arrive. Your character leaves the story.

## Design Notes (not player-facing)

**Sources:** Heretic chassis (MINISTRATIONS, THE NAME SPOKEN OVER WATER = Oath of Sagacity, SACRED TATTOO, generic structure) + Incarnadine priestcraft (INTERCESSION = Broker with the full Invest/Transferral/Inflict trio, THE HUNGRY CHURCH = Network with Tithe/intelligence subs) + Pass 1 navigator identity (THE CHART AND THE PRAYER, THE PERFECT COURSE, STORM CALLING, the omen-reading minors). Two cores rather than the Heretic's one: Identity/engine (Chart) + the priestly support loop (Ministrations).

**Checklist compliance:**
- Core combo Navigate+Faith — unique (Castaway: Endure+Wilderness; Errant: Hunt+Colonial).
- Minors: 3 generic + 10 named (4 skill+text: Tend, Reckon, Command, Carouse; 4 domain+text: Outlaw, Port, Arcane, Deepwater; 2 bare +2). Named skills incl. core: 5. Named domains incl. core: 5.
- Protection matrix: **Luck 2 (Sacred Tattoo), Tide 2 (Divine Ward), no Blood** — Blood excluded from UNWAVERING FAITH and granted nowhere.
- **Forbidden skills: Deceive, Vanish** — the sin bosun stands in the open and speaks true. Natural opposite of the Sharper.
- Majors: 5 — support (Intercession), faction/social (Hungry Church), weapon (Name Spoken Over Water), spatial/travel + core upgrade (Perfect Course extends Chart and the Prayer), area (Storm Calling). All actives roll [Skill]+Faith. Sub-minors each carry power/expansion/alternate roles.
- Zeniths: 3 — surrender/apotheosis, martyrdom (Testament of Faith reskin), transcendent departure.

**Deviations & open questions:**
1. Pass 1's *Divine Sanction* (named deity, requirements/forbiddances, ask-for-a-sign) was cut as a third core; the named-deity requirement could move to character creation guidance or a calling. Worth deciding before playtest.
2. *Avatar Moment* and *Divine Compact* from Pass 1 didn't make the five-major cut; both are strong candidates if a sixth major is wanted (the guide allows 6 for spell-heavy classes, and this is one).
3. Hull stress (Storm Calling) assumes the ship-stress track from Pass 1 carries forward.

**Version 3 changes:** CHART AND THE PRAYER info gated once per session and its auto-success converted to mastery; CONFESSOR, TITHE and EYES OF THE FAITHFUL standardized to once per session (in port / in church); READ THE OMEN per scene to per session; THE PERFECT COURSE leaves one chosen incident in play; THE IMPOSSIBLE PASSAGE once per campaign; LIGHTNING once per situation.
