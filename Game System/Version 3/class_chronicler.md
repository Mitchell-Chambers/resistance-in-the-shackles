# Chronicler

_The Shackles has been drowning civilisations since before it had a name. Azlant sank whole. The cyclops empire of Ghol-Gan left its stonework on every island, geometries that do not quite resolve into sense. Lirgen, nation of astrologers, watched the sky for a thousand years and never saw the Eye coming. The sea swallows them all, and the Eye grinds what remains into something half-preserved and half-destroyed — and you read what they left behind. You learned early what the colonial universities refuse to admit: that the magic you practise and the history you study are the same subject. A dead tradition is not gone. Its workings are still down there in the wrecks and the ruin-chambers, written in scripts no living person was supposed to read, waiting for someone with the patience to recover them and the nerve to speak them aloud. The other scholars call what you do grave-robbery. The grave-robbers call it scholarship. They are both correct, and neither of them can do what you do: stand in a drowned temple, read the wall, and make the past pick its tools back up._

## Core Traits
- SKILL: Reckon
- DOMAIN: Ruins
- RESOURCE: A coin from an empire no living person can name D6 Ruins
- EQUIPMENT: Pick one:
    - Excavator's pick Plunder D8, Brutal, Tiring
    - Lantern-staff of salvaged Azlanti glass Brave D6; sheds clean light that needs no fuel
    - Field conservation kit and a pocket pistol Tend Gold D6; Hunt D6, Ranged, Reload

## CORE ABILITIES

### THE ARCHIVE
_You carry an annotated spellbook that is also a historical record; to you these are the same document._ At character creation, establish one dead language you can read and one drowned civilisation that is your particular study — these are always available as knowledge, without rolls. When you first encounter a ruin, wreck or significant relic, the GM tells you one true thing about its history and one true thing about its dangers.

### WHERE THE PAST LINGERS
_The old places remember their own workings, and they recognise a practitioner._ When you are in a ruin, a wreck, or any place abandoned since before living memory, roll with mastery when you attempt to cast a spell.

## MINOR ABILITIES

### ACADEMIC NETWORK
_You correspond with scholars, archivists and obsessives across the Inner Sea._ Gain the Colonial domain. Once per session, when you make port, a letter is waiting for you containing one piece of relevant historical intelligence. Your correspondents live in the colonial world; keeping them requires navigating its politics carefully.

### COMPARATIVE STUDIES
Gain access to one of the following domains: Deepwater, Shallows, Outlaw, Arcane, Ruins, Faith, Colonial. You can take this advance more than once.

### EXCAVATOR'S EYE
_You can tell at a glance what is valuable, what is cursed and what is load-bearing._ Gain the Plunder skill. When you spend time working any ruin or wreck site, you always find something worth taking; the GM tells you what it is and what it will cost to extract.

### FIELD RESEARCH
Gain access to one of the following skills: Carouse, Deceive, Endure, Hunt, Navigate, Plunder, Reckon, Tend, Vanish. You can take this advance more than once.

### GRAVE GOODS
_The dead were buried with their power. They have stopped needing it._ Gain the Shallows domain. Before you cast a spell from this class, you can opt to destroy a resource with the Ruins domain. Roll the resource's dice; the amount rolled is added to your Protection value against any stress incurred as a result of casting the spell.

### OBJECT READING
_Objects remember; you know how to ask._ Gain the Tend skill. Once per session, when you handle a significant object with a minute's careful attention, you learn who made it, approximately when, what it was used for, and the most significant event it witnessed; reading further objects is a Reckon+Arcane roll.

### PRESERVATION
Gain +1 Protection in the Blood, Tide, Reputation or Gold resistance. You can take this advance more than once.

### PROVENANCE
_Every artefact is worth what its story says it is worth. You write excellent stories._ Gain the Deceive skill. You can authenticate — or convincingly fabricate — the provenance of any artefact, relic or historical document. Your forgeries pass all but expert scrutiny, and you always know who in port will pay for what you're holding.

### THE ANNOTATED CHART
_Your charts overlay the living Shackles with everything that drowned beneath it._ Gain the Navigate skill. When you are seeking a wreck, a ruin or a settlement that no longer exists, you always know where to start looking, and your estimate of what condition it will be in is reliable.

### THE OLD WAY
_The god may be dead, but the working remembers._ Gain the Faith domain. Once per session, perform a rite from a dead faith: a small blessing, a temporary protection against supernatural harm, or words that a specific kind of spirit will stop and answer.

### TONGUE OF THE DEAD
_Ancient, dead and deliberately obscured scripts are your speciality._ Gain the Arcane domain. You can read any written language you have encountered before without a roll, and you can attempt any script you have never seen with a Reckon+Arcane roll.

### WARDED SCHOLAR
_Your body carries inscriptions copied faithfully from tombs that were never robbed._ Gain +2 Tide protection.

### WHAT KNOWLEDGE FETCHES
_There is always someone who will pay for what you know, and usually before the situation becomes critical._ Gain +2 Gold protection.

## MAJOR ABILITIES

### THE SAOC STARMAPS
_Lirgen's astrologers read the sky for a thousand years. You recovered their charts from the Sodden Lands, and they still work — for whatever that proved to be worth._ Roll Reckon+Ruins to cast this spell beneath an open sky. On a success, ask the GM what significant event will occur within the next session relating to a topic you specify. The answer is specific, accurate and literal — and occasionally misleading in implication. The Saoc Brethren were never wrong. They were also all drowned.
- MINOR: THE BROAD VIEW. Once per campaign, your reading extends to the shape of the campaign itself.
- MINOR: THE NARROW HOUR. Once per situation, you read one opponent's chart in miniature: the GM tells you the shape of what they intend, and you roll with mastery on your next action against them.
- MINOR: THE INSCRIPTION REMAINS. After a reading, you may inscribe the vision. The GM adjusts one aspect of the predicted event in your favour. The stars are not changed; the margins are.

### THE CYCLOPS CANTO
_Ghol-Gan's war-chants were built for throats with more room than yours. Spoken correctly anyway, they still break things._ The canto functions as the following weapon: (Hunt D6, Spread, Debilitating, One-Shot). It is the sound of a siege happening to the people in front of you.
- MINOR: THE BREAKING WORD. The canto gains the Brutal tag.
- MINOR: THE WALL-CANTO. Once per situation, instead of attacking, you may speak the building-cantos: rubble, cargo and broken stone heave themselves into a rough rampart, providing cover for you and nearby allies until you move.
- MINOR: ONE EYE OPEN. When you speak the canto, you glimpse the field as the cyclops seers did. Allies who act on what you tell them roll their first attack with mastery.

### THE LIGHT OF DROWNED AZLANT
_The first empire made light do things light does not do. The sea took them anyway, but it kept their lamps._ Roll Reckon+Ruins to cast this spell. On a success, you conjure the cold, clean light of Azlant across an area: it illuminates as full daylight, and within it the invisible, the hidden and the disguised are revealed as what they are. The light lasts until the end of the situation.
- MINOR: THE SEARCHING BEAM. Once per situation, focus the light on a single target: they lose all benefit of concealment, disguise or illusion until the end of the situation.
- MINOR: LIGHT BELOW. The light works perfectly underwater, and you can send it ahead of you — down a passage, into a wreck, through a hatch — and see what it touches.
- MINOR: THE HONEST HOUR. While your light holds, lies discolour the air as they are spoken. You see the stain. So does everyone else.

### THE ANCESTOR RITES
_The islands' first peoples did not consider death a reason to stop talking to family._ Roll Reckon+Ruins to cast this spell at any place where people have died. On a success, you call up the dead of that place, and they answer your questions about what they saw, what they knew and what was done to them. They are not compelled to be kind. They are compelled to be honest.
- MINOR: DEBTS REMEMBERED. The dead tell you where they left what they valued — the cache, the stash, the buried share. The GM confirms what is still there.
- MINOR: THE GRATEFUL DEAD. Once per session, a spirit you have treated with proper rites does you one small favour in the physical world: a door unbarred, a candle snuffed, a word whispered to a sleeper.
- MINOR: THE OLD QUARREL. Once per situation, you may point the dead at the living. The haunting is immediate and terrifying: one adversary treats every action as Risky until they leave the place the dead claim.

### RUIN SOVEREIGN
_The things that guard the old places have categories: intruder, prey, and colleague._ Hostile entities native to a ruin or wreck — undead, bound spirits, guardian constructs — treat you as a peer rather than an intruder unless you give them cause. You never trigger a site's passive hazards — collapses, floods, traps left by dead hands — without warning enough to act.
- MINOR: NAMED BY THE DEAD. The dead in a site know your name and speak to you voluntarily, offering its history and its current occupants without being summoned.
- MINOR: CLAIMED SITE. Claim a site as yours — you may hold one at a time. Other factions cannot use it without your permission; attempts encounter inexplicable difficulties that you understand and they do not.
- MINOR: THE LIVING RUIN. A site you have claimed begins to respond to you: lights that kindle, passages that open, mechanisms that turn over after centuries of stillness. The GM describes what becomes available.

### THE SPELL BEYOND MEMORY
_You recovered it from a tradition so old that no one alive has seen it cast. It does something current magic cannot._ Work with the GM to define your Spell Beyond Memory: unmake a curse permanently without knowing its origin; open a sealed divine space; speak across time to someone now dead; restore a broken thing to its original state. Casting it costs D8 Tide stress and can be done once per session.
- MINOR: PRACTISED HAND. Deeper understanding reduces the cost to D4 Tide stress.
- MINOR: SHARE THE KNOWLEDGE. You can teach the spell to one other character. Their version is real but unreliable — they roll to cast it. Yours simply works.
- MINOR: THE SECOND RECOVERY. Your research yields a second Spell Beyond Memory, from a different tradition. Define it with the GM.

## ZENITH ABILITIES

### THE COMPLETE RECORD
_Not the pirate myths. Not the colonial propaganda. What actually happened._ You have assembled enough to write the true history of the Shackles, from the cyclops empire to the present hour, and you release it. The consequences are seismic: legitimacies collapse, claims dissolve, buried debts surface, and every power in these waters learns what its founding actually looked like. Work out with the GM what breaks and what is set free. You do not get to stand apart from it — the author of that book cannot stay in the Shackles, or cannot stay alive in it, or cannot stay the person who wrote it. Your arc ends in the reverberation.

### THE ARCHIVIST ETERNAL
_You carried too much of the record, and the record has opinions about being put down._ You stop ageing. Your memory extends backward past your own birth, into the living history of these waters — every drowning, every founding, every name. A person cannot crew a ship carrying all of that; you take a seat instead. A lighthouse archive, a claimed ruin, a reading room in the back of a Port Peril chart-house: somewhere findable by those who truly need it. You are retired as a player character. Once per campaign, the surviving characters may consult you, and you will give them the truth — the whole of it, which is rarely the same thing as help.

### THE UNFINISHED WORKING
_Every drowned tradition died mid-sentence. You have found the end of one._ Once, you perform the final working of a dead civilisation — the one its practitioners were building toward when the sea took them. Define it with the GM, at the scale of the people who attempted it: a drowned city raised for a single day; the Eye quieted for a season; every restless dead of a vanished people settled at once. The working is titanic, and it takes its caster — as it took theirs, which you knew, because you read it, because that is why the tradition died and the working was never finished until you. The tradition is complete. So is your story.

## Design Notes (not player-facing)

**Sources & rebuild rationale:** The scholar slice of the Junk Mage. Where the Junk Mage steals from slumbering patrons, the Chronicler recovers from drowned civilisations — so the majors are tradition-suites exactly as the Junk Mage's are patron-suites: Lirgen/Saoc (divination, with the delicious irony that the tradition failed its own test), Ghol-Gan (war-craft; canto profile borrowed from Oath of Sagacity/Brisk Conjuration), Azlant (light), the islands' drowned cultures (ancestor rites). WHERE THE PAST LINGERS is Ravening Knowledge with location replacing stress threshold — the Powder Mage burns the body, the Chronicler haunts the right places. GRAVE GOODS is the SACRIFICE core demoted to a minor, mirroring the Powder Mage's SLOW MATCH. THE SPELL BEYOND MEMORY and RUIN SOVEREIGN survive from Pass 1, trimmed.

**Aggressive cuts from Pass 1:**
- *Historical Magic* (define-your-own spell list, flat D4 cost per cast) deleted: Heart spells live in majors with roll-to-cast, and the Junk Mage chassis already provides this. *Recovered Grimoire* (+5 freeform spells, then +5 more) deleted for the same reason — unbudgetable.
- *The Archive* cut from three established facts + three free info per site to one language, one civilisation, and two facts per site. *Ruin Sovereign* loses blanket all-rolls-mastery in ruins (replaced by the casting-mastery core, which is the Heart-shaped version of the same promise).
- Cut entirely: Curse Archaeology and Magical Archaeology (this is what Arcane-domain rolls and the Curse-Reader knack are for), Recovered Technique, The Useful Find, The Long Memory, Protective Inscription (the flavour survives in WARDED SCHOLAR), Annotated Map's once-per-arc auto-true map, Theoretical Combat, The Recovered Truth and Ghol-Gan Initiated (rebuilt as THE SAOC STARMAPS; the recovered-truth material is better as campaign content than a class ability).

**Checklist compliance:**
- Core combo Reckon+Ruins — unique. Cores: identity/info (Archive) + engine (Where the Past Lingers).
- Minors: 3 generic + 10 named (4 skill+text: Tend, Navigate, Plunder, Deceive; 4 domain+text: Colonial, Faith, Arcane, Shallows; 2 bare +2). Named skills incl. core: 5. Named domains incl. core: 5 — matching the Junk Mage.
- Protection matrix: **Gold 2 (What Knowledge Fetches), Tide 2 (Warded Scholar), no Luck** — excluded from PRESERVATION and granted nowhere. Thematically: Lirgen's astrologers believed knowledge was a defence against fate. The Chronicler knows exactly how that ended, and has no protection from it either.
- **Forbidden skills: Brave, Command** — the scholar neither charges nor leads. Knowing is different from daring.
- Majors: 6 (spell-heavy, matching the Junk Mage) — info/scrying ×2 (Starmaps, Azlanti Light), weapon (Canto), social/control (Ancestor Rites), area/environment (Ruin Sovereign), wildcard/core-extension (Spell Beyond Memory). No category exceeds two.
- Zeniths: 3 — the truth released, the living archive, the tradition completed (Herald-analog: the class's relationship to its "patrons" consummated at the cost of the self).

**Open questions:**
1. Tide 2 protection + Tide-costed casting coexist deliberately (protection slows accumulation from *other* sources; the work's own price is paid in full), but watch whether players read it as contradictory.
2. THE NARROW HOUR and ONE EYE OPEN both lean on GM-foreknowledge; if the table finds prediction abilities heavy, Starmaps is the one to keep and ONE EYE OPEN can become a simple mastery grant.

**Version 3 changes:** ACADEMIC NETWORK and OBJECT READING gated per session (further reads rolled); THE NARROW HOUR converted from GM-foreknowledge to intent plus mastery; THE BROAD VIEW once per campaign; CLAIMED SITE one at a time; SPELL BEYOND MEMORY once per session — the D8 Tide cost is the real price; light duration standardized to situation.
