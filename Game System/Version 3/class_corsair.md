# Corsair

_You have fought on more decks than you can count, in storms and calms, outnumbered and alone. Your body carries the testimony — the scars, the missing pieces, the things that healed wrong. You are not merely a fighter. You are a philosophy of controlled destruction, an argument made in blood and roaring iron that some people simply should not be crossed, and the Shackles has slowly come around to your point of view. Subtlety is for people who cannot afford to be seen coming; you have made yourself expensive to ignore. Every coin you take, you spend — on the crew, on the wake, on the night in port that becomes a story — because gold was never the point. The point is the moment when the grappling hooks bite, the rails come together, and everyone on both decks looks to see where you are standing. The sea made you this way. You have made your peace with it. The sea, as far as anyone can tell, is still deciding._

## Core Traits
- SKILL: Brave
- DOMAIN: Deepwater
- RESOURCE: The tooth of something that nearly ate you D6 Deepwater
- EQUIPMENT: Cutlass Brave D6
    - Pick one:
        - Boarding axe Plunder D8, Brutal, Tiring
        - Brace of pistols Hunt D6, Ranged, Reload
        - Sea-chest surgeon's kit and belaying pin Tend Blood D6; Brave D6

## CORE ABILITIES

### THE TESTED BODY
_You have been broken before. You know what it costs to keep moving._ Once per situation, when you suffer Minor Blood fallout, you may ignore its effects until the end of the situation. When the situation ends, the fallout arrives in full and you mark D4 Blood stress — the body keeps honest books.

### VIOLENCE AS ARGUMENT
_Your reputation for controlled, purposeful violence precedes you._ When you make a credible threat of violence to someone who knows your history, roll with mastery to compel, cow or halt them. Once per session, when you publicly demonstrate your capability — breaking something, surviving something, ending a fight with impossible efficiency — clear D4 Reputation stress.

## MINOR ABILITIES

### BOARDING MASTER
_People don't need your orders. They just move when you move._ Gain the Command skill. When you lead a boarding action, you and all allies following your lead gain +1 Blood protection for the first exchange.

### EARNED FEAR
_The hesitation your name creates is real, and it buys the time that keeps a legend alive._ Gain +2 Reputation protection.

### FIGHTING TRIM
Gain access to one of the following skills: Brave, Carouse, Command, Endure, Hunt, Navigate, Plunder, Tend, Vanish. You can take this advance more than once.

### HARBOUR LEGEND
_Every dockside from Bloodcove to Drenchport holds someone who saw you fight._ Gain the Port domain. In any port where you have fought before, at least one person will stand you a drink, back you in a brawl, and tell you what the harbour is saying about you.

### IRON STOMACH
_The sea has poisoned you so many ways it has run out of new ones._ Gain the Endure skill. When you mark stress from disease or poison, mark it one dice size smaller; Major Blood fallout from these sources is Minor for you instead.

### THE KILLING GROUND
_You move through the aftermath with deliberate efficiency._ Gain the Plunder skill. When you loot a battlefield, a boarded ship or a defeated enemy, you always find the most valuable thing first.

### OLD SALT'S STITCHES
_You have closed your own wounds in a rolling sea by lamplight._ Gain the Tend skill. You can treat your own Blood stress and fallout without assistance and without the Risky penalty.

### RAIDER'S COVES
_Every coast keeps a place to careen, hide or wait with the guns run out._ Gain the Shallows domain. Within a day's sail of any coastline, you always know an anchorage suited to hiding, repair or ambush, and whether anyone else knows it too.

### SCAR TISSUE
_The sea has tried to kill you in very specific ways. It will have to try differently next time._ Gain +2 Blood protection.

### SEA LEGS
Gain +1 Protection in the Blood, Tide, Reputation or Luck resistance. You can take this advance more than once.

### WEAPON OF CHOICE
_One weapon, known the way a priest knows scripture._ Gain the Hunt skill. Choose one weapon type — cutlass, boarding axe, pistol or ship's cannon. Weapons of that type permanently gain the Brutal tag in your hands.

### WHAT'S MINE
_The Code is clear, and so are you._ Gain the Outlaw domain. When someone tries to take what's yours — your ship, your share, your crew — your attacks gain the Brutal tag when you attempt to stop them.

### WHERE THE WIND TAKES YOU
Gain access to one of the following domains: Deepwater, Shallows, Outlaw, Port, Colonial, Wilderness. You can take this advance more than once.

## MAJOR ABILITIES

### THE UNSTOPPABLE
_You are filled with every fight you have ever refused to lose._ When you are suffering from ongoing Blood fallout, increase the stress dice of your attacks by one step.
- MINOR: LIMPING ONWARD. THE UNSTOPPABLE also increases the stress dice you inflict on journeys by one step.
- MINOR: SCARS LIKE MEDALS. Gain +1 Blood protection for each ongoing fallout you have.
- MINOR: ON YOUR FEET. Once per session, downgrade a Major Blood fallout you have to a Minor Blood fallout.

### DREAD NAME
_Your name is a weapon, and you have spent a career sharpening it._ Your announced name functions as a weapon: (Brave D6, Ranged, Spread). It only works on opponents who have heard of you and have the capacity to fear you, and it will not kill — if you reduce an opponent's resistance to 0 with it, they stand down, surrender, or flee. The GM determines how well-known you are in any given context. In the Shackles, the answer is usually 'very'.
- MINOR: THE LEGEND GROWS. Each session in which you do something worthy of the stories, clear D4 Reputation stress. The legend maintains itself.
- MINOR: PREEMPTIVE SURRENDER. Once per session, invoke your name before a fight begins; the GM rolls to see if the opposition folds before violence starts. Ordinary pirates and soldiers almost always do.
- MINOR: HUNTED. Once per campaign, the GM introduces a credible challenger who wants to make their name by ending yours. Defeat them and gain +1 Reputation protection for the remainder of the campaign.

### BOARDING ACTION
_The rails come together and the chaos organises itself around you._ When you direct a boarding action or ship-to-ship engagement, allies acting under your direction roll with mastery for the first exchange.
- MINOR: FIRING SOLUTION. Once per situation, direct cannon fire with precision: one ship takes D8 Hull stress directly, bypassing normal crew actions.
- MINOR: NO QUARTER. When the engagement is going your way and the opposing ship attempts to disengage, roll Command+Deepwater to cut off their escape; on a success, they fight or surrender — they cannot simply run.
- MINOR: AFTERMATH. After a successful boarding, the captured crew is cowed and compliant without further violence. They know what the alternative looks like.

### DECK-FIGHTER
_Cramped quarters, rolling footing, bodies underfoot — this is your parlour._ When you attack at close range, your attacks gain the Piercing tag, even if you're unarmed.
- MINOR: THROUGH THE SMOKE. Gain +2 Blood protection against ranged attacks.
- MINOR: FIRST INSULT. The first time you suffer Reputation stress in a situation, do not roll for fallout. One barb never bothers you; it's the accumulation that draws blood.
- MINOR: HOMECOMING. When you enter a friendly port after a voyage, refresh stress according to the size and importance of the port — a three-shack anchorage is D4; Port Peril is D12.

### SHIPSHAPE AND BATTLE-READY
_Never get into a fight you haven't drilled for._ Once per session, when you have an hour or two in port or at anchor, run preparations for the coming trouble. Pick one benefit; you and everyone who takes part gain it until you next make landfall:
- Whetstone Drill: one weapon gains the Brutal tag.
- Rig Check: one piece of Navigate or Tend equipment gains the Trusty tag.
- Toughen Up: gain +1 Blood protection.
- Steady Hearts: gain +1 Reputation protection.
- Stow Smart: gain +1 Gold protection.
- MINOR: DOUBLE DUTY. Choose two benefits when using this ability; participants gain both. You can't choose the same benefit twice.
- MINOR: MID-WATCH DRILL. You may run preparations during a journey; add a D6 bane to the journey's resistance if you do.
- MINOR: EMERGENCY ISSUE. With a few minutes to spare, grant one benefit to yourself or one ally by consuming a resource of D6 or higher value. This doesn't count as the per-session use.

## ZENITH ABILITIES

### THE LAST STAND
_Outnumbered, outgunned, cornered — finally, a fair fight._ Once, when retreat is impossible and surrender unthinkable, invoke the Last Stand. For the rest of the situation you cannot be killed, stopped or driven back; every attack you make inflicts maximum stress, and every attack against you inflicts the minimum. When the situation ends — one way or another — the reckoning arrives, and the GM determines what the sea finally claims. It has been waiting a long time, and it is owed.

### LIVING LEGEND
_The Shackles has decided you belong to it permanently._ You retire as the unkillable old corsair: the one they name ships for, the one new captains are measured against, the one drinking quietly in the corner whom no one — no one — troubles. Your name becomes a unit of measurement for violence. Work out with the GM where you settle and what it takes to rouse you, because once per campaign, something will be foolish enough to try, and the surviving characters will get to see what the stories were underselling.

### DOWN WITH THE SHIP
_To activate this ability, go down with your ship._ The sea takes you both, and the sea keeps what it takes. Your vessel becomes a ghost ship and you its master, sailing the Shackles forever — glimpsed off storm-heads, becalmed in impossible fog, running before winds that touch nothing else. Once per campaign, when the surviving characters are at sea and beyond hope, your sails appear: one broadside, one rescue, or one escort through water that should kill them. Then you are gone over the horizon, where the dead keep their own watch and the wake never closes.

## Design Notes (not player-facing)

**Sources & rebuild rationale:** The Hound's remaining combat kit, reflavoured from haven-protector to deck-predator: THE UNSTOPPABLE is the Hound's major with all three sub-minors (Limping Onward converted delve→journey), DECK-FIGHTER is Trench-Fighter (Keep Smiling's Mind→Reputation, Homecoming's haven→port), SHIPSHAPE AND BATTLE-READY is Forewarned and Forearmed with the benefit list mapped to Shackles tracks. WHAT'S MINE borrows the Incarnadine's Red Marketeer. Pass 1's DREAD NAME and the boarding suite survive trimmed; THE TESTED BODY rebuilt to drop the invented "extra stress slot" mechanic for Heart-legal grammar.

**Aggressive cuts from Pass 1:** *Iron Will* deleted (+2 to ALL protections plus GM death-veto is beyond anything Heart grants below zenith; its Spite/Not Yet subs were zenith-grade). *The Long Memory* deleted (Reckon is now forbidden — see below — and Threat Sense/Scene Reading knacks cover it). *Hard to Kill, Scar Tissue's stacking version, Two-Handed, Damage Assessment, Warcry, Relentless, Intimidating Presence, Fleet Feet, Armour of Scars, Iron Frame/Veteran's Hide* all cut or folded: most were unpriced passives; the survivors became EARNED FEAR/SCAR TISSUE (matrix pair) and OLD SALT'S STITCHES.

**Checklist compliance:**
- Core combo Brave+Deepwater — unique. Cores: engine (Tested Body) + social loop (Violence as Argument).
- Minors: 3 generic + 10 named (5 skill+text: Command, Endure, Plunder, Tend, Hunt; 3 domain+text: Port, Shallows, Outlaw; 2 bare +2). Named skills incl. core: 6. Named domains incl. core: 4.
- Protection matrix: **Blood 2 (Scar Tissue), Reputation 2 (Earned Fear), no Gold** — excluded from SEA LEGS and granted nowhere. Thematically: every coin is spent in port; the Corsair is famously, permanently broke.
- **Forbidden skills: Deceive, Reckon** — the argument is made in the open, and the Corsair resolves situations rather than reading them. Subtlety and analysis are someone else's watch.
- Majors: 5 — core upgrade (Unstoppable extends the Tested Body loop), social (Dread Name), area/ship (Boarding Action), technique (Deck-Fighter), support (Shipshape). No spatial major, matching the Hound.
- Zeniths: 3 — violent apotheosis, triumphant retirement, sacrificial sea-myth (the party-gift zenith, Deus Ex Machina analog).

**Open question:** THE UNSTOPPABLE + ON YOUR FEET makes ongoing Blood fallout a resource; that's intended (it's the Hound's own loop), but the Corsair has more Blood protection than the Hound did, so watch whether fallout actually lands often enough to fuel it.

**Version 3 changes:** VIOLENCE AS ARGUMENT threats roll with mastery (GM-fiat clause cut); DREAD NAME converted to Staredown grammar (Brave D6, Ranged, Spread); NO QUARTER rolls Command+Deepwater; FIRING SOLUTION once per situation; HUNTED once per campaign.
