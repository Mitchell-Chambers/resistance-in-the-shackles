# Timing & Gates

*Status: drafted 2026-06-10. Canonizes the pure-Heart timing rulings: four gates, fiction-scoped durations, no rounds anywhere.*

---

## No Rounds, No Initiative

Play is a conversation, paced by the fiction. There are no rounds, no turns and no initiative order — at any scale, including ship engagements. Adversaries never "take a turn": they inflict stress through your action results (failures and partial successes) and through the GM's framing when you stall, hide or do nothing. At sea, the chaos die (see Ships of the Shackles) is a GM pacing tool, not a clock.

## The Four Gates

Every recurring ability is gated by exactly one of these. No other gating currencies exist.

- **Once per situation.** A situation is a single contiguous scene of pressure — a fight, a storm, a negotiation, a delicate job. It ends when the pressure breaks: you win, escape, surrender, or the fiction moves on. The GM calls the boundary.
- **Once per session.** One real-world sitting of play.
- **Once per journey.** A journey is the passage between two landmarks (Heart's delve). The gate spans the whole passage, however many sessions it takes.
- **Once per campaign.** The ability can be used **a single time across the entire campaign by that character, and never refreshes.** Where a zenith grants the surviving party a once-per-campaign effect, the single use belongs to the party, not to any one character.

## Legal Durations

Timed effects are scoped fictionally, never numerically. The full vocabulary:

- **"Your next action"** / **"on their first action"**
- **"Until you next act"** (the nearest thing to "a moment" the rules recognise)
- **"Until the end of the situation"** / **"for the remainder of the situation"**
- **"Until the end of the session"** / **"until the start of the next session"**
- **"Until the end of the journey"** / **"until you next make landfall"**
- Fictional conditions: *"while you remain still," "while the veil holds," "until extinguished," "until someone acts on it."*

If an effect needs to repeat — a fire spreading, a storm battering a hull, poison working through a body — it repeats through results: those caught in it mark the listed stress when they fail or partially succeed against it, and when the GM judges it simply finds them. (The Degenerating tag is the printed model: the consequence resolves at the end of the situation, by a roll.)

## Writing New Abilities

A recurring ability takes one gate. A timed ability takes one duration from the list. If a draft wants a tick-tock clock, rounds, or a new currency ("once per port visit," "per duel," "D3 rounds"), it's mis-shaped — re-express it through results, one of the four gates, or a fictional condition.
