# V4 Class Identity — Proficient / Neglect Rules

*The firm per-class fingerprint we redistribute against. For each class and each of the six play-style axes, the axis is one of:*

- **● Proficient** — the class is *built* here; its best, most repeatable abilities live on this axis. A player who picks this class is buying this.
- **◐ Incidental** — present and allowed, but not load-bearing. A touch of flavour or a single ability, not a pillar. Safe to add to or trim without changing what the class *is*.
- **○ Neglect** — deliberately absent. This gap is part of the identity. We do **not** fill it just to round the class out; doing so would blur the character.

**Skill/Utility is structural, not discriminating.** Every class is Proficient there by the architecture (generic skill/domain/protection picks all land in this bucket). It's marked ● for all ten and tells us nothing about identity, so the real identity lives in the other five axes. Counts shown as **[Cmb / Soc / Kno / Skl / Trv / Heal]** from the distribution analysis.

## Per-class notes & rebalancing implication

**Castaway** — [6,0,0,9,2,3]. Proficient Combat. **Both Social and Knowledge are hard zeros — and that's the character:** a person the island ate and gave back wordless. Protect those zeros. Heal is currently incidental (self-medicine) but the class *has a Tend Blood kit*, so this is a clean place to promote Heal ◐→● with an **ally**-heal — rounding the class without touching its deliberate Social/Knowledge neglect.

**Tidecaller** — [3,0,6,8,2,2]. Proficient Knowledge (omens/scrying/pact-questions). Part of the Knowledge spike. Social is deliberate neglect (an omen, not a charmer). Has a Tend Blood kit; Heal could rise ◐→● to round it and pull a little weight off the Knowledge concentration.

**Corsair** — [6,4,2,6,0,2]. The dual-proficient Combat+Social fighter; this is a *good* balanced identity, leave the pillars. Travel is its one true neglect (a boarder, not a navigator). Has a Tend Blood kit — strong candidate to take an ally-heal (its Hound donor had NO SYMPATHY / Mend-ranged precedent), promoting Heal ◐→●.

**Powder Mage** — [9,0,1,10,0,1]. The most Combat-spiked class (9). Social, Travel and Heal are all deliberate neglect — a dangerous specialist who patches *himself* and little else. **Note the tension:** it carries a Tend Blood kit, but its identity says keep Heal at ○/◐. Read the kit as "field-patch myself," not party medicine. Do **not** make this a healer.

**Sharper** — [1,8,3,8,0,0]. The Social spike (drives ~half the Social over-shift). Combat, Travel, Heal all deliberate neglect — and **it has no Tend kit**, so healing cannot be the rounding lever here. Rounding the Sharper means *flattening the social spike itself* (re-pointing one or two social abilities toward its Secondary Knowledge axis), not adding heal. Flagged below as a cross-cutting problem.

**Daredevil** — [4,2,1,9,3,0]. Proficient Skill/mobility, with a genuine **Travel ●** (escape routes / impossible paths — one of only three real travel leans in the set). Knowledge and Heal are deliberate neglect; no Tend kit. Leave it; it's already distinct. A useful model that *Travel can be a pillar*.

**Sin Bosun** — [1,3,5,5,3,3]. The **balance model**: proficient across Knowledge, Travel and Heal, low Combat by design. This is what "spread identity" looks like and proves the six-axis spread is achievable in one class. Don't over-correct it. Combat is its single deliberate neglect.

**Sawbone** — [0,0,1,11,1,6]. The Heal spike (6 — highest in the set) and the redistribution's main source. Plan stands: **shift Heal toward Hull/ship** (the "Vermissian Knight on the ship" role — CANNIBALISED PARTS is already a Vermissian Plate port), demoting everyday personal-Blood healing to a rare miracle tier so other classes can own routine medicine. Combat/Social neglect is correct and stays.

**Chronicler** — [1,1,11,8,0,0]. The Knowledge spike (11 — most one-note class in the set, drives ~60% of the Knowledge over-shift). Combat, Social, Travel, Heal all deliberate neglect; **Tend kit is Tend *Gold*, not Blood**, so — like the Sharper — healing can't round it. Decision point: either **accept the Chronicler as a deliberate spike** (a pure scholar is a legitimate identity) or flatten it by re-pointing 1–2 knowledge abilities toward Travel (drowned-route navigation fits the fiction). Flagged below.

**Errant** — [5,5,3,5,0,2]. Dual-proficient Combat+Social, like the Corsair — a healthy balanced identity. Travel is its deliberate neglect. Has a Tend Blood kit; Heal ◐ could stay incidental or rise slightly. Low priority.

## The cross-cutting finding (important)

The principle was: *distribute healing to the lopsided social/knowledge classes to round out their identity.* The table exposes a wall:

**The two worst spikes — the Sharper (Social) and the Chronicler (Knowledge) — are exactly the two classes where healing CANNOT be the lever.** Neither carries a Tend Blood kit (the Chronicler's is Tend Gold), and Heal is deliberate neglect for both. So:

- **Healing rounds the *moderate* lopsiders** — Castaway, Tidecaller, Corsair (all have Tend Blood kits, all have Heal sitting at ◐ ready to promote to ●). This is the easy, fiction-safe win, and it nudges Heal % back toward Heart's level while spreading it off the Sawbone.
- **The two real spikes need a different lever:** either flatten the spike in place (re-point a social ability on the Sharper toward Knowledge; re-point a knowledge ability on the Chronicler toward Travel), or make a deliberate decision to *let them be spikes* as intentional pure-archetype identities.

So the firm rules are now twofold: (1) promote Heal ◐→● on the three Tend-Blood moderates and pull the Sawbone toward Hull; (2) decide separately whether the Sharper and Chronicler are flattened or blessed as deliberate spikes — that decision is a content choice, not a healing one.

*Tier calls are design judgements anchored to the current ability counts; revise a class's row if its abilities are rebuilt.*
