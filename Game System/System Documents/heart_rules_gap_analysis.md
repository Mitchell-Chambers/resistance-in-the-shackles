# Heart Rules vs Our Language — Gap Analysis

*Source: full text extraction of the Heart corebook PDF (Reference/). Scope: Location Tiers, Equipment, Resources, plus the cross-cutting vocabulary both depend on. No changes made to any class or system file — this maps the gaps so we can align our language to Heart's first, then make changes against a cohesive whole.*

---

## 1. Location Tiers

### What Heart actually says

Tiers "correspond to how close locations are to the Heart Itself, the epicentre of unreality." Tier 0 = City Above, barely touched; Tier 1 = City Between, people and pathways; Tier 2 = "where the weirdness creeps in," landmarks shift, havens unsettling; Tier 3 = "the weirdness rules here," physics unreliable; Tier 4 = the Heart Itself, no true return. Two extra categories we never mirrored: **Fractures** (otherworlds/pocket dimensions, easier to reach the deeper you go) and **Rogue** locations (unbound by tier, appear anywhere).

The tier system is load-bearing in three mechanical ways:

1. **Default stress by tier** — the GM's rule of thumb for any unclear stress dice: *Tier 0: D4, Tier 1: D4, Tier 2: D6, Tier 3: D6, Tier 4: D8.* Landmarks and delves carry a DEFAULT STRESS line derived from this.
2. **Statblock field** — every landmark and delve lists TIER (delves can span, e.g. "TIER: 1–2") alongside DOMAINS, STRESS, RESISTANCE, EVENTS, CONNECTION.
3. **Economy hook** — NPC job rewards = "one dice size up from the default stress dice for their current tier" (a Tier 1 NPC, default D6, offers a D8 resource).

### What we have

The Castaway's inline draft (carried since V2): Tier 0 colonial harbours → Tier 4 the Eye/drowned trenches, keyed to *wildness/lawlessness*.

### Gaps

| Heart | Ours | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Tiers = proximity to the Heart Itself | Tiers = distance from civilisation | **Convergent** — if the Eye is our epicentre-of-unreality analogue, our scale is structurally identical (Spire:Heart :: colonial port:Eye). Language needs the framing sentence, not a redesign. |
| Default stress table per tier | None | **Missing** — this is the table's main mechanical job. Several of our abilities already assume it exists (Hungry Church-style reward scaling, NPC rewards). |
| Landmark/delve statblock with TIER field | No statblock template at all | **Missing** — ports, islands and journeys need the DOMAINS/TIER/STRESS/RESISTANCE/EVENTS/CONNECTION template. |
| Fractures and Rogue locations | Nothing | **Missing category** — the Shackles has obvious candidates (the Eye's interior, drowned otherworlds, ghost-ship waters; "What the Eye Keeps" creates exactly a Fracture-like zone). |
| "One tier deeper" as a transformation effect (Witch's Final Form) | "One tier stranger" (What the Eye Keeps) | **Convergent**, wording differs by one word. |
| Abilities priced off tier ("default stress dice for their current tier") | Islandsblood (protection = tier), Remuneration ("double the value of your current tier") | Ours read tier as a *number* (protection points, Gold cleared); Heart reads it as a *dice size*. Both of ours are ports that did this in Heart too (Heartsblood, Remuneration) — fine, but the doc must define tier-as-number and tier-as-dice both. |

## 2. Equipment

### What Heart actually says

**Four types, not skill-keyed:** DELVE (terrain), KILL (weapons), MEND (healing/resupply), MISCELLANEOUS (narrative only). "When rolling to inflict stress (on an adversary for Kill or a journey for Delve) or to remove stress (for Mend), roll the dice listed." Default without equipment: D4.

**A named dice ladder:** Unequipped D4 / **Civilian D6 / Professional D8 / Exotic D10 / Legendary D12** — "most delvers won't ever get access to exotic or legendary equipment."

**Mend is split by resistance** — Mend Blood, Mend Mind, Mend Supplies — and crucially: *"Fortune and Echo stress can't be removed with equipment unless it's very special."*

**Quality is a separate axis from dice size:** Standard (baseline) / **Good** (Risky→Standard or Dangerous→Risky; +1 dice size cost) / **Excellent** (Risky *or* Dangerous→Standard; +2 dice sizes cost; all Excellent items are "rare") / **Renowned** (unique items granting a knack; one renowned item's benefits at a time; GM-awarded, not purchased).

**Armour rule:** Blood protection comes from class advances, not gear — armour descriptions are "purely narrative concerns." The **Block** tag (+1 Blood protection) is "the only way equipment can increase protection," and Block items are rare.

**Buying/selling:** characters have no defined money; the Supplies resistance is purchasing power. Costs: D4 minor / D8 moderate / D12 expensive, marked as Supplies stress (or trade resources of equivalent value, or spend them to reduce the stress). Haggling = **Compel+Haven** roll: success shifts the price one step in your favour, partial = pay full, failure = one step worse. Finding a trader for rare items can need **Discern+Haven**.

**Optional rule:** four equipment slots; D6+ items need a slot; overloaded = Risky on fast/quiet movement; +2 slots available as a minor advance.

**The tag glossary (canonical 26):** Block, Bloodbound, Brutal, Conduit, Dangerous, Debilitating, **Degenerating**, Distressing, Double-Barreled, Expensive, Extreme Range, Limited X, Loud, Obscuring, One-Shot, Piercing, Point-Blank, Potent, Ranged, Reload, Smoke, Spread, Tiring, Trusty, Unreliable, Wyrd. Notable mechanics: Brutal/Trusty/Potent stack ("roll two dice and pick the highest," each extra instance adds a die); Spread makes *targets* roll Evade+Domain to avoid (NPCs "simply take the stress" — relevant to our Knife Storm debate: Heart's Spread does put dice in PC hands when PCs are targets, but never in NPC hands); Degenerating is **a real, defined tag** (Endure+Domain at end of situation or mark D6/D4).

**Heart's own inconsistencies worth knowing:** *Maddening* (Heretic's Oath of Sagacity) and *Desperate* (Apiarist's Release the Swarm) appear in class weapon profiles but are **never defined in the glossary**. So Heart itself ships undefined boutique tags; we inherited Maddening via the Sin Bosun.

### What we have

The V2 overview's weapon-notation paragraph: weapons are tagged with their natural attack skill (**Brave** desperate melee, **Hunt** deliberate/ranged, **Plunder** breach-and-board), "the GM may allow another applicable skill at no bonus." Tend equipment exists (Tend Blood D6, Tend Blood/Tide D6, Tend Gold D6, Tend Hull D6), plus Navigate, Vanish and Deceive equipment in class lists.

### Gaps

| Heart | Ours | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Type system: Kill / Delve / Mend / Misc | Skill-tag system: Brave/Hunt/Plunder weapons, Tend healing, Navigate journey gear | **Structural divergence, ours is richer but undocumented.** Heart's Kill→our three combat skills; Heart's Delve→our Navigate; Heart's Mend→our Tend. Workable, but the rule sentence ("roll the listed dice when inflicting/removing stress; D4 without equipment") was never written down for ours. |
| Mend split by resistance; Echo & Fortune not removable by equipment | Tend Blood, Tend Gold exist; **Tend Tide (Sin Bosun) and Tend Hull (Sawbone) exist** | **Two deliberate breaches to ratify or revoke.** Tide = Echo, which Heart bars from equipment "unless it's very special" — the Sin Bosun's scripture-inked bandages may qualify as exactly that, but it should be a stated exception, not an accident. Tend Hull has no Heart analogue at all (depends on the Hull decision, §5). No equipment removes Reputation stress anywhere — Heart's Mend Mind (drugs, therapy) has no mapped equivalent; decide whether Reputation is equipment-healable at all. |
| Named dice ladder (Civilian→Legendary) | Never adopted | **Missing vocabulary** — cheap to adopt, gives GMs pricing instincts. |
| Quality axis (Standard/Good/Excellent/Renowned + knack) | Referenced by Engineering Mind ("standard becomes good, good becomes excellent") but defined nowhere | **Missing system that V4 now depends on.** Renowned-grants-a-knack also solves where our knack list plugs in. |
| Armour = advances only; Block is the sole protective tag | Followed in practice (no armour items in any class) | **Convergent** — just needs the rule stated. |
| Buying via Supplies stress D4/D8/D12; haggle Compel+Haven | Nothing written; Gold is clearly our Supplies | **Missing** — and the haggle roll needs a skill mapping (Compel+Haven → presumably Command+Port or Carouse+Port; Discern+Haven → Reckon+Port). |
| Equipment slots (optional) | Nothing | Optional in Heart too — note it, decide later. |
| Tag glossary with resistance hooks: Dangerous→Blood, Loud→Fortune, Expensive→Supplies, Distressing→Mind, Wyrd→Echo, Conduit→Mind | We use Dangerous, Loud, Expensive, Wyrd, Bloodbound unchanged | **Every resistance-keyed tag needs a remap line:** Loud→Luck, Expensive→Gold, Wyrd→Tide (this is the long-pending rename), Distressing→Reputation(?), Conduit→(unused; its Mind cost has no obvious home). Also: we deleted Degenerating in V3 believing it non-canon — **it is canon**; restoring it to the Daredevil's Specialty Load is legitimate. Maddening should either get a definition (Heart never gave one) or be cut. |
| Spread: targets roll Evade+Domain to avoid; NPCs just take it | We removed the "opponents roll" from Knife Storm | Heart's Spread text means our V4 Knife Storm profile *already* behaves correctly via the tag — no change needed, but the tag glossary must carry the full Spread text so it works. Evade→our equivalent needs naming (we have no Evade; nearest is Vanish or Brave). |

## 3. Resources

### What Heart actually says

"Resources is a catch-all phrase for consumable or tradable items of value… The primary use of resources is to access haunts and remove stress and fallout." Each has a **value** (D4 common → D8 valuable → D12 remarkable) and a **domain** ("most of the time a resource's domain doesn't matter, but some character abilities require resources of a particular domain"). Notation: *Relic Vermissian guidebook (D10, Technology)*.

**Resource tags** (distinct from equipment tags, "hints towards what might go wrong"; GM springboards off Fortune fallout): Harmful, Fragile, Awkward, Deteriorating, Taboo, Volatile, Mobile, Beacon, Niche.

**The haunt economy** (where resources get spent):
- Trade a resource to refresh up to the haunt's listed dice ("Frumious, an alchemist… D8 Blood, D8 Echo").
- **Downgrade instead of consume**: a D8 resource accessing a D6 haunt can be stepped down D8→D6 and kept, "but you can't break down a single valuable item into smaller parts."
- **No resources? Pay in stress** to a different resistance — Supplies, Fortune, Blood, Echo or Mind, fiction permitting.
- **Upgrading**: six marks on a haunt → refresh dice +1 size; you can buy marks (3 at a time) by paying full price without refreshing.
- Fortune fallout commonly damages/downgrades haunts; D4 haunts downgraded are removed.
- Distribution guidance: Plentiful / Standard / Scarce campaign settings; "the harder something is to get hold of, the more valuable it is."
- A random generator (Value D10 / Domain D10 tables).

**Related rules our classes already lean on:** removing stress (Mend+Domain heals D4 + equipment; self-treatment is Risky — our OLD SALT'S STITCHES is built on this); Minor fallout clears its resistance's stress, Major clears all; ongoing fallout removal is haunt/ability territory; **bonds** (max three; transfer up to D8 stress per visit; D8 stress to a bond removes Minor fallout or downgrades Major; bonds roll their own fallout and can be lost — the Witch's LAIR "acts as a bond," which our Back Room dropped).

### What we have

Resource notation matches Heart exactly (*A twist of dried meat… D6 Wilderness*). Class abilities consume resources by domain (Red Feast, Slow Match, Grave Goods, Cannibalised Parts, Vermissian-style menus) — all Heart-grammar ports. "Refresh DX" is used in eight-plus abilities. Haunts are name-checked (Homecoming, Hungry Church, Sea-mark zenith) but never defined for the Shackles.

### Gaps

| Heart | Ours | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Resource = value + domain | Identical notation | **Convergent.** |
| Resource tags (9) | Never adopted | **Missing** — free flavour + the Luck-fallout springboard fits our doom-engine theming perfectly. |
| Haunt economy (trade / downgrade / pay-in-stress / upgrade track) | "Refresh" used, never defined | **Missing — highest priority.** Eight abilities reference refresh/haunts; the Luck question (below) hinges on it. |
| Pay-in-stress list includes Fortune and Mind | Our stress doc reportedly restricts Luck clearing ("only when fallout fires") | **Collision to resolve**: Heart freely lets you *mark* Fortune to pay haunts, and haunts *refresh* Fortune (Frumious example: D4 Fortune). If our Luck track forbids haunt-refresh, that's a real divergence from Heart's economy — deliberate doom-engine design, but it must be stated in the haunt rules, and it explains why Sharper/Daredevil Luck-clears felt contentious. |
| Bonds (3 max, D8 transfer, bond fallout) | Never mentioned in any Shackles doc | **Decide: do bonds exist?** They're load-bearing for Heart's Mind economy. With Mind→Reputation, bonds become *very* thematic (your reputation lives in other people), and the Back Room/Hungry Church/Web all have natural bond hooks. |
| NPC reward scaling off tier | Nothing | Comes free once the tier table exists. |

## 4. Cross-Cutting Vocabulary Map

The translation table that should headline every system document:

| Heart | Shackles (current usage) | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Blood / Echo / Mind / Fortune / Supplies | Blood / Tide / **Reputation** / Luck / Gold | Mind→Reputation is our deepest divergence; every Mind-keyed rule (Mend Mind equipment, Distressing, Conduit, pay-in-stress, bond transfers) needs an explicit ruling, not assumed symmetry. |
| Compel / Delve / Discern / Endure / Evade / Hunt / Kill / Mend / Sneak | Command / Navigate / Reckon / Endure / **(no Evade)** / Hunt / Brave+Plunder / Tend / Vanish (+ Carouse, Deceive) | Evade has no mapped heir — matters for the Spread tag and dodge rolls. Kill splitting into Brave/Hunt/Plunder is documented only in the V2 overview. |
| Delve (journey) | Journey / voyage | Settled. "Once per delve"→"once per journey." |
| Landmark / haven / haunt | Port, anchorage, island… used loosely | Need formal definitions: what counts as a landmark, what makes one a haven-equivalent ("friendly port"?), haunts as named services. |
| Connection (halve resistance on repeat) | Never defined | Missing — obvious Shackles shape: *charted route*. |
| Situation / session / campaign | Adopted (V3) | Settled. |
| — (Heart has no combat time unit) | "Exchange" (used ~10 times: first exchange, per exchange) | **Our invention, never defined.** Heart's nearest grammar is "until you next act" (Better Part of Valour). Either define Exchange in the core rules or rewrite to Heart's phrasing. |
| Tier (0–4, dice table) | Tier (0–4 draft, number only) | Convergent; needs the dice table. |
| Boon / bane (D4–D12 vs delve resistance) | Adopted correctly | Settled. |
| Refresh | Used, undefined | Define with haunts. |
| — | Hull stress | **No Heart analogue.** Heart would likely stat a ship as either an adversary (single Resistance + protection) or a delve (resistance pool). Decide: sixth resistance vs ship-as-adversary vs ship-as-delve before writing the Hull doc — the Heart-native answer is one of the latter two. |

## 5. Decisions Needed Before Drafting the Documents

1. **Tier framing** — confirm the Eye as the epicentre (tiers = proximity to the Eye's unreality, which conveniently co-varies with distance from colonial law), and adopt the default-stress-per-tier table. Proposed mapping deferred until you confirm.
2. **Hull** — sixth resistance, ship-as-adversary, or ship-as-delve. Everything in the ship combat doc hangs on this.
3. **Tend Tide / Tend Hull equipment** — ratify as named exceptions to "Echo/Fortune not equipment-healable," or revoke.
4. **Reputation economy** — what refreshes it (haunts? bonds? Carouse?), and whether any equipment can.
5. **Bonds** — adopt, adapt (reputation-flavoured), or cut. Affects Back Room, Web, Hungry Church.
6. **Luck and haunts** — confirm whether haunts can refresh Luck (Heart says Fortune haunts exist) or our "fallout-only" rule overrides; state it either way.
7. **Tag glossary** — adopt Heart's 26 with remapped resistances (Loud→Luck, Expensive→Gold, Wyrd→Tide rename, Distressing→Reputation?), restore Degenerating, define or cut Maddening, name the Evade-equivalent for Spread.
8. **Quality axis** — adopt Standard/Good/Excellent/Renowned wholesale (Renowned = knack delivery mechanism).
9. **Exchange** — define it or rewrite to "until you next act."
10. **Equipment slots** — adopt the optional rule or skip (Heart treats it as optional too).

Once these are called, the three documents — *Location Tiers*, *Equipment & Resources*, *Ports & Haunts* (landmark/haunt/connection rules fall out of the resource economy and probably deserve their own doc) — can each be written to read like a missing chapter of Heart rather than a patch on top of it.
