# Sawbone — Draft: Combat-to-Par + Hull/Vermissian Shift

*Implements the re-grade action for the Sawbone: **Combat 0 → par (3)** and **Heal +4 → ~par, retargeted from everyday party-Blood healing to Hull/ship and a miracle tier.** Skill spike retained; Social/Knowledge neglect retained (correct identity). Combat runs entirely on **Brave** — the desperate close violence of a brawler with trade tools — never Hunt (forbidden, and not the fantasy).*

**The budget move in one line:** the two new combat units are paid for by demoting **THE SAME SAW** and **CANNIBALISED PARTS** off the everyday-Heal axis (Same Saw → diagnosis/utility; Cannibalised Parts → the Hull-and-harm flex engine), and by shedding party-wide Blood healing to the Tend-kit moderates (Castaway/Corsair/Tidecaller get the ally-heals). The Sawbone keeps *self/single-target* field medicine and gains the ship.

---

## NEW — Combat content (brings Combat 0 → 3)

### Minor — THE SAW CUTS BOTH WAYS  *(Brave)*
*Flesh comes apart the way timber does — along lines you have spent a career learning to read. Taking a thing apart is the same knowledge as putting it back together, run backwards and without the care.*

Gain the **Brave** skill. Your trade tools — bone saw, maul, pick, mallet — function as proper weapons in your hands with no improvised penalty. Once per situation, when you attack a living target at close range, your strike finds an anatomical weak point and gains the **Piercing** tag.

### Major — BREAKING POINT  *(Brave; or Tend against a vessel)*
*Everything built is a diagram of how it fails. You have read the diagram for the human body, the cyclopean wall and the ship's frame, and they rhyme. Show you a thing and you will show you where to hit it.*

When you attack a person, a structure or a vessel, roll **Brave** (or **Tend** against a vessel) to find its breaking point before you strike. On a success, you **reduce the target's Blood protection by 2 until the end of the situation** — or, against a vessel, your attack inflicts +1 step of Hull stress.

- **MINOR: COLLAPSE.** Against a structure or vessel, a found breaking point spreads: the GM escalates the damage one stage — a breach becomes a flood, a sprung plank becomes a falling spar.
- **MINOR: THE UNKINDEST CUT.** Against a living target, a found breaking point leaves a wound that worsens untended: the attack gains the **Degenerating** tag.
- **MINOR: FIELD STRIP.** Once per situation, apply BREAKING POINT to a weapon or device a foe is holding or relying on. It fails — jams, snaps, misfires — at the worst possible moment for them.

*(Third combat-leaning unit comes free from the CANNIBALISED PARTS reframe below — its "inflict stress on an adversary" option is foregrounded, not new.)*

## REFRAMED — the Hull/Vermissian engine (retargets Heal)

### Core — THE SAME SAW  *(now diagnosis/utility, not everyday heal)*
*The ship is a body. The body is a ship. Both can be read.*

Your Tend rolls apply equally to flesh and timber — you never choose a specialism. Once per situation, when you spend a minute examining a person **or a vessel**, the GM tells you what is broken, how badly, and what it would take to fix it. *(Unchanged text; reclassified — this is the diagnostic thesis, not a source of party healing. It now also feeds BREAKING POINT: a thing you have examined, you know how to break.)*

### Major — THE LIVING SHIP  *(the Vermissian role — Hull, not party-Blood)*
*You have mapped every weakness, tracked every repair, predicted every failure. You are the ship's second body.*

Once per session, the GM warns you of a threat to your vessel before it lands — the shot that finds the magazine, the reef that opens the hull, the plank that gives in the next storm. While you are aboard, your vessel counts as having **+1 Hull protection.**

- **MINOR: REPAIR IN MOTION.** Once per situation, clear D4 Hull stress without it costing you your action. You were already down there.
- **MINOR: THE SOUL PERSISTS.** If your ship is lost, salvage one essential piece of her from the wreckage. The next vessel you fit it to becomes your ship immediately — you know her weaknesses as if you'd served aboard a year.
- **MINOR: NO DEAD SHIPS.** When weather, flooding or reef damage alone would sink a vessel you actively maintain, roll Tend+Deepwater; on a success, you find the solution just in time.

*(Unchanged mechanically — this is the class's identity major now, the "Vermissian Plate on the ship." It is Hull/refresh, never personal Blood.)*

### Major — CANNIBALISED PARTS  *(flex engine — Hull + harm foregrounded, Blood demoted)*
*Everything is parts, if you're honest about it — and parts can mend a hull, arm a trap, or come apart in someone's hands.*

Once per session, consume a resource with the Port or Colonial domain by stripping it for materials. Roll the resource's dice and choose one:

- **Inflict stress on a journey or an adversary** equal to the amount rolled, as you jury-rig exactly the wrong surprise for them. *(combat/utility — now listed first)*
- Remove stress from a **vessel's Hull** equal to the amount rolled. *(Hull)*
- (D8+) Gain access to a skill or domain for the rest of the session — the manual was in the crate.
- (D8+) Increase your Blood protection by 1 for the rest of the situation — armour the work.
- Remove stress from a **person's Blood** equal to the amount rolled. *(retained, but demoted to last)*

- **MINOR: ANYTHING'S PARTS.** Resources with any domain qualify.
- **MINOR: THE GOOD STUFF.** When you consume a resource of D8 or higher value, choose two benefits instead of one.
- **MINOR: TWICE OVER.** Use CANNIBALISED PARTS twice per session; the second time, mark D4 Luck stress.

*(Same Vermissian Plate menu — reordered so the everyday party-Blood heal is one option of five, not the default. The Sawbone can still heal Blood, but it now competes with arming a trap and patching the hull.)*

## KEPT — retained self/field medicine + miracle tier

- **Minor — AMPUTATION** *(Heal)* — kept as-is. Self/single-target fallout removal; iconic. Not party-wide.
- **Minor — BONESETTER** *(Heal)* — kept as-is. Field treatment of the person in front of you.
- **Major — NOT TODAY** *(Heal — miracle tier)* — kept as-is, once-per-campaign. THE IMPOSSIBLE SURGERY / IMPOSSIBLE REPAIR subs are the *rare* whole-situation saves, explicitly not everyday medicine.

## SHED — moves off the Sawbone (handled on other classes)

Everyday **party-wide Blood healing** leaves the Sawbone and is picked up as ally-heals by the Tend-kit moderates — **Castaway, Corsair, Tidecaller** — per the redistribution map. The Sawbone stops being the single owner of routine party medicine; it becomes the ship-doctor/engineer who can *also* save a specific dying patient as a set-piece.

## Balance pass (against the tag glossary & Hull rules)

Checked every number against `equipment_and_resources.md` and `hull_and_the_ship.md`. Verdict: the design holds; four wording/number fixes applied (above), nothing structural.

**Validated as-is:**
- **Piercing on a minor (THE SAW CUTS BOTH WAYS).** Precedent: Heart's Cleaver *Unmaking Claws* (minor) grants *permanent* Piercing on unarmed attacks. Ours is gated to once-per-situation on tool attacks — strictly **more conservative** than the precedent. Safe.
- **+1 Hull protection (THE LIVING SHIP).** A sound vessel starts at Hull protection 1, so this doubles a base ship's protection — but it's the communal-track equivalent of the Vermissian's *Get Behind Me* (+1 Blood to allies, a major), and it's already the accepted number in the live class (2026-06-10 errata). Unchanged.
- **CANNIBALISED PARTS Hull option.** Not new — the original already listed "remove stress from a person's Blood **or a vessel's Hull**." We only reordered the menu so harm/Hull lead and Blood-heal trails. No power change.

**Fixed to match precedent:**
1. **BREAKING POINT base** — was "ignores 2 points of protection" (an undefined mechanic). Changed to **"reduce Blood protection by 2 until end of situation,"** matching the Powder Mage's *Shattering Load* (the one calibrated "−2 protection" precedent in our set). Piercing (ignore *all* protection) would have been too strong as a major's base effect.
2. **THE UNKINDEST CUT** — was "inflict a guaranteed Minor Blood fallout," which is unusually strong (fallout is normally the GM's D12 roll against accrued stress, not granted outright). Changed to the **Degenerating tag** — the defined, precedented way a weapon makes a wound worsen.
3. **REPAIR IN MOTION** — "free action" is round-language; the Hull rules are explicitly fiction-scoped with no rounds/exchanges. Reworded to "without it costing you your action."
4. **(noted)** Confirm BREAKING POINT's roll-then-strike doesn't stack its −2 with THE SAW CUTS BOTH WAYS' Piercing on the same blow — pick one per attack. Flag for the class file's combo notes.

All gates are session/situation/campaign only — no D3s, conforming to the V4 gate rule.
