# Sharper — Draft: Combat-to-Par + Social-Spike Trim

*Implements the re-grade action for the Sharper: **Combat 1 → par (3)** and **Social +6 → ~+4** (the other half of the imbalance the Chronicler drives on Knowledge). Same elegant move: the budget spent reaching the Combat floor is taken straight from the over-built Social pile — fix and trim are one operation, fully zero-sum.*

**Identity preserved.** The Sharper stays the information spider — Social remains its single signed-off Proficiency. It does not become a brawler; it becomes a spider **whose violence is the same as its cons: nobody sees it coming.** Combat runs on **Hunt** — the sleeve pistol, the sword-cane, the knife produced from a handshake — never Brave or Endure (both forbidden, and neither is the fantasy). It already owns THE CHEAP SHOT.

**The budget move in one line:** two of the five social minors are converted *into* combat minors — Social −2 and Combat +2 in the same stroke, no slots juggled, total flat.

---

## NEW — Combat content (brings Combat 1 → 3, by converting two social minors)

### Minor — THE SLEEVE  *(Hunt)*  — replaces UNCANNY TRADE
*A pistol in the cuff, a blade in the cane, a needle behind the lapel. The trick was never the weapon. The trick is that no one searched the man buying them a drink.*

Gain the **Hunt** skill. Regardless of searches, disarmament or the social conventions of the room, you always have a small, concealed, sufficient weapon. When you strike someone who does not yet know you are a threat, your first attack gains the **Brutal** tag.

*(UNCANNY TRADE's supernatural-fence flavour folds into THE WEB's "arcane rumour" contact — the Sharper can still move cursed goods, it's just no longer a standalone tap.)*

### Minor — THE LONG CON'S END  *(Hunt)*  — replaces MARKED CARDS
*Every relationship you build is a position you can close. The mark who trusts you is the mark who lets you stand behind them.*

Gain combat use of your social setup: once per session, when you attack a target you have cultivated over a scene or worked with THE HOOK, you roll with **mastery** and increase your stress dice by **one step**. They trusted you — that is the whole point.

*(MARKED CARDS' blackmail-leverage function is redundant with EVERYBODY OWES and THE WEB, which keep the "I hold your secret" fantasy alive. Converting it spends the social engine on the kill it was always building toward.)*

*(Plus existing **THE CHEAP SHOT** — already combat, Piercing on the unaware — gives the third unit. Combat now ≈ par.)*

## RECLASSIFIED — honest re-tag (no mechanical change)

### CONTROLLED PROFILE → Skill/Utility
It grants +2 Reputation protection and nothing active — it is defensive utility, not a social *action*. Re-tagging it off the Social axis (it still exists, still serves the Reputation 2 matrix) corrects the count without removing anything. *Social −1 on paper; the class plays identically.*

## Extreme sign-off (required)

The Sharper remains a deliberate Extreme, now reading:

- **One Proficiency: Social (+3).** A signed-off proficiency — the spider's defining strength, no longer a runaway. Approved by design.
- **Combat at par (floor met).** ✓
- **Neglects: Travel (0) and Heal (0).** A double-Neglect — the urban information-broker neither leads voyages nor heals. Heal is intrinsic (no Tend kit); Travel fits the "everything I own fits in a back room" identity. Flag as an **approved double-Neglect Extreme** (same class of choice as the Castaway's Soc+Kno). If we want to soften it, the only cheap lever is a Travel touch — but it's defensible as-is.

So the Sharper ends as a **one-Proficiency, two-Neglect** Extreme with Combat lifted to the protected floor. The runaway Social spike is resolved, and ~half the set's Social over-shift comes off with it.

## Balance pass (against the tag glossary)

- **THE SLEEVE — always-armed + Brutal first strike.** "Always have a concealed weapon" is a verbatim echo of the Powder Mage's QUICK DRAW (minor). Brutal on a first strike against the unaware mirrors the Daredevil's RIGGING RAT (first attack after a drop = Brutal, minor). Both at minor tier. Safe.
- **THE LONG CON'S END — mastery + 1 step, once per session, conditional.** Deliberately *not* Piercing, to avoid stacking with THE CHEAP SHOT's Piercing (positional) — these are different triggers (relational vs positional) and must not combine on one strike. Mastery + one die step is gentler than CHEAP SHOT's NEVER SAW IT COMING sub (+2 steps, costs Luck), so it sits correctly below the major. Safe.
- **Gates** session/situation only — no D3s.
- **Flag for the class file:** THE LONG CON'S END and THE CHEAP SHOT do not stack on the same attack; pick one.
