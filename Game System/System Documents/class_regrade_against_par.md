## What the re-grade says

**Four floor-breaks to fix (the combat-floor rule).** In priority order by size of the gap:

1. **Sawbone (−3)** — the worst. Currently Combat 0. Needs a real, repeatable combat option. The fiction is right there: the bone saw and carpenter's maul (its own equipment) are brutal tools. A "the saw cuts both ways" surgical-violence minor plus a maul-based major brings it to par without making it a duellist.
2. **Chronicler (−2)** — has one combat major (Cyclops Canto). Needs ~2 more: the excavator's pick and further dead-empire war-cantos are the natural source.
3. **Sharper (−2)** — has Cheap Shot. Needs ~2 more underhanded-violence options (sleeve pistol, sword-cane, the ambush the con sets up). This *also* helps flatten its Social spike by spending budget off Social.
4. **Sin Bosun (−2)** — already has combat-adjacent (Name Spoken Over Water, Storm lightning). The smallest lift — formalise one or two into competent at-par combat.

**Spikes to keep or trim (the Extreme sign-offs).** "Steel, smoke and blood" gives a clean rule of thumb — *combat* spikes are on-theme, *social/knowledge* spikes are the imbalance:

- **Powder Mage Combat +6** — **keep.** A glass-cannon gunner is exactly the fantasy; its neglects (Soc/Trv/Heal) are the honest price. This is the model of a *well-formed* Extreme.
- **Chronicler Knowledge +9** — **trim.** The single biggest driver of the Knowledge over-shift, and now that it must spend budget reaching the Combat floor, that budget should come *out of* the Knowledge pile. Target ~+4/+5, not +9.
- **Sharper Social +6** — **trim.** Same logic: budget spent reaching the Combat floor comes out of Social. Target ~+4.
- **Tidecaller Knowledge +4** — **borderline keep.** An omen-witch should read high on Knowledge; it's at par on Combat already. Leave unless the set still reads Knowledge-heavy after the Chronicler trim.
- **Sawbone Heal +4 / Skill +4** — **redistribute, don't just cut.** Heal moves toward Hull/ship (the Vermissian role) and sheds everyday Blood healing to the moderates below; the freed budget partly funds its new combat floor.

**Heal redistribution (now funded by the same moves).** The moderates with Tend Blood kits and Heal at/just-above par absorb the Sawbone's shed everyday healing: **Castaway (+1 → promote via ally-heal), Corsair (par → ally-heal), Tidecaller (par → ally-heal).** This nudges each toward a fuller identity and keeps party healing alive once the Sawbone turns ship-ward.

**Healthy classes — leave alone.** **Daredevil** (clean Travel Proficiency, the set's proof Travel can be a pillar), **Corsair** and **Errant** (well-formed Dual-Proficiency Cmb+Soc, paid for by a single Travel Neglect). These are the template for "interesting but not extreme."
