# Sin Bosun — Draft: Combat-to-Par (small lift)

*Implements the smallest of the four floor fixes: **Combat 1 → par (3)**. The Sin Bosun is the "median made real" — near par on everything, no spike to fund from — so combat is built by **formalising violence it already gestures at** (the divine name, the storm's lightning) plus one new rite-weapon minor, funded by a light trim of its mild Knowledge lean. No identity change: the navigator-priest simply stops being a non-combatant in a piracy game.*

Combat runs on **Brave** (the rite-knife, the swung storm-lantern — its equipment is already Brave) and on **divine action cast on Faith** — never Deceive or Vanish (both forbidden). It already owns one combat major.

**The budget move:** the Sin Bosun sits at total budget with a mild Knowledge proficiency (+3). One redundant info-tap is merged to free the slot; combat fills it. Knowledge −1, Combat +2 (the second point comes from reclassifying STORM CALLING's lightning, which was always combat).

---

## NEW — Combat content (brings Combat 1 → 3)

### Minor — BLESSED STEEL  *(Brave)*
*The rite-knife that opens a sea-burial opens other things. The god does not mind which.*

Gain the **Brave** skill. Your consecrated weapon (rite-knife, storm-lantern, belaying pin) is a true weapon in your hands. Once per situation, when you strike someone who has broken faith, oath or the Code — a mutineer, an oathbreaker, a desecrator — your attack gains the **Brutal** tag. The god keeps the books.

### Reclassified — STORM CALLING is combat, not just travel
STORM CALLING (and its **LIGHTNING** sub: *call a strike on a target you can see — D10 Blood or Hull*) is the Sin Bosun's heavy divine weapon and should be counted as combat, not folded into the travel/weather column as it was in the re-grade. No text change — it is reclassified, and with THE NAME SPOKEN OVER WATER (the existing combat major) and BLESSED STEEL, Combat reaches par.

*(Existing **THE NAME SPOKEN OVER WATER** — Brave D6, Spread, Debilitating, Maddening, One-Shot — remains the signature divine weapon.)*

---

## TRIMMED — light Knowledge trim to fund the new minor

### Merged → CONFESSOR absorbs STAR-READER's function
Both are once-per-session "the GM tells you something true" taps (CONFESSOR: an NPC's secret in port; STAR-READER: an omen from the sky). Collapse into one:

**CONFESSOR** (retained): *Once per session, someone unburdens themselves to you — an NPC in port confesses a fear, a plan or a thing they've done; or, read at night, the sky gives an omen about a person, ship or course. The GM chooses the medium and answers honestly.* The standalone STAR-READER minor is removed, freeing its slot for BLESSED STEEL.

*(LIAR'S BURDEN and THE CHART AND THE PRAYER keep the class's truth-and-navigation knowledge intact — this only removes a redundant third tap.)*

## Extreme sign-off

**None required.** With Combat raised to par, the Sin Bosun has no spike, no multi-Neglect, and only mild proficiencies (Knowledge +2, Travel +1, Heal +1). It is the clean, un-extreme baseline the median describes — the proof the par is achievable.

## Balance pass (against the tag glossary)

- **BLESSED STEEL — Brutal vs the faithless, once per situation.** Conditional Brutal on a minor, gated to oathbreakers/desecrators — narrower than the Daredevil's RIGGING RAT (unconditional Brutal first strike). Thematically the god's-judgement clause echoes the Corsair's WHAT'S MINE (Brutal when defending what's yours). Safe.
- **STORM CALLING reclassification** — no mechanical change; the LIGHTNING sub (D10) is existing, already balanced as the set's model big-active.
- **Gates** session/situation only — no D3s.
- **Note:** Travel dips to +1 but stays above par, so the reclassification doesn't open a Travel neglect.
