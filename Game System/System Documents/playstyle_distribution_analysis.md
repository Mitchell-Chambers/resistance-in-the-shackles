# Play Style Distribution — Heart vs. Version 4

*A content analysis of class abilities, skills and equipment across six play-style categories: **Combat**, **Social**, **Knowledge**, **Skill/Utility**, **Travel/Journey/Delve**, and **Heal/Refresh**.*

---

## Method

Every ability on every class was tagged to one **primary** category — the thing it most lets a player *do at the table*. The counting unit is the named ability: both **core** abilities, every **named minor** (including the generic skill/domain/protection picks, which fall under Skill/Utility), and each **major** counted once by its dominant sphere. **Sub-minors** were not counted separately (they reinforce their parent major's category) and **zeniths** were excluded (they are narrative endgame, not repeatable play). Equipment is treated as a signal rather than a counted unit — see its own section.

A few deliberate tagging rules, because they shape the totals:

- **Skill/Utility** is the catch-all: stealth, mobility, crafting, domain access, generic protection, and "competence" perks all land here. It is large by design in both systems.
- **Protection** perks (+2 to a resistance, etc.) are Skill/Utility *unless* they protect *other people* in a fight, which makes them Combat (e.g. an ally-shield ability).
- **"Ask the GM" / divination / lie-detection / read-a-person** abilities are **Knowledge**, even when flavoured as magic.
- **Refresh / remove-stress / remove-fallout / Mend / Tend** are **Heal/Refresh**, even when the fiction is a feast or a sermon.
- This is judgement-based content analysis. Counts are accurate to ±1–2 per class; the **patterns and percentages** are the deliverable, not the decimals. Heart has 9 classes (n ≈ 178 tagged abilities); V4 has 10 (n ≈ 201). Compare via percentage, not raw count.

---

## Headline: the two distributions

| Category | Heart % | V4 % | Shift |
|---|---|---|---|
| Combat | **22%** | 18% | ↓ −4 |
| Social | 6% | **11%** | ↑ +5 |
| Knowledge | 10% | **16%** | ↑ +6 |
| Skill / Utility | **46%** | 39% | ↓ −7 |
| Travel / Journey | 4% | 5% | ≈ |
| Heal / Refresh | 12% | 9% | ↓ −3 |

**The story in one line:** V4 took weight *out* of the two biggest Heart buckets (raw Combat and the Skill/Utility catch-all) and redistributed it into **Social** and **Knowledge** — almost doubling Social and adding half again to Knowledge. Travel barely moved, and Heal dipped slightly.

This is a coherent, intentional drift, and it tracks the setting: the Shackles is a place of politics, reputation, factions, hidden agendas and lore-from-drowned-empires. The skill list itself encodes the change — Heart's single *Compel* became the Shackles' three-way **Carouse / Command / Deceive**, and Heart's *Discern* became **Reckon** with a much wider mandate. Splitting those skills gave designers more category "slots" to hang abilities on, and the classes filled them.

## Where the differences come from

**Social roughly doubled (6% → 11%).** Three classes carry it: the **Sharper** (8 social abilities — the heaviest social load in either system, with no Heart equivalent that concentrated), the **Corsair** (4 — reputation-as-weapon, where the Hound was more healer), and the **Errant** (5 — a duellist built half out of colonial politics). The split of Compel into Carouse/Command/Deceive is what made this possible: there are simply more social verbs to design around.

**Knowledge grew by half (10% → 16%).** This is the biggest proportional jump and it comes from three places: the **Chronicler** is a near-pure Knowledge class (11 of 21 abilities — a scholar archetype Heart never built), the **Tidecaller** leans its Witch chassis hard into omens and scrying (6), and the **Sin Bosun** turns the Heretic's healing into navigation-and-divination (5). Heart's most "knowing" class (Incarnadine, 3) wouldn't crack the V4 top three.

**Combat eased back (22% → 18%).** Note this is *proportional* — V4 still has hard hitters. The **Powder Mage** is actually *more* combat-concentrated than its Junk Mage donor (9 combat abilities; the Junk Mage's social court-magic was traded for gunnery). But the set as a whole spread out: the **Sharper**, **Sawbone**, **Chronicler** and **Sin Bosun** are all near-pacifist by design, which pulls the average down even as the fighters stay sharp.

**The Skill/Utility catch-all shrank (46% → 39%).** This is the mirror image of the Social/Knowledge gains — abilities that in Heart would have been generic "competence" perks were, in V4, given a sharper social or informational identity instead. It's a sign of *more deliberate* design, not less content.

**Heal/Refresh dipped slightly (12% → 9%)** but **concentrated**. The **Sawbone** is now the unambiguous medic (6 heal abilities, the single highest in either system, plus the ship-repair "Hull" dimension Heart lacked). Heart spread healing across three Mend classes; V4 gives one class clear ownership and lets the others (Castaway, Corsair, Errant, Sin Bosun) keep a token heal or two.

**Travel/Journey barely moved (4% → 5%).** This is the one place the redistribution *didn't* happen, and it's worth flagging against the project's stated intent. The premise was to borrow **Wildsea's Journey** as a nautical centrepiece — yet dedicated journey-facing class content is as thin in V4 (11 abilities across 10 classes) as Delve content was in Heart (8 across 9). The journey *subsystem* may be carrying the weight (it lives in `journeys_at_sea.md`, not on class sheets), but if you want journeys to feel like a pillar rather than a connective tissue, **the classes currently under-invest in them.** The closest things to "journey specialists" are the Sin Bosun (3) and Daredevil (3, via escape/route abilities), and neither is a dedicated voyage class the way the Cleaver is a dedicated combat class.

## Equipment — identical posture in both systems

Equipment was not counted into the percentages, but it tells a consistent story across both systems and is worth noting: **every class's loadout is combat-forward.** The standard pattern is a baseline melee weapon plus a "pick one" that is *usually a second weapon* (a bigger Kill/Brave die, a ranged option, or a brutal/tiring trade-off), with a healing kit (Mend/Tend) offered as one of the choices for the support-leaning classes.

- In **Heart**: Cleaver, Deadwalker, Hound, Incarnadine, Junk Mage, Vermissian all lead with weapons; Apiarist, Heretic, Witch offer a Mend kit as one branch.
- In **V4**: identical — Castaway, Corsair, Powder Mage, Daredevil, Errant lead with weapons; Sawbone, Sin Bosun, Tidecaller, Chronicler offer a Tend kit branch.

So the *equipment* layer reads as slightly more combat-heavy than the *ability* layer in both systems, and V4 inherited this faithfully. If anything, this means the on-paper Combat percentages **understate** how combat-ready every character is at creation — even the Sharper and Chronicler walk in armed.

---

## Summary verdict

V4 is a **deliberate rebalancing toward the social-and-informational**, achieved mostly by spending the Compel→Carouse/Command/Deceive and Discern→Reckon skill splits, and by building two archetypes Heart never had: a pure information class (Chronicler) and a pure social operator (Sharper). It reads as a more *evenly spread* set than Heart — fewer classes are dominated by the Combat/Skill pairing, more occupy distinct niches.

Two things to watch:

1. **Travel/Journey is the unfilled promise.** Given the Wildsea-Journey ambition, class-level journey content is no richer than Heart's Delve content. Consider whether one class should become an explicit voyage specialist, or whether more classes should get a journey-facing major.
2. **Heal narrowed to one owner.** The Sawbone is now load-bearing for party healing in a way no single Heart class was. That's fine if every party is assumed to field one, but it's a sharper single-point-of-failure than Heart's three-Mend-class spread. Worth a deliberate decision rather than an accident of the rebuild.
