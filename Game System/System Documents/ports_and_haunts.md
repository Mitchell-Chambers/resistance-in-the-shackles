# Ports & Haunts

*Status: drafted from the decisions of 2026-06-10. Mirrors Heart's landmark, haunt, healing and bond rules (pp. 76, 98, 130–131) with the Mind economy moved to Reputation and the Luck/Tide tracks held to Heart's Echo/Fortune restrictions.*

---

## LANDMARKS

Some places are infamous or important enough to become **landmarks**: ports, anchorages, wrecks, island interiors, sea-marks. Landmarks are used for navigation, take the tier of their region (see Location Tiers), and carry the statblock fields DOMAINS, TIER, DEFAULT STRESS and HAUNTS.

A landmark where you can rest, trade and find people who'd rather not kill you is a **friendly port** — our haven. Anywhere that isn't a landmark is protean: the cove you sheltered in last season may not be the same cove when you return, and the open sea between landmarks operates on themes, not promises.

## JOURNEYS

Travel between two landmarks without an established route is a **journey** (Heart's delve). Each journey has DOMAINS, TIER, STRESS and RESISTANCE. Working toward completion is a normal action with an appropriate skill+domain; success or partial success inflicts D4 stress on the journey by default (equipment and abilities can raise this); failure or partial success marks stress on the character — not lost, just worn: supplies burned, the long way round, attention drawn.

**BOONS AND BANES.** Actions that make the journey easier (clearing the wreckers off the passage) subtract D4–D12 from its resistance as a boon; actions that risk extending it (an extended rest to heal) add D4–D12 as a bane.

**ABANDONING A JOURNEY** is a new journey home with resistance equal to half what you'd already cleared from the old one, rounded up.

**CHARTED ROUTES** (Heart's connections). A stable route between two landmarks. When you undertake a journey along a charted route, halve its resistance. You can turn a journey into a charted route by achieving the goal in its CHARTED ROUTE entry — silencing the false beacons, killing the thing that hunts the strait, fixing the leading-lights. Routes can still collapse: storms, war and the Eye unmake them.

## HAUNTS

Haunts — individual establishments and services within a friendly port — are where stress and fallout are removed. Each lists what it refreshes and a dice cap:

> **TIDEWATER REST** *(friendly port)*
> DOMAINS: Port, Outlaw · TIER: 1 · DEFAULT STRESS: D4
> HAUNTS:
> • Mother Quay's surgery, no questions kept (D8 Blood)
> • The Leeward Shrine, salt-stained and patient (D6 Tide)
> • Shark-Tooth Row gambling pits (D6 Luck, D4 Gold)
> • The Commodore's Wake, where stories are currency (D8 Reputation)
> • Hagar's yard and careening beach (D6 Hull)

**ACCESSING A HAUNT.** You pay for the service. The common way is trade: exchange a resource to refresh up to the haunt's listed dice. If your resource outranks the haunt and it makes narrative sense, **downgrade it by the equivalent steps instead of consuming it** — a D8 cask of rum buys the D6 surgery and leaves you a D6 cask. You can't break a single valuable item (a painting, a famous gem) into smaller parts.

**PAYING IN STRESS.** No resources? Take equivalent stress to a different resistance: Gold (spending down your reserves), Luck (pushing it), Blood (hard or dangerous work in payment), Tide (letting something uncanny take its taste) or Reputation (doing something people will talk about, and not kindly). Whether an option is viable is up to the GM and the fiction.

**UPGRADING.** Each use, mark the haunt. At six marks, clear them and increase its refresh dice one size (takes effect next visit). You can invest — pay full price without refreshing — for three marks at a time. **Donating a quest reward or significant resource to a haunt counts as investing, and the donor refreshes Reputation equal to the amount rolled on the donated resource's dice.** Generosity, in the Shackles, is the loudest thing you can buy.

**DAMAGE.** Luck fallout commonly damages haunts — downgrade one or all haunts in the port. A D4 haunt that is downgraded is gone.

## REMOVING STRESS — BY TRACK

A successful Tend action removes D4 stress; equipment increases this. On a failure or partial success the helper marks stress to Gold.

- **BLOOD** — medical attention. Tend+[Domain] to patch someone up; treating yourself is Risky.
- **GOLD** — resupply and careful stewardship. Tend+[Domain] to share out, scavenge or stretch what you have, for yourself or an ally.
- **HULL** — repair. Tend+[Domain] aboard your vessel; see Hull & the Ship.
- **REPUTATION** — deeds, drink and word of mouth. **Carouse+[Domain]** to stand the room a round, hold court, or make sure the story gets told right; doing it for *your own* name is Risky — nothing looks worse than telling your own tale. Also refreshed at haunts, by bonds, and by donating to haunts (above).
- **TIDE** — cannot be removed by equipment or skill rolls. Specific abilities and haunts only.
- **LUCK** — cannot be removed by equipment or skill rolls. Specific abilities and haunts only. (Luck haunts exist — gambling dens, shrines to Besmara, the old woman who unknots curses — exactly as Fortune haunts do in Heart.)

Suffering fallout still converts stress to consequence as in Heart: Minor fallout clears the stress in its resistance; Major clears all stress in all resistances. Ongoing fallout is removed at appropriate haunts, by paying their price — or by the rare ability that says otherwise.

## BONDS

When you make a real connection with someone in the Shackles — a quartermaster who owes you, a tavern-keeper who keeps your seat, a rival who'd be insulted if anyone else killed you — they can become a **bond** at the GM's discretion. A bond is a strong relationship, not necessarily a kind one. **You can't have more than three bonds**; to take a new one, let an old one drift.

Bonds are where your name lives when you're not in the room. **When you visit a bond, you can transfer up to D8 stress onto them — Reputation is the most common**, as they carry your story, calm the rumours, vouch for you in rooms you can't enter; other resistances are possible where the fiction supports it (a fence absorbing Gold stress, a sawbones absorbing Blood). A bond can also remove a Minor fallout, or downgrade Major to Minor, at a cost of D8 stress to the bond — explain how they help you carry it.

**Each transfer, the GM rolls for fallout on the bond.** Minor: they have a problem and ask you for help. Major: they're in serious trouble. Critical: they're removed as a bond — and if they're alive, they might really hate you. In the Shackles, the people who hold your name are also the people your enemies can find.

*(Optional rule, as in Heart: bond actions — the GM rolls 1D10, +1D10 if the request is in the bond's home area, +1D10 if it's in their expertise, and reads the result as a player roll.)*
