# Location Tiers

---

## What a Tier Is

Every region of the Shackles — a stretch of sea, a coastline, an island interior — has a **tier from 0 to 4** that measures how dangerous and how strange it is. The higher the tier, the less the region cares about charts, law, or your survival.

Tiers describe *regions*, and landmarks take the tier of the region they sit in.

**The inland rule.** Danger deepens away from the water. As a rule of thumb, each step inland from the shore increases the tier by one. A large island in a Tier 1 sea has a Tier 1 shore landmark, a Tier 2 interior landmark, and — if it is large or old enough — a Tier 3 deep interior. The sea gives you a reading; the land corrects it.

## The Five Tiers

**TIER 0 — Lawful waters.** Colonial harbours, naval stations, merchant towns, patrolled sea lanes. The Shackles barely reaches here; the law does.

**TIER 1 — Pirate waters.** Pirate ports, settled coasts, busy shipping lanes. Rough, armed and dangerous, but inhabited — there are people here, and most of them are still people.

**TIER 2 — Open waters.** The open sea, the shores of charted islands, the coastal wilds. This is where the strangeness creeps in: landmarks shift character between visits, things prowl below the keel, and help is far away.

**TIER 3 — Uncharted waters.** Deep island interiors, waters no chart agrees on, Ghol-Gan ruins. The strangeness rules here. Weather disobeys the season, geometry disobeys the eye, and the things you meet were not waiting for *you*, exactly, but they were waiting.

**TIER 4 — The drowned places.** The Eye of Abendego, the drowned trenches, places with no name. You cannot come back from here; if you do, you are not the same person who entered.

## Default Stress by Tier

If you are ever unsure how much stress something inflicts — a fall, a reef-strike, a tavern brawl gone wrong — apply a dice size based on the tier of the region:

| Tier | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Default stress | D4 | D4 | D6 | D6 | D8 |

The GM is free to alter these values as the situation demands. Landmarks and journeys list a DEFAULT STRESS derived from this table.

## Tier as a Number

Some abilities read the tier as a number rather than a dice size — the Castaway's ISLANDSBLOOD (minimum protection equal to tier) and the Sharper's REMUNERATION (clear Gold equal to double tier). When an ability does this, use the tier of the region or landmark the character currently occupies.

## Rewards by Tier

NPCs offering work pay **one dice size up from the default stress dice of their tier** for basic tasks — an NPC in a Tier 1 port (default D4) offers a D6 resource; one in a Tier 2 anchorage offers a D8. Tasks requiring multiple people pay all of them. Haggling for a better rate is always possible, and always has the capacity to go wrong (see Equipment & Resources).

## Statblock Template

Landmarks and journeys use the same fields Heart uses:

> **CROSSWIND SHOALS** *(journey)*
> A reef-maze between two anchorages, passable at the right tide and lethal at the wrong one. Wreckers light false beacons on the northern cays.
> DOMAINS: Shallows, Outlaw
> TIER: 1–2
> STRESS: D6
> RESISTANCE: 10
> EVENTS: False beacon; sudden tide-race; wrecker ambush; a drifting hulk, recently abandoned.
> CHARTED ROUTE: Chart the true passage and silence the false beacons — thereafter, halve this journey's resistance.

(For journeys, charted routes and boons/banes, see Ports & Haunts.)

## Beyond the Tiers

Two location categories sit outside the ladder, mirroring Heart's Fractures and Rogue locations. Both are optional until the setting gazetteer is written:

**OTHERWHERES.** Pocket worlds and breached realities reachable from the Shackles — the calm country inside the Eye, drowned heavens where dead navies muster, First World shallows where the islands dream. The deeper the tier, the easier they are to reach. They contain strangeness found nowhere else and act as shortcuts, if you don't mind what walks them.

**ROGUE WATERS.** Some places aren't bound by tier and can appear anywhere if you know how to look: ghost ships keeping their own schedules, the wandering isle that is never twice on the same heading, the floating market that finds *you*. Generally, the deeper they're met, the stranger they are.
