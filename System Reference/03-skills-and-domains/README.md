# Part Three — Skills & Domains

Two things define what a character can do: **skills** and **domains**.

**Skills** are things your body and mind know how to do under pressure — active competencies, the capacity to *act*. **Domains** are areas of lived experience, knowledge, and connection — where you have expertise and who you know. A skill gets you in the door; a domain tells you what to do once you are inside.

When you have a relevant skill or domain for an action, add one D10 to your pool and keep the highest. If both apply, roll two. Gaining a skill or domain a second time grants a **knack** — a narrow named facet that lets you roll with **mastery** (one extra die), to a maximum of four dice. You can never gain more than one mastery die on a single roll, however many knacks or abilities apply.

The combination is what makes a character: *Tend + Deepwater* is a ship's surgeon who has worked in sea conditions; *Tend + Ruins* is someone who has patched people up in collapsing Ghol-Gan chambers. Same skill, entirely different people.

- **[The Eleven Skills](skills.md)** — Brave, Carouse, Command, Deceive, Endure, Hunt, Navigate, Plunder, Reckon, Tend, Vanish.
- **[The Nine Domains](domains.md)** — Deepwater, Shallows, Port, Outlaw, Colonial, Arcane, Faith, Ruins, Wilderness.
