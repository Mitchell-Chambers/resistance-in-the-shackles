# Equipment & Resources

---

## EQUIPMENT

Equipment is broken down by what it does. When rolling to inflict stress (on an adversary, or on a journey) or to remove stress, roll the dice listed on the equipment. **Without access to specific equipment, the default stress dice is D4.**

**WEAPONS** are tagged with the attack skill that suits their natural use:
- **Brave** — desperate, personal melee: cutlasses, knives, mauls, swung lanterns.
- **Hunt** — deliberate or ranged violence: pistols, duelling swords, harpoons, hexes.
- **Plunder** — breach-and-board tools: boarding axes, excavators' picks, grapnels swung in anger.

The tag marks the weapon's natural grip. The GM may allow another applicable skill at no bonus, as with any untagged equipment.

**JOURNEY GEAR** is tagged **Navigate** and lets a character inflict stress on a journey by succeeding on an appropriate roll: charts, leadlines, spyglasses, weatherglasses, a good pilot-boat.

**TEND GEAR** removes stress, and is divided by the track it can treat: **Tend Blood** (bandages, sutures, rum and a steady hand), **Tend Gold** (spare stores, trade goods, a well-stocked sea chest), **Tend Hull** (tar, canvas, copper and pins — see Hull & the Ship). **No equipment can remove Tide, Luck or Reputation stress.** Tide and Luck are removed only by specific abilities and by haunts, as in Heart; Reputation is removed by deeds, drink and word of mouth (see Ports & Haunts), not by anything you can carry.

**MISCELLANEOUS** equipment doesn't inflict or remove stress and isn't valuable enough to be a resource; it has narrative effect, and tags if appropriate.

### The Dice Ladder

| Dice | Grade |
|---|---|
| D4 | Unequipped / improvised |
| D6 | Civilian |
| D8 | Professional |
| D10 | Exotic |
| D12 | Legendary |

Most sailors will never touch exotic or legendary equipment — it's very rare.

### Using Equipment

The action must make sense in the fiction for the player to access the increased stress dice. A diving bell helps in a flooded wreck, not on a rooftop. Using equipment in ways it isn't designed for can, at the GM's discretion, reduce its stress dice, make the action Risky, or be outright impossible. Ammunition isn't tracked — assume a ready supply, and remember that Minor Gold or Luck fallout can leave a character dry until they resupply.

### Quality

Quality is a separate axis from dice size:

- **STANDARD** — good enough; follows the rules above. Standard equipment also permits tasks you otherwise couldn't attempt: a pistol shoots at range, a lockpick opens doors quietly, a jolly-boat floats.
- **GOOD** — if the item was specifically designed to help with the task, treat a Risky action as Standard or a Dangerous action as Risky. Increase cost by one dice size.
- **EXCELLENT** — treat a Risky *or* Dangerous action as Standard. Increase cost by two dice sizes. All Excellent items are rare.
- **RENOWNED** — a unique, storied item. Its bearer gains access to a **knack** associated with it; lose the item, lose the knack. Renowned items can't be purchased — they're inherited, won, or awarded by the GM. A character benefits from at most one renowned item at a time.

*Example renowned items: the boarding axe that took the* Dominion's *mainmast (Plunder knack: First Over the Rail); Mistress Cyradel's weatherglass, which has never once been wrong about her killers (Navigate knack: Storm's Edge); a deck of cards confiscated from a devil (Deceive knack: The Honest Game).*

### Armour

Blood protection comes from class advances, not gear — descriptions of it as scarred hide, boiled leather or blessed tattoos are purely narrative. The **Block** tag (+1 Blood protection) is the only way equipment can increase protection, and Block items are rare. Good or Excellent armour can be bought and may confer weapon tags, but grants no protection of itself.

### Buying & Selling

Characters don't track coin; the **Gold resistance** is purchasing power. The GM sets a cost — **D4 minor, D8 moderate, D12 expensive or exotic** — and the buyer marks that stress to Gold, trades resources of equivalent value instead, or spends resources to reduce the stress by the value rolled. Some things can't be bought without first finding a seller and doing what *they* want.

**Haggling** — to buy lower or sell higher without an ability that grants it, you must put something on the line. Roll **Command+Port** (the most common combination; others are possible). Buying: success drops the price one step; partial pays full; failure raises it one step as something goes awry. Selling: the same in reverse. Tracking down a buyer or seller for a **rare** item (all Excellent items are rare) can require a **Reckon+Port** roll.

*(We skip Heart's optional equipment-slot rule. The GM makes ad-hoc calls on whether someone is carrying too much.)*

### Tags

Heart's full glossary, adopted with resistances remapped (Echo→Tide, Mind→Reputation or Tide as noted, Fortune→Luck, Supplies→Gold). Tags only take effect when a player uses the item, except where noted (e.g. Piercing works for both sides).

- **BLOCK:** +1 Blood protection.
- **BLOODBOUND:** Mark D4 stress to Blood to roll with mastery when using this equipment for the rest of the situation.
- **BRUTAL:** When you roll for stress against an adversary with this item, roll two dice and pick the highest. Multiple instances stack — each adds another die.
- **CONDUIT:** Mark D4 stress to **Tide** to roll with mastery when using this equipment for the rest of the situation. *(Heart: Mind. Channelling is the sea's business here, not the crowd's.)*
- **DANGEROUS:** When you inflict stress with this item and roll the maximum, mark D6 stress to Blood.
- **DEBILITATING:** Once per situation, when you inflict stress with this item, the next attack against that target is rolled with mastery.
- **DEGENERATING:** If you take damage from a weapon with this tag, roll Endure+[Domain] at the end of the situation. Failure: mark D6 stress; partial: D4; success: none.
- **DISTRESSING:** When you inflict stress with this item and roll the maximum, mark D6 stress to **Reputation**. *(Heart: Mind. Some weapons make ugly stories.)*
- **DOUBLE-BARRELED:** As Reload, but two uses before reloading.
- **EXPENSIVE:** When you inflict stress with this item and roll the maximum, mark D6 stress to **Gold**.
- **EXTREME RANGE:** Usable at extreme range.
- **LIMITED X:** Usable X times before it gives out.
- **LOUD:** When you inflict stress with this item and roll the maximum, mark D6 stress to **Luck**.
- **MADDENING:** Stress inflicted by this weapon strikes the mind; against player characters it is marked to **Tide**. Against adversaries it has no effect beyond the fiction. *(Shackles definition — Heart uses this tag but never defines it.)*
- **OBSCURING:** The bearer and nearby allies reduce the stress dice of incoming and outgoing ranged weapons by 1 step.
- **ONE-SHOT:** Takes a very long time to prepare; once per situation.
- **PIERCING:** Stress from this equipment can't be reduced by Blood protection, and adversaries don't benefit from their protection value.
- **POINT-BLANK:** As Ranged, but at very close range its stress dice increases by one step; at long range it decreases by one step.
- **POTENT:** When you roll for stress removed with this item, roll two dice and pick the highest. Stacks as per Brutal.
- **RANGED:** Usable at range.
- **RELOAD:** Must be reloaded between uses, giving enemies a chance to close or flee.
- **SMOKE:** As Obscuring, but only when the item is used, and only around where it was used.
- **SPREAD:** Anyone standing near the target on a successful use must roll **Vanish+Domain** (or another applicable skill) to avoid marking stress as well; on a partial success, downgrade the stress dice by one size. NPCs caught in a blast simply take the stress. *(Heart: Evade. Vanish is our dodge — Brave is for standing in it.)*
- **TIRING:** When you fail an action using this equipment, its stress dice decreases by 1 for the remainder of the situation.
- **TRUSTY:** When you roll for stress against a **journey** with this item, roll two dice and pick the highest. Stacks as per Brutal.
- **UNRELIABLE:** When you fail an action using this equipment, it can't be used again for the remainder of the situation (in a landmark) or the journey (at sea).
- **WYRD:** When you inflict stress with this item and roll the maximum, mark D6 stress to **Tide**. *(The name stays; only the track it bites changed.)*

## RESOURCES

Resources are consumable or tradable items of value. The most fundamental resource is coin, but the Shackles runs on barter, and runs on it harder the further from a colonial harbour you sail. **The primary use of resources is to access haunts and remove stress and fallout** (see Ports & Haunts). Some classes also destroy, sacrifice or eat resources of particular domains to fuel abilities — those uses are listed in the individual advances.

**VALUE:** D4 for common goods, D8 for valuable or rare items, D12 for truly remarkable things.

**DOMAIN:** indicates where it's from, who wants it, or what it can fuel. Our nine: Arcane, Colonial, Deepwater, Faith, Outlaw, Port, Ruins, Shallows, Wilderness. Most of the time the domain doesn't matter; some abilities require resources of a particular domain.

**Notation:** *Storm-water from the Eye that never stops spinning (D6, Arcane).*

### Resource Tags

Hints toward what might go wrong while harvesting, hauling or selling. If a character suffers **Luck fallout** while holding a tagged resource, the GM can use the tag as the springboard. These can also be applied to equipment.

**HARMFUL** (hurts its carrier — black magic, contagion, strange energies) · **FRAGILE** (destroyed if dropped or damaged) · **AWKWARD** (heavy, hard to stow) · **DETERIORATING** (rotting, rusting — find a buyer fast) · **TABOO** (most haunts won't take it) · **VOLATILE** (may explode if mistreated) · **MOBILE** (left unattended, it leaves) · **BEACON** (draws something dangerous to it) · **NICHE** (valuable only to a select few).

### Distributing Resources

GM: anything with potential value is a resource — gold, a fabulous diadem, the intestinal tract of a sea-drake — but: (1) if it's valuable, someone or something is probably guarding it; (2) if no one's guarding it, it's probably hard to reach; (3) if it's unguarded and easy to reach, it's probably not very valuable. Calibrate the campaign: **Plentiful** (almost every situation yields something, D6–D8 common), **Standard** (not every situation; D6+ resources that can clear fallout are prized), **Scarce** (resources only come from deliberate action; clearing a BROKEN LEG is a campaign event).
