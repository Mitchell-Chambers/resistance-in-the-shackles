# Part Fifteen — Worked Landmarks

*Ready-to-run places, built to the templates in [Part Eleven](11-ports-havens-and-haunts.md) and [Part Twelve](12-location-tiers.md). Drop them straight onto a map, or read them as worked examples and build your own. Statblocks are deliberately light — a frame for your table, not a gazetteer to memorise. Names and geography are the Pathfinder Golarion Shackles; the detail is yours to bend.*

How to read these: **havens** (friendly ports) carry `DOMAINS / TIER / DEFAULT STRESS / HAUNTS`; **journeys** carry `DOMAINS / TIER / STRESS / RESISTANCE / EVENTS / CHARTED ROUTE`. Haunts list what they refresh and a dice cap. See Part Eleven for how haunts are paid and upgraded, and Part Twelve for what a tier means.

## Havens & Ports

> **PORT PERIL** *(haven — the capital of the lawless)*
> A teeming city poured into a natural harbour, where the Pirate Council sits, every vice has a price, and your name travels faster than your ship. The closest thing the Shackles has to a heart, and it beats on plunder.
> DOMAINS: Port, Outlaw · TIER: 1 · DEFAULT STRESS: D4
> HAUNTS:
> • The Bloody Hand surgery, no questions and no anaesthetic (D8 Blood)
> • Old Gristle's careening yards on the mudflats (D8 Hull)
> • The Council floor, where reputations are made and unmade (D10 Reputation)
> • The Hurricane's Eye gambling pit and worse (D6 Luck, D6 Gold)
> • Mother Sed's, who unknots curses for a price you'll regret (D6 Tide)
> USE: The default home port. Everything can be bought here, which means everyone can find you here. Great for refit and rumour; dangerous for anyone with a bounty.

> **TIDEWATER COVE** *(haven — a small free port)*
> A three-tavern anchorage on a forgotten island, friendly to the Brotherhood and useful precisely because nobody important has heard of it.
> DOMAINS: Port, Shallows · TIER: 1 · DEFAULT STRESS: D4
> HAUNTS:
> • Mother Quay's surgery, kept clean (D6 Blood)
> • The Leeward Shrine to Besmara, salt-stained and patient (D6 Tide)
> • The Commodore's Wake, where stories are currency (D8 Reputation)
> • Hagar's careening beach (D6 Hull)
> USE: A quiet home port for a young crew. Low tier, modest haunts, and an obvious thing to lose when a calling beat or a Luck fallout comes calling.

> **BLOODCOVE** *(haven — but barely)*
> An Aspis Consortium port wearing the mask of legitimate commerce over a throat-deep market in people, ivory, and worse. You can dock and trade — and be watched, taxed, and sold out the whole time.
> DOMAINS: Port, Colonial · TIER: 0–1 · DEFAULT STRESS: D4
> HAUNTS:
> • The Consortium counting-house — anything, for the right paper and the right price (D8 Gold, D8 Colonial goods)
> • A back-room surgeon who reports to the Aspis (D6 Blood — and a Reputation risk)
> • The slave-pens, which a *Freedom* character cannot walk past
> USE: Resupply behind enemy lines. Every haunt here has strings; paying in stress often means paying in Reputation. A natural source of *Freedom* and *Vendetta* hooks, and the home of the Aspis adversaries in Part Fourteen.

## The Wild & the Ruins

> **THE GREEN THROAT** *(journey — island interior delve)*
> The interior of a large jungle island, where the trees close overhead and the old Ghol-Gan stonework starts showing through the roots. Each step inland is a step up the tier (Part Twelve's inland rule).
> DOMAINS: Wilderness, Ruins · TIER: 2–3 · STRESS: D6 → D8 deeper in · RESISTANCE: 12
> EVENTS: A trail that wasn't there yesterday · a predator that has never feared a human ([Part Fourteen](14-adversaries.md)) · fresh water guarded by something · a clearing where the stones are arranged wrong · the bloodfly mangroves.
> CHARTED ROUTE: Map the safe path and find what the interior is hiding — thereafter the sea-leg's landfall reaches the ruin at half resistance.
> USE: The classic two-journey island: a sheltered sea leg, then this, with no ship to hide behind. Home of the Castaway and the Chronicler.

> **THE DROWNED VAULT OF AZLANT** *(landmark — Ghol-Gan / pre-human ruin)*
> A flooded structure older than the Eye, half-sunk off an uncharted reef, its geometry refusing to resolve and its guardian still at its post. What it keeps is worth a fortune and probably a curse.
> DOMAINS: Ruins, Arcane · TIER: 3 · DEFAULT STRESS: D8
> FEATURES: A Ghol-Gan stone sentinel (Part Fourteen), answerable rather than beatable if someone reads the script · a toll the place demands · a relic worth D12 (Ruins) and a Tide problem to match.
> USE: The score at the end of a *Hunger* or *Monument* arc. Spend Tide freely here; let the prize be real and the price realer.

## The Deep & the Eye

> **THE EDGE OF THE EYE** *(journey — the rim of the immortal storm)*
> The permanent hurricane's outer wall, where the weather disobeys the season and ships go to be never seen again — or to take a shortcut no sane captain would risk.
> DOMAINS: Deepwater, Arcane · TIER: 3–4 · STRESS: D8 (Hull; the sea's own work) · RESISTANCE: 16
> EVENTS: A wave that comes from the wrong direction · a calm that shouldn't exist · a ship keeping pace that isn't there · the pull toward the centre · the price the Eye asks of a *Cursed Tides* heart.
> CHARTED ROUTE: Only the Bonuwat *Eye-Reader* knack or a witch's bargain charts this, and never for long — within reach of the Eye, no route stays charted.
> USE: A Tier-4 threshold. Beyond it lies *What the Eye Keeps* (Part Fourteen) and the kind of ending a campaign is built toward. Going in changes you; coming out, more so.

> **CROSSWIND SHOALS** *(journey — reef-maze passage)*
> A lethal shortcut between two anchorages, passable at the right tide and a hull-opener at the wrong one. Wreckers light false beacons on the northern cays and pick the bones.
> DOMAINS: Shallows, Outlaw · TIER: 1–2 · STRESS: D6 · RESISTANCE: 10
> EVENTS: A false beacon · a sudden tide-race · a wrecker ambush · a drifting hulk, recently abandoned (and not empty).
> CHARTED ROUTE: Chart the true passage and silence the false beacons — thereafter, halve this journey's resistance.
> USE: A repeatable early-campaign journey that rewards local knowledge (Shallows) and punishes haste. Turning it into a charted route is a tidy *Monument* or crew win.

## Building your own

Steal the shapes above. A **haven** is two or three domains, a tier, and three to five haunts — at least one for Blood, one for the soul (Tide or Reputation), and one for the ship (Hull). A **journey** is two domains, a tier band, a stress die, a resistance roughly `8 × the legs`, a handful of events drawn from its EVENTS line, and a CHARTED ROUTE goal that, once met, halves it forever. Tier sets the danger and the default stress (Part Twelve); the inland rule turns every island into two journeys stitched at the beach. Populate it from [Part Fourteen](14-adversaries.md), price its recovery from [Part Eleven](11-ports-havens-and-haunts.md), and you have a place to run tonight.
