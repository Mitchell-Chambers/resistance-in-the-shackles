# Part Fourteen — Adversaries

*Everything the Shackles sends against a crew, statted to one simple shape. The ship adversaries in Part Ten are the worked precedent; this part generalises that grammar to people, beasts, and the things beneath the water, and gives you a starting bestiary to raid.*

## How adversaries work

Adversaries do not have character sheets and do not roll dice. An adversary is an **obstacle the fiction pushes back with** — the players act against it, and you read the consequences. Stat any threat with the same fields a ship uses:

- **DIFFICULTY** — the difficulty (Standard / Risky / Dangerous, Part Two) of actions taken *against* it. A drilled marine line is harder to act against than a drunk press-gang.
- **RESISTANCE** — a single pool standing for its capacity and will to keep fighting. The players whittle it down by inflicting stress on it through their successes. When it hits **0**, the thing breaks the way the fiction wants — it surrenders, flees, founders, dies, or is driven off. (This is the same Resistance an enemy ship carries: crew, hull, and nerve, taken together.)
- **PROTECTION** — reduces the stress players inflict on its Resistance, before it's marked. Hard targets (stone guardians, ironclad hulls) have more.
- **STRESS** — the dice it inflicts on the crew, the track(s) it threatens, and any tags. Adversaries deal this through the players' **failed and partial rolls** and through your framing when the crew stalls, hides, or does nothing (Part Two: no rounds, no turns). A `D6 (Blood; Ranged, Reload)` musketeer marks D6 Blood when someone fails to cross its fire-lane — and the `Reload` gives a window to close before it fires again.
- **RESOURCES** — what it yields when beaten (value, domain), per Part Nine.
- **MOVES** *(optional)* — one or two signature pressures: the specific bad things this adversary does, written as fiction with a mechanical edge, exactly like fallout.

**Reading difficulty vs. stress.** Difficulty is *how hard it is to act against them*; stress is *how badly they hurt you when you slip*. A thing can be easy to hit but devastating when it lands (a powder-keg), or nearly untouchable but a slow bleeder (a ghost-light).

**Groups and mobs.** A pack, a squad, or a boarding party is one adversary with one Resistance — don't track bodies. Its stress often gains `Spread` or steps up when the crew is surrounded; its Resistance falls in chunks as the fight turns. A single weak foe (one panicked sailor) usually needs no statline at all: it's a Standard action and a D4.

**Renowned & Legendary.** Most threats are beaten in a scene. A **Legendary** adversary (the last entries here) is a story in itself: a large Resistance, Dangerous difficulty, protection that shrugs ordinary blows, and **moves that change the battlefield**. You do not defeat one in a single roll; you survive it, wear it down across an arc, or pay a calling's price to end it. Treat a Legendary as you would a zenith — the table should feel the weather change when it arrives.

> **Stat ranges, for calibration.** Resistance: a mook 2–3, a tough 4–5, an elite or officer 6–8, a boss 8–10, a Legendary 12–16+. Protection: 0 (soft) to 3 (stone/iron). Stress: D4 rabble, D6 trained, D8 dangerous, D10 monstrous. Adjust to the moment — these are instincts, not laws.

## People of the Inner Sea

> **CHELISH NAVAL OFFICER** *(adversary)*
> Pressed, pale, and certain of his righteousness; he has a devil's contract where his conscience should be and a line of marines at his back.
> DIFFICULTY: Risky · RESISTANCE: 6 · PROTECTION: 1
> STRESS: D6 (Blood from his blade; Reputation when he dresses you down in front of your own crew)
> RESOURCES: Sealed orders and a signet of rank (D6, Colonial); a fine duelling sword (D8, Hunt)
> MOVES: **By the book.** While he stands and gives orders, nearby marines act at one step better. Cut him out of the fight — kill, cow, or disgrace him — and the line wavers.

> **COLONIAL MARINES** *(adversary — squad)*
> A disciplined firing line in red coats, more frightening together than the sum of frightened men.
> DIFFICULTY: Standard · RESISTANCE: 5 · PROTECTION: 0
> STRESS: D6 (Blood; Ranged, Reload, Spread)
> RESOURCES: Powder, shot, and bayonets (D6, Outlaw); a regimental colours worth a fortune in shame (D8, Colonial)
> MOVES: **Volley.** When the crew is caught in the open, every marine fires at once — `Spread` catches anyone near the target. Then `Reload` opens the door: close the distance before the next volley or pay for it.

> **ASPIS CONSORTIUM ENFORCER** *(adversary)*
> The Consortium does not forgive a debt; it sends this instead — a professional with a ledger for a heart and instructions to make an example.
> DIFFICULTY: Standard · RESISTANCE: 5 · PROTECTION: 1
> STRESS: D6 (Blood; the first strike is `Brutal` — he plans his violence)
> RESOURCES: A writ of collection naming someone aboard (D8, Colonial); a purse of bribe-money (D6, Port)
> MOVES: **It's only business.** He would rather you paid. He'll offer terms once; refuse them and the price goes up, in Gold or in Blood.

> **ASPIS FACTOR** *(adversary — social)*
> No blade at all: a smiling man with a ledger, a warehouse, and the patience to ruin you legally.
> DIFFICULTY: Risky · RESISTANCE: 6 · PROTECTION: 0
> STRESS: D6 (Reputation and Gold — undercut prices, called debts, doors quietly closed)
> RESOURCES: A ledger of who owes whom across three ports (D10, Port); a strongbox key (D8, Colonial)
> MOVES: **The long column.** Every slight is written down. Cross him and a Gold or Reputation fallout you suffer later traces back to a line in his book.

> **RAHADOUMI PURE LEGION ZEALOT** *(adversary)*
> The godless republic sends its faithful-to-no-god to drown the divine wherever it surfaces, and the Shackles is thick with gods.
> DIFFICULTY: Risky · RESISTANCE: 5 · PROTECTION: 1
> STRESS: D6 (Blood; against the Sin Bosun, the Tidecaller, and the god-touched, steps to D8)
> RESOURCES: Manacles forged to bind a caster's hands (D6, Outlaw); a list of marked heretics (D8, Faith)
> MOVES: **No gods here.** While she presses you, divine and pact-born abilities are Risky to use — she is trained to break exactly that kind of power.

> **PRESS-GANG** *(adversary — mob)*
> Crimps and bravos with saps and a waiting longboat, who want you breathing and below-decks by morning.
> DIFFICULTY: Standard · RESISTANCE: 4 · PROTECTION: 0
> STRESS: D6 (Blood, but they pull blows — they're paid by the live head; a partial means you wake up at sea, not dead)
> RESOURCES: A captain's bounty per warm body (D6, Port); the keys to a waiting boat (D4, Shallows)
> MOVES: **Take him alive.** They fight to capture, not kill. Going down to them is its own story — the *Indentured* or *Seized* fallout made flesh.

> **SEA-WITCH** *(adversary)*
> She trades in storm-debts and keeps the ocean's confidence. She would rather make a bargain than a corpse — but she can make either.
> DIFFICULTY: Risky · RESISTANCE: 6 · PROTECTION: 1
> STRESS: D8 (Tide; her workings carry `Wyrd` — roll the max and the deep takes a deeper interest in you)
> RESOURCES: A jar of bottled wind (D8, Arcane); a debt-token good for one weather-working (D10, Arcane, Taboo)
> MOVES: **The offer.** She names a price — a memory, a name, a year — for what you want. Taking it is a Tide problem you'll meet again. **The tide turns.** Once, she calls the water against you: a Hull or Blood crisis, the GM's framing.

## Beasts of the Shackles

> **REEF SHARK PACK** *(adversary — mob)*
> Drawn by blood in the water, and there is always blood in the water.
> DIFFICULTY: Standard · RESISTANCE: 5 · PROTECTION: 0
> STRESS: D6 in the water (Blood; `Spread` — they take anyone bleeding nearby); D4 at the rail
> RESOURCES: Jaws, hide, and oil (D6, Wilderness, Deteriorating)
> MOVES: **Frenzy.** Every time a crewmate marks Blood in the water, the pack's next stress steps up. Get out of the sea, or stop bleeding into it.

> **REEF LURKER** *(adversary — giant moray / ambush predator)*
> A length of muscle and teeth in a hole in the coral, patient as the reef itself.
> DIFFICULTY: Risky · RESISTANCE: 4 · PROTECTION: 1
> STRESS: D8 (Blood; `Piercing` — its bite ignores armour and grip)
> RESOURCES: A pearl from its lair, and the diver who didn't come back for it (D8, Shallows)
> MOVES: **The strike.** It does not chase. It waits, and the first to reach into its world pays the full price — surprise, and `Piercing`.

> **BRINE-SCUTTLER** *(adversary — giant crab)*
> Hull-cracking claws and a shell that turns a cutlass, scuttling sideways out of the surf.
> DIFFICULTY: Standard · RESISTANCE: 6 · PROTECTION: 2
> STRESS: D6 (Blood; `Brutal` on the claw — roll two dice, keep the highest)
> RESOURCES: Sweet meat enough to feed a crew (D6, Wilderness); a shell that makes good armour-plate (D8, Shallows, Awkward)
> MOVES: **Carapace.** Its protection 2 shrugs ordinary blows; you need `Piercing`, a clever angle, or to flip it before it opens you up.

> **JUNGLE PREDATOR** *(adversary — dire cat / great boar / island megafauna)*
> The interior keeps things that have never learned to fear a human, because the humans here have always been the prey.
> DIFFICULTY: Risky · RESISTANCE: 6 · PROTECTION: 1
> STRESS: D8 (Blood; `Brutal`)
> RESOURCES: Hide, tusk, and a name worth telling (D8, Wilderness)
> MOVES: **Its ground.** Inland (Tier 2+), it acts at one step better — it knows the dark and the dense green, and you do not.

> **BLOODFLY SWARM** *(adversary — vermin)*
> A humming cloud off the mangroves that wants what's in your veins and leaves the fever behind.
> DIFFICULTY: Standard · RESISTANCE: 3 · PROTECTION: 0
> STRESS: D4 (Blood; `Degenerating` — the fever works after, at the end of the situation)
> RESOURCES: — (you do not loot a swarm; you survive it)
> MOVES: **The fever after.** Anyone bitten rolls Endure+[Domain] at the situation's end or marks more Blood — the bite is nothing; the disease is the threat.

> **GREAT SQUID** *(adversary — kraken-spawn)*
> Forty feet of arm and a beak that takes a longboat in one bite, rising out of black water with terrible curiosity.
> DIFFICULTY: Dangerous · RESISTANCE: 9 · PROTECTION: 1
> STRESS: D8 (Hull when it takes the ship; Blood when it takes the crew — GM's call per stakes)
> RESOURCES: Ink, oil, and a beak the size of a man's chest (D10, Deepwater, Beacon)
> MOVES: **Grapple.** It seizes the ship or a sailor; until the arm is cut (a focused effort), it drags, and the stress repeats through results. **Sounding.** It can break off and vanish into the deep — and decide to come back.

## The Drowned & the Deep

> **THE DROWNED** *(adversary — undead mob)*
> The crew of a wreck, still wearing their work, walking the sea-floor or hauling themselves up the anchor-chain, patient and unhurried and wrong.
> DIFFICULTY: Standard · RESISTANCE: 6 · PROTECTION: 1
> STRESS: D6 (Blood from cold hands; D6 Tide for what it does to a living mind to fight your own dead shipmates)
> RESOURCES: The wreck's manifest, barnacled but legible (D8, Ruins); coin that no living mint struck (D6, Outlaw, Taboo)
> MOVES: **They do not stop.** Stress does not rout them; only destroying the Resistance or removing the cause (the thing that raised them) ends it. They have all the time in the drowned world.

> **GHOST-LIGHT** *(adversary — lure)*
> A soft light on the water or in the rigging, and a voice you are almost sure you know, leading sweetly toward the rocks.
> DIFFICULTY: Risky · RESISTANCE: 4 · PROTECTION: 0
> STRESS: D6 (Tide; `Maddening` — it strikes the mind, marked to Tide; it does not bleed you, it *calls* you)
> RESOURCES: — (it leaves nothing but the wreck of those who followed it)
> MOVES: **Follow me.** Each time the crew fails to resist it, the ship drifts one step toward a reef, a fog, a drop. It wins by being followed, not by fighting.

> **GHOL-GAN STONE SENTINEL** *(adversary — construct)*
> A cyclops-carved guardian that has stood in the dark since before the Eye, and has not been told its watch is over.
> DIFFICULTY: Dangerous · RESISTANCE: 8 · PROTECTION: 3
> STRESS: D8 (Blood; `Brutal`, `Piercing` — a stone fist does not care about your guard)
> RESOURCES: The relic it guards (D12, Ruins); its still-humming heart-stone (D10, Arcane, Harmful)
> MOVES: **Implacable.** Protection 3 turns aside anything but the cleverest or the most `Piercing` strike — fight the room (the pillars, the floodgate, the old mechanism), not the statue. **Condition of passage.** Many sentinels can be *answered* rather than fought, if someone reads the Ghol-Gan and knows the toll.

> **TRENCH-THING** *(adversary — lesser deep horror)*
> Something from where the water goes black and the pressure would fold a ship flat. It should not be up here. It is up here for you.
> DIFFICULTY: Dangerous · RESISTANCE: 8 · PROTECTION: 1
> STRESS: D8 (Tide; `Wyrd`, `Maddening` — it marks the body *and* the mind, both to Tide)
> RESOURCES: A scale, a tooth, an organ that keeps moving (D10, Deepwater, Taboo, Beacon)
> MOVES: **Wrongness.** Its mere presence marks Tide on those who look too long. **It knows your name** if any character has ever taken Tide fallout near the deep — and it uses it.

> **REVENANT OF THE CODE** *(adversary — vengeful dead)*
> A pirate murdered against the Code, risen to collect — not from you, necessarily, but from someone, and you are in the way until the account is settled.
> DIFFICULTY: Risky · RESISTANCE: 6 · PROTECTION: 1
> STRESS: D6 (Blood; D6 Reputation — it names your crimes aloud where the crew can hear)
> RESOURCES: Proof of an old murder the Free Captains would act on (D10, Outlaw)
> MOVES: **The grievance.** It cannot be permanently put down until its wrong is righted — give it justice and it rests; refuse and it returns, stronger, at the worst time.

## Legendary Threats

*Each of these is an arc, not an encounter. Run them like weather that has decided to hate you.*

> **OLD SORROW** *(legendary adversary — leviathan)*
> The whalers gave it the name before it ate the last of them. It is older than the Eye, the size of an island that swims, and it remembers every harpoon. When it surfaces, harbours empty.
> DIFFICULTY: Dangerous · RESISTANCE: 16 · PROTECTION: 2
> STRESS: D10 (Hull as it breaches beneath you; Blood as the deck pitches and the water takes the unlucky)
> RESOURCES: Enough ambergris, bone, and oil to make a crew rich for life (D12 ×3, Deepwater); a harpoon from a ship a century gone, still in its hide (D10, Renowned)
> MOVES: **Sounding and breaching.** It cannot be fought toe-to-toe; it submerges and returns where you are weakest. Survive its passes, lure it onto a reef, or pay a zenith to end it. **The wake.** Its mere passage is a Hull crisis; being near it when it dives is a Luck reckoning for every audacious soul aboard.

> **WHAT THE EYE KEEPS** *(legendary adversary — storm-bound horror)*
> Inside the immortal hurricane, in the impossible calm at its centre, something waits that the Eye was perhaps built to cage — or built around. To reach it is to leave the world you can come back from.
> DIFFICULTY: Dangerous · RESISTANCE: 14 · PROTECTION: 2
> STRESS: D10 (Tide; `Wyrd`, `Maddening` — everything it does is marked to Tide; it does not wound, it *unmakes*)
> RESOURCES: A truth that reorders the Shackles (D12, Arcane, Harmful); whatever you went into the Eye to find, now changed
> MOVES: **The calm.** In its presence, Tide fallout escalates one step. **It offers.** Like the sea-witch but vaster — it will give you anything, for everything. This is where a *Cursed Tides* character's road ends, one way or the other.

> **THE LORD-ADMIRAL'S HOUND** *(legendary adversary — pirate-hunter)*
> Cheliax tired of losing prizes and sent a professional: a cold lord-admiral with a fast, over-gunned frigate, a devil's patience, and your flag pinned to a chart in her cabin. She is hunting *you*, specifically, and she does not stop.
> DIFFICULTY: Dangerous · RESISTANCE: 12 · PROTECTION: 2 *(the ship; see Part Ten for the engagement)*
> STRESS: D10 (Hull at sea — *Disciplined Gunnery*, she Presses the Advantage even on your partials; Blood and Reputation ashore, where her agents and her warrants reach)
> RESOURCES: A famous warship, if you can take her (Renowned); the lord-admiral's commission and the names of everyone she's hunting (D12, Colonial)
> MOVES: **Relentless.** Escape her and she finds you again — closing ports, buying allies, shrinking the map (the Piracy "Hunt" campaign arc made flesh). **The reckoning.** She is the kind of enemy a crew's whole story can be about, and the kind whose defeat is a campaign zenith.

---

*A general bestiary is meant to be raided and rewritten — change a track, swap a tag, rename the beast, and it's yours. When you need something not here, build it to the shape in "How adversaries work" above and the stat ranges in the calibration box. The Shackles always has one more thing under the keel.*
