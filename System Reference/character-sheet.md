### *Resistance in the Shackles* (Heart-based pirate RPG) — designer build brief

Paste everything below the line into a layout/design tool. It is written as direct build instructions for an aged-parchment / nautical character sheet, populated entirely with the *Resistance in the Shackles* labels and content.

---

## 0. THE JOB

Build a **two-page, print-focused, fill-by-hand tabletop RPG character sheet**. Static layout (not interactive — no clickable fields), designed to be printed on **US Letter (8.5 × 11 in), portrait**, and filled in with a pen. It must mirror the structure of the *Heart: the City Beneath* sheet — same regions in the same places — but with a pirate / nautical "aged sea-chart" aesthetic and the labels listed in this brief.

Deliver it as a clean, print-ready layout with crisp lines, generous write-in space, and high contrast so it photocopies and prints in grayscale without losing legibility.

---

## 1. VISUAL STYLE — "AGED SEA CHART"

Reskin Heart's torn-paper grunge into a pirate world. Keep Heart's *bones* (banner headers, panelled regions, checkbox tracks); change the *skin*.

**Palette**
- Background "paper": warm aged parchment, `#EFE6D2` to `#E4D6B8`, with a faint mottled/foxed texture and subtly darkened, irregular edges (like a chart that's been at sea).
- Primary ink / body text & checkboxes: dark sepia-black, `#2B2118`.
- Header / accent: deep oxblood red, `#7A1F12` (this is the equivalent of Heart's red — use it for all banner titles and section labels).
- Secondary accent for hairlines and rules: faded brown, `#9B7E55`.
- Everything must remain legible printed in pure black-and-white; treat colour as a bonus, not load-bearing.

**Typography**
- Section headers / banners: a bold condensed serif or a weathered slab/"woodcut" display face, all-caps, letter-spaced — evoking carved nameplates and broadside type. (Heart uses a heavy condensed sans; the pirate equivalent is a chunky engraved serif.)
- Field labels: same family, smaller, all-caps.
- Handwritten-style accents (the two italic prompts on page 2): a flowing script/italic, in oxblood.
- Body/write-in areas: leave blank with light ruled lines for handwriting.

**Texture & motifs (subtle, never busy)**
- Torn / darkened panel edges, like Heart, but reading as aged vellum rather than torn newsprint.
- Banner headers rendered as small ribbon/plank/scroll labels (Heart pins its banners with a tack; here use a tied-rope, nail, or wax-seal motif).
- A faint compass rose watermark behind the NOTES area; a thin rope or chain border framing each page is optional and must stay low-contrast.
- Do not let any texture reduce the contrast of text or checkboxes.

**Layout discipline**
- Tight, panelled, single-page-dense like Heart — every region is a defined panel with a header.
- Honour a ~0.4 in print margin; keep all content inside it.

## 2. PAGE 1 — LAYOUT MAP

Same arrangement as the Heart sheet. Page 1 is two columns. Left column ≈ 58% width, right column ≈ 42%.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  TITLE BLOCK (top): "RESISTANCE IN THE SHACKLES" wordmark     │
│  upper area; small tagline beneath.                          │
├───────────────────────────────┬─────────────────────────────┤
│ IDENTITY                       │ ACTIVE BEATS                │
│  Name ____________             │  (2–3 ruled write-in lines) │
│  Class ____________            │                             │
│  Calling ____________          ├─────────────────────────────┤
│                                │  ╓── ABILITIES ──╖ (banner)  │
│ RESISTANCES (5 tracks) +       │                             │
│   PROTECTION column            │  Large blank panel for      │
│                                │  writing class/calling      │
│ SKILLS | KNACKS  ·  DOMAINS |  │  abilities (fills most of   │
│   KNACKS  (two paired columns) │  the right column).         │
│                                │                             │
│           ╓── FALLOUT ──╖      │                             │
├───────────────────────────────┴─────────────────────────────┤
│ EQUIPMENT (ruled lines)        │ RESOURCES (ruled lines)     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### 2.1 Title block
Top of page: the wordmark **RESISTANCE IN THE SHACKLES** (display face, oxblood), with a small italic tagline beneath, e.g. *"liberty, and the reckoning that follows."* Keep it compact — Heart's logo sits top-right; here center or right-align the wordmark so the identity fields start cleanly below.

### 2.2 Identity block (upper left)
Three labelled write-in lines, stacked, labels in oxblood caps:
- **NAME**
- **CLASS**
- **CALLING**

(Do **not** add other identity fields — mirror Heart exactly.)

### 2.3 Resistances (left, below identity)
Five resistance tracks, one per row. Each row: the **label** (oxblood caps), then a horizontal row of **10 stress checkboxes**, and to the right under a small **PROTECTION** header, a group of **5 protection checkboxes**. Identical structure to Heart's Blood/Mind/Echo/Fortune/Supplies block — only the names change.

The five tracks, in this order:

| # | Label | (what it is — for your reference, do not print) |
|---|-------|--------------------------------------------------|
| 1 | **BLOOD** | the body; physical harm |
| 2 | **REPUTATION** | your name, standing, the story told about you |
| 3 | **TIDE** | the supernatural toll; the sea's attention |
| 4 | **LUCK** | fortune, providence, the wrong turn |
| 5 | **GOLD** | purchasing power, supplies, coin |

Layout note: a single **PROTECTION** column header sits above the protection box groups (as on Heart), aligned to the right of the stress rows.

### 2.4 Skills & Domains (left, below resistances)
Two side-by-side paired columns, exactly like Heart's `SKILLS | KNACKS` and `DOMAINS | KNACKS`. Each entry is a **checkbox + the name**, with a **ruled KNACK write-in line** to its right.

> ⚠️ **Count difference from Heart — plan the columns for this.** This system has **11 skills** (Heart has 9) and **9 domains** (Heart has 8). Size the type and row spacing so all 11 + 9 rows fit cleanly in the two columns without crowding. The skills column will be a little taller than the domains column; balance the block visually.

### 2.5 Fallout banner
A small ribbon/scroll banner reading **FALLOUT**, positioned at the foot of the skills/domains block (where Heart places its FALLOUT tab). It can sit over a thin divider; no write-in lines are required beneath it (matches Heart — it's a labelled tab marking the boundary).

### 2.6 Active Beats (top right)
Header **ACTIVE BEATS** (oxblood caps) with **2–3 ruled write-in lines** beneath. This is where the player notes their currently active calling beats.

### 2.7 Abilities (right, large)
A ribbon banner **ABILITIES**, then a **large blank panel** filling most of the right column — generous open space for hand-writing class and calling abilities. This is the single biggest write-in area on page 1. Keep it mostly blank (optionally faint ruled lines).

### 2.8 Equipment & Resources (bottom band, full width)
Two panels across the bottom, side by side:
- **EQUIPMENT** (left) — header + ~5 ruled write-in lines.
- **RESOURCES** (right) — header + ~5 ruled write-in lines.

## 3. PAGE 2 — LAYOUT MAP

Mirrors Heart's second page.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┬───────────────────────────┐
│ ╓ HISTORY AND RELATIONSHIPS ╖    │  ┌──────────────────────┐ │
│  (ruled write-in area)           │  │  PORTRAIT BOX        │ │
│                                  │  │  italic script:      │ │
│                                  │  │ "Reminder: this is   │ │
│ ╓ BONDS ╖                        │  │  what I look like"   │ │
│  (ruled write-in area, room      │  └──────────────────────┘ │
│   for several named bonds)       │                           │
│                                  │   NOTES                   │
│  ┌── ROLL RESULTS ──┐            │   (large ruled / blank    │
│  │  d10 reference    │           │    write-in area, fills   │
│  │  (the dice table) │           │    the rest of the page)  │
│  └───────────────────┘           │                           │
└─────────────────────────────────┴───────────────────────────┘
```

### 3.1 History and Relationships (top left)
Ribbon banner **HISTORY AND RELATIONSHIPS** with a ruled write-in area beneath (several lines).

### 3.2 Portrait box (top right)
A pinned "photo/sketch" frame (aged, slightly askew, held by a nail or wax seal). Inside, in **flowing oxblood script**, the prompt: *"Reminder: this is what I look like."* Leave the interior blank for a drawing.

### 3.3 Bonds (mid left)
Ribbon banner **BONDS** with a ruled write-in area — room to name several relationships/crewmates.

### 3.4 Roll Results reference (lower left)
A **d10 dice-shaped reference panel** (Heart renders it as a die with "ROLL RESULTS" across it). Use a ten-sided-die silhouette in oxblood with the label **ROLL RESULTS** across its face, surrounded by the result table. Print these exact entries (this is the standard Heart d10 resolution, which this system keeps):

- **10 — Critical Success:** Step up your outgoing stress dice.
- **8–9 — Success:** No stress.
- **6–7 — Success at a cost:** Take stress.
- **2–5 — Failure:** Take stress.
- **1 — Critical Fail:** Take double stress.

(Arrange around the die as Heart does: crit success and crit fail flanking the top, the three middle bands below.)

### 3.5 Notes (right, large)
Header **NOTES** with a large open write-in area (faint ruled lines or blank) filling the remainder of the page. A faint compass-rose watermark may sit behind it at very low contrast.

## 4. PRODUCTION / PRINT SPECS

- **Page size:** US Letter, 8.5 × 11 in, portrait, two pages.
- **Margins:** ~0.4 in safe margin; keep all text and checkboxes inside it.
- **Checkboxes:** square, hand-fillable (~3–3.5 mm), clearly outlined in sepia-black; even spacing within each track.
- **Ruled lines:** thin faded-brown hairlines, spaced for adult handwriting (~7–8 mm).
- **Contrast:** all labels, checkboxes, and rules must survive grayscale printing and photocopying. Texture and colour are decorative only.
- **Fonts:** embed or use widely-available faces; prefer a weathered slab/engraved serif for display and a clean serif/sans for labels.
- **No interactivity:** static art only — this is meant to be printed and filled by hand.
