# Cohesion Gap Analysis — What the System Reference Still Needs

*A structural comparison of the System Reference against the chapter inventory of* Heart: The City Beneath *— the system this game is built on — to find what is missing before the master reads as a complete, self-contained game. This maps subsystem **categories**; all content written to fill a gap is original Shackles material, never imported text.*

The earlier [`heart_rules_gap_analysis.md`](../../Game%20System/System%20Documents/heart_rules_gap_analysis.md) (vocabulary, equipment, resources, tiers) is now **largely resolved** — its findings were folded into Parts Two, Four, Eight, Ten, and Eleven. This document covers what remains at the *chapter* level.

## The gaps, prioritized

### 1. Ancestries — *missing, high priority*

Heart makes ancestry a pillar of character creation, and this project already has a full [Ancestry Design Guide](../../Game%20System/ancestry_design_guide.md) — but **not one ancestry has been written**. Part Seven asks the player to choose one and there is nothing to choose from. Ancestries are deliberately **mechanics-free** (lore block + three background questions + names + a d20 trinkets table), so this is pure writing, not balancing.

**Recommended set** (drawn from the Shackles' web of power, per the guide): a Shackles-born "child of the islands"; a dispossessed/colonised people (e.g. Mwangi/Garundi coast); a Chelish defector or exile; a Mordant Spire or Sodden Lands refugee; the non-human peoples the guide assumes (half-orcs, the sahuagin-adjacent or other sea-folk, tieflings of Chelish blood). Target **6–8 ancestries** to match the class/calling breadth. → would become **Part Seven-A** or a `07-ancestries/` folder.

### 2. Running the Game — *missing, high priority*

There is no GM chapter. GM guidance currently lives scattered in asides (tier default stress, journey framing, "the GM frames it"). A cohesive game needs one consolidated **Part Twelve — Running the Game** covering: the GM's job and principles; **assigning stress and choosing the track**; setting difficulty (Standard/Risky/Dangerous); **framing fallout** as fiction; pacing the doom (how Luck and the callings' Downfalls are meant to land); building landmarks, haunts, and journeys from the templates; **session zero** (crew, ship, home port, tone, safety tools); and running engagements. Much of this can be assembled from rulings already implicit across Parts Two, Four, Nine–Eleven — it needs *gathering and front-matter*, not invention.

### 3. Adversaries & a Shackles Bestiary — *missing, medium-high*

Part Nine stats **enemy ships** as adversaries and gives the adversary statblock shape (Resistance, protection, difficulty, stress, resources). But there is no general **adversary chapter** for people and creatures, and no **bestiary**. A cohesive game needs: the adversary rules generalized off the ship case (one Resistance, protection, difficulty, stress dice, tags, resources-when-defeated), plus a **starter bestiary** of ~15–25 Shackles threats — colonial marines and a Chelish officer, an Aspis enforcer, a sea-witch, a Ghol-Gan guardian/construct, the undead of a drowned wreck, sharks and a krakenish thing, jungle megafauna, a rival pirate crew — and a couple of **legendary** threats (a named leviathan, something the Eye keeps). → **Part Thirteen — Adversaries**, with original creatures.

### 4. Healing & character-scale combat — *covered but scattered, medium*

Both exist but are spread thin:

- **Healing** is defined in Part Four (clearing each track), Part Eight (Tend gear), and Part Ten (Tend rolls, haunts, bonds). It works, but a player can't find "how do I heal?" in one place. *Fix:* a short consolidating section (in Part Two or Four) that points to all three, or a one-page "Recovery" summary.
- **Combat at character scale** is implied by the core loop (no rounds, stress on fail) and the tags, but there is no section that says, in one place, how a fight *runs*: range and positioning as fiction, taking an NPC out (their Resistance hitting 0), surrender and capture, ganging up, fighting hurt. Heart devotes a short chapter to this. *Fix:* a one-page "Combat" section in Part Two.

### 5. Worked examples & gazetteer — *light, medium*

Parts Ten and Eleven give the landmark/journey/haunt **templates** and one or two inline examples, but there is no set of **worked Shackles landmarks** — Port Peril, Quent, Bloodcove, a Ghol-Gan ruin, the Eye's edge — statted out as ready-to-use havens, haunts, and journeys. Likewise Part One is a tight setting primer with no **gazetteer**. Neither is strictly required to play, but both are what turn a rules document into a *campaign* you can run tonight. → a `gazetteer/` section or an expansion of Part Ten with examples.

### 6. Rules Summary / quick reference — *missing, low*

A single **one-page cheat sheet** — the roll and result band, difficulty, marking stress, the fallout check, the four gates, and the d10 result table — would serve play and double as the back page of any shareable edition. Cheap to produce from Part Two.

### 7. Character sheet in the tree — *low*

The character sheet exists as a build brief in the *Personal* project folder, outside this repo. For cohesion, the finished sheet (or a link to it) should live with the reference, referenced from Part Seven.

---

## Suggested build order

1. **Ancestries** (unblocks Part Seven; pure writing).
2. **Running the Game** (the biggest cohesion win; mostly gathering existing rulings).
3. **Adversaries & bestiary** (makes the game runnable against opposition).
4. **Combat + Healing consolidation** and the **quick-reference page** (small, high-utility).
5. **Worked landmarks / gazetteer** (turns rules into a campaign).
6. **Character sheet** into the tree.

None of these require changing existing rules — they are additive. With items 1–3 written, the System Reference would stand on its own as a complete game rather than a rules-and-character toolkit that leans on Heart for the GM half.
