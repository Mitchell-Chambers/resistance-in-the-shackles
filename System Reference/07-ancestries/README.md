# Part Seven — Ancestries

Your ancestry is who you are *before the dice hit the table*. It tells the crew where you come from, how the world has treated you, and what ghosts you carry onto a pirate deck. It is the third pillar of a character, beside your **Class** (what you can do) and your **Calling** (why you sail) — and unlike those two, it is **purely narrative**.

> **An ancestry answers: who are you? Where do you come from? Why are you here? It never answers: what can you do?**

## Ancestries grant no mechanics

No bonuses, no abilities, no protection, no modifiers — none. The storm does not care whether you are a Bonuwat free-diver or a Chelish defector; the sea drowns everyone the same. What an ancestry gives you is *texture*: a place in the Shackles' web of power, a reason you ended up at the edge of the world, and a fistful of objects that imply a life. Everything you can *do* comes from your class and your calling. This is a deliberate inheritance from the Resistance system: a question about *who you are* should never become a question about *which combination is strongest*.

## How to use an ancestry

At character creation (Part Eight, Step 4):

1. **Read** your ancestry's lore.
2. **Answer one** of its three background questions, out loud, at the table. Each question names real places, factions, and people of the Shackles — engaging with the world is the point.
3. **Choose a name** (each ancestry lists examples and how names work).
4. **Roll twice on the d20 trinkets table** and keep both. They are not equipment — they are evidence.

## The Set

The Shackles is a contested archipelago in the Pathfinder *Golarion* setting, off the western coast of Garund and under the shadow of the Eye of Abendego. Its people are everyone the Inner Sea spat out, fled, or could not hold. This opening set covers the breadth of who ends up sailing under a black flag:

| Ancestry | In a line | Stands where, in the web of power |
|---|---|---|
| **[Shackleborn](shackleborn.md)** | Born to the islands, of every blood and none | The sea is home, not escape |
| **[Bonuwat](bonuwat.md)** | The Mwangi coast's own seafarers | Indigenous to these waters; dispossessed by everyone who charts them |
| **[Chelaxian](chelaxian.md)** | Children of the empire, turned against it | Colonial and privileged origin, now in exile |
| **[Half-Orc](half-orc.md)** | Belonging nowhere on land, welcome on a deck | Stateless and outlawed by birth |
| **[Halfling](halfling.md)** | Escaped the Chelish house that owned them | Freed, and never going back |
| **[Tengu](tengu.md)** | Wandering crow-folk, born to the rigging | A diaspora at home on any ship |

## Building your own — and what's coming

Ancestries are mechanics-free, so making one with your GM is easy: follow the [Ancestry Design Guide](../../Game%20System/ancestry_design_guide.md) (lore block · three world-anchored questions · names · a d20 trinkets table). Strong candidates to add next, all canonical to the Shackles: **Gillman** (the drowned-Azlanti remnant of the deep — a natural fit for the Tide and *Cursed Tides*), **Tiefling** (Cheliax's diabolic legacy worn on the skin), **inland Mwangi** and **Grippli** of the Sodden Lands, **Undine** of the water-touched bloodlines, and the **Sahuagin-touched** sea-folk of the trenches.
