# Half-Orc

You were born from a violence or a love that the respectable world cannot bear to look at, and it has been punishing you for the fact of your existence since before you could speak. On land, in the cities of the Inner Sea, a half-orc is a thing to be watched, taxed twice, hired last and blamed first — too orc for the humans, too human for the orcs, welcome nowhere a roof keeps off the rain. And then you found the deck of a ship, where the only questions that matter are *can you haul, can you fight, will you hold the line* — and the answer to all three was yes, and for the first time in your life a crew looked at your tusks and your shoulders and saw an asset instead of an omen. The Shackles did not make you welcome out of kindness. It made you welcome because it needed you, which is the closest thing to belonging you have ever been offered, and you took it with both hands.

## Background Questions

- You were muscle for a crew or a gang somewhere on the Inner Sea coast — perhaps in the workhouses of **Cheliax** or the docks of a colonial port — until you decided your strength would only ever work for *you* again. Who did you break with to get free, and are they still looking?
- You grew up in the Shackles itself, one of the few who never had to learn that the world hates you, because the crew that raised you never taught you that. Who were they, and what happened to make you sail alone?
- You are trying to prove something — to a parent, to a city, to yourself — that a half-orc can be more than the fist people reach for. What's the thing you're chasing, and what will it take before you believe you've done it?

## Names

Half-orc names run to two patterns: a hard, plain name meant to sound human and unremarkable, or a defiant one — a deed-name, an insult reclaimed, a thing said with the chin up. Examples: *Grust, Mara, Oggun Pier-Breaker, Vell, Sister Tongue-of-Iron, Dado.*

> A half-orc raised among humans often carries a deliberately forgettable name. One raised among a crew often carries the name the crew gave them — and wears it like a flag.

| 14 | A tooth that isn't yours, taken from someone who learned not to test you twice |
