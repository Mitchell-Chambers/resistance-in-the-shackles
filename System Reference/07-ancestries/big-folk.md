# Big Folk

*A kindred, not one people: half-orcs, minotaurs, hobgoblins, and the other large, strong, martial folk the Inner Sea greets with a drawn weapon — and a job offer.*

You are one of the big peoples, built on a scale the polite world finds threatening and the working world finds invaluable. Maybe you are a **half-orc**, born from a violence or a love the cities cannot bear to look at, too orc for the humans and too human for the orcs, welcome nowhere a roof keeps off the rain. Maybe you are a **minotaur**, raised among the old labyrinth-ruins, carrying a strength and a shame and a sense of direction that never fails even when everything else does. Maybe you are a **hobgoblin**, bred for a war-society of iron discipline and brutal order, who looked at the rank ahead of you and the chain around your neck and decided to desert the whole arrangement. What you share is that the Inner Sea taught you your body was a problem to be managed — and then you found a deck, where the only questions are *can you haul, can you fight, will you hold the line*, and for the first time the answer being yes made you an asset instead of an omen.

## Background Questions

- You were muscle for someone — a Chelish workhouse, a hobgoblin legion, a gang on the docks of a colonial port — until you decided your strength would only ever work for *you* again. Who did you break with to get free, and are they still looking?
- You carry a discipline or a code from where you came up — a soldier's order, a labyrinth-keeper's patience, a half-orc's hard-won restraint — that the chaos of piracy tests every single day. What is it, and what happens on the day it finally breaks?
- You are trying to prove something — that you are more than the fist people reach for, more than the monster the stories promised. What's the thing you're chasing, and what will it take before you believe you've done it?

## Names

Big Folk names run hard and plain, or defiantly proud: half-orcs take a forgettable human name to pass or a reclaimed deed-name to stop passing; minotaurs carry a birth-name and a place-name from the maze that raised them; hobgoblins are named by rank and unit until they shed it for one of their own. Examples across the kindred: *Grust, Oggun Pier-Breaker, Mara, Korthax of the Sunken Maze, Vell, Iron-Twelve "Twelve."*

> A Big Folk raised among humans often carries a deliberately unremarkable name; one raised among a crew often wears the name the crew gave them, like a flag. A deserter hobgoblin's first free act is usually choosing a name that is not a number.

## Trinkets

*Roll twice on a d20 and keep both.*

| d20 | Trinket |
|---|---|
| 1 | A filed-down tusk, sawn horn-tip, or cut rank-braid — a piece of yourself you removed to pass, and kept because you stopped |
| 2 | A workhouse token, slave-tally, or legion service-disc, the number still legible, that you've never been able to throw away |
| 3 | A belaying pin worn smooth as a handle, that has settled more arguments than the captain |
| 4 | A child's toy you carved for someone, never delivered |
| 5 | A list of everyone who ever called you a thing instead of a name, kept for no reason you'll admit |
| 6 | A surprisingly delicate piece of scrimshaw or knotwork, your own work, that no one believes you made |
| 7 | A field-manual, drill-book, or labyrinth-map you were trained on, annotated in the margins by a younger, truer believer |
| 8 | A big ugly animal that chose you and will not be un-chosen (name it) |
| 9 | A medal or commendation from a force that then refused to let you truly belong |
| 10 | A folded square of cloth — a flag, a banner, a blanket — from the first crew or unit that took you in |
| 11 | A wanted notice that describes you only as "a large half-orc / a minotaur / a hobgoblin," which you find insulting |
| 12 | A pair of brass knuckles or a knuckle-bone, cast or carved from a chain you used to wear |
| 13 | A letter from a parent or a sworn-sibling, read so often the fold has worn through the page |
| 14 | A tooth, a tine, or a tag that isn't yours, taken from someone who learned not to test you twice |
| 15 | A drinking horn that holds exactly as much as you can carry and still climb the rigging |
| 16 | A small shrine to Cayden Cailean, the Accidental God, who took no one's pedigree into account |
| 17 | A length of anchor-chain you can still lift one-handed, that you keep to be sure you still can |
| 18 | A debt freely owed to you by a person who, for once, was glad to be in your debt |
| 19 | A ball of red thread or a knotted cord — the trick that gets you out of any maze, or any port, the same way every time |
| 20 | A name carved into a beam below decks — yours — because you wanted there to be proof you were here |
