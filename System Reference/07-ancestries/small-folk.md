# Small Folk

*A kindred, not one people: halflings, gnomes, dwarves, and every other small civilised folk the Inner Sea reckons "ordinary" — as opposed to the goblins and kobolds it calls vermin.*

You are one of the small peoples — the ones the tall world finds useful, underestimates, and forgets to fear. Maybe you are a **halfling**, born into a Chelish household that owned you or one generation removed from one, who got over a wall and never looked back. Maybe you are a **gnome**, fey-touched and restless, forever one new wonder ahead of the grey creeping numbness your people call the Bleaching, and the deck of a pirate ship is the most interesting thing you have ever found. Maybe you are a **dwarf**, exiled from the stone-holds by oath or feud or sheer wanderlust, carrying your craft and your grudges out onto a sea your ancestors never trusted. What you share is not blood but *position*: you are small in a world built for the big, and you have learned that being overlooked is a kind of armour, and that the small hands get into the places the large ones cannot.

## Background Questions

- You came up under a roof that did not consider you a full person — a Chelish estate near **Corentyn**, an Aspis counting-house in **Bloodcove**, a dwarf-hold that cast you out. Who decided your size was the same as your worth, and how close have they come to finding you since?
- You are chasing something the tall world doesn't value enough to guard properly — a wonder, a craft, a freedom, a debt of honour older than anyone alive. What is it, and what has the chase already cost you?
- You were born free in the Shackles, small among giants, and learned early that the deck doesn't care how tall you are if your hands are quick and your nerve holds. Who taught you that, and what did you have to prove to them?

## Names

Small Folk names span their peoples: halfling names are warm and plain, often a trade or a place (*Pim Saltcellar, Lula, Mosey*); gnome names are long, musical, and collected like keepsakes, usually shortened by everyone else (*Fonkin, Zither, Bee*); dwarf names are hard and clan-bound, an honorific or a hold attached (*Hekka Stonebound, Durgan, Vess Oath-Kept*). Examples across the kindred: *Pim, Bee Quick-Fingers, Durgan No-Hold, Hessa Freeborn, Tock, Corrin Tallows.*

> Many Small Folk carry two names — the one their people gave them and the one the tall world can be bothered to pronounce. Halflings born to bondage often choose a free name on getting out; gnomes change theirs whenever the old one stops delighting them.

## Trinkets

*Roll twice on a d20 and keep both.*

| d20 | Trinket |
|---|---|
| 1 | A household tag, collar, or hold-brand — cut through, filed off, or pried loose — kept as a reminder of what you left |
| 2 | A recipe, a formula, or a clan-mark recorded in three hands: your grandmother's, your mother's, and now yours |
| 3 | A skeleton key you cut yourself, that opens more than it has any right to |
| 4 | A tiny, intricate, useless mechanism — a gnome's whim — that does one charming thing and nothing else |
| 5 | A page torn from a ledger, an oath-tablet, or a property-roll with your old name on it, stolen on the way out |
| 6 | A set of weighted dice you only use on people who deserve it |
| 7 | A list of names — others still inside the house, hold, or cage you escaped — that you are working down, one at a time |
| 8 | A small animal that runs the rat-lines and answers only to you (name it) |
| 9 | A free-paper or forged writ of passage, made out to a person who never existed |
| 10 | A spoon, a chisel, or a thimble worn thin — the one tool of your old life that made it out with you |
| 11 | An iron token of Cayden Cailean, patron of the freed, that you rub before anything dangerous |
| 12 | A wanted notice offering a reward "for the return of property," meaning you, that you carry out of spite |
| 13 | A coil of fine cord and a grapple small enough to hide in a fist |
| 14 | A pressed sprig, a chip of stone, or a fading sketch of the one good thing from where you grew up |
| 15 | A pouch of something the Bleaching can't touch — a colour, a song, a joke — that a gnome carries against the grey |
| 16 | A whistle-code, taught by runaways or by miners, that means *get out now* in any place that knows it |
| 17 | A debt owed you by someone you freed or saved, who swore they'd come when you called |
| 18 | A craftsman's kit — needle and palm, files and picks, lockwork tools — small enough to vanish, good enough to live on |
| 19 | A jar of good salt or fine ale, because you swore you would never again take what they handed you |
| 20 | The first coin you ever earned as your own person, that you will starve before you spend |
