# The Made

*A kindred, not one people: androids, automatons, homunculi, and the other thinking things that were built rather than born — and woke up anyway.*

You were not born. You were *made* — assembled, cast, grown in a vat, wound up, or breathed into being by someone who wanted a tool and got a person instead. Maybe you are an **android**, a synthetic body of ageless flesh and buried machinery, carrying a soul that has worn other bodies before this one and will move on after, hunting in this single lifetime for what it means to be a *self* and not a thing. Maybe you are an **automaton**, a frame of brass and old enchantment, a soldier or a servant whose makers are long dust, still walking because no one ever told you that you could stop. Maybe you are a **homunculus**, a small made-thing built to serve a master who is dead, or worse, *not* dead, and still believes they own you. The Inner Sea cannot decide whether you are property, miracle, or abomination, and reaches for a different one of the three depending on who can profit. The Shackles, which is built entirely of people who refused to be owned, is the one place that looks at a made thing claiming itself a free person and simply says: *prove it on the next watch.*

## Background Questions

- You had a maker or an owner — a Chelish artificer, a Numerian salvage-crew, a wizard whose tower you walked out of. Are they dead, hunting you, or worst of all still legally entitled to you under Inner Sea law, and how close have they come to collecting?
- You are trying to become — or to prove you already are — a real person, with all the mess that entails. What specifically are you chasing (a feeling, a choice, a death you can call your own), and what have you done that frightened *you* by how alive it was?
- The crew took a while to decide you weren't a clever machine playing at a soul. Who vouched for you first, what did you do to earn it, and what do you owe them now?

## Names

The Made are named in three ways: a maker's designation, dry and functional (*Unit Seven, Cataloguer, Vessel-9*); a name a Made chooses on waking, often abstract or aspirational (*Vigil, Ash, Nine, Tuesday, Hope*); or a name a crew gives them, affectionate and plain (*Tin, Brass, Doc*). Examples: *Vigil, Unit Seven (now just "Seven"), Ash, Brass, Cataloguer, Tuesday.*

> Many of the Made keep their maker's designation the way a freed person keeps an old scar — not from love, but to remember. Choosing a *new* name is, for most, the first act that was truly their own.

## Trinkets

*Roll twice on a d20 and keep both.*

| d20 | Trinket |
|---|---|
| 1 | Your maker's mark, stamped or etched somewhere on your frame, that you have tried and failed to file off |
| 2 | A single component you replaced yourself, badly, the first time you chose to keep living |
| 3 | A logbook in your own hand, beginning the day you woke, that you are using to find out who you are |
| 4 | A bill of sale, a commission, or a deactivation order with your designation on it |
| 5 | The tools and oil that keep you running, which you guard more carefully than any weapon |
| 6 | A small repeating fault — a tic, a hum, a flicker — that you have stopped trying to fix because it feels like *you* |
| 7 | A list of human things you are teaching yourself to do convincingly: blinking, flinching, lying, grieving |
| 8 | A wind-up bird, a lesser made-thing, that you tend like a child of your own (name it) |
| 9 | A keepsake from a previous life your soul half-remembers but your body never lived |
| 10 | The last order your dead maker gave you, that you are deciding whether you still have to obey |
| 11 | A relic of Numeria or some other place of fallen sky-metal, that hums when you hold it |
| 12 | A perfectly kept human object — a comb, a ring, a pipe — belonging to no one you ever met, that you find comforting |
| 13 | A diagram of your own internals that you did not draw and do not fully understand |
| 14 | A "wanted" notice listing you as *recovered property*, that you carry to remind yourself it's a lie |
| 15 | A charm or icon a religious crewmate pressed on you, insisting you have a soul; you have not thrown it away |
| 16 | A jar of the vat-fluid, sky-metal filings, or clockwork dust you woke from |
| 17 | A debt owed to you by the first person who treated you as a person; you intend to never call it in |
| 18 | A small idol — perhaps to Brigh, goddess of invention, who might be the nearest thing you have to a maker who loves you |
| 19 | A scrap of music, a joke, or a poem that made you feel something the first time, that you replay deliberately |
| 20 | A self-destruct phrase, a kill-word, or a shutdown command your maker built into you, that someone out there still knows |
