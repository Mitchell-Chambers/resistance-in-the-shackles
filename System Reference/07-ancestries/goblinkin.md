# Goblinkin

*A kindred, not one people: goblins, kobolds, and the other small folk the Inner Sea does not call "ordinary" but "vermin" — and hunts accordingly.*

You are small like the halflings and gnomes, but where the world merely *overlooks* them, it actively wants you *dead*. Maybe you are a **goblin** — sharp-toothed, fire-mad, gleefully alive — raised in a warren that the nearest town burned out at least once in your memory, who learned that the only safe place is moving and the only currency is being too useful or too funny to kill. Maybe you are a **kobold** — scaled, clever, and proud — bred to traps and tunnels and the worship of things much larger than yourself, until you slipped your warren's hierarchy and struck out to be nobody's small and expendable cog. The respectable peoples treat you as a problem with legs; the Free Captains, gloriously, do not care, so long as you can fit down the hawsehole to clear a fouled cable, rig a trap no boarder expects, or do the loud dangerous thing nobody taller will volunteer for. You have spent your whole life being underestimated and hunted. On a pirate deck, for the first time, the first half is an advantage and the second half is just *Tuesday*.

## Background Questions

- A town, a baron, or a "pest-control" bounty out of a colonial port like **Bloodcove** put a price on your whole warren — maybe on you specifically. What happened to the rest of them, and what did you do that they still remember?
- You are dangerously, delightedly good at the thing that scares everyone else — fire, traps, heights, the powder magazine, the narrow flooded crawlspace. What's your specialty, and what has it already cost you or someone near you?
- You attached yourself to this crew the way your kind always survives: by being indispensable to someone bigger. Who did you decide to be useful to, do they know it's mutual, and what happens the day they stop needing you?

## Names

Goblin names are short, percussive, and often gross or boastful, earned and re-earned by deeds (*Snag, Chuff, Reekfinger, Last*); kobold names hiss and click, frequently invoking a draconic patron or a clever trap they're proud of (*Skritt, Vex, Az-the-Snare, Little Tooth*). Examples across the kindred: *Snag, Skritt, Reek, Vex Trapwright, Chuff, Az.*

> Goblins rename themselves constantly — after a meal, a fire, a near-death — and sing the new name until it sticks. Kobolds guard their true-name and offer the surface a use-name, often the name of the trap or trick they're best at.

## Trinkets

*Roll twice on a d20 and keep both.*

| d20 | Trinket |
|---|---|
| 1 | A tinderbox you made yourself that lights wet, in wind, on the first strike, always — your proudest possession |
| 2 | A string of "treasures" the tall folk would call rubbish: a bent spoon, a doll's eye, a perfect pebble, a tooth |
| 3 | A trap, in miniature, that actually works, which you set off to demonstrate at the worst social moments |
| 4 | A scorch-scar with a story you tell three different ways depending on the audience |
| 5 | A tally of every time you should have died, scratched somewhere on your person, that you are quietly proud of |
| 6 | A whistle pitched so high only your kind and the ship's dogs flinch at it |
| 7 | A song — yours, improvised, endless — about everyone aboard, updated after every fight |
| 8 | A small mean creature even uglier than you, that you love ferociously (name it) |
| 9 | A wanted poster offering coin for you "dead or in a sack," that you have signed |
| 10 | A length of fuse-cord coiled where a sensible person would not keep fuse-cord |
| 11 | A draconic scale, a dragon's cast-off tooth, or a relic of the big thing your warren worshipped |
| 12 | A set of picks and pry-bars worn from a lifetime of getting into and out of places |
| 13 | A jar of something that should not be in a jar, that you are keeping for "an emergency" |
| 14 | The charred remains of the one thing you ran back into the fire to save |
| 15 | A map of a town's sewers, drains, and gaps, drawn from the inside, that has saved your life twice |
| 16 | A "lucky" object that has been on fire, underwater, and through a cannon's near-miss, and survived like you |
| 17 | A debt of loyalty to one taller person who, once, did not try to kill you — and you have never forgotten it |
| 18 | A pouch of caltrops, fish-hooks, and broken glass, for making any floor regret following you across it |
| 19 | A trophy from the thing that was supposed to eat you, taken from the wrong end of it |
| 20 | A secret way off this ship that you have not told anyone about, just in case |
