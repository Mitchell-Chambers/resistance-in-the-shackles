# Tengu

You are crow-folk — black-feathered, bright-eyed, sharp of beak and sharper of tongue — and your people have always lived in the cracks of other people's cities, a diaspora that put down roots everywhere and belonged nowhere in particular. Tengu drift toward ports the way water finds the low ground, and from there onto ships, because a life spent in the rigging and the markets and the rumour-rich dark of the wharves suits you down to the ground. You collect things: shiny objects, certainly, but mostly *words* — you have an ear for languages and a gift for picking up a phrase, a password, a curse, an accent, the exact thing a person does not want overheard. Other sailors are superstitious about you, half-convinced a tengu aboard is either the best luck or the worst, and you have never once corrected them. The sea is just one more city with cracks to live in, and you have always known how to make a home of the spaces other people overlook.

## Background Questions

- You worked the wharves of a great port — **Port Peril**, **Quent**, somewhere busier and crueller — trading in overheard words and small stolen things, until a deal went bad enough to need a fast ship out. What did you hear that you shouldn't have, and who's still angry about it?
- You come from a tengu community that has lived in the Shackles for generations, tight-knit and quietly everywhere, with eyes in every harbour. What does your people's network expect of you out here, and what do you send back to them?
- You are chasing a *word* — a name, a song, a phrase in a dead tongue, a story half-heard — that you believe leads somewhere worth the going. What is it, and what have you already given up following it?

## Names

Tengu often carry a birth-name in their own speech and a "deck-name" for everyone else — frequently onomatopoeic, a sound, a word collected because they liked the shape of it. Examples: *Kaw, Shrike, Two-Penny, Rattle, Hark, Semi "the Echo."*

> Many tengu take names that are simply *good words* — collected, traded, sometimes won off another tengu. A tengu may change deck-names between voyages; the birth-name they keep.

## Trinkets

*Roll twice on a d20 and keep both.*

| d20 | Trinket |
|---|---|
| 1 | A hoard of small bright things — a button, a coin, a tooth, a bead — sorted by no logic but your own |
| 2 | A notebook of phrases in nine languages, each one labelled with where you'd use it and on whom |
| 3 | A spyglass lens with no spyglass, that you look through anyway because you like the world it makes |
| 4 | A single perfect black feather that is not one of yours — you won't say whose |
| 5 | A set of lockpicks filed from gull-bones, light as nothing, that you made yourself |
| 6 | A password to a door in a port you can no longer safely enter |
| 7 | A list of debts owed *to* you in favours and secrets, which you consider better than coin |
| 8 | A clever bird, not a tengu, that you have taught three words and one very rude gesture (name it) |
| 9 | A coin from a country that no longer exists, that you keep because the word stamped on it is beautiful |
| 10 | A folded map of a city's rooftops and gutters — not the streets — annotated for a fast escape |
| 11 | A tin of the specific tea that tastes like the wharf you grew up on |
| 12 | A song you collected off a dying sailor, that you have never sung for anyone |
| 13 | A ring of keys, none of which you can remember the locks for, all of which you keep |
| 14 | A wanted notice for you that lists your crimes in the wrong order, which offends you professionally |
| 15 | A glass eye that you found, not yours, that you are oddly fond of |
| 16 | A charm against drowning given by a tengu elder, that has either worked perfectly or never been tested |
| 17 | A scrap of paper with a single word in a script no one you've shown can read |
| 18 | A small idol of Norgorber, god of secrets, who appreciates a creature that knows how to keep them |
| 19 | A whistle-language, taught by your people, that carries across a crowded deck as gossip no human hears |
| 20 | A secret you are carrying for someone, sealed and unread, that you have been paid not to open |
