# The Shackles — Skills

> *Skills are things you can do. They speak to lived competence — not what you know, but what you have trained your body and mind to perform under pressure.*

---

## How Skills Work

When you have a relevant skill for an action, add one D10 to your dice pool and pick the highest result. There is no such thing as an untrained skill — anyone can attempt most actions. Having a skill denotes serious, tested competence in that area.

Skills are strictly about action. They don't grant knowledge or contacts — that's what domains are for. A character with Brave and no Raider domain is dangerous but has no idea what they're doing in a broader tactical sense. A character with the Raider domain and no Brave or Hunt is full of tactical knowledge they can't personally execute.

The combination of skill and domain tells you who someone is and how they move through the world. Tend + Deepwater is a ship's surgeon who has worked in sea conditions. Tend + Ruins is someone who has patched people up in collapsing Ghol-Gan chambers. Same skill, entirely different people.

---

## Knacks

If you gain a skill or domain a second time, you gain a Knack — a named, narrow facet of that skill or domain. Knacks are also granted by certain class abilities (the Castaway's RED FEAST) and by Renowned equipment (see Equipment & Resources). Each skill below lists suggested facets; work with your GM to name one that fits your history — narrow enough that it doesn't swallow the skill.

A Knack does exactly one thing, always the same thing: **when your knack applies to an action, roll with mastery** — one additional D10, pick the highest. You cannot gain more than one die from mastery per roll, no matter how many knacks or abilities apply. A knack never removes a roll, never changes dice sizes, and never compels the GM.

## Brave

*Act in the face of danger, brawl when you must, and hold your nerve when it matters*

Brave covers the full range of courage under pressure — physical, social, and moral. It is not about cold-blooded, skilled violence (that's Hunt) or enduring punishment (that's Endure). It is about the heat of the moment: throwing yourself in before fear catches up, brawling when brawling is the only option, holding your ground when every instinct says to run.

In the Shackles, Brave is how a pirate earns standing. It covers both the decision to fight and the execution of that fight when it is desperate, messy, and personal — the boarding action chaos, the tavern brawl, the moment where technique goes out the window and all that remains is whether you're willing to take and deal punishment. Brave rewards characters who live in the heat of things and punishes hesitation.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Charge into a boarding action, a burning ship, or a position under fire
- Brawl, fight dirty, or throw yourself into close violence without the luxury of technique
- Hold your nerve against intimidation, torture, or a credible threat of death
- Make a public declaration that invites dangerous consequences
- Commit to a desperate plan that requires full commitment to work
- Stand your ground when an adversary tests whether you'll back down
- Perform a reckless physical action under pressure — leaping, diving, throwing yourself into it
- Fight with improvised weapons, belaying pins, broken bottles, or bare hands

### Knacks

- **First Over the Rail** — leading the charge in a boarding action.
- **Dirty Fighter** — fighting with the environment, improvised weapons and no honour.
- **Hard to Break** — holding out under torture, interrogation or intimidation.
- **Stare It Down** — standing your ground against something that should make you run.
- **All In** — desperate actions with no fallback and everything staked.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Brave and Hunt are different sides of violence: Brave is the desperate, personal, get-it-done fighting of people who survive on nerve — brawling, boarding chaos, reckless commitment. Hunt is cold, skilled, deliberate violence with a specific target in mind. Brave and Endure are different: Brave is active defiance, Endure is passive resistance — holding on rather than pushing forward. Brave and Command are different: Brave inspires by example, Command works by authority.

## Carouse

*Drink, gamble, make friends in low places, and work the social theatre of port life*

Carouse is the art of belonging. It covers the full social performance of a pirate's life ashore: drinking with strangers until they feel like allies, working a gambling table, telling the kind of story that makes people want to hear the next one, finding out what you need to know by becoming the most interesting person in the room.

Carouse is not manipulation — that's Deceive. It is genuine, if temporary, connection. People Caroused with are not deceived; they simply like you, or respect you, or owe you a round. It is the skill of social ease, of being welcomed in places where being an outsider is dangerous.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Drink, gamble, or feast with strangers to build trust or extract information
- Work a tavern, market, or dockside crowd to improve your position
- Find someone specific in a port through social navigation rather than investigation
- Recover from social damage — smooth over an insult, rebuild a fractured relationship
- Perform: tell stories, sing, play music, or otherwise command an audience
- Tend a name: a Carouse+[Domain] roll removes Reputation stress — standing the room a round, holding court, making sure the story gets told right. Doing it for your *own* name is Risky (see Ports & Haunts)

### Knacks

- **Drinker's Constitution** — keeping your head while drinking, and outlasting those who can't.
- **Cardsharp** — gambling, and cheating at it.
- **The Toast** — holding a room with a story, a song or a speech.
- **Old Friend** — finding welcome and working the crowd in ports you have caroused in before.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Carouse and Deceive both work socially but differently: Carouse builds genuine if transient connection, Deceive manufactures false ones. Carouse and Command both address groups, but Command operates through authority, Carouse through warmth and belonging.

## Command

*Lead, intimidate, inspire, and make people move — by love, fear, or force of will*

Command is the skill of making people do things. It covers the full spectrum from beloved captain to feared tyrant: coordinating a crew in a crisis, breaking someone's will through sustained pressure, rallying a mob, keeping panicking sailors at their stations in a storm. The mechanism changes — love, fear, respect, desperation — but the outcome is the same: people move.

Command is primarily directed at groups, or at individuals in moments of confrontation. It is not the slow social work of building trust (Carouse) or the subtle art of implanting false beliefs (Deceive). It is immediate, public, and consequential. A Command roll is something other people witness.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Coordinate crew during combat, a storm, or any shipboard emergency
- Intimidate an individual or group into compliance through force of presence
- Rally demoralised allies — give them something to hold onto in a crisis
- Establish authority over a new or hostile group quickly
- Prevent a crew from panicking, mutinying, or making a catastrophic decision
- Issue orders in a complex situation and have them followed without question

### Knacks

- **Captain's Voice** — orders given to your own crew in a crisis.
- **Presence** — imposing yourself on a room the moment you enter it.
- **Break Them** — sustained intimidation of a single target.
- **Hold the Line** — rallying a crew on the edge of breaking.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Command and Brave both involve facing danger directly, but Brave is about personal commitment, Command is about moving others. Command and Deceive both affect people's behaviour, but Command does so openly and forcibly, Deceive through misdirection. Command and Carouse both address groups, but Command operates through authority, Carouse through belonging.

## Deceive

*Lie, cheat, forge, disguise, bluff, and talk your way out of a noose*

Deceive covers everything that involves creating a false impression: outright lies, forged documents, disguises, bluffs under fire, misdirection, impersonation. It is the skill of people who survive by being something other than what they are — and in the Shackles, that is most people most of the time.

Deceive is not limited to words. A disguise is Deceive. A rigged weighing scale is Deceive. An ambush concealed as a surrender is Deceive. The common thread is that someone believes something false because of your deliberate action. The quality of the lie matters less than the quality of its delivery.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Lie convincingly under questioning, in negotiation, or in a social situation
- Forge documents — ships' papers, letters of marque, cargo manifests, letters of introduction
- Pass as someone else: a naval officer, a merchant factor, a different pirate captain
- Bluff through a situation where the truth would end badly
- Plant false information that someone else will act on
- Run a con, a misdirection, or a scheme that depends on being believed

### Knacks

- **Straight Face** — lying under direct, suspicious questioning.
- **Paperwork** — forging documents, seals and records.
- **The Long Con** — building and maintaining a false identity over time.
- **Read the Mark** — telling a mark exactly what they most want to believe.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Deceive and Carouse both work socially but differently: Carouse builds genuine connection, Deceive manufactures false ones. Deceive and Vanish are different: Vanish is about not being seen, Deceive is about being seen as the wrong thing. Deceive and Reckon are often paired: Reckon reads the situation, Deceive exploits it.

## Endure

*Resist physical punishment, exposure, disease, and the sea's many ways of wearing a body down*

Endure is the skill of survival through persistence rather than action. Not fighting back — that's Brave. Not cold-blooded pursuit — that's Hunt. Endure is staying functional through things that would stop other people: fever, exhaustion, poison, prolonged captivity, the relentless physical attrition of life at sea.

Endure is also the skill used to resist stress — when a character rolls to push through Blood fallout, or to weather a supernatural effect on their body, or to keep moving at the end of a three-day chase with no sleep and no food. It is the last resource when everything else has run out.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Resist poison, disease, infection, or magical contamination affecting the body
- Stay functional through exhaustion, deprivation, injury, or extended hardship
- Push through Blood fallout to act despite a wound's penalties
- Survive extreme environmental conditions — cold, heat, pressure, lack of air
- Maintain concentration or physical function during torture or sustained pain
- Stay conscious, upright, or active when the body is signalling that it is done

### Knacks

- **Iron Constitution** — resisting poison, disease and spoiled provisions.
- **Second Wind** — pressing on while wounded.
- **Sea-Hardened** — bearing cold, wet, sun, salt and the sea's motion.
- **The Long Watch** — staying awake, alert and working long past reason.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Endure and Brave are distinct: Brave is active defiance, Endure is passive resistance — holding on rather than pushing forward. Endure and Tend are different: Endure is the body refusing to quit, Tend is someone actively fixing what's broken. Endure covers physical resistance; psychological punishment lands on other tracks.

## Hunt

*Track, pursue, and end things — people, creatures, or ships — with deliberate, skilled violence*

Hunt is cold, purposeful violence. Where Brave covers the heat of a brawl or a desperate boarding action, Hunt covers the patient, skilled application of lethality: tracking a target through a port, stalking prey through jungle, putting a pistol ball precisely where it needs to go, or manoeuvring a ship into the perfect firing position before the other captain knows what is happening.

Hunt is the skill of those who have moved beyond survival into predation. It implies training, patience, and a specific relationship with violence — not as something that happens to you in moments of desperation, but as something you initiate, control, and conclude. A pirate with Hunt is not someone who fights. They are someone who hunts.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Track a specific person, creature, or vessel across distance and time
- Stalk a target through a port, a jungle, or a ship without alerting them
- Execute precise violence: a clean shot, a targeted strike, a kill that had to look like something else
- Pursue a fleeing target and run them down — on foot, by ship, or across a port
- Read a target's vulnerabilities, habits, and likely behaviour before moving against them
- Conduct an ambush with planning and patience rather than opportunistic force

### Knacks

- **Pistoleer** — shooting from a moving deck, in poor light, under pressure.
- **Duelist** — formal single combat, when both parties know what is happening.
- **Patient** — the long stalk: surveillance, ambush, waiting for the shot.
- **Manhunter** — tracking a specific, named person.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Hunt and Brave are different sides of violence: Hunt is cold, deliberate, and targeted — you chose this target and you are ending them on your terms. Brave is the desperate, messy, personal violence of someone who is in the thick of it and surviving. Hunt and Vanish are natural partners: Vanish gets you into position undetected, Hunt makes use of it. Hunt and Reckon work in sequence: Reckon identifies the target and reads their behaviour, Hunt moves against them.

## Navigate

*Chart courses, read weather, pilot vessels, interpret tides and stars*

Navigate covers everything involved in getting a ship from one place to another without dying: celestial navigation, dead reckoning, reading charts, interpreting weather, piloting through difficult waters, and the thousand small decisions that determine whether a voyage ends at the intended destination or on a reef.

Navigate is not just about the open sea. Threading through a reef passage at night, calculating when the tide will unground a beached ship, reading the clouds to know whether to run for harbour — all of these are Navigate. It is the application of knowledge about how the physical world moves, and how to move through it.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Plot a course across open ocean, through island chains, or around known hazards
- Pilot the ship through difficult conditions: fog, storm, reef, shoal water, or darkness
- Read weather signs to predict what is coming and how much time there is
- Dead reckon a position when stars, landmarks, and instruments are unavailable
- Interpret charts, portolan maps, and sailing directions — including damaged or unfamiliar ones
- Understand tidal patterns, currents, and seasonal conditions that affect a given voyage

### Knacks

- **Starreader** — fixing a position by stars, moon and sun alone.
- **Storm Instinct** — reading the weather before it arrives.
- **Reef Pilot** — shallow, reef-strewn and uncharted coastal waters.
- **Known Waters** — sailing waters you have sailed before.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Navigate and the Deepwater domain are closely related: Navigate is the skill, Deepwater is the experience and contacts. A character can have Navigate without Deepwater (technically skilled but sheltered) or Deepwater without Navigate (a veteran who navigates by instinct rather than formal training). Navigate and Reckon both involve reading the world, but Navigate is specific to the physical environment of the sea.

## Plunder

*Board, break in, loot, and extract value from places and people — fast, bold, and with an eye for what matters*

Plunder covers everything involved in acquisition by force or cunning: boarding a prize, breaking into a locked space, identifying and extracting the most valuable items from a situation under time pressure, and moving that value out before anyone can stop you. It is the central practical skill of piracy as a profession.

Plunder is not about violence — that's Brave or Hunt. And it's not just lockpicking — that's a particular application of a wider competency. Plunder covers the full arc from identifying the prize to securing it: spotting the strong room, getting into it, knowing what's worth taking, and getting out before the situation deteriorates.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Break into locked or secured spaces: strong rooms, warehouses, ship's cabins, vaults
- Identify what is valuable in a space that needs to be looted quickly
- Run a boarding action with an emphasis on extraction rather than fighting
- Pick locks, break seals, force hatches, and bypass physical security
- Move a large or unwieldy prize — cargo, a prisoner, a piece of equipment — under pressure
- Assess the likely value of a prize ship or location before committing to a raid

### Knacks

- **Appraiser's Eye** — spotting what is worth taking, fast, under pressure.
- **Keys and Locks** — defeating locks, seals and physical barriers.
- **In and Out** — getting out fast with the take.
- **Prize Crew** — seizing and securing a prize vessel.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Plunder and Brave both happen during raids, but Plunder is about securing the objective, Brave deals with opposition when it comes to you. Plunder and Hunt are different: Hunt actively pursues and eliminates a target, Plunder moves around resistance when possible. Plunder and Vanish often work together — the best plunder is quiet and uncontested. Plunder and Reckon work in sequence: Reckon reads the situation, Plunder exploits it materially.

## Reckon

*Observe, deduce, and understand what is actually happening before you act*

Reckon is the skill of paying attention and drawing correct conclusions. It covers careful observation, reading people and situations, piecing together what has happened from available evidence, and the kind of dead-reckoning intelligence that tells you where you are when you have no fixed point of reference.

In the Shackles, the ability to correctly read a situation before committing to it is survival. Is that merchant vessel carrying what its manifest claims? Is the harbormaster being straight with you or playing for time? What happened on this ship before you boarded it, and is whatever happened to the crew still here? Reckon is the answer to questions the world isn't offering to answer voluntarily.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Read a person's intentions, emotional state, or the lie buried in what they are saying
- Piece together what happened in a location from physical evidence and context
- Assess whether a situation, person, or offer is what it appears to be
- Identify a threat or danger before it has fully declared itself
- Understand the power dynamics in a social situation — who defers to whom, and why
- Draw correct conclusions from partial, ambiguous, or actively misleading information

### Knacks

- **The Tell** — spotting a lie as it is told.
- **Scene Reading** — reconstructing what happened from the evidence left behind.
- **Threat Sense** — sensing the ambush before it springs.
- **Dead Reckoning** — estimating accurately from incomplete information.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Reckon and Navigate both involve reading the world accurately, but Navigate is specific to the maritime environment. Reckon and Deceive are natural opposites — and natural complements: a character with both can run the whole arc of a deception themselves. Reckon corresponds to Heart's Discern skill; Reckon is the Shackles-flavoured name for the same underlying competence.

## Tend

*Heal wounds, maintain gear, manage ship systems, brew medicines and poisons*

Tend is the skill of repair — of bodies, equipment, ships, and the delicate systems that keep a crew alive and functional. It covers everything from binding a wound in the middle of a fight to diagnosing an infected injury to maintaining the rigging to brewing the tinctures that keep tropical disease from sweeping through the lower decks.

Tend is one of the few skills that explicitly interacts with the stress system mechanically: it is the primary means of removing Blood stress and treating Blood fallout. A crew without a competent Tend user will accumulate attrition they cannot reverse, and in the Shackles the sea finds every weakness eventually.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Treat wounds, set bones, manage infections, and perform surgery
- Remove Blood stress from yourself or an ally through direct medical attention
- Maintain, repair, or jury-rig ship systems, weapons, and equipment
- Brew medicines, antidotes, poisons, and compounds from available materials
- Identify the nature and severity of an injury, illness, or medical condition
- Manage the health of the crew over long voyages — diet, disease prevention, mental load

### Knacks

- **Ship's Surgeon** — surgery on a moving deck, in battle, by lamplight.
- **Poisoner** — brewing poisons and their antidotes.
- **Field Surgery** — treating serious wounds with whatever is to hand.
- **Shipwright** — hull, spar and rigging repairs.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Tend and Endure both relate to the body's survival, but Tend is active intervention and Endure is passive resistance. Tend + Deepwater covers sea-medicine; Tend + Wilderness covers jungle medicine; Tend + Ruins covers operating in archaic and contaminated environments. The domain changes the flavour and availability of materials entirely.

## Vanish

*Hide, shadow, move unseen, slip away from pursuit, and conceal what shouldn't be found*

Vanish covers the full range of invisibility: hiding yourself, hiding objects, moving through space without leaving a trace, following someone without being noticed, and getting out of situations where being visible would be fatal. It is the skill of people who understand that the best position in many conflicts is not being part of the conflict at all.

Vanish is not just about stealth in the physical sense. Concealing contraband in a ship's hidden compartments is Vanish. Moving through a port without being remembered is Vanish. Approaching a prize vessel with lights doused and crew silent is a ship-level expression of the same skill — in which case the ship's Stealth rating matters, but the character rolling to execute it uses Vanish.

### This Skill Applies When You...

- Hide yourself in any environment — aboard ship, in port, in jungle, in built structures
- Follow or surveil a target without being detected
- Move through a space without leaving evidence of your passage
- Conceal objects, contraband, or people from search and inspection
- Approach a target — person or vessel — without being noticed until you choose to be
- Escape from pursuit: disappear into a crowd, a port, or a landscape

### Knacks

- **Shadow** — following someone unseen.
- **Dead Ground** — finding cover, concealment and shadow.
- **Nothing There** — hiding objects: contraband, weapons, messages.
- **Ghost Approach** — closing on a vessel or position by night or in poor weather.

### Overlaps & Distinctions

> Vanish and Deceive are different: Vanish is about not being perceived, Deceive is about being perceived wrongly. They work together naturally — Vanish gets you into position, Deceive handles anyone who catches you there. Vanish and Plunder work in sequence: Vanish gets you into the space, Plunder gets you back out with the goods.
