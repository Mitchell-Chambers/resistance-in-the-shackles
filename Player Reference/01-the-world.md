# Part One — The World

## The Shackles

The Shackles is an archipelago of hundreds of islands stretching along the western coast of Garund, a large tropical continent reminiscent of Africa. It lies touching the south of the Eye of Abendego, an immortal hurricane that has not stopped spinning in over a thousand years. Nobody made it. Nobody can stop it. The northern Shackles are its permanent shadow.

The region exists in permanent opposition to civilised power. To the north, Cheliax is the dominant naval force of the Inner Sea — disciplined, aggressive, convinced of its own righteousness and the power of its devil gods. To the south, colonies support trade routes for far-flung empires in Tian Xia and eastern Garund. The Shackles exists, in large part, to rob both — along with everyone else they can find — and to drink well afterwards.

## The Free Captains & the Pirate Council

The Shackles has no state. What it has instead is the Free Captains: independent pirate lords who rule from their ships and their islands through reputation, violence, and the fragile web of obligation known as the Pirate Code. The most powerful of these captains sit on the Pirate Council, which meets in Port Peril, the largest city in the Shackles. The Council does not govern so much as manage conflict. It sets prizes, arbitrates disputes, and occasionally decides to make someone's life very short.

Port Peril is the heart of the Shackles — a teeming, dangerous, magnificent city built into a natural harbour, where every vice is available and every transaction has an angle. It is where careers are made and ended, where the Pirate Code is law, and where the smell of salt and smoke never fully leaves the air.

## The Eye of Abendego

The Eye is the defining feature of the Shackles. When it arrived a thousand years ago it destroyed the nation of Lirgen, the kingdom of Yamasa, and the empire of Ghol-Gan — and it has not relented since. It is supernaturally powerful; no natural storm sustains itself for a millennium. Scholars of the event believe it is either a manifestation of divine wrath — perhaps the death of a god — or the consequence of a ritual that went catastrophically wrong. Either way, it is not going anywhere.

The Eye shapes Shackles weather, Shackles culture, and Shackles theology. Besmara, the Pirate Queen — goddess of strife, sea monsters, and plunder — is worshipped here more fervently than anywhere else in the world. She does not promise safety. She promises that if you are strong enough, fast enough, and sufficiently disrespectful of consequences, you might deserve what you take.

## The Islands & the Deep

The Shackles' islands range from thriving pirate ports to uninhabitable jungle ecosystems to ruins that predate human civilisation. The cyclops empire of Ghol-Gan built its monuments across the entire archipelago before vanishing. Their ruins dot the landscape above water and below, still full of traps, guardians, and things that have been waiting in the dark for a long time. Older things still lurk in the deepest trenches and beneath certain islands that have never been properly settled, for reasons that become obvious quickly.

The sea itself is alive with magic: currents that seem to make navigational decisions; creatures of impossible size; ghost-lights that lead sailors to beautiful deaths; sea witches who trade in storm-debts and claim the ocean as their territory; liches and dragons hiding deep in the jungle from adventurers seeking glory.

This is not a safe sea. But it is a magnificent one.

## The Crew

The crew is everything. Not an abstraction, not a resource, not a means to an end — people, specific people, whose names you know and whose faces you could draw from memory in the dark. Pirates talk about freedom like it is a solo act. The best of them know better: the only real freedom most ever feel is the kind they share. Whatever your character's private obsession, the ship and the hands aboard her are the ground you stand on — and, in this game, the thing the sea will most reliably use to hurt you.
