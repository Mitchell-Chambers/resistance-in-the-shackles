# The Shackles — Stress Tracks

> *Stress is the toll the Shackles takes on a person. It is not a health bar. It is the accumulation of pain, bad luck, supernatural attention, and lost coin — everything that erodes a pirate until something finally breaks.*

---

## How Stress Works

Each stress track represents a different kind of harm. When you fail or partially succeed on a roll, you take stress to a relevant track — the GM assigns the track based on what is actually happening in the fiction, not the skill you used. The eleven skills are: Brave, Carouse, Command, Deceive, Endure, Hunt, Navigate, Plunder, Reckon, Tend, and Vanish.

Each track has a stress total. When you mark stress, the GM immediately rolls a D12 and compares it to your current total stress across all tracks. If the result is equal or lower, the character suffers fallout.

| D12 Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Higher than total stress | No fallout. Stress accumulates. |
| Equal or lower — roll 1–6 | Minor fallout. Clear all stress in this track. |
| Equal or lower — roll 7–12 | Major fallout. Clear all stress from all tracks. Something has permanently changed. |

Stress dice are assigned by the GM based on severity: D4 for minor troubles, D6 for serious ones, D8 for genuinely dangerous situations, D10 for catastrophic ones. Protection — from equipment, abilities, or circumstances — reduces stress before it is marked.

---

## The Five Tracks

| Track | What It Represents |
|---|---|
| **BLOOD** | Physical damage: wounds, exhaustion, disease, drowning, fire, and the sea's many ways of killing a body. |
| **TIDE** | Supernatural contamination: the old things getting in, curse-exposure, and the cost of brushing what predates humanity. |
| **REPUTATION** | Social damage: your name and legend in the Shackles — your standing among the Free Captains and the fear you command. |
| **LUCK** | Fated misfortune: the universe collecting on audacious acts, recklessness, and the debt that piracy inevitably accrues. |
| **GOLD** | Material damage: your personal share of plunder — coin spent, prizes lost, debts called in, the cost of staying free. |

---

## Blood

*Pain, wounds, exhaustion, disease, and everything the sea does to a body*

Blood stress is the most immediate and legible harm in the Shackles. A cutlass opened your side. You've been swimming in cold water for two hours. The fever that started on the third day out won't break. You fell from the crow's nest and something in your back isn't right. The shark took two fingers and the wound is already going bad.

Blood is not just violence. It is the physical cost of a pirate's life in all its forms — the labour, the exposure, the deprivation, the thousand ways the sea and its inhabitants conspire to unmake a human body. Shock and fear that express through the body also land here — the adrenaline panic, the hands that won't stop shaking, the way a near-death experience rewires something physical in you.

### Common Causes

- Taking a wound from a blade, bullet, cannon shot, or unarmed strike
- Prolonged physical exertion without rest — rowing, climbing, swimming, hauling
- Exposure to extreme weather: storm, heat, cold, or the Eye's unnatural pressure
- Disease, infection, poison, venom, or contaminated water and food
- Falling from significant height — rigging, cliffs, masts
- Drowning, near-drowning, or extended submersion
- Surviving something terrifying — the body's response to shock and trauma
- Pushing through a serious wound to act anyway

### Fallout

**Minor Fallout** *(clear all stress in this track)*

- **Winded** — You're hurt and it shows. Until you rest, the GM can declare that any strenuous action is Risky. *[Ongoing]*
- **Bleeding** — A wound that needs binding. If you don't deal with it before the end of this scene, mark D4 Blood stress. *[Immediate]*
- **Fever** — Something has got in. You function but poorly. Roll two dice and pick the lower when you next mark stress to Blood. *[Ongoing]*
- **Limping** — Something in your leg. Any action requiring swift or precise movement is Risky until you are treated. *[Ongoing]*
- **Shaken** — You came very close to something final. Your hands aren't steady and your eyes keep going to the door. You cannot use Blood protection until you rest properly. *[Ongoing]*
- **Adrenaline** — Your body makes a decision before your mind catches up. The GM picks one immediate action your character takes — fight or flight — before you regain control. *[Immediate]*
- **Bad Scar** — The wound closes ugly. Work out with the GM what it is and where. It will matter to someone, eventually. *[Ongoing]*

**Major Fallout** *(clear all stress from all tracks. Something has changed.)*

- **Broken** — A bone, a joint, something structural. You cannot use the affected part of your body until treated by a surgeon. Work out with the GM what becomes impossible. *[Ongoing]*
- **Festering** — The wound went bad. Mark D6 Blood stress at the start of each scene until you receive real medical attention. This will kill you if ignored. *[Ongoing]*
- **Lost** — An eye, fingers, a hand — the GM decides what, based on the fiction. This is permanent. Work out together what it now means to act in the world. *[Ongoing]*
- **Organ Damage** — Internal and serious. Any time you push yourself physically, the GM may ask you to mark D4 Blood stress. Only surgery or powerful magic addresses this. *[Ongoing]*
- **Shell-Shocked** — Something in the experience broke your relationship to danger. Work out with the GM one type of situation you now find genuinely difficult to enter. It takes a story arc to address. *[Ongoing]*
- **Near Death** — You are out of the current situation entirely. When you return, you carry a permanent mark — a scar, a tremor, something gone. The GM will tell you what. *[Immediate]*
- **Dead** — You die. Character death is always a choice — if you'd rather not, downgrade this to Near Death. But the option is there. *[Immediate]*

### Clearing This Track

> Rest, food, water, and time clear Blood stress at port or during a ship recovery montage. A Ship's Surgeon can clear Minor fallout without downtime. Shock and fear that manifest as Blood fallout clear through the same physical recovery as wounds — the body and mind are not as separate as people prefer to believe. Major Blood fallout requires a port surgeon, magical healing, or significant recovery time. Some Major fallouts are permanent.

## Tide

*Supernatural contamination, the old things getting in, and the cost of brushing what predates humanity*

The Shackles is soaked in old power. The Eye of Abendego is not a natural storm. Ghol-Gan didn't simply disappear. The things in the deep water have been there longer than humanity and they have opinions. Tide stress is what happens when the supernatural world takes notice of you — when you spend too long in cursed waters, invoke forces you don't fully understand, or survive an encounter that was supposed to end you in a specific, spiritual way.

Tide is the most insidious track. Unlike Blood, it doesn't announce itself with pain. Sometimes Tide stress feels like luck, like clarity, like hearing something just at the edge of perception. It is only when fallout arrives that you understand you have been changed, and that the change was not yours to choose. Tide is not limited to sea-magic — a dragon's draconic presence, a wizard's reality-bending power, a demon-haunted warship — all of these touch the same deep-wrong place the sea does. Mark Tide.

### Common Causes

- Spending extended time in Ghol-Gan ruins or other pre-human sites of power
- Performing or witnessing significant ritual magic, especially sea-rites or storm-pacts
- Making a bargain with a supernatural entity — even a small one, even one you think was fair
- Surviving something that should have killed you in a way that felt arranged
- Invoking Besmara's name and having her actually respond
- Extended contact with a significantly cursed object or person
- Being the subject of a powerful wizard's direct magical attention
- Encountering creatures or forces that belong to a register of reality older than the human world

### Fallout

**Minor Fallout** *(clear all stress in this track)*

- **Salt Weeping** — Your eyes weep brine. It is not painful but it is visible. You cannot hide what you have been near, and some people will react badly to what they see. *[Ongoing]*
- **Compass Wrong** — Compasses near you point toward you rather than north. This makes navigation aboard your vessel Risky for as long as this fallout persists. *[Ongoing]*
- **Followed** — Something is keeping pace with the ship at a distance. It does not attack. It watches. The crew can feel it, and morale suffers for it. *[Ongoing]*
- **Voices at Depth** — You hear something at the edge of sleep — consistent, quiet, never quite repeatable. The GM will decide when it says something you can actually use. *[Ongoing]*
- **Nightmares** — The Tide visits you in sleep. At the start of each session until this clears, mark D4 Blood stress — the toll of nights spent in the wrong places. *[Ongoing]*
- **Cold Patch** — A cold spot follows you from room to room. Candles near you gutter. Experienced sailors will know what this means. *[Ongoing]*
- **Exodus** — You retch up a handful of wrong things — pallid moths, grubs with too many legs, something that shouldn't exist. Anyone who witnesses it marks D4 Blood stress from shock. *[Immediate]*

**Major Fallout** *(clear all stress from all tracks. Something has changed.)*

- **The Mark** — Something has marked you physically and visibly. Work with the GM on what it looks like and what it means to those who know how to read it. You cannot hide it. *[Ongoing]*
- **Compulsion** — You are required to do or avoid something specific. The compulsion is not violent, but it is absolute. You do not get to decide when it applies. The GM does. *[Ongoing]*
- **Traded Away** — Something of yours is gone — a memory, an emotion, the ability of certain beings to perceive you as human. The GM determines what has been taken and by whom. *[Ongoing]*
- **Perceived** — Something significant in the supernatural world now knows your name and face. It is not hostile. It has an interest in you. What that interest is will become clear in time. *[Ongoing]*
- **Obsessed** — Something the Tide showed or suggested has lodged. You will pursue it — a place, a name, a question — to the exclusion of good sense. The GM tells you what it is. This governs you until resolved. *[Ongoing]*
- **Changed** — Your body has changed in some permanent, supernatural way. Work with the GM on what this means in play — mechanically and socially. There is no reversing it without a major undertaking. *[Ongoing]*
- **Owned** — A specific entity has a valid claim on you. The nature of the claim is unclear but real. This is the beginning of an arc. How it ends depends on you. *[Immediate, Ongoing]*

### Clearing This Track

> Tide stress is the hardest track to clear and the only one that sometimes resists clearing entirely — sharing that quality with Luck, for different reasons. Rest and port do nothing. Ritual purification performed by someone with the Arcane or Faith domain can clear Minor Tide fallout. Major Tide fallout generally requires a full arc of story work — finding what marked you, understanding why, and making some kind of account. Some changes are permanent.

## Reputation

*Your name, your legend, and the fear and respect you command across the Shackles*

In the Shackles, who you are perceived to be is nearly as important as what you can actually do. Reputation is the store of fear, respect, and legend that a pirate accumulates — and loses. It determines whether captains will treat with you as an equal, whether merchants will yield or fight, whether the Free Captains council will hear you out or laugh you off the docks.

Unlike the other tracks, Reputation stress is not always bad in origin. Sometimes you take it from doing the right thing in a world where that reads as weakness. Sometimes it comes from being bested by someone — not because you were harmed, but because people saw it. Reputation fallout is social and institutional; it doesn't hurt your body but it can destroy your career.

### Common Causes

- Being publicly bested, outmaneuvered, or humiliated in front of witnesses who matter
- Failing visibly at something you are known for being good at
- Running from a fight that people expected you to take
- Breaking the Pirate Code in ways the community does not forgive
- Being caught in a significant lie by people who now hold the story
- Showing mercy or softness in situations where it reads as weakness
- Angering or making an enemy of a captain with more standing than you
- Having a major prize taken from you or a plan publicly fall apart

### Fallout

**Minor Fallout** *(clear all stress in this track)*

- **Whispers** — Someone is talking about what happened, and they are not being generous. You don't know who or exactly what yet. The GM knows. *[Immediate, Ongoing]*
- **Undercut** — Word of your difficulty has reached the fences and merchants. Your plunder fetches a poor price at the next port as people test how desperate you are. *[Immediate]*
- **Crew Restless** — Your crew have heard things. They follow orders but something is missing from it. The GM may treat the next Command action aboard ship as Risky without warning. *[Ongoing]*
- **Cold Reception** — A specific port, captain, or faction is noticeably less friendly than they were. Getting what you need from them requires demonstration of worth. *[Ongoing]*
- **Challenged** — Someone with something to prove has decided you are the right target. The GM will introduce them at a dramatically inconvenient moment. *[Immediate]*
- **Paranoid** — You cannot stop looking for the betrayal. Until this clears, the GM may add an extra layer of suspicion to any social interaction — you ask one too many questions, read hostility into friendliness. *[Ongoing]*
- **Rival Emboldened** — A named rival has heard about your difficulty and is moving. The GM tells you what they are doing; you may not be able to stop it in time. *[Immediate]*

**Major Fallout** *(clear all stress from all tracks. Something has changed.)*

- **Bounty** — A price is on your head. The GM tells you how much and who posted it. Legitimate ports and ambitious bounty hunters are now a constant factor. This does not go away on its own. *[Ongoing]*
- **Port Denial** — A specific major port — the GM chooses which — will not permit your ship to dock under your colours. Making port there requires a disguise, a favour, or a confrontation. *[Ongoing]*
- **Crew Lost** — Specific undercrew — the GM tells you who — leave at the next port. They have heard too much. Some may be persuadable back; others are gone. *[Immediate]*
- **Laughing Stock** — A story is circulating that makes you a figure of mockery in the Shackles. Lose one tier of Infamy. Any action that depends on being feared is Risky until you do something that changes the story. *[Ongoing]*
- **Betrayed** — Someone you trusted has done something that cannot be excused. This does not just damage your standing — it changes how you see people. Work out with the GM what you now find it hard to offer. *[Immediate, Ongoing]*
- **Council Censure** — The Free Captains have formally noted your transgression. Until you make account before the Council, specific pirate customs, ports, and codes no longer extend their protections to you. *[Ongoing]*
- **Name Destroyed** — Your reputation in a specific part of the Shackles is gone. Rebuild it through story — this is a multi-session undertaking, not a single roll. *[Ongoing]*

### Clearing This Track

> Reputation stress clears by doing impressive things in public — taking a significant prize, winning a fight against better odds, demonstrating competence in a way that spreads. A successful raid, a duel well-fought, or a bold move against a colonial target will clear Reputation stress without a roll if witnessed by people who matter. Major Reputation fallout requires dedicated story arc work.
>
> It also clears through the channels the system documents define: a **Carouse+[Domain] roll** (telling your own tale is Risky), **haunts**, transfer to **bonds** — your name lives in other people — and **donation: giving a quest reward or significant resource to a haunt counts as investing in it, and the donor refreshes Reputation equal to the amount rolled on the donated resource's dice** (see Ports & Haunts).

## Luck

*Fated misfortune: the universe collecting on recklessness, audacity, and the debt that piracy accrues*

Luck stress is what happens when you tempt fate and fate takes note. Every audacious plan pushed too far, every time you showed off when you should have stayed quiet, every near-miss that felt arranged — that is Luck stress accumulating. It does not hurt like Blood. It does not haunt like Tide. It simply builds, quietly, until the universe decides it is time to collect.

Luck is the thematic heart of the Shackles system. A pirate can heal wounds, drink away sorrow, rebuild a shattered reputation, earn back lost gold. But luck runs out. This is the track that tells the story of inevitable fate — not as tragedy, but as the natural consequence of a life lived spectacularly. Luck stress cannot be cleared by rest, by carousing, or by spending resources. It clears when the fallout fires — when something goes wrong in the world — and not before.

### Common Causes

- Showing off, boasting, or making a spectacle of yourself unnecessarily
- Pushing a plan further than it needed to go when it was already working
- Acting with unearned confidence and getting away with it — for now
- Desecrating a shrine, ignoring an omen, or disrespecting something that was owed respect
- Leaving a visible trail: a witness, a loose end, a story that will travel
- Invoking Besmara's name casually, as decoration rather than prayer
- Surviving something that should have killed you — the universe noticed
- Drawing the attention of powerful people who had not previously noticed you

### Fallout

**Minor Fallout** *(clear all stress in this track)*

- **Foreboding** — Something bad is coming. The GM hints at it — smoke on the horizon, a name mentioned twice, a ship that has been keeping pace since morning. This fallout can be upgraded to Crisis. *[Ongoing]*
- **Word of Mouth** — Word of your recent actions has travelled faster than you. Wherever you are headed next, someone knows you are coming and is planning accordingly. The GM shows you how. *[Immediate]*
- **Separated** — At a dramatically inconvenient moment, you find yourself cut off from your allies. You will have to reach them or hope they find you. *[Immediate]*
- **The Hard Way** — You led everyone into this the difficult way. The next significant obstacle the group faces is Risky where it would have been Standard, or Dangerous where it would have been Risky. *[Immediate]*
- **In Trouble** — You have upset someone important — a captain, a faction, a merchant with powerful friends. You do not know exactly who yet. The GM knows. *[Immediate, Ongoing]*
- **Unlucky** — Things are going to get worse before they get better. You cannot use Luck protection until this fallout is cleared. *[Ongoing]*

**Major Fallout** *(clear all stress from all tracks. Something has changed.)*

- **Crisis** — The foreboding arrives. Something goes badly wrong in a way that directly implicates your recent recklessness. The GM frames it. You do not get to prepare. *[Immediate]*
- **Collateral** — Your luck spills onto someone near you. The next time any ally marks stress, they mark double. You remove this fallout when it fires — but you do not get to choose who it hits. *[Ongoing]*
- **Long Way Round** — You arrive just late enough. Whatever you needed to prevent, intervene in, or reach in time — you missed it by exactly enough that it matters. The GM tells you what the gap cost. *[Immediate]*
- **The Tab** — A debt you thought was forgotten has come due — not necessarily financial. Someone you wronged, slighted, or escaped from has decided the time is right. They are already moving. *[Immediate, Ongoing]*
- **Wrong Place** — You take something — a blow, an accusation, a consequence — that was meant for someone else. That person is unharmed. You are not. The GM decides what it is. *[Immediate]*
- **Fool's Gold** — Something you thought you had secured — a prize, an alliance, a safe harbour, a secret kept — turns out to be exactly what it seemed too good to be. The GM reveals what was false and what it now costs you. *[Immediate]*

### Clearing This Track

> Luck stress cannot be cleared by rest, medical attention, carousing, or earning money. It clears only when fallout fires — the misfortune happening is how the stress resolves. Between fallouts, Luck stress simply accumulates. Certain class abilities, divine favours from Besmara, and specific rare story outcomes can clear Luck stress directly, but these are exceptional. Luck is the track that does not forgive. It is the mechanical expression of the game's central promise: the good times are real, and so is what comes after.

## Gold

*Your personal share of plunder — coin spent, prizes lost, debts called in, and the cost of staying free*

A pirate lives and dies by what they've got. Gold stress represents the erosion of your personal material position — the share of prizes that didn't come through, the coin spent or stolen or lost to bad luck and worse cards, the debt that came due, the equipment that broke at the worst possible time, the bribe that had to be paid. Gold is the texture of financial precarity.

Gold in pirate mythology is never just money — it's freedom, it's proof, it's what you risked everything for and what runs through your fingers in port and is gone by morning. Gold stress is personal. A pirate with their Gold track intact can drink, bribe, equip, and barter their way through most problems. A pirate running on empty is desperate, and desperation makes you predictable.

### Common Causes

- A prize vessel sinking before it could be properly looted or brought to port
- Your personal share being short-changed, stolen, or gambled away
- Paying a significant bribe, fine, or toll to avoid a worse fate
- Equipment being destroyed, lost, or rendered unusable in the field
- A debt being called in — by a fence, a banker, a former ally, or a motivated enemy
- Being robbed, pickpocketed, or conned in port
- Losing a significant wager, trade, or investment
- Covering the costs of someone else's crisis when there was no other option

### Fallout

**Minor Fallout** *(clear all stress in this track)*

- **Light Pockets** — You're short and it shows. Non-essential purchases are not possible. Accessing services and port haunts costs a favour instead of coin. *[Ongoing]*
- **Equipment Damaged** — One piece of gear — the GM chooses which — is damaged. It functions, but poorly. The GM may treat its use as Risky at a dramatically appropriate moment. *[Ongoing]*
- **Creditor Circling** — Someone you owe has found out where you're sailing. They will make contact at the next port. What they want can be negotiated; whether you can afford it is another question. *[Immediate]*
- **Priced Out** — You cannot afford something you need before the situation resolves. Find another way, pay in kind, or go without. The GM decides what it costs you to proceed. *[Immediate]*
- **Reputation Barter** — You covered a material gap by spending social capital. Mark D4 Reputation stress as well as clearing this fallout. *[Immediate]*
- **Debt Accrued** — You have borrowed against future earnings to stay afloat. The GM notes who holds this debt. It will be called in at a time of their choosing. *[Ongoing]*

**Major Fallout** *(clear all stress from all tracks. Something has changed.)*

- **Broke** — You have nothing liquid. No coin, no useful equipment beyond what you carry. Accessing port services or any resource costs a favour, a secret, or a sacrifice. *[Ongoing]*
- **Seized** — Something significant has been taken — by a creditor, a rival, or an authority with leverage. A named piece of gear or a stash you cared about is gone. The GM decides what, and it does not come back easily. *[Immediate]*
- **Indentured** — You owe someone substantial labour or service to clear a debt. This is not optional and cannot simply be refused. Work out with the GM what it requires and for how long. *[Ongoing]*
- **Sold Out** — To cover your position, you gave up something that was not entirely yours to give — information, access, a crewmate's secret. The consequences will arrive. The GM tracks when. *[Immediate, Ongoing]*
- **Stripped** — Your share of the last prize is forfeit — by crew vote, captain's decision, or something you did that voided your claim. The circumstances are humiliating. The loss is real. *[Immediate]*
- **Marked for Collection** — A specific and dangerous creditor — a Free Captain, a guild enforcer, a Chelish privateer with a writ — has decided to collect from you personally. They are coming. *[Immediate, Ongoing]*

### Clearing This Track

> Gold stress clears through successful plunder. A good prize, a lucrative contract, a successful trade, or a debt settled clears Gold stress in proportion to the material gain. Port carousing and gambling can clear Minor Gold stress but risk adding more. Major Gold fallout requires a proper windfall — a significant prize, a found stash, or a successful long con — to resolve.
