# MONUMENT

*Most pirates take. You are making something — out of ships, stories, favours and fear — and every port that knows your name is a course of bricks laid true. One day the work will stand without you. The trick is living long enough to step back and see it.*

## CORE ABILITY

**BRICK BY BRICK.** You strive to live up to the thing you are building. When you gain a minor advance, refresh D6. When you gain a major advance, refresh D8.

When you create your character, answer these questions:

- What are you building, and what do you call it in your honest moments?
- Choose another player character. They hold up part of the structure without knowing it. What part?
- Choose another player character. They knew you when you were nobody. What do you need from them now?
- Which port already tells a story about you, and how wrong is it?

## MINOR BEATS

- Take Minor Reputation fallout.
- Be mistaken for something greater than you are.
- Publicly humiliate a rival, effortlessly.
- Make a speech meant for the retelling.
- Hear your story told by someone who doesn't know you're listening.
- Refuse to retreat — the ground is yours now.
- Show mercy because the story of it is worth more than the kill.
- Have a ship change course at the sight of your flag.
- Donate a D8 or higher resource to a haunt, loudly.
- Take credit for another pirate's work.
- Plant influence in a port you've never held: a favour, a debt, a flag.
- Dictate terms to someone holding the upper hand.
- Acquire a bond who owes you something they can't repay.
- Describe the finished work to another PC.
- Choose a person over the structure, and let a brick fall.
- Find your name somewhere you never put it.
- Pick a side in a port dispute because the outcome serves the work.
- Hear yourself used as the measure of someone else.
- Pay to make a story go away.
- Commission a song, a broadsheet or a rumour about yourself.

## MAJOR BEATS

- Take Major Reputation fallout.
- Enter an enemy power's records: a Chelish mandate, an Aspis dossier.
- Win formal recognition from the Free Captains or the Pirate Council.
- Take and hold a berth, a route or an allegiance against opposition.
- Upgrade a haunt to D10 or higher and put your name on it.
- Make another PC load-bearing, and tell them what that means.
- Watch a piece of the structure fall in public.
- Learn that someone you trusted has been hollowing the walls.
- Be offered legitimacy — marque, pardon, seat — and decide if it's a brick or a trap.
- Win the thing you reached for, and feel the foundation crack beneath it.

## ZENITH BEATS

- Step back from what you built, and watch it stand without you.
- Tear it down yourself, before anyone else can take it.

Roll 1d10 on the following table or choose to determine what you carry with you to mark your calling:

1. Flag of your own design, never yet flown
2. Custom seal and a stick of black wax
3. Ship's manifest naming you owner of a vessel you don't yet possess
4. Bundle of captains' debts tied in red cord
5. Broadsheet about you, wrong in three ways you've never corrected
6. Ledger of your deeds, with dates and witnesses
7. Portrait of you, commissioned at great expense, that doesn't quite look like you
8. The Pirate Code, annotated with your corrections
9. Key to a lock you haven't found yet
10. An unsigned proclamation you are not yet ready to make
