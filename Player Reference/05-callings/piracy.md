# PIRACY

*The ship, the sea, the prize, the port, the Code. Whatever else drives you, this is the life you all chose, and it keeps its own ledger. Every crew gets a story. The sea decides how long it runs; you decide what it is about.*

Every character holds Piracy alongside their personal calling. At the end of each session, choose two active beats from either pool.

When you create your crew, answer these questions together:

- What is your ship, and what did it cost to get her?
- What was the crew's first prize together, and what went wrong?
- Which port counts as home, and who there wishes you wouldn't?
- What enemy already knows your flag?

## MINOR BEATS

- Take Minor Blood fallout.
- Take Minor Gold fallout.
- Take Minor Luck fallout.
- Take Minor Reputation fallout.
- Survive a storm, a rogue wave or the Eye's weather.
- Make the navigational call the crew must live with.
- Outrun a superior sail — or turn and face it.
- Board a prize and deal with what's actually aboard.
- Complete a journey through Tier 2 waters or deeper.
- Stand the night watch alone, and come in changed.
- Acquire a bond in a port: fence, keeper, harbourmaster.
- Be recognised for something you did — or didn't.
- Find trouble ashore that has nothing to do with your obsessions.
- Pass a port inspection with something to hide.
- Drink a port dry and stay welcome.
- Invoke the Code, win or lose.
- Share a dock with a rival crew without incident — barely.
- Be asked to do something the Code allows and your stomach doesn't.
- Watch the Free Captains' politics up close.
- Take a prize and learn what it's actually worth.
- Divide shares, and feel the division bite.
- Lose a prize to weather, pursuit or a fair decision.
- Sell loot to someone who knows where it came from.

## MAJOR BEATS

- Take Major Blood fallout, and keep sailing.
- Take Major Reputation fallout, as the Shackles reassesses you.
- Survive what should have killed you, with the crew watching.
- Draw the formal attention of the Chelish Navy, the Aspis Consortium or a rival Free Captain.
- Lose a ship.
- Make an enemy with the standing to matter.
- Tip the politics of the Shackles, even slightly.
- Gain or lose enough to change the ship's quality or a haunt's dice.
- Hear your deed told wrong in a port you've never visited.
- Choose between the Code and your own judgement, lastingly.

## ZENITH BEATS

- Carve the crew into the Shackles for good — a council seat, a free port, a flag that sails on without you.
- Lose the ship, the sea or each other, and scatter the way all crews end.

Roll 1d10 on the following table or choose to determine what you carry with you to mark your calling:

1. The crew's mark, tattooed somewhere you chose carefully
2. A share-token from your first prize, drilled and worn
3. Your copy of the articles, signed by every hand
4. Dice carved from the mast of a ship that didn't make it
5. A storm-glass that has never once been wrong
6. One of the three keys to the spirit locker
7. A pistol ball they dug out of you, kept as change
8. Your dead predecessor's hat — it fits
9. An IOU from the quartermaster, gathering interest
10. A chart of home waters, corrected in your own blood at least once
